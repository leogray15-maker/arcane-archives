You grow and you get stuck for a while

Who you are inside determines who you are outside, and that determines what you can do and what you have.

This is true because we must "be" (inside, who we are) before we can "do, (before we can dare to have capability)" and we can "do" only to the extent which we "are, (goes back to who we are limiting what we can do)" and what we "are" depends upon what we "think."

Who you are is a trick, and a trap.

Biggest trap of your life.

Any time you define you confine.

I am __________ means I am not anything but that blank.

I am a copywriter, which means I am not an offer owner, CRO, etc...

This doesn't ALWAYS have to be true, but most of the time it's true.

If a copywriter makes say $10,000 avg.

That means based on that identity, their mind will be CONFINED to those limitations.

And that's who you BECOME, so you go in a state of BEING.

o you can't BECOME more than you ARE If you say "I'm a copywriter = I'm not anything else"

You need OTHER things to be added for you to grow.

Whenever we buy things, achieve things, etc...we have to clearly define them and if we make them part of who we are, that confines us and our growth.

The key is to create separation from the things we have, do and achieve from WHO we are.

We must be greater than that of which we have and external identifiers.

If we are not, growth becomes very difficult and we stay stuck for 6-18 months before we can let go and move up.

Instead of your mind looking at GROWTH, it looks at KEEPING it.

Because KEEPING is preventing LOSS.

Loss and gain in the mind.

Most of us buy things to become someone else.

To be defined as that new, and then we end up being confined.

Most brand marketing is based on the identity of them becoming someone else.

Porsche, lambo, ferrari, rolex, patek, AP, LV, Hermes, etc...

If you did not become someone else with these products you would not want them.

I have a life, but I'm not my life.

In my life, I have a home, a business, but I'm not my home or business.

I have a partner, but I'm not my partner.

I have behaviors, but I'm not my behaviors.

I have thoughts, but I am not my thoughts.

I have a supercar, but I'm not my super car.

How often do I find myself thinking about the car = the more, the more confined I am, because thinking is a problem so it owns me, instead of me owning it.

Do you ever think about the forks you have in your kitchen?

Whenever you identify with something, you automatically identify with its limitations.

It imposes limitations on you, because limitations are part loss.

Laziness

Firstly, PUT this in your mind now.

You are NOT a lazy person.

This isn't some personality trait you were born with, it's not who you're doomed to be forever, and you CAN fix it.

Once again, you are not a lazy person.

You may have acted lazy in the past, you may be acting lazy now, but that does not define who you are.

Now that that's been established, let's dive into why you're acting lazy and how to fix it.

Reason #1: You have no momentum

Momentum is everything.

When you have momentum you have a clear direction, a sense of purpose, and positive energy that you use to achieve your goals.

Momentum is the ultimate flow-state.

It's incredibly easy to keep going when things are hot, you've piled up some wins in a row and you're in a rhythm.

Let’s have a look at a professional athlete who plays basketball.

Getting hot in a basketball game is a real thing, just like it is in real life with your goals.

Micheal Jordan was one of the best basketball players of all time.

Everytime he threw the ball in the net he gained momentum and he used that to keep scoring big game changing shots.

So how can YOU do what Micheal Jordan does?

The hardest part of establishing momentum, and the reason most people never experience it is that they never start.

And if they do start, they never persevere to the point that they see any small wins to help establish a positive feedback loop.

The best example for describing this is fitness.

If you're brand new to the gym it's significantly harder to get yourself to go than it is for someone who has already been going regularly for a month and has lost some weight and seen some progress.

Person A starts going to the gym, doesn't take it all that seriously, and misses a few workouts while still not eating well.

By week 3 they haven't lost any weight, the gym is hard and unrewarding, they're sore, and eventually, they quit by the end of the month.

Person B starts going to the gym and understands habit formation and establishing momentum is the goal initially, not perfection.

They hit all their lifts, still aren't perfect on the diet but are 50% better, and week by week the numbers slowly go down on the scale.

Person B is going to have a significantly easier time continuing on their fitness journey than person A who is still frustrated.

Their habits are starting to be established.

Although they weren't perfect, they pushed through enough to see some wins in the form of weight loss.

Wins themselves are the motivation that pushes you to continue.

Searching for motivation isn't nearly as effective as being motivated by progress.

Whether it's dropping a little weight on the scale, your first sales meeting booked, or even your first Shopify sale wins empower you.

As these wins start to add up, you become more naturally motivated to do the stuff that used to be hard for you to do.

Now you naturally want to go to the gym, or it's easier to say no to that cookie because the vision and end goal has become clearer and more obtainable.

Eventually, you have momentum.

You're stacking up small wins left and right, and even seeing some major progress compared to where you started.

You have a clear goal, you know what needs to be done to achieve it, and you're excited and filled with energy to do so.

Reason #2: You have too many distractions around you

It may feel like a meme at this point, but social media, porn, Netflix, TV, fantasy football, etc are all MAJOR distractions.

Your dopamine baseline is a mess and it's making focusing on what needs to be done impossible.

I won't spend too much time on this because there are a ton of resources out there talking about dopamine

A few quick steps you can take:

1) Delete all apps that distract you

2) Use grayscale

3) Put your phone on Do Not Disturb and put it in another room while working

Reason #3: You don't know what you want or what your goals are

If you have no idea what you actually want to achieve, it makes escapism incredibly attractive because you have no clarity.

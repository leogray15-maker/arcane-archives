The Creative Process Behind Big Ideas.

This is a bit of an advanced / high level section. I hope this gives you all some ideas and direction. :)

Have you ever seen brands like Naval, Mark Manson, Jack Butcher and others just to be consumed by the depth behind their ever-so-simple ideas?

The ideas that make you stop and think, "Damn, I wish I wrote that..."

The ideas that stick in your head for eternity — making you market their brand through word of mouth?

This is also why some books do better than others. It is why you have your favorite books that you always recommend to your friends.

It isn't the quality of their writing, it is the quality of their ideas.

The best one-liner posts are big ideas — but few have actual depth behind them. This is the next step in building a highly authoritative brand. Not just generating good ideas, but developing good ideas into big ideas.

You may have noticed that my newsletters, ideas, and depth/articulation of concepts on the podcast have had a considerable jump in quality. This is due to my creative workflow. Moreso, this is due to me working LESS. I will break it all down here.

Idea Generation

We've talked about this many times. You generate good ideas by consuming good content and noting down what sticks out to you.

Books, podcasts, X threads, articles, audiobooks, and content from those you aspire to be like.

Time must be scheduled for consumption. Instead of thinking of it as consumption — think of it as research.

Content creation is 80% research, 20% creating content. (GOOD content, that is).

The ideas that spark excitement — dopamine — as you are "researching" are the things you should put in your notes. You must get in the habit of writing everything down that sticks out to you.

You must also schedule time for nothingness or no thought. Meditative activities have the potential to push you into a flow state. Self-competition.

Martial arts. The gym. Meditation. Disc golf (been playing this a lot recently). Yoga. Running. Swimming. Sunbathing. Long walks. Nature bathing. Hiking. Etc.

This is more important than you think. "Idea generation" time — or active recovery — takes up much more time in my day than actual focused work does.

Start replacing those negative habits with some of these meditative activities.

While you are doing these activities, better ideas will pop into your head (thank you subconscious mind!)

Take note of these immediately when they come to mind.

I have a running note in my phone that I call "The Queue" for all of my research notes and ideas that pop into my head as I'm going about my day.

Idea Development

Consumption = good ideas.

Active recovery = better ideas.

Now we must develop these into BIG ideas. The ideas that we will be known for. Here's how I do it.

I have scheduled time in my day to go through The Queue.

You can use whatever note-taking software you want. Obsidian, Evernote, or Notion are probably your best bet.

Personally, I would treat these as training wheels. Over time you will develop your own way of writing, deciphering, and turning your notes into big ideas.

Here is how I have been doing it:

  1. All of my notes, insights, and ideas are inside Kortex drafts of quick-capture
  1. I scroll through and pick something that interests me, or just start going down and organizing them
  1. I expand on that note with my personal insights and summary of the idea
  1. Personal insights are experiences I've had with that idea and WHY it is so relevant to my life. The summary is just me expanding on it to get more ideas flowing
  1. After I have 2-3 paragraphs of information — I read through it and summarize the entire thing as a one-sentence BIG idea as an Element
  1. Then, I add in links or connections to other relevant notes or big ideas and start to go back through my drafts
  1. I can start the process over again or find things that relate to that big idea to develop it further.

Now, when it’s time to create content, you have an incredible concept with a lot of depth behind it.

This is how you create a week’s worth of content without trying. Some call this their "second brain" or similar.

What can you do with these big ideas?

Use them as content inspiration or just let them stack up.

I personally write a good newsletter, write posts along the way, use the newsletter as a YouTube script, and have those newsletters saved for later when I want to start releasing blog content

Simple Frameworks & Systems

To take this even further, start to combine your big ideas or turn them into concepts (using Elements in Kortex) that you can make your own.

"Personal Monopoly" is a big idea from Jack Butcher — the HOW behind this big idea is his framework or system. Now, I haven't taken his course, but I am sure this concept is brought up and broken down in a simple, step-by-step process.

One of my big ideas from the other day was "You can turn negative thoughts, feelings, and emotions into a positive potential outcome in life."

As I was writing out my summary (what) and personal experiences (why), I began formulating a framework (how).

I called it the Conscious Conditioning Process.

I can talk about this process whenever I am asked about "how to deal with negative thoughts and emotions."

I am writing about this in my book.

Hell, I could even create a product on this alone. (People buy systems that get results.)

People will start to attribute this process to me, word will spread, and my brand will indirectly grow because of it.

The frameworks/systems you create, name, and tie to yourself are what you will be remembered for. People will pass these along.

Why? Behavior change.

This is the only thing that matters. "What's" and "why's" are good for content, they grab attention. But people want to implement the "how."

The "what" gets them interested, the "how" gets them results (that they attribute to you), the "why" gets them to take action (because it is emotion-based).

If your framework gets them results, they will tell a friend about it. Your name will spread and you will start getting tagged on social media when other people are talking about similar topics.

Creating These In A Productive Way

You should have a product / project you are working on that you can tie all of these ideas back to.

Since this research, nothingness, and idea development time is worked into my days — my current book, future book, and future products are creating themselves.

I let my curiosity guide me to learn and implement new things. Then I just turn them into content quickly.

30. I Give Myself to You, My World

When you think about your relationships, your interactions, and your desires in the world, what’s usually at the core of it? We tend to focus on what we want. "I want respect, I want love, I want attention, I want success..." These are the things we are often asking from the people around us, whether consciously or subconsciously. It’s natural—we are human, after all, and we have needs and desires. But here's where it gets interesting: the key to receiving what you want from others actually comes from shifting your focus away from yourself and towards them.

Instead of constantly thinking, “What do I want from this person?” ask yourself, “What does this person want ?” Get curious about their needs, their desires, what makes them feel seen, valued. This shift in focus is powerful because, in a way, the energy we put into understanding others reflects back onto us. If you’re focused on fulfilling someone else’s desires, you begin to form a connection with them, and they, in turn, start to align their actions toward fulfilling your desires. Even if they aren’t consciously aware of it, there’s an energy exchange happening. This is what’s meant by external intention—the force that aligns with the needs of others, drawing back the same energy to you.

You’ve probably heard the saying, "What you give is what you get." It’s not just about the physical actions or gifts you give—it’s about the intention behind them. When you help others achieve happiness, you indirectly set the stage for your own happiness. People are far more likely to support and give to those who show a genuine interest in their well-being, who help them reach their goals, and who make them feel good.

It’s like a law of nature: you feed into others, and they feed back into you. Think of it as an exchange of positive energy, even if it’s unspoken. You may think that by asking for help, you’re focusing on what you need. But by giving your attention to helping others get what they want, you open the door for them to turn their attention to you, responding to your needs in ways you may not have expected.

The trick here is to truly connect with others in a way that honors their needs as much as your own. So, the next time you want something from someone—whether it’s guidance, respect, or support—stop and ask, “What does this person want? What can I do to make them happy?” By helping them fulfill their desires, you align the Universe to work in your favor. The people around you will start to reciprocate, often without even realizing it, and you’ll receive what you’ve been asking for all along. It’s a beautiful cycle of mutual exchange.

This principle works not only with close relationships but also in every interaction you have, from business connections to casual encounters. When you make it your intention to serve others in the same way you want to be served, you naturally attract the help, love, and respect that you seek. This is how the world works. This is how you create a flow of abundance that doesn’t just start with you, but moves through you to others and comes back to you. It’s all interconnected.

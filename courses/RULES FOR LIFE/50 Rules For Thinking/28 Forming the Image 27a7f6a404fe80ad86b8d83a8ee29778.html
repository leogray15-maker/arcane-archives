28. Forming the Image

Alright, let's break this down.

So, you’ve got this desire, this goal, and you’re staring at the gap between where you are and where you want to be. The answer seems so simple, yet so many struggle with it: repetition. The simple act of constantly, intentionally shaping and focusing on your mental image every single day.

But why is it so effective? Because this isn't about one-off, sporadic bursts of inspiration or action—it’s about consistency. When you keep bringing that vision into your mind, day after day, it starts to mold your reality in subtle ways. But here's where the catch is: people often don’t see immediate results, and that’s where they give up. You might not believe it’s this simple, but it is: mental work every single day is what shifts things. Not just a couple of times, but a consistent, unwavering focus on what you want, despite what’s happening around you.

Your thoughts are incredibly powerful—they have the ability to shape your physical world, but only if you can maintain that focus, and the patience to wait for things to shift. Changes in the world around you take time, and when you’re trying to bring about big changes, you’re not just doing mental work for a week or two. You’re in it for the long haul. As Zeland puts it: there's no magic here, no grand miracle—there’s just solid, daily work to be done. Do the work, and you will eventually reach your goal. Don’t do the work, and you won’t. It’s that simple. But where it becomes tricky is that people expect it to be easier than it is. They expect instant gratification, but transformation doesn’t work that way.

What’s really happening in this process? Zeland highlights the concept of external intention , which is this incredible force that aligns you with your goal when your mind and your heart are in harmony. When your thoughts and your emotions aren’t in conflict, when they’re both aligned toward the same purpose, a powerful energy is released that moves you through time and space to your goal. It’s like being in the right flow of life—your intentions are clear, your energy is directed, and your path is made. But, and here’s the paradox: most of the time, we end up achieving what we don’t want. Why? Because our minds create these fears, doubts, and overthinking, breaking that harmonious flow. The mind and soul are meant to protect us, to keep us alive, but their protection can sometimes work against us. They both tune in to our biggest fears, and, ironically, this is what they end up manifesting.

You can feel this happening: you think about your goal, but then doubts creep in—what if I fail? What if it doesn’t work out? What if I’m not good enough? The mind starts to overthink, and in that moment, the focus shifts from a positive, future vision to your fears. The energy you pour into those fears will eventually start to materialize those very things you don’t want.

remember what I said before?

you feel doubt, then you hit the gym, or create a piece of content, and all the sudden the vision comes back.

So how do you break this cycle? You need to redirect your focus. Instead of feeding your fears, you must begin focusing on positivity. You must imagine yourself already achieving your goal—see yourself happy, fulfilled, and at peace. Stop letting the doubts and negative thoughts hijack your energy. Don’t give life to them. Redirect everything into the positive, into the belief that your goal is already happening.

Now, let’s bring this into practice: daily, consistent visualization. Every day, you imagine your goal as if it’s already done. You feel it, you breathe it, you live it. It becomes part of your reality, even if nothing has changed in the physical world yet. Over time, as you keep this practice up, that vision becomes clearer, stronger, more real—and eventually, it starts to show up in your life.

Patience is key here. It’s not about expecting things to shift overnight. It’s about understanding that you’re laying the foundation, day by day, piece by piece, for the life you want to create. Every time you visualize, you send out that energy into the world, and over time, that energy will return to you. The more consistent you are, the clearer the vision becomes, and the closer you move to that goal. But here’s the kicker: if you break that cycle, if you allow fear and doubt to take over, you disrupt the flow. So the goal is to keep going. Every day, maintain that vision. No matter what’s happening around you, no matter how things look in the physical world, stay focused on that mental image of your success.

The work is simple but powerful. Consistency in your thoughts. Daily practice. And a refusal to let your fears define your path. Your thoughts shape your reality—make sure they’re always aligned with what you truly want. Keep that mental image strong, and the Universe will start to reflect it back to you.

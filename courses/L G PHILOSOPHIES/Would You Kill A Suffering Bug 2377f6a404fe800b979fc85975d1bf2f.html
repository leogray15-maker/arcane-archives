Would You Kill A Suffering Bug?

if you saw one… would you do it?

this is an odd predicament I’ve faced a couple times.

and I’m oddly philosophical, so I question the decision.

I’ve thought both decisions both.

but recently, the answer has became clear…

I mean, why would you kill something that is just going through some suffering?

to “put it out it’s misery”?

it seems like a good deed… but here’s the question.

is death better than a miserable life?

it’s eternal suffering, or just nothingness.

I mean, nothingness is just nothingness.

but here’s the deep question.

you’ll experience that nothingness sooner or later.

so is there a meaning go through the suffering?

because you’ll experience the nothingness sooner or later.

I mean, you’re not going to remember any of this anyway.

so… would you cut it’s life short?

the reality of this, is that there is no right answer.

because it depends on what the bug wants.

it could just want to experience life as it is, in suffering, because there’s only one chance.

but at the same time, there will be no meaning to it and it will not remember any of it.

I mean, the bug doesn’t even think about shit like this.

it just works and fulfils the script.

it doesn’t have time to think about it.

it’s probably struggling with rent.

it’s probably just stuck in a cycle.

sounds oddly familiar.

the only difference between you and them?

you have a weekend.

2 days that lasts 1 day.

do you not get this? you’re an injured bug in my eyes.

and there’s no hospitals that can save you, since no one cares about a little bug.

only the bug can heal himself.

I’m higher on maslow’s hierarchy of needs, I’m just passing you down my thoughts.

I only can think about these philosophies because I’m not a suffering bug anymore.

but you? you need to escape it.

if you don’t change.

you’ll be a suffering bug till death.

you won’t remember any of this, so why are you going to stay a worker for the other people that will not remember any of this either?

I mean, you can buy a little BMW when you’re 65 and spend half your pension on it…

If you’ve not entered nothingness yet.

I mean, it’s your choice.

it’s a gamble, but the biggest gamble is hoping not to die before you can experience life.

it doesn’t matter anyway, you won’t remember it.

remember, it’s the bugs choice.

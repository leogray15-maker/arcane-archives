What Do You Even Sacrifice To Reach Success?

what do you even sacrifice to reach success?

you have to "sacrifice everything" for success, but what is everything?

I'll just list some things...

your skin will become pale because of the lack of sunlight.

you will look like death because of the constant stress.

you will always have some type of FOMO, especially when in your teens/20s.

and there's much more, let's talk about it...

so... let's get one thing straight.

your life is simply made up of what you focus on the most.

focus is a finite currency.

a currency that "levels up" something in particular.

for example... you have 24 hours in your day.

if you spend 2 hours a day reading, you are levelling up your intelligence skill.

that is happening because you are spending your "currency" of focus on reading.

and with bad habits...

if you spend "focus" on a bad habit, for example tiktok...

you are just spending your limited currency into thin air.

it just goes to nowhere, no benefits.

so... with this theory.

how do you become successful as quick as possible?

because that's what we want, right?

I mean, the answer is pretty simple.

you just spend more "focus" per day into success.

this can be either reading books, doing work, things of that nature.

you see... I can see exactly where you are going in your life if you just tell me what you do in a day.

I mean, it's a law of the universe, each reaction has an equal and opposite reaction.

if you do the correct things for success... for example working and learning...

you are going to reach success, it's common sense.

so, let me ask you a question...

is your life right now where you want it to be?

no? well that's a shame.

but you know why it's not, right...?

it's because for the past couple years you've spent your finite resource of "focus" in the wrong areas.

if you had spent it in the right areas and cut out all of the distractions around that, you would be where you wanted to be...

which brings me back to the main point...

what do you actually sacrifice for success?

the answer is simple.

everything that doesn't include success.

to become successful, you cut out everything that doesn't include success.

if your "focus" is spent anywhere else but to that goal of success, you are wasting your time.

of course, rest days are productive.

go for a night in the shisha lounge, smoke a cigar, cool off with some women.

but you only do that for a "mental reset", so you don't mess up your productivity.

but in a way... that's still kind of having your "focus" spent on success.

you are having a rest just because you know it will avoid burnout, leading for you to reach success quicker.

in short, you need to literally sacrifice everything that isn't correlated to success.

just imagine this...

you are living in a straight line, that straight line ends up at success.

anything that takes you off from that line of success.. you need to bring yourself back.

you can willingly go off the line of success for a little holiday, or just a rest break.

but you do that with the intention to stay on that line of success.

you do not quit, you just take breaks.

and you sacrifice everything that isn't work, or a break that is aimed at helping your work.

basically, you sacrifice everything.

but honestly... it's common sense to do it.

you stay down low for 7-10 years.

and you come out with everything you've ever wanted before, and more.

that's when the sacrifice pays off.

I mean, the time is gonna pass anyway, why not spend it reaching your ultimate dreams?

I read this book called efficiency by the wall street playboys, and it changed my perception on this.

they say you sacrifice everything until your finances are ticked off, then you go and get everything back up to scratch.

you go to the gym, eat a clean diet (for your performance in work and healthy body)

and you work.

that's it, that's your life until you're successful.

you don't do anything else, that's it.

literally, that's it.

work, gym, clean diet, breaks so you don't go insane.

rinse and repeat.

it's easy to say, but it's hard when you see your friends go out with women, have fun, etc.

but... that's a small price to pay for everything you've ever wanted.

sacrifice now for later.

I don't mean that in the common "self improvement" way of doing it, i hate the word.

but I mean it in the grand scheme of things.

take it as a life lesson, not a tactic.

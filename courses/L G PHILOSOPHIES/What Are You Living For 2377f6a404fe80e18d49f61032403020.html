What Are You Living For?

money doesn't buy happiness.

money buys freedom.

so the question is, does freedom brings happiness?

and the answer is not what you think.

after you are freed, you are likely to go on holidays and live the "laptop lifestyle"

or you go and buy a car that you wanted.

sure, what's next?

did you think this far?

here's a question I asked myself a year back.

if you had everything you wanted, what would you do?

the question makes you question your life because you've never thought this far.

this is where you realise all materialistic possessions aren't even real.

you're probably 16-25 reading this, so you're due to get all of those things.

but what happens after you get all of those things?

you're not going to sit on a beach for the rest of your life fulfilling dropshipping orders.

you're not going to spend every second of your day in your fast car with a loud exhaust.

you're going to make an instagram post showing off your new boujee lifestyle, get some numbers and pictures on your screen with random people that you don't even know saying they liked your photo.

if this is what you're currently living for, it's most likely to get back at someone.

so the question is, what do you do all of this for?

you'll retire your mother, that's a nice goal.

but what after that?

everything in this world is finite.

you will enjoy your dates with the 10/10 eastern european models, but that will get boring after a while.

you will enjoy your m4 competition go fast for a while, but what really brings the enjoyment in that?

the adrenaline of the launch? the risk of death? or the loud noises that come out the back that make people turn their heads then forget about you in 5 seconds?

you're living for a finite resource, I would think your ambition was greater than that.

there's only one thing that isn't finite in this world.

and it all stems down to this quote.

“all I know is that I know nothing.” - Socrates.

the only thing that isn't finite is knowledge.

so am I saying you should chase knowledge? I'm not sure, but that's what I am doing.

the dopamine and from learning new things puts me in a flow state.

but you may say... can't you get into a flow state doing anything?

the graph of Csikszentmihalyi's flow state says if there is  an equal challenge to your skill level in something it will be a flow state.

but what is the difference between a flow state in a video game vs a flow state in learning new things?

a video game can get boring.

if money and status was no object, you would chase new information.

in other words, you're chasing good dopamine and a flow state.

a flow state that can never end for you, that is your purpose.

the definition of your purpose is something that you work on all day for your whole life without getting bored.

you can only be interested in something if it raises your dopamine level.

and dopamine works on novelty, which means you need constant new stimuli.

and everything is finite, you will get bored of everything.

but not new knowledge, you are never omniscient.

however, this always comes back to this disturbing point.

we live for flow states.

the most depressed day of the week for people is a sunday, since that is where your rest is about to end, but you also have no work.

it's you and your mind.

but a flow state occurs when you forget your mind is a thing.

so, are we living and pursuing a "purpose" to just get away from our minds?

a constant flow state is what we desire, but there's a couple reasons behind it.

I mean, I don't know them.

all i know is that i know nothing.

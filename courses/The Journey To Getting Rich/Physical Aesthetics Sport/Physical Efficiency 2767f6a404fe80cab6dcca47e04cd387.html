Physical Efficiency.

This is a lot more important than it seems. Knowing your physical talents will help you adjust your entire schedule as you’ll be able to “listen” to your body easier and develop the lacking portions faster (or invest in your gifted abilities). Importantly, we go beyond the basic items you can get for free on the internet such as a workout routine. Instead we combine physical efficiency with everything we do with our bodies as it impacts how you’ll plan out your day, week or year.

We begin with the most obvious ideas related to physical well-being and move onto every item we use in our day to day lives that drain the most important resource we have… time.

No Monetization Decision Tree

The vast majority will fall in this category and there is no problem at all with this situation. Assuming you’re unable to monetize your physical gifts, you should pursue business.

You’ve already attempted to push your body as far as it can go and will have a much better understanding of your body than a regular person. all that matters is that you're in shape.

I cannot stress this enough. Being in tune with your body will allow you to be well ahead of your peers as your entire daily, weekly and monthly schedule will change based on the answers your body gives you

Step One: Now that we know where you stand in terms of physical gifts, we’ll adjust your schedule. If you’re typically a better power athlete and easily put on weight, you’re going to spend more time doing sprints and monitoring your diet.

Conversely, if you’re typically thin and have trouble putting on weight you’ll be lifting more weight with less reps and spending less time running around the city or gym.

if you're fat, you do more fat loss work (e.g sprints)

if you're thin, you do more resistance training (weightlifting)

Step Two: Once you’ve decide which category you fit into (broadly) it’s time to adjust your body to fit the correct aesthetic mold.

it's easy to see if you're aesthetic, but if you want REAL measures.

The rough guideline for looks is known as the golden ratio with the following rough metrics:

Measure your wrist measured at connection point to arm. Chest: 6.57x; Waist: 4.57x; Hip: 5.57x; Thigh: 3.43x; Bicep: 2.43x; Neck: 2.43x; Calves: 2.29x; Forearm: 1.86x.

don't obsess over it, just keep it as a metric.

Step Three: With your ideal metrics largely in place, I suggest picking up a sport for recreational purposes. This does not need to be daily as you will simply use the commitment to make sure you’re involved physically and don’t decline at a rapid rate over night. You do not need to attend every game. If the sport you choose is individual that is also fine. The point is to make sure you get to the right aesthetic look (roughly) and then maintain functional strength to prevent injury.

something like boxing, that will be good.

you need to be both aesthetic and functionable.

Step Four: Choose two options for maintenance of your body, you’ll either leave the workout to the end of the day or you’ll do it at the beginning. If you have a hard time motiving yourself, then you’re going to choose the morning and if you’re already a gym rat, go ahead and push it into the evening giving yourself more motivation to finish all of the work you intended to do before hitting the gym.

Step Five: Remind yourself that the worst item for your health is unnecessary stress. You’re going to create enough stress on your body when you go to the gym, build your career/business and deal with the punches that life throws your way. Worrying about things you cannot control or if you should eat the apple instead of the banana are completely useless. You’ll lose more brain cells (and time) by worrying about nonsensical items which won’t move the needle. Don't stress anything, don't count calories, eat clean and train.

follow the testosterone diet laid out, it will do the work for you.

Work Out Regimen for Busy People

We’ve historically provided information on training when young (21+ or so). Essentially, within that age range, you can work out every single day and the idea that you’ll be “over training” is largely a myth. Talk to any athlete in that age bracket and you’ll see that they exercise daily. There are no “off days” because your body recovers rapidly.

The big issue is that once you start building your Career or building your business… the amount of time you have to spend in the gym or focusing on your diet decreases by a large step function. You’re forced to become more efficient.

Starting with working out, you’re going to become more creative. As you are forced to travel more and as your business picks up, the training times become variable. Sometimes you only have time to get to the gym in the morning, sometimes at night and other times you’re going to be thrown off entirely and are forced to skip a day.

This is the real world, not a high school or college experience where there is always a set schedule. Importantly, I'm assuming you’re not working a 9am-5pm job because the vast majority of careers do not have set schedules at all. it's never actually a "9-5", it's just shifts.

Based on my experience, 4-6 days a week is more than enough to maintain your current physique (Specifically, you need to work out at least 4x a week, and if you want to see more sustained gains 5-6 works best.). This is a critical part of the equation. If you’re out of shape, you have to exercise more (5-6) and if you’re in shape you can do 4x and maintain. Your body needs rest to grow and you’re going to be strapped for relaxing days during the early years. 4-6x training days a week allows your body to rest more than enough.

How To Work Out

You’re going to do compound lifting exercises while doing isolations (one muscle group a day, preferably) and sprints/functional strength. Every single workout product is essentially the same with some gimmick attached to it to make it seem “different”. I'm giving it all out here (no need to pay $30 for some ridiculous “trick” to get fit it is all the same)

the gym is simple.

there's a few tips of advice I'd like to give you, i've been in the gym for around 2.5 years. (started in june 2022)

first of all.

KISS.

keep it simple, stupid

no need to obsess over the gym, reps, rest times, no need to do any of that.

simply do this :

one muscle group a day.

this in my experience maximises growth.

you often see people doing PPL, and they have small arms.

simply because the arms are already fatigued after doing the main excersize.

instead. do this :

one muscle group a day.

2 compound exercises, 1 isolation/compound, 1 complete isolation.

example, chest.

this is what i do...

incline dumbbell press (30 degree angle)

incline smith machine press (45 degree angle)

incline machine press.

you want to do these 2 compounds for a couple sets.

heavy, to failure, every time.

4 sets of each should do you well.

low reps, 4-6. go as HEAVY as you can.

now, for the third exercise, i like to do a "compound/isolation"

something that is KIND OF a compound, but also kind of an isolation.

example for chest, incline machine press.

it's directly targeted towards your chest, and your triceps and shoulders still get a load, but it's more minimal.

and lastly, follow a complete isolation that only involve said muscle group

example for chest, chest flies.

just do this until you can't anymore, that's my rule.

pretty simple, and I'm sure you got it.

so, when in doubt...

one muscle group a day (also, keep biceps, triceps, shoulders seperate)

2 HEAVY compounds for 3-4 sets each.

1 compound/isolation, medium weight.

1 complete isolation, low weight.

I won't tell you exact exercises, each enjoy different.

this workout will maximize growth, and time efficiency.

also, the mindset you gain when you know you've just smashed a certain part of your body to failure is unbeaten.

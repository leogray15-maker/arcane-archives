Why Efficiency?

While it may seem like an odd title for a course dedicated to making money, staying in shape and becoming happy with your life in general, we think Efficiency is our only “passion”. Efficiency embodies everything from improvement in technology to happiness as a whole. People are simply trying to make better use of their time. This would be the very first lesson we would teach a young family member (our own children as well), “Your goal in life is to operate as efficiently as possible and improve upon that qualitative metric every single year”

Broadening the Definition of Efficiency

I believe all physical improvement is an increase in efficiency as we’re simply making our bodies adapt to new environments. Your body learns to become more efficient when you lift heavy weights or bike several miles per day. In the end, all physical training results in an improvement in your physical efficiency.

Technology’s entire premise is also surrounded by efficiency. When moving from mail to phone calls to emails to PDFs with the ability to issue a digital signature, all of this is simply making a process more efficient.

Improvements in screen quality can be viewed as an efficiency gain as we attempt to make each pixel improvement approach real life quality.

Perhaps Virtual Reality will become incredibly accurate and we won’t attend stadium games for football and instead will be plugged into a high definition VR login to get closer to the action (equivalent of floor level seating).

all money in technology is simply profiting off time efficiency.

faster loading times, it's called "convenience", but really, it's efficiency.

efficiency will always be the key to life.

amazon is the worlds biggest company because it can ship whatever you want to your door, the next day. time efficiency.

Finally, improvement or progress is also another word for efficiency (if looked at broadly). If you go from working for someone, to working alone to paying people to do the work for you… You’re simply using your time more efficiently… Are you not?

Similarly, if we look at all of your goals, they likely circle around improvements in your efficiency at XYZ task.

The sole reason for your goals "happening" is because of some tasks that move the needle.

the "frog', as we call it.

if we make those "needle moving" tasks at highest efficiency and we do MORE of them, we will reach our goal quicker.

the 80/20 rule is efficiency, working on the 20% of tasks that actually move the needle and removing all others is EFFICIENCY.

I'm sure you get it by now.

If we were to summarize our goals in life it could be put into two simple words: Maximize Efficiency.

This is why we never set goals, just visions.

We set a vision, we don't set a timeframe, and we just maximise work efficiency.

just working, and detaching from outcome. WHILE keeping the end goal in mind.

that's efficiency.

If we look at these three broad items we find that the reverse is also true. When we try to lift a heavier weight and fail or try to win a state championship and come in second… we get upset (physical efficiency). When our time is wasted (flight delays, traffic jams etc.) we become upset as our time is not being used efficiently (time efficiency). When we fail to improve in the categories that are important to us we become upset or frustrated as well (life efficiency).

we will talk a lot more about this, this is just getting you a little understanding, it doesn't matter if you don't fully get it yet.

just keep in mind, efficiency.

PART 1: MIRROR NEURONS (THE UNCONSCIOUS COPYING SYSTEM)

what they are: neurons that fire both when YOU do something and when you WATCH someone do it.

discovered accidentally when researchers noticed monkey brain cells firing while watching humans eat.

located in:

  • inferior frontal gyrus
  • inferior parietal lobe
  • superior temporal sulcus

here's what actually happens: you watch someone yawn → your mirror neurons fire → you yawn.

you see someone smile → your mirror neurons fire → you feel happier.

you observe success → your mirror neurons fire → you practice being successful.

the research found: salespeople with high mirror neuron activation in the pars opercularis had higher customer orientation scores.

translation: they unconsciously became what customers needed them to be.

how to weaponize this: for yourself:

  • surround yourself with people achieving what you want
  • watch videos of desired behaviors before important meetings
  • your brain literally practices being them

against others:

  • demonstrate the emotion you want them to feel
  • show the behavior you want them to copy
  • they can't help but mirror it

example in practice:

PART 5: EMOTIONAL CIRCUIT CONTROL

the brain processes emotions in predictable patterns:

the fear circuit: amygdala → hypothalamus → stress response 180ms from stimulus to fear response. faster than conscious thought (250ms).

the reward circuit: ventral tegmental area → nucleus accumbens → prefrontal cortex dopamine release in 100-150ms. wanting activated before knowing why.

the disgust circuit: insula → basal ganglia → motor cortex automatic withdrawal in 200ms. evolutionarily hardwired.

emotional hierarchy (speed of processing):

  1. fear (100ms) - survival
  1. disgust (150ms) - contamination
  1. anger (200ms) - violation
  1. pleasure (250ms) - approach
  1. complex emotions (500ms+) - social

exploit sequence: fear (create urgency) → pleasure (offer solution) → social emotion (add proof) brain can't process all three simultaneously. defaults to first strong signal.

PART 6: THE WANTING VS LIKING DISSOCIATION

biggest discovery in addiction neuroscience: wanting and liking are completely separate systems.

WANTING (incentive salience):

  • dopamine driven
  • ventral tegmental area → nucleus accumbens
  • creates craving, motivation, pursuit
  • activated by cues, anticipation
  • can be triggered without awareness

LIKING (hedonic impact):

  • opioid/endocannabinoid driven
  • tiny "hedonic hotspots" in brain
  • actual pleasure experience
  • much harder to activate
  • requires specific conditions

why this matters: you can make people desperately want something they won't actually like. entire industries built on this dissociation.

examples:

  • social media: constant wanting (dopamine), minimal liking (satisfaction)
  • shopping: wanting the purchase, not liking the product
  • gambling: wanting to win, not enjoying the process

how to create wanting without liking:

  1. intermittent reinforcement (17-23% reward rate)
  1. near-miss experiences (almost got it)
  1. social comparison (others have it)
  1. scarcity cues (limited availability)
  1. novelty rotation (always something new)

WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU FALL OFF.

of course there will be days where its bad, no sales for a couple days.

but you chose this life of entrepreneurship for a reason correct?

a life of high ups and low downs.

some days are in that bottom 10%

and some are in the highest 10%.

WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU START FALLING OFF IN BUSINESS.

the truth is, you will never fully fall off.

you know like a graph of success.

there's always a exponential j curve.

but you know that from the mindset section.

and there's always a little dip at the end.

you know j curve, and a little dip.

you have just experienced the high of the high.

now the little dip is scaring you because you lost some customers ect.

the thing is.

you just have to know, it happens with everyone.

there's due to be a dip in your sales one day.

and what is there to do when that happens?

do what you always do.

just never stop working hard.

because you'll just get another j exponential curve again.

HIGH UPS AND LOW DOWNS.

as long as you are never complacent and stop working and "maintaining"

because there is no such thing as maintaining, if you are not making progress.

there is guys below you making progress which will eventually smoke you.

all it is, is just keep working hard not regarding your feelings, not letting results define your life.

work without results.

THINK OF THE WORST SCENARIO.

if you lose all this success and money (which will never happen)

what will realistically happen?

will you die?

or will you just have a little bit less money for a couple days.

always map out the worst scenario, and counter it.

THERE'S NOTHING TO DO, OTHER THAN WORK HARD.

FALLING OFF IN GOOD HABITS LIKE GYM / MEDITATION ECT.

happens to every guy, you fall off gym or meditation or something of that nature.

the best hack to do it is put it in your morning routine, like meditation and gratitude journaling before your work sesh.

and just do gym after your work sesh.

BUT WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU FALL OFF AND STOP DOING THE HABIT.

you do not get pissed at yourself.

3/4 of the work is actually just writing it down.

"why am I not going to the gym frequently anymore?"

is it because you don't like the pain, or don't like the commute, or you play video games instead and get your development in there instead of irl?

and I assure you by the end of the journaling session about the problem, you will eventually start doing that habit again.

because realistically, your brain wants you to make progress, but if you mask it with things like video games, you are already making progress in a goal of becoming better at that game, so why would you need to do it irl?

ALWAYS DO THIS WHEN GETTING BACK INTO A HABIT.

lets say for example you used to meditate for 20 mins a day.

and you fell off for a couple months.

you will not stick to 20 minutes a day straight away.

you have to start again at 3 mins.

then next week 5 mins.

then 2 weeks after maybe 8 mins

then 10 mins.

slowly progressively overload back into it.

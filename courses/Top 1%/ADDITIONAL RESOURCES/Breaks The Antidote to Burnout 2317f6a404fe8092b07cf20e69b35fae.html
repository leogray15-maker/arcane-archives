Breaks: The Antidote to Burnout

Does this sound familiar? You feel worn down by stress. You feel exhausted. You feel burnt out.

I've experienced burnout many times in my life. First in college. Then in my career. Then in my business.

I rarely see gurus and experts talk about burnout. Yet I do often see them telling us "hustle 24/7", "the grind doesn't stop", and "sleep is for the weak." They say while we're sleeping, someone else is working towards our dreams!

These are toxic and harmful perspectives.

I'm not a guru or expert. I'm a brain with glasses who is here to help you. I've studied for years to learn how we can do our best work and live our best life - sustainably.

The most powerful way to prevent burnout from eating you alive is to take regular breaks during your day. It doesn't take much. All you need is a 10-15 minute break after each 20-75 minutes of focused work.

I'm sure you've heard of the Pomodoro method. You work for 20-50 minutes, then take a 5-15 minute break. You fill your work schedule with these blocks. Productivity research suggests the optimal average is between 50:10 and 75:15, but you should experiment to see what works best for you.

But here's the key - when I say take a break, I mean a break for your brain . Don't reach for your phone, a game, or Netflix.

When you take your breaks, do things that don't require conscious thought . A quick shower, a quiet walk, or a little meditation are great things to do during your breaks.

"But I don't have time!" you say. "I have too much to do to take a break!"

Well, I have a cold hard fact of the universe for you: If you don't take care of yourself, you won't ever be able to do your best work. You're less productive because you don't take breaks.

Do you think you're doing your best work when you're 3 hours into something and your head and your body hurt?

Do you think you're at your best when you skipped a few hours of sleep so you could work longer?

More time doesn't always mean more gets done. Even if more is getting "done", it may not be getting done WELL. Busy does not mean productive.

Look, I get it. You're busy. I'm busy too.

It's hard to remember to take a break, much less find the time to take such a break. But you must schedule breaks just like how you schedule your work.

I admit, it feels a little strange to put such an emphasis on taking breaks. But you know what? I've read enough about performance to know that those break periods are just as important as the work periods.

I have to rest to be my best. Same goes for you and every other human.

Each day this week, I challenge you to find a few 10-15 minute periods in your day to take a break. Take a breather. Put down your phone, close your laptop, and get away from the things you spend the rest of your day doing.

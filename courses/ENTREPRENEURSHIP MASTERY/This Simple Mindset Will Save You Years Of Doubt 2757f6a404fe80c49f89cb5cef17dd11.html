This Simple Mindset Will Save You Years Of Doubt.

There’s a simple mindset that will transform your mentality In business.

Here :

They’re not the right fit.

When someone leaves your product, when a client leaves you from your 1-1 coaching…

And you thought you did EVERYTHING.

Then it’s not that your “product” is bad.

They wasn’t a good fit.

By all means, your product may be bad.

But if you’re getting good feedback from people, and there’s just a little percentage of people that leave…

You should never instill doubt in yourself, doubt is the thing that will break you down.

Instead, have the mentality that “they wasn’t meant for my product”

You will inevitably have people that leave, or have people that refund.

That. Is. Not. A. Problem.

It will ALWAYS happen.

And here’s the thing..

Most of the people that leave are simply not action takers.

It’s shown that less than 2% of people even finish a >1000$ course

Do you see the reality of things?

Honestly, you will never get a bad testimonial. Unless your product is REALLY shit.

I’ve never got a bad testimonial on this network. Never. Not even close.

Even the people that leave tell me “there was so much sauce in your network”

But they’ll still leave.

You know why?

They’re not a good fit.

I got told by the best copywriter I know to ask people who left “what made you leave, was life just getting in the way?”

Most of the time, it was. I’ve never got a bad review.

And something like this just makes you realise that they didn’t hate your product and thought it was a scam, they just wasn’t the right fit.

Instill this mindset, it will save you months/years of doubt.

And a final note…

To make people “take action” on your info product more.

Make sure you have some type of “onboarding”

You know how businesses have “onboarding calls”

It’s not to show them around, it’s made for the client to take action.

So... to onboard you could make a very detailed welcome course, or even hop on a call with them.

This will reduce churn rates by a lot.

Just a little tip.

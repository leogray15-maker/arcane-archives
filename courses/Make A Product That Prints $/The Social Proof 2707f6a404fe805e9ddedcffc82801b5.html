1) How To Get Social Proof Before Launching

You don't absolutely need social proof when you are first launching a product. Too many people hesitate to launch when it doesn't have social proof.

If you are writing content and building an audience, this becomes even less necessary because you've already built trust with that content. When you are running paid ads or running other marketing campaigns, yeah, you have to optimize a lot more.

But if you want to get a few testimonials, you can DM people within your direct network. In the creator economy, this usually means creators that are in your tribe. The one's you've connected with using strategies from "The Growth Strategy" module.

  • Ask if you can help them with whatever your product helps with on a call in exchange for a testimonial. If they're a friend, they will.
  • Walk them through parts of your curriculum, send them the templates you've made, and help them get 1% better at the thing you help with.
  • Ask them for a written or video testimonial.

The beautiful thing about testimonials from creators is that they are appreciating assets.

If somebody got a testimonial from me when I was at 1000 followers, they can still leverage it today when I have millions. Their product sales increased because they were smart and got testimonials from their friends. They played the long-term game.

2) Collecting Testimonials Manually

After your product launch you should go into "delivery and social proof" mode.

Your job is to reach out to vocal customers, help them 1-1 via email or direct message, and ask for their thoughts and testimony regarding the product.

Another reason I like starting with a cohort is that you will usually have a community feature for that cohort.

You can create a "post your wins" space inside of Circle, Discord, Telegram, or whatever other platform you use.

This is both where customers can give you praise and where other students can gain inspiration to attempt to get more results. When people post their wins, you can screenshot those and save them as testimonials, and other customers can use them as motivation to use your product to its maximum potential.

3) Automating Testimonials

Now, whether you have a cohort, course, or community it is wise to create a testimonial automation by:

  • Creating a free bonus that you will send to people who leave testimonials.
  • Adding a module to the end of your product asking people to submit a testimonial in exchange for the free bonus.
  • Automating or manually sending out an email after the cohort ends or after people have been in the course/community for enough time to get results.

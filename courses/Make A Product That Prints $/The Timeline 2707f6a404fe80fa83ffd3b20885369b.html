Promoting In Your Content

Once you have created a sustainable content ecosystem for yourself, you should be promoting nearly every day. You shouldn’t have to promote directly on the timeline.

When you write your newsletters, you can have promotions in a few places:

Wherever relevant and your product ties in to what you are talking about — you can put something as simple as “If you want help with this, check out [your offer]”

In the middle of the newsletter — in almost every newsletter, you can add a reminder that newsletter readers can join.

I recommend having a "cheap" product like i have my e book that you can promo that then UPSELLS into your main money maker.

At the end of your newsletter — you can either add a P.S. or have a consistent section that says something like “What’s next?” or “How I Can Help You Further”

Having a special offer for newsletter readers might push them over the edge.

or in a newsletter you can direct the reader to a DIFFERENT newsletter that gives them that "what's next"

and that newsletter should be linked to your product with a P.S

This is how you “sell without selling.”

People will read through your high-value newsletter and be exposed to your promotion.

Promoting On The Timeline

When you promote on the timeline, you are trying to make a quick and clear call to action.

This can happen at the bottom of your threads, in a reply to your tweet, at the end of your Instagram caption, in an Instagram story, anything.

The Value Framework

When considering a direct promotion on the timeline or under a post, you want to be as clear as possible:

  • Who is it for? — You can use the sentence “If you are…” followed by the pains they experience.
  • What will they get? — List out the clear promise or a big benefit that implies a transformation based on what they are struggling with.
  • Why should they care? — This is where you fill in the blank with one of your most compelling arguments in your marketing strategy.

You aren’t trying to sell them right then and there, you are trying to make them curious enough to click through and read your full landing page.

Promotion Frequency

For your lead magnets or newsletters, you should promote every single day, no questions asked, they're signed up to your email list because they ADMIT they have a problem.

For products and services, depending on the platform, you should post every 1-2 days under a relevant post.

If you can keep the ratio to 1:4 (1 promotion per 4 value posts) then you should be good to go. Just don’t over-promote unless it is launch week.

Start With A Lower Price

By now you should understand that launches and scarcity drive the most sales.

So, it makes sense to space out these "launch events" wisely. I tend to do these every 2-3 months.

"Launch events" are as follows:

  • Price increases for your course, or community.
  • Adding new modules to your course.
  • Running special promotions (like a low-cost first month of your community or a limited time bonus for your course).
  • Holidays like Black Friday.

It's your job to space these out and plan these wisely whenever you want to kick sales back up.

I wouldn't solely rely on standalone course sales to make you "passive income." That's not how this stuff works.

YoungLA, a fitness fashion brand that exploded onto the scene, makes almost all of their money with limited "drops."

They don't hold much of the same clothes in inventory. They have special drops every few months with clothing designs that people want. They have all of their sponsors talk about the drops, and when they're sold out, they're sold out. This creates a lot of scarcity and results in huge surges in revenue for them.

One problem with creators is that they start with a high price too early.

  • They don't have a large audience that trusts them enough.
  • They can't decrease the price because that's not ethical to those who have already bought.
  • They get discouraged with their sales and stop promoting.

In short, start with a lower price. That way you can have multiple "launch events" for price increases.

I recommend giving yourself space for 2-3 reasonable price increases.

At this point in your journey, when you have your product stack built out, your main focuses are:

  • Growing an audience with broad attractive content about topics you like to teach.
  • Nurturing that audience with lead magnets and emails (while promoting your products inside).

When you only promote hard every 2-3 months, people see you as trustworthy. You aren't spamming them to make a living every single day, and you make more because of it.

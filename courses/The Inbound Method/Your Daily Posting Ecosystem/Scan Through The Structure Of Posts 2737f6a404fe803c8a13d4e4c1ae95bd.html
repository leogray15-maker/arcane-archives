So now… you should have read the first part of the copywriting course.

Now... we’re actually going to get into how to “write” the post.

We have covered the mindsets for writing… we’ve looked through how copywriting and emotions work…

Now we need to move into the practical.

How to actually write a post that captures attention, captures interest, gets the reader to take action.

And this module is the first step.

Now... you already know we’re going to use the final part of the copywriting course to write our posts…

So, here’s what I need you to do.

Scan through these 4 modules.

And I did not put this in the last module, because we are going to SCAN through, not read through..

The purpose of “scanning through” these modules?

It’s to have an understanding how a post will work, grabbing attention, keeping attention, making them believe you, everything of that nature.

And here’s what I’d like for you to keep in mind while reading this…

You need to look through it AS IF this “post” or “piece of copy” is just about solving a problem for the reader, or just giving them a benefit.

So... here’s what I need you to do.

Do it with the intention of just “getting an understanding” of how it will work.

THIS IS IMPORTANT :

DO NOT USE "TEMPLATES" DO NOT USE COPYWRITING WORD FOR WORD.

LISTEN, LET YOUR BRAIN WRITE, DON'T ACT LIKE A ROBOT.

THE POINT IS TO UNDERSTAND ATTENTION GRABBING... BRIBES.. ETC.

BUT DO NOT OPTIMISE EVERYTHING FOR "COPYWRITING"

THERE'S A SIMPLE POINT. AND THAT POINT IS TO GET THE INFORMATION OUT TO THE READER.

THE "COPYWRITING" DOESN'T MATTER AS MUCH AS YOU THINK IT DOES. IT SIMPLY JUST PLANTS A SEED IN YOUR MIND SO YOU JUST KNOW HOW IT WORKS.

I made this mistake before... and I just optimised everything for "perfect" copywriting.

don't do that, only do that when you're writing a strict "salesy" email. for your posts. don't do it. it's a "personal brand" not a copywriting ai.

my best posts have been the posts that come from my HEART AND PASSION, not the ones that i optimised.

don't look for numbers, look for connection with the readers heart. that's what I've learnt from being a writer for the past 1.5 years.

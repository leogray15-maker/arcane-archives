have you ever felt like you're not good enough? Like you're a fraud? Like you're going to get found out and exposed?

If you have, then you've experienced a psychological pattern called imposter syndrome .

You're not alone - an estimated 70% of people experience imposter syndrome at some point in their lives. I've experienced it off and on throughout my own life - in college, in work, and in my business.

This may surprise you, but imposter syndrome is particularly common among high-achieving individuals. Since you're here, you yourself are a high-achieving individual. It's ironic - even when people achieve more, they may doubt themselves more.

I've seen it happen at engineering school, I've seen it happen in the programming industry, and I often see it happen on social media (for those who are running a business on social media). I’ve helped a few of my clients overcome it.

Even if you're currently thinking "well I've never had it", chances are, you probably have or will in the future. As with many patterns of thought, imposter syndrome can sneak up on you when you least expect it.

So, I thought I'd take this time today to tell you - and remind myself - how to break out of imposter syndrome.

Reprogram Your Thoughts

Your brain is kind of like a computer. It has subconscious programs it runs to do the things you do. Luckily, none of those programs include Internet Explorer.

Most people don't know this, but... you can consciously change many of those programs so that they help you rather than hurt you. The most powerful way I've found to reprogram your thoughts is this:

Throughout the day, keep track of the negative things you think and say about yourself in a notes app on your phone. Every time you tell yourself you're not good enough, note it. Every time you think everyone else is better than you, note it.

When work is done for the day, review the notes you took. Read the negative things you've said to yourself.

Then (and this is the key), start actively telling yourself the OPPOSITE of those things. Do it for the rest of the day, and all of the next day.

You told yourself you're not good enough? Tell yourself you ARE good enough. You told yourself everyone else is better than you? Tell yourself you're just as good - if not BETTER - than those around you.

Focus on the Facts

When you're dealing with imposter syndrome, your brain has a warped view of reality.

You could be at one of the best engineering schools in the world, yet feel like you don't know enough.

You could be gaining hundreds of followers a month on social media, yet feel like you're losing the "race" against others.

Because your brain has this negative perspective, it starts looking for things that confirm that perspective. Your brain hates being contradicted.

So what do you do? Start consciously focusing on the positive things . You got yourself into a good college. You got yourself a great job. You're in the top 1% of your social media platform.

This is another way of reprogramming your thoughts - rather, you're reprogramming your perspective.

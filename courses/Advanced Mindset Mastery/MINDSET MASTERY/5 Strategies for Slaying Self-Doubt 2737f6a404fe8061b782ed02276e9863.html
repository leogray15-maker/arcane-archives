you know how you start doubting yourself every time you think about doing something new?

It's actually a survival mechanism. Your brain uses it to keep you safe and alive.

But the safe life isn't the satisfying life. Your brain wants you to stay inside all day stuffing your face with candy, but that won't lead to a fulfilling life for you.

Life can be a constant battle between what your brain wants you to do and what you actually should do. It doesn’t have to be. To optimize your performance, you and your brain must be working together as much as possible.

Self Doubt and Threat Avoidance

Your brain's #1 job is to keep you alive. It's evolved a system to deal with threats - the fight-flight-freeze threat response system. When your brain detects a threat, that system responds to keep you safe.

When your brain senses a threat, you may feel like taking action (fighting), like running away (fleeing), or unable to act at all (freezing).

One of the biggest threats is uncertainty . When you're faced with a new situation - a new job, new people, or starting a new hobby/business, that new situation is unfamiliar. It's uncertain . Therefore, it's threatening .

So, your brain tries to protect you from the perceived threat. It tries to steer you away from the new situation. How?

  • It reminds you of your past failures
  • It tells you that you're not good enough
  • It makes you worry that things won't work out

What happens? Maybe you don't go to that job interview. Maybe you don't ask for that cute girl's number. Maybe you don't start that business.

Safe? Maybe. Happy and fulfilled? Absolutely not .

Even if you do try, your self-doubt amplifies every mistake. It makes you think any success is just luck.

How to Overcome Self-Doubt

1) Meditate

Self-doubt is an emotional response to a perceived "threat". It begins with your amygdala, the fear and threat response center of your brain.

Studies have shown regular meditation can slightly reduce the volume of the amygdala. As it gets smaller, your emotional control grows. The more you meditate, the less you'll allow self-doubt to stop you from doing something.

2) Just do it

It's not just a great marketing slogan - just doing it will lower your self-doubt. Why? Because you'll gain experience. Try this exercise:

Tell yourself you'll try that scary thing for just 5 minutes, or just one time. This will give you experience with the situation, which reduces its uncertainty. Less uncertainty = less threatening.

If you want to lead a happy and fulfilling life, you have to get used to pushing through fear and discomfort. There's no way around it.

3) Use affirmations to interrupt patterns

Write down every negative thing your brain says to you. Then start telling yourself the positive version of those things.

"I can't" becomes "I can"

"I won't" becomes "I will"

"I'm not <x> enough" becomes "I will become <x> enough"

You get the picture. Note: for best results, you need to actually start believing in what you tell yourself.

4) Adopt a growth mindset

Your skills aren't fixed. You can learn, improve, and succeed.

Question : Do you disagree with either of those statements? If you do, look up the book Mindset by Carol Dweck. Buy it and read it. It'll improve your mindset.

Having a growth mindset means you can shrug off failures because you know you'll learn and improve. It means you know you can get better at something. It means you know that failure doesn't say anything about your worth as a person.

5) Record your wins and achievements

Your brain has a negativity bias. It's another survival mechanism that isn't fun to deal with.

The bias makes you forget about all your wins and achievements. Meanwhile, it remembers your losses and failures.

So write down your wins. Record your victories. I don't care how small they are - do it! Remind yourself of them!

You've won before. You'll win more.

Bonus round) Accept that you will always doubt yourself a little

You may always doubt yourself to some degree. Even people at the top of their game, like Michael Jordan, Tom Brady, and Kyle Larson experience self-doubt. Just because you're successful doesn't mean you don't fear losing your success.

But one of the many things separating those GOATs from the also-rans: They accept that they will doubt themselves at some point. By accepting this, you will be prepared for it. You'll know the negative voice will speak up again. And then you'll be more prepared to overpower it with your positive, self-affirming voice.

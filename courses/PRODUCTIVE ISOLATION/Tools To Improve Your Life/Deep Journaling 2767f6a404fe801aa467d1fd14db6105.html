Deep Journaling

By writing and questioning, you'll gain clarity, reduce anxiety, and gain valuable insights.

Preparation:Create a routine: Set a designated time and place. For example, brew tea (optional!), put on your calming playlist, and grab a pen and paper. Consistency is key.
**Choose a thought: **Pick something that pops up frequently, causing anxiety.

Dive Deep:Write: Pour your heart out about this thought. No judgment, just honesty. Aim for a few pages, but go at your pace. Write until you feel a sense of release.
Get specific with anxieties: If anxiety is present, dive deep. Who/what triggered it? Exact details? Be specific.
Question yourself: As you write, constantly ask "why?" Why am I thinking like this? What does this mean? What would happen if...? This helps uncover deeper meaning.

Key Tips:Write for yourself: This is a private space for raw thoughts, not for someone else to read.
Don't overthink: Write freely, ignoring grammar and spelling.
Embrace discomfort: Exploring difficult emotions isn't always pleasant, but it's worth it.

Summary:Routine: Set up a specific time and place for deep journaling, and create a consistent routine.
Write for the writer, not the reader: Write for yourself, not for anyone else.
Ignore mistakes: Write whatever comes to mind, without worrying about grammar or spelling.
Be honest with yourself: Deep journaling is a personal exploration. There are no right or wrong answers, just honest exploration and self-discovery.
Not a to-do-list: Instead of just listing things you plan to do tomorrow, use deep journaling to help you figure out why you feel the way you do and less about the decisions you need to make.

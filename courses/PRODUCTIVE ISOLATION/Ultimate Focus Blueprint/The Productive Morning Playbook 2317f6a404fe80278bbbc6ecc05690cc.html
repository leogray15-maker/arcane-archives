The Productive Morning Playbook.

Your morning sets the tone for your entire day. This 4-step morning routine will prime your day for productivity:

1) Get out of bed at your first alarm

If you're starting your day off with snoozing your alarm, you're shooting your brain in the foot.

Snoozing your alarm and going back to sleep makes you feel extra groggy and sluggish when you finally drag yourself out of bed. It also screws up your circadian rhythm, making it harder to sleep well the next night.

Charge your phone and/or keep your alarm as far away from you as possible throughout the night. Why? So you have to take a few steps out of bed to shut the alarm off. Also, so you can't just grab your phone, roll over in bed, and start scrolling on social media.

2) Make your bed

After you get out of bed, immediately turn around and make the bed . I recommend everyone make their bed, especially people who feel like they don't have their life together.

Making your bed is the first decision of your day after your feet hit the floor. If you leave your bed unmade, you're starting your day off on the path of a messy life. If you make your bed, you're starting your day off on the path of an organized life.

You may be thinking "who cares? no one will ever know." False. You'll know . Your life is a mess because you don't believe in your ability to get it together .

Doing "little" things (like making your bed) will start rebuilding that belief.

3) Drink Some Water.

Before you have your caffeine, drink a glass of water first. Your body and brain get dehydrated throughout the night, which negatively affects your performance. Water rehydrates that incredible mound of fatty tissue between your ears. It also helps your system flush out toxins and garbage.

4) Journal

As you're drinking your water, grab a notebook and a pencil and do a little morning journaling.

Some people find journaling to be a boring, intimidating, or pointless chore. It's actually incredibly powerful. Here are a few of the many benefits:

  • Reduces stress
  • Improves your writing
  • Increases mental clarity
  • Helps you process your feelings
  • Lets you see how you're doing on your goals

How should you journal? By doing a brain dump . Start with a blank page and write down everything on your mind. Brain dumping is one of the most life-changing habits I will teach you.

simply, just write down what you're thinking, plans for the day, etc.

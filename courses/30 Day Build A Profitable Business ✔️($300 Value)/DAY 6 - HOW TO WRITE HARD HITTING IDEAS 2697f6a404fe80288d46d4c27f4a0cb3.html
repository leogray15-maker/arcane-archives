DAY 6 - HOW TO WRITE HARD HITTING IDEAS

Here's Day 6:

For the sake of not overwhelming you (since we've learned a substantial amount already), the next few days are still going to be difficult, but shorter. Then we will pick speed back up again.

As a reminder, we need to absolutely nail content ideas and writing. It's the crux of every other aspect of this style of business. Even monetization. If people love your posts, they are much more likely to purchase what you sell without optimizing your marketing.

Yesterday, we took our own ideas and turned them into 3 list posts.

I want to continue working with post structures a bit longer. That way, I can expose you to a variety of types of posts that you can write.

There will be times when you feel like you have nothing to write about.

When that happens, you can go back through these days of the challenge, choose an idea, choose a few post structures, and while you may not have the best posts in the world, you have a post that is likely to do somewhat well (allowing you to stay consistent).

Today I want to focus on "short posts."

By "short posts" I mean something very short. Like 1-2 sentences.

This isn't a set definition used by everyone. This is just the type of post I want to focus on today to help you write short ideas with impact.

Now, a few caveats here:

  • Short posts tend to get more engagement (likes), but a lot less followers
  • As a beginner, you're better off displaying your expertise or unique thoughts in longer form posts - more attention held = more followers
  • Short posts are great for practicing punchy writing, but are more of something you do occasionally rather than something you rely on

You probably don't have the status of Naval. You aren't Marcus Aurelius. You need to build authority before your short posts actually move the needle (unless they're really really really good).

That said, learning to write them makes your writing more impactful, because all of your writing is composed of sentences.

I would recommend writing the short posts below, then using that as the starting/ending point of a post. Add a hook and body, or start the post with what you wrote.

Personal Definition

This one is simple.

Pick a topic from your topic map.

Choose a term, trait, or word that is important.

Redefine it in a way that brings more clarity.

You don't need to follow the dictionary definition here. You can follow what most people think that term means.

For "productivity," most people associate that with working extremely long hours.

You redefine it as, "Productivity is getting from point A to point B as fast as possible, not dragging point B out to 16 hours a day."

Could be better.

You get the point.

If I wanted to expand on this to make the post longer, I could:

  • Start with a pain point hook like, "A lack of confidence ruins your life."
  • Then, I could add a sentence that transitions into the core idea like, "Most people overcomplicate to the point of becoming even less confident."
  • Last, I would include the my personal definition of confidence above.

Harsh Truth

This one comes in two parts:

  • A hook that hints at the fact that what comes next is a harsh truth. You can say "harsh truth:" but it's better to get a bit creative.
  • One to two sentences that illustrate the harsh truth according to one of your main topics.

Instead of "hard pill to swallow" I could have said:

  • You don't want to hear this, but:
  • Most people don't realize that:
  • Successful people understand that:
  • This won't sit well with you:

I could go on, but I want to make a point that you don't have to copy the exact hook someone else uses. The amount of times I see people copy hooks after one does well is astounding, when their post could do equally as well if they changed it.

If I wanted to expand on this post to make it longer:

  • It already has a hook and body, so I'd keep that
  • But it's lacking a solution or tangible value
  • I could add a short list of actionable steps to shed limiting beliefs
  • Then, I could end it with a short last sentence to tie it all together

If this, then that

This one is a go-to of mine because I can easily kickstart my thinking by having "if you" in mind and reading a certain idea or pain point.

The premise is simple.

Pair "if you" with a flawed perspective, common action, pain point, or desired outcome with "then" followed by the solution or completion.

This post is already good, it has a pain point and it hints at a solution, but if I wanted to make it longer I could:

  • Write a second paragraph on how to start reeducating yourself
  • Write a list of reasons why quitting after 2 weeks is the worst decision you could make

It really depends how long you want this post to be, but this idea has a lot of room to be expanded on.

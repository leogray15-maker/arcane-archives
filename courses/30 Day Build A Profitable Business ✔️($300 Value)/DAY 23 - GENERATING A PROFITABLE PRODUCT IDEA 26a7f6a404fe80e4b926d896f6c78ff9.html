DAY 23 - GENERATING A PROFITABLE PRODUCT IDEA

I want to reiterate a portion of what we learned yesterday.

You should create one product, and offer it in 2 different ways.

First, is as the actual product that costs $25 to $99 or so.

Second, is as some form of one-on-one calls that walk them through the main points of the product, but tailored to their specific problem and situation.

The one-on-one calls can be sold as a "program" that consists of 4 calls and the ability to text you to ask questions. It could also be 8 calls, or 1, or 12, and the price would vary based on that. The reason I say 4 calls as the first option is that it's great for beginners. It justifies a $1000 price tag, so you have the incentive to actually try to sell it, and it doesn't require that much upfront work. You just need to be clear on what you are helping the other person achieve.

For the product, you'll need a simple landing page, which we will create on Stan. I'll give you my AI prompt for writing the landing page soon.

For the one-on-one calls, you don't need a landing page. You need someone to reply to your newsletter or DM you. Then you explain what would have been on the landing page to them. Then you send a payment link.

If you have some kind of valuable skill or interest and a bit of marketing knowledge, you can start making money today.

But most people are scared to do so or overcomplicate it to the point of doing nothing. Or they're so used to a job that looks so complex so they think they need to replicate that in order to make any kind of money.

With all of that said… we first need to start with a good product idea.

That's what we're focused on today.

To start:

Stop trying to create a fancy product that nobody has ever invented.

Not gonna happen.

Especially for your first product.

Give people what they want.

Give people what they are already buying.

Give people a simple, basic solution that helps them improve by 1%.

Then, slap your own mechanism on it to outcompete everyone else (we will go over this a future lesson).

That’s all they want. That’s what will make you a fortune (relatively).

For your first product, you are going to sell something beginner-level.

Why?

I'm assuming most of you are growing on social media or will be growing on social media to fuel this product.

That means you are attracting a rather broad audience and need a first foundational product to sell.

This allows you to grow and tap into a somewhat broad market (so you can pivot and do what you want down the road). The market may be broad, but your personal brand is what makes you "niche."

This demands that you sell something beginner-level.

Do not overestimate how much people know.

Do not overestimate how much people remember .

There are plenty of 50-year-olds who don't know how to make $1 in business, have terrible relationships, and overall don't enjoy their life.

And if there are plenty of 50-year-olds with that experience, imagine how many younger people there are, or how many people there are in general.

That's what makes this business meaningful. You are playing your role in improving humanity from the ground up. You are helping society change at its root: identity, belief, and behavior through education.

Since you are attracting an audience where 95% of them are beginners, it only makes sense to provide a solution to their problems.

That way, you can promote your product no matter what you talk about. And, you can branch out into other interests of yours whenever you please, like how Zuby creates music, sells a fitness product, talks politics and self-improvement, and gets paid for public speaking.

As an example, I have my product 2 Hour Writer.

I can write about something unrelated, like “how to meditate,” and still promote it.

“If you want to use writing as a meditation practice (and earn a living doing so), purchase 2 Hour Writer here.”

That could be better, but you get the point.

Some of the strategies are advanced, but I position writing as an opportunity for people to increase their skill set in a unique way (by building an audience on social media).

The point:

A beginner-level digital product allows you to monetize no matter what you want to talk about. As long as that writing is impactful and keeps you growing on social media.

Now, I don't want to make it seem like you can ONLY create an entry-level digital product. You can and should include advanced teachings.

Just remember : most of your customers will be beginners, and even if they're advanced, they need a refresher on the fundamentals.

Your product can contain advanced teachings and systems, but it should be positioned toward beginners to attract more people with your marketing AND should have some of the fundamentals included inside.

Step 1) Choose The Skill Or Interest You Want To Monetize

Think about the topics you often read or write about.

What are the topics that fill your search history?

What products are people already buying?

Who are the creators doing what you want to do? What are they selling?

Just like writing content, all you need to do is take what works and make it a tiny bit better.

  • Think back to courses you’ve purchased.
  • Think back to your favorite articles, podcasts, or videos.
  • Think back to the products you’ve used and enjoyed.
  • Think about what you do on a daily basis and how it gets you results for the life you want (for me, it’s writing).

Now, use that as the starting point for your product.

Remember that this is a starting point. You won't have the perfect idea now. It may take a few days or weeks for the right idea to culminate.

With a small idea of what you want to do, it's time to immerse yourself in content, ideas, and products relevant to that until you crack the perfect idea.

Step 2) Do Simple Market Research

Your job is to find an idea that is already popular and capitalize on it.

Here are better ways of doing market research, in this order:

1) Pay Attention To Your Best Posts

Social media is a testing ground for ideas.

The more content you write, the more you understand what you should double down on.

As you write content, pay close attention to which ideas perform the best each month.

If you repurpose good posts into threads and newsletters, and all of those do well, you may have a winner.

You can use any of those as a starting point for a lead magnet or product.

My 2-Hour Writer course was born from a Twitter thread that got great engagement. I taught people how I write my newsletter and turn it into content.

My Digital Economics course (no longer available) was built on a whim after my “One-Person Business” YouTube video went viral.

I built a landing page as fast as I could to start capitalizing on that traffic. I made more videos about it. I started selling before the product was ready. I built the landing page, accepted pre-order payments, and set a launch date. When they purchased, there was just one module inside that said the material will be added on the launch date.

When you hit the right idea, capitalize on it, fast.

It shouldn't take you more than a day to go from idea to profit.

Of course, that will become easier when you understand the landing page, product creation, and launch process.

2) Immerse Yourself In High-Performing Ideas

For your first product, we are selling what is already selling, but with your unique mechanism (as you will learn).

Understand that social media is FLOODED with validated ideas.

You can find them on YouTube:

  • Go to the accounts of creators that are doing what you want to do.
  • If you don't know any creators, start searching variations of your topic in the search bar and start subscribing to people.
  • When you are on their account, filter their videos by most popular and study all of them.
  • Note down the video title, thumbnail text, and take notes on the video.

If the video did extremely well, it will probably do well as marketing for your product. And, you can take the ideas that did well, create content around them, and promote your product inside of them.

You can also find them keeping an eye on what content does well for the accounts you follow and what ideas you save to your idea museum.

Most of my headlines, modules of my courses, and ideas for my copywriting are all swiped from top-performing ideas.

If they capture attention on social media, they will probably capture and hold attention on my sales pages, in my products, and in my marketing for the product.

You can find them anywhere on social media:

  • Stop being a passive consumer.
  • Pay close attention to posts you come across that do well relating to your topic (you will have to be following people who talk about that topic).
  • Save those ideas to use in your product and content.

This may be difficult to make sense of.

It can be hard to see how a YouTube video can turn into a product idea, so let's run through a few.

Topic : Social dynamics

YouTube account : Charisma On Command

High-performing idea : The Formula For Extreme Confidence

Product idea : a self-confidence ebook

Great title, lots a views, definitely validated.

It even has a unique mechanism to catch attention, "The Fire Method."

That is a great starting point for a product.

Another example.

Topic : Productivity

YouTube account : Ali Abdaal

High-performing idea : How I Manage My Time

Product idea : a time management system as a Notion template with an accompanying course

There are two videos with this "How I Manage My Time" angle with an absurd amount of views. That's pretty validating.

The second one also has a unique mechanism, "The Trident Method."

You can start to see a pattern here.

For now, start researching potential ideas to use for your product and marketing.

Back into more ways to do market research:

3) Interact With Your Audience

You should already be doing this, but you may not be conscious of how it relates to your product.

Reply to others, reply to those who engage with your content, and use those interactions as inspiration for your social posts. Then, gauge how well those posts do. There is no set metric for this.

As you interact with others over the course of weeks / months, note down common questions that people ask and the problems associated with them.

4) Get On 5-10 Calls For Free To Help People

Getting on calls with people you can help is the best form of market research.

It's pretty simple to do:

  • Pay attention to potential customers on social media. Really anyone with a beginner level problem, which is almost anyone. Small creators are best for those as they are talking about things online and looking to connect with people.
  • Follow them, comment on their posts for a few days with something insightful, not just "great post!"
  • Shoot them a message giving them a compliment on one of their posts.
  • Ask about their work, what they are building, or what goals they have (people love to talk about themselves).
  • If they mention something relevant to the product you want to create, ask them if you can test your product on them to help them achieve their goals.

Don’t overthink this. Ask them to get on a call, talk to them like a normal human being, and see if you can help them in your area of expertise.

5) Research & Swipe

This part stresses the importance of being immersed in the topics you want to monetize on social media.

  • Following your favorite accounts and studying their content (the best pieces hit on the deepest problems).
  • Reading your favorite books to understand the tone, problems, and writing that you resonate with (because that’s what your ideal audience will resonate with).
  • Writing content to validate ideas and see what resonates with your audience.
  • Bonus: Studying the funnels of your mentors. Join their email lists, read their blog, watch all of their content, study their product landing pages, and buy their courses to get ideas for your own (not necessarily ideas for the topic of the course, but the structure so you can learn how to build one).

If you don't know how to structure your course, template, community, ebook, etc, it's because you haven't bought any… or you just haven't gone through them from the lens of building your own.

Same goes for content, emails, and sales pages. You read them as a consumer, not a creator.

All of this happens with time. The longer you are immersed in your domain, the more and more aware you will become of the big problems within that domain, products that are selling well, and how they sell their offers (don’t imitate someone that you know isn’t making the amount of money you want to make).

If you don’t know what products, funnels, or landing pages to study from your mentors — you need more time and awareness in your domain.

You don't know "who to follow" or "who to study" because you haven't leaned into the algorithm.

Once you watch one video, more get shown. Once you follow one person, you find more in the comments. Start drowning yourself in the area you want to monetize on social media.

Step 3) Choose A Mode To Sell From

There are 3 different ways to start selling your product:

1) A Tutorial

This works best for teaching a skill like:

  • Web design or development
  • Graphic design or animation
  • Custom car builds or another hobby
  • Video editing or photography

Simple: create a tutorial and slap a price tag on it.

For your one-on-one calls, teach them how to build a project they're building.

I once hired someone for $5000 to help me learn After Effects. I was creating animations for one of my YouTube videos, wanted to do it faster and felt lost, so I hired someone who could teach me what to do so I didn't waste my time going through a ton of YouTube tutorials that didn't help my specific situation.

Yes, there may be tons of courses or digital products on the market, but the only way people buy those is when the intention is there.

By intention, I mean people only find all of those products when they are actively searching for them.

When you are on social media, you are shown what's on your feed. People will only buy from you if you are persuasive as to how your interest or skill can benefit their life. People aren't on social media searching for specific information. They go to Google or ChatGPT for that. That's exactly why saturation doesn't matter as a personal brand.

Even then, people love buying multiple courses on the same topic (I’ve purchased 20+ on web development, 5+ on content creation, and even more on photography and digital art).

A business lesson that most people overlook:

Sell what's already selling.

People on social media are beginners.

They aren’t typing “how to build a website” in the search bar of the social media platform. Lol.

You are the one who will introduce them to the importance of that skill, as an example:

  • Web design is a high-value skill to add to your career.
  • Web design is a way to start freelancing in minimal time so you can replace your income.
  • Web design is how you create a website and landing page for your own business.

When you market the importance of a topic in your content and marketing, people BECOME more interested in buying. They don't need to be interested from the start. Meaning, you can target and profit from most people on social media, which is a lot.

You will be the only one who provides a solution to learn that skill because you are top of mind.

Stop worrying if someone else is already selling it. That is a very good thing. If someone isn’t selling it, you probably won’t succeed with your first product.

2) A Program

This works best for interests like:

  • Fitness
  • Nutrition
  • Productivity or performance
  • Self-improvement

A program is when you give people a step-by-step action plan to achieve a desirable end result.

A fitness or nutrition regimen is an obvious example.

For something like self-improvement, you would give things like a dopamine detox, meditation, or reading regimen.

3) A System

This works best for unique systems you’ve built for things like:

  • Building a social media audience
  • Lead generation for businesses
  • Productivity systems in Notion

My 2-Hour Writer course is an example. It's the writing system I've created over time. (You don't need to purchase it btw, you are learning what you need to know and focus on here.)

This is more complex than just teaching the skill.

This comes from months of trial and error to build a system that is unique to you.

Remember that we are just ideating what to sell right now. Things will start to make more sense as we progress.

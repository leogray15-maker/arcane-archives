DAY 17 - THE BIG PICTURE OF SOCIAL GROWTH

Now that we have social content and newsletter writing out of the way, we can get into the good stuff.

The big problem is that most people post and post and post just to get mad at the algorithm for not spreading their content.

Please understand this:

POSTING content is not a growth strategy.

Posting content IS NOT a way to get people to SEE your content.

Your content can be verifiably amazing, but if nobody sees it, and the algorithm doesn't push it (which it probably won't) then you're not going to have a good time.

This is the case for 95% of beginner creators.

  • Their content isn't the best, so the algorithm doesn't care
  • They think that the algorithm is the only way to grow
  • If they don't go as viral as the people they compare themselves to, they quit

Beginners have the most distorted perspective of what it takes and what dictates meaningful results. People are perfectly fine with going to college for 4-8 years with no promise of making a decent income, but when it comes to spending 1 year experimenting with their own business growth, they quit because they don't make millions of dollars.

Please don't be that person.

Now, there are 4 things that lead to sustainable growth.

Number 2 is what most people are missing, and that will be our main focus for the next few days of this challenge.

Once you understand this, you can grow on any platform at whatever pace you'd like to.

1) Write LOUD Content

We've done this.

Being loud implies a few things:

  • A hook . Be mindful as to whether or not your first sentence will grab their attention. This goes beyond just the words you use. The structure and design of the post can do this, even on X.
  • A big problem . Imply or state a problem that people can relate to. Don’t make it too high level.
  • A clear solution . An actionable way to overcome the problem, preferably with a unique way of doing so from personal experience.
  • A big benefit . Imply or state a desirable benefit that people will receive from your advice.
  • Confidence or polarization . Stand firm in your beliefs but be willing to change them. Nobody wants to follow someone who doesn’t sound confident or can’t choose a side. Say what you want to say. You will get hate like everyone else.
  • A novel perspective OR big idea . This is why reading or consuming content is important. If you can find a way of explaining an idea that isn’t normal, it will give your readers a dopamine hit and advance their understanding.

The best way to solidify these in your head is to save them somewhere safe and try to spot them in the content you see online.

Your content won't be seen as a beginner if you are a soft spoken person when you post.

2) Get Eyes On Your Content

We will discuss it in depth below.

For now, nail into your head that your content is meaningless if you don’t get eyes on it. This should be obvious.

It’s fine to write content into the void for practice, but don’t let that turn into an excuse because you are afraid to grow and face criticism.

3) Iterate On Your Ideas With Data

You can only reach this step if you have eyes on your content.

You don’t know what ideas work best for you if you don’t have any engagement on them.

When you do, review your highest-performing content in your analytics each month. Copy and paste them into a notes document.

When you go to write more content, sprinkle in those ideas and write about them from different angles.

This is how you increase your engagement with time (you are increasing how often you post about ideas that work).

4) Create Depth & Span For Your Best Ideas

You can do well on one platform like X, but you will eventually become a slave to that platform.

You will have to continue using growth strategies to keep your content in front of people. It won’t live on very long.

Modern power belongs to those who hold the most attention in their lifetime.

A short post that holds 10 seconds of attention is nothing compared to a video, podcast, or newsletter that holds 15-60 minutes of attention. But that doesn't mean short content is useless. Short content attracts a broad net of people, while long form filters your audience for fans and superfans.

You must take your best ideas and turn them into longer-form content.

This is how you get your name spread without you trying.

This is how you build the roots of your brand deep into the collective psyche.

This is how you build trust and authority orders of magnitude higher than those who only post short content.

This is why you will almost always attribute the results in your life to a book or an author who taught you how. Books are the most difficult to get people to sit down and read, but those who spend multiple hours with your ideas care about you more.

Traffic Mechanisms – How To Get Your Writing Shared

Now that you are hopefully writing and posting content, it helps to understand the mechanics of the social media game.

Just like a video game, there are mechanics you can learn to win the game faster. Those who don't understand, practice, and become a master at these will only rarely achieve short-lived success. We don't want to bank on luck.

When you understand how these allow you to grow, you can get creative with how you get your content shared.

These are in no specific order.

Test them all and see what works best.

When what you do stops working, you will need to return to the experimentation phase until you find a strategy that works again. Most creators - especially YouTubers - fail for this reason. They hit a plateau, aren't willing to try new things, and get left behind.

At the time of sending this lesson, I just got out of a 2 month lull on YouTube. I was getting half the views I normally do, which feels terrible, but I've done this enough to know that with enough experimentation with ideas, pacing, and editing I can get out of that rut.

Growth Mechanic 1) Replies

This is the most obvious one that everyone does and tells you to.

I'm going to go over the basics here, but we have an entire lesson of this challenge dedicated to an advanced reply method. You'll love that.

Why do replies matter?

If you reply to somebody else’s posts, and readers go into the comments, they have a chance of seeing your reply.

If your reply is good, they may click to your profile, read your content, and follow you if it’s good.

Think of a reply as a post, but it's "hidden" under someone else's post. People who go into the comments will see your post (your reply) and if it sparks interest, they'll visit your profile and start looking at your actual content.

There are a few things here that people miss:

  • Boring content doesn’t make people read the replies . I see people reply to boring posts all day and get a few impressions on their replies. Then they wonder what went wrong. Tip : be careful commenting on posts that don't give people a reason to look in the replies for further opinions. It's not bad, but you may waste your time.
  • Boring replies don’t make people click on your profile . Stop repeating what was said in the first post. Talk about yourself here. Tell people how you’ve experienced the same thing in your life. Give tips that you found helpful. Use the knowledge you have about writing content and apply it here. Talking about yourself leads to the most profile clicks.
  • Small accounts get fewer impressions overall . It’s good to reply and network with accounts at your size, but don’t think it will help you grow by much (that’s fine, you should still do it). Just be sure you are replying to bigger accounts with interesting content.

Growth Mechanic 2) Quote Posts Or Commentary

Quote posts are when someone reposts your content with their own commentary. This can be on X, Threads, LinkedIn, or if someone shares your post to their IG story with their own commentary.

On X, this can work very well if you know how to write persuasive content.

If you quote post someone with valuable advice or something interesting, the original creator may repost you to their audience. This means you get more eyes on your content, which increases the potential for people to click on your profile and follow you.

Be wary here, don’t aim too high with this one like you would with replies.

Bigger creators have so many notifications that they will probably miss your post.

Be able to view your growth efforts from the lens of the creator’s audience you are trying to leverage.

Again, we are going to discuss strategies for this in a future lesson in this challenge. This is a big picture overview for now.

You can also provide commentary on someone else's ideas without quote posting them. You can tag them in a longer form piece of content and if they like what you said about their ideas, they may share you.

This will be a major strategy we discuss in a future lesson.

Growth Mechanic 3) Reposts

Your content has to be good enough, and relatable enough, for people to want to share your ideas on their feeds.

Always think this through before hitting post.

Will people share it? Why should they share it? Do they identify with anything I am saying? Will the right people agree with me enough to share it? Is it polarizing enough to spark discussion and get comments?

Growth Mechanic 4) Sharing links in group chats

By far the most powerful way to build an audience is to write content that is polarizing enough to be shared in niche group chats.

I share maybe 5-10 posts I see a day in various group chats I'm a part of with friends and business partners. Consumers have group chats with their friends as well.

Pay attention to why you share links with someone else, write that down, and attempt to incorporate it into your content strategy.

Growth Mechanic 5) Manual DMs

This one is more difficult and slow, but it works for networking and getting big accounts to share your posts (if you’re a normal person who isn’t a LinkedIn corporate robot in their messages).

Nobody wants to receive a message like, “Hey! Great to connect! I love your stuff! We should have a virtual coffee and talk about the complexities of zapier automations to enhance the overall experience of your loyal email subscribers so that you can build an incredible digital presence!!!!”

You can make friends online to the point where they are happy to just share whatever you send them.

We will discuss how to DM people without sounding weird in a day or two.

The manual DM method can also cross over into a method for controlling your growth.

Sending 1 DM may get 1 impression on your posts, but the potential that they share those posts is higher if you have a genuine conversation.

And again, we will have a separate lesson for this.

The purpose of today's lesson was to make you aware that you aren't growing because you ONLY post content and expect something magical to happen.

No.

Not how it works.

Social media growth is a manual process until you hit breakout velocity, which takes a year or two.

Over the next few days, you will understand this on a much deeper level.

And once you do, it will make a lot more sense how you can monetize and sell a product, because you're in control of how many people see it.

Today's action step is a bit different.

I want you to continue writing as you have been.

But when you're on social media and notice a post that is doing very well, ask yourself, "Why?"

What makes people share the link to the post in group chats? What makes people repost, like, and quote post?

If you can make it a habit to reverse engineer why people grow so fast, you can as well.

DAY 26 - CREATING YOUR FIRST PRODUCT

So far, we have a product idea, customer avatar, and offer strategy.

We now have enough information to start creating the actual product.

Now, I wish I could just tell you exactly how to create it, but I can't, because I don't know what kind of product you are choosing to create.

However, I can give you some tips for how to structure your product.

I can also guide you through my thought process for having AI help with this.

With that said, I'm going to rapid-fire a few tips:

1) Create a hyper-detailed outline first

Just like writing a newsletter.

When you start with an outline, you free up brain space to come up with better ideas. When you scroll, read, listen to, or watch any kind of content, especially while on a walk, ideas will pop into your mind that you can use to flesh out the outline further.

Spend a week or two obsessing over the outline. It will take a different shape multiple times.

As a whole, the outline should follow a general structure (based on your offer):

  • The big problem and desired outcome
  • Various sections on key points they need to reach the desired outcome
  • Your unique mechanism or system - this can be drawn out over multiple sections

Problem > education > system > solution.

That's how you create a digital product that gets results.

If you plan to sell something like a template, tracker, or other type of consumable that doesn't really require a "curriculum" - like a resume template or digital planner - than most of this advice goes out the window. You just need to create the thing.

2) Write each section/module like you're writing a newsletter

Once the outline is 90% of the way there, start fleshing out each section, chapter, or module in its own note or document.

If you are going down the video training route, it may be wise to create slides and examples for this part that you can record for your video.

Note: You don't have to create videos unless your type of product absolutely demands it. Writing is perfectly fine.

When it comes to actually writing each section, treat them as a newsletter.

  • Start with the problem or a personal experience related to the section topic (capture attention and make them realize the importance of that section)
  • Flesh out the body with key points. These key points can just be points, or they can be action steps, lessons, etc. This should be rather straightforward.

I'd personally recommend blocking off an hour or two per day to finish 1-2 sections for your digital product. You can try to bust it out all at once, but I'm a fan of chunking tasks down into manageable daily work blocks.

3) It's okay if it's short and to the point

Some of the best digital products I purchased were very short. 5-10 course modules that were 1000 words or so each.

I bought the product for specific information, and I got specific information.

As a beginner, you'll probably feel like you need to hit some arbitrary number of sections or fill it up with a bunch of knowledge to make it feel dense, but this is entirely unnecessary.

4) You may have to learn a bit of design

For your cover images, thumbnails, and other things like that, you will probably have to create them.

This isn't as daunting as it sounds.

Watching a few Canva tutorials on YouTube will set you on the right path. You can also use sites like Midjourney or AI images, Pexels or Unsplash for free stock images, and Phosphor Icons for free icons.

If you don't know how to do it, have a conversation with AI. It can guide you through the steps of most popular software like Photoshop, Figma, or Canva.

5) Purchase other people's products that are similar to yours for inspiration

For something like an ebook, this is less relevant.

For something like a course, cohort, or community, this is invaluable.

Remember our Day 4 lesson? The one where we made a distinction between content and structure?

When you buy someone else's product, you do it so you can study how the structure the curriculum to give yourself more clarity on how to structure your own.

A community, as an example, isn't straightforward. Most people would just create random chat rooms on Skool, Circle, or Discord and send a weekly article or something like that.

When you join a successful community, you start to pick up little structural details that help you drastically improve your own.

How To Get AI To Help With Your Digital Product

For this step, we're going to do the exact same thing we've done previously with AI.

But this time we're going to do things a bit different.

You can have AI write or create the product for you, but that doesn't feel right.

Instead, we're going to have AI coach us through building the product day by day.

Let's start with a prompt like this in a new chat:

You are an expert at creating digital products, specifically writing-based (not video) self-paced courses. I want you to break down a comprehensive guide to building out a course that get's results. Focus on the course structure, student psychology that makes a successful course experience, and how to build the course out. Don't worry about the course title or anything that doesn't involve the course material itself. Present your output as a detailed step-by-step guide.

For that prompt, you can exchange the relevant information for whatever type of digital product you plan to create.

The output of that prompt should give you enough detail for the next step.

Again, we're going to:

  • Start a new chat
  • Paste this prompt that helps create a prompt
  • Turn the instructions into a multi-phase coach

To create the coaching prompt, use this after you've sent the prompt creator prompt.

I want to create a prompt that helps me build a self-paced course with you as my coach to build it out step by step. Your goal is to help me create an extremely impactful course material that get's students results. You will create this prompt to have multiple phases

Phase 1) Context gathering

  • Ask me questions one by one
  • Ask me for my customer avatar
  • Ask me for my offer information
  • Ask any other relevant questions needed to start the coaching process using the additional context provided below

Phase 2) Course outline coaching

  • Using the additional information below, guide me through step-by-step creating an outline for the course
  • For each step, remind me to provide sources of information or inspiration to pull concepts or lessons from (like books, videos, or my past writing)
  • Once you have enough information to create the outline, output it as one cohesive outline to work off of

Phase 3) Course creation coaching

  • Based on the outline and additional information, coach me through creating each section
  • Give me useful pointers for each section
  • Ask me to give you the complete section before continuing to the next

Additional information:

[paste the instructions from the last step here]

When you hit send on that, it should spit out a rather comprehensive prompt.

This is the prompt it gave me , but it will vary depending on what type of digital product you are creating (be sure to edit the prompts above to reflect that).

Since this is a "coaching" style prompt, you will do this all in one chat inside your AI tool of choice. Whenever you complete a step, you will go back to that same chat and continue where you left off.

Note: The longer an AI chat gets, the more likely you are to hit the context window. For a chat like this that could get very long, it may be wise to use a model like Gemini 2.5 flash (or 2.5 Pro for reasoning) since it has a much longer context window than something like Claude.

I hope you found this lesson valuable.

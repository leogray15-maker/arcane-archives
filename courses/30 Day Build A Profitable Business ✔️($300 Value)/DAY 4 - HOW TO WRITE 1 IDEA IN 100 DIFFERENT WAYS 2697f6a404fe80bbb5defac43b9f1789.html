DAY 4 - HOW TO WRITE 1 IDEA IN 100 DIFFERENT WAYS

Welcome to day 4

Today's lesson will be short and impactful.

This is one of the best-kept secrets of content creators.

This is how you "steal like an artist."

We really need to nail down content writing before we move onto anything else.

Please use this as a time to practice writing engaging ideas whenever you have the time or bandwidth to do so.

In yesterday's challenge, we started creating an "Idea Museum" filled with ideas and social posts we can use as a growing source of inspiration.

Our main focus was on the ideas we were saving in our Idea Museum.

Today, I want to focus on the structure of the ideas.

Becoming a good writer or speaker isn't only about the idea, but how the idea is articulated.

The idea does a lot of the heavy lifting, but the structure is what makes it engaging, unique, and impactful.

Let's go over an example from this document I have with 15+ post structures:

One pattern I've noticed in happy people: They're obsessive about maintaining their mental clarity.

The idea here is that happy people maintain their mental clarity.

The structure is formatted in 2 parts, a hook in the form of an observation and the delivery of what the observation is.

It seems simple, but the difference in the structure of an idea can make all the difference.

Now, if I take another post structure from that document (like a "list" structure) I can reformat the same idea like this:

Happy people are clear-minded people:
  • They take time for rest
  • They focus on one singular goal
  • They ruthlessly eliminate distractions

In other words, happy people are obsessive about maintaining their mental clarity.

If you wanted to, you could practice writing the same idea with every single post structure that's in the document above.

Today's challenge comes in 2 parts:

1) Break down 3 ideas into their structure

Choose 3 ideas from your Idea Museum.

Then, either in the space under that idea or in a new note/document, try to break down each part of the idea and write why it works.

If you don't have experience with content psychology, that's okay. You learn it as you practice.

This is the perfect time to employ AI for help. Try this prompt for each of the 3 posts:

Do a comprehensive analysis on this social post. The overall idea, how the sentences are structured, and choice of words. Analyze why people engage with it, why it works so well, what psychological tactics are being used, and how I can replicate this style step-by-step with my own ideas.

[paste the social post]

Paste that prompt your AI tool of choice, then replace the bottom text with the text from the social post you are wanting to break down.

I'd recommend Claude Sonnet as the model to use for this over ChatGPT or Google Gemini.

Note: I would continue doing this for any idea you find along your journey that you want to incorporate as a part of your writing or content style. You can use this for videos as well, not just posts.

2) Rewrite 3 ideas with different structures

Go back to your idea museum and choose one idea that you didn't use in step 1, or find an idea using one of the methods we've discussed previously.

Then, try rewriting that idea with the 3 post structures you broke down with the AI prompt.

As you write the idea with new structures, remember to use the writing process we talked about on day 1 of this challenge if you get stuck.

Why are we doing this?

Well, you now have all of the secrets to creating viral content and coming up with good ideas.

Seriously, those are the secrets. Any results that come from them are a matter of practice.

If you ever get stuck, you can refer back to these first 4 days of the challenge.

Over the next few days, we will practice this process a bit more by going over 3 types of posts that always tend to do well. That way you have a "fallback" for content if you ever feel like you're stuck in a rut.

Of course, 4 days is still a short amount of time. Don't expect to be an expert just yet. Your brain needs practice to rewire itself to the point of content being easy to write.

THE DISCOVERY THAT CHANGES SALES FOREVER

berridge and robinson found:

dopamine primarily drives WANTING (craving, desire, motivation)

opioids drive LIKING (pleasure, satisfaction, enjoyment)

they're completely separate systems.

proof: 6-OHDA lesions eliminating 99% of dopamine don't affect 'liking' reactions.

rats still show pleasure responses to sugar. but won't work to get it.

this explains why:

  • people buy courses they never finish (90% incompletion rate)
  • customers want your product desperately then feel empty after
  • gym memberships print money (4.8 visits/month average, $58/month revenue)

you're not selling satisfaction. you're selling the craving.

THE INCENTIVE SALIENCE BREAKTHROUGH

"incentive salience" = unconscious magnetic pull toward rewards. your brain marks things as "MUST HAVE" without conscious decision.

berridge's experiments proved:

  • amphetamine in nucleus accumbens increases wanting by 400%
  • doesn't increase liking at all
  • subjects work harder for rewards they don't enjoy more

how to create incentive salience:

  1. PARTIAL REINFORCEMENT SCHEDULE 17-23% reward rate creates maximum response. slot machines use 19%. creates 3.5x stronger neural response than consistent rewards.
  1. CUE ASSOCIATIONS pavlovian conditioning works unconsciously. pair product with existing desires. brain transfers wanting automatically. measured: 250ms response time, before conscious thought.
  1. NEAR-MISS ENGINEERING "almost got it" spikes dopamine harder than winning. fiorillo et al (2003): uncertain rewards create 2-4x dopamine response. limited spots, countdown timers, waitlists. the almost-loss creates measurable desperation.

THE DARK TRUTH ABOUT REPEAT CUSTOMERS

dopamine response decreases with repetition:

  • 1000th exposure: habituation complete

that's why customer lifetime value drops. your product becomes "normal." dopamine stops firing.

solution: constant novelty

  • new features every 30 days
  • new positioning quarterly
  • new competition framing
  • new scarcity triggers

keep them in wanting mode, never satisfied.

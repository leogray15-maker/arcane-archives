THE INVISIBLE SCRIPT

everyone has a core sentence that runs on loop:

  • "i have to work harder than everyone else"
  • "i can't trust anyone"
  • "i'm not the type of person who..."
  • "that's just not how i was raised"
  • "i don't deserve..."

you've said it 10,000 times. it feels like truth. it's not.

HOW TO FIND YOUR SENTENCE

notice what you say after:

  • "i can't do that because..."
  • "i'm just not someone who..."
  • "that works for others but..."
  • "i've always been..."

the next words are your programming. not your truth. your programming.

THE PATTERN INTERRUPT

i had a client who always said: "i have to earn everything the hard way"

showed him his last 10 decisions:

  • chose the difficult client over the easy one
  • complicated a simple business model
  • turned down help he needed
  • made everything take twice as long

he wasn't living life. he was proving his sentence true.

YOUR SENTENCE CREATES YOUR REALITY

"money doesn't come easy" = you'll reject easy money

"i have to do it alone" = you'll sabotage partnerships

"good things don't last" = you'll sabotage what’s working

"i'm not lucky" = you'll overlook opportunities

you're not unlucky. you're obedient to your sentence.

WHERE IT CAME FROM

usually between age 5-12:

  • parent struggled with money: "money is hard"
  • got betrayed by friend: "can't trust anyone"
  • succeeded and got punished: "don't shine too bright"
  • failed and got mocked: "i'm not smart enough"

one experience became your entire worldview.

THE UNCONSCIOUS PROOF CYCLE

your brain needs your sentence to be true. so it:

  1. filters out contradicting evidence
  1. amplifies confirming evidence
  1. creates situations that prove it
  1. attracts people who reinforce it

you're not living life. you're defending a thesis.

BREAKING THE PATTERN

step 1: catch yourself saying it

  • ask: "what story did i just tell myself?"
  • write it down exactly

step 2: question it

  • is this actually true?
  • true for everyone or just me?
  • what evidence contradicts this?

step 3: run an experiment

  • do the opposite once
  • just once
  • notice what happens

it’s rewriting what you’ve always believed

the patterns that have been going on for so long you think it’s reality

once you confront it, you are freed.

THE RESISTANCE

when you try to change your sentence, you'll feel:

  • anxiety ("this isn't safe")
  • guilt ("this isn't who i am")
  • fear ("something bad will happen")

that's not intuition. that's your programming defending itself.

your old identity is scratching at the surface trying to stay alive.

you can’t let it happen, you need to override it.

PRACTICAL APPLICATION

tomorrow, catch yourself three times:

  1. morning: what's your first limiting thought?
  1. afternoon: what excuse did you make?
  1. evening: what story did you tell about your day?

these aren't random thoughts. they're the code running your life.

THE BUSINESS IMPACT

your sentence determines:

  • your prices (what you "deserve")
  • your clients (who you "attract")
  • your growth (what you "can handle")
  • your income (what's "realistic")

change the sentence, change the business.

THE MOMENT OF CHOICE

every time your sentence runs, you have a choice:

  • obey it automatically (unconscious)
  • question it directly (conscious)

consciousness is just seeing the pattern. once you see it, you can't unsee it.

BOTTOM LINE

you're not broken. you're programmed.

you're not limited. you're obedient.

you're not unlucky. you're loyal to an old story.

the unconscious runs your life until you make it conscious. and consciousness begins with catching yourself in the sentence.

i hope this clears things up, and i hope you take a deep introspective dive into your psyche.

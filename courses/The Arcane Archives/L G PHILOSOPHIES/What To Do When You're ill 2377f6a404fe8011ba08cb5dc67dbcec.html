What To Do When You're ill.

i've not fallen ill in a while...

but right now... it's 3:16am...

my head is throbbing, my joints hurt, i've got a blocked nose.

i've not felt like this for a while... and I wonder why.

it could be the fact that winter is starting...

it could be the fact that god is testing me...

or, it could be both.

the law of synchronicity is a beautiful thing.

the last couple months... i've not been "grinding" like I do every winter.

I simply was enjoying the fruits of my labour.

but now... i've gone back to it.

cold plunge in the freezing cold, every day.

gym sessions every day.

strict diet, every day.

working on courses/products/writing every day, all night.

sending out emails, every day...

I've gone back to the script of the "grind"

and now...? I've fallen ill.

it's nothing significant, but it's enough to make me question it.

realistically, i've not been ill in I don't even know how long...

you know that smell you get when you have a blocked nose? it's bringing me nostalgia.

but here... I've got a philosophy on why I'm ill.

i'm not muslim.. but I love the philosophy then have on illness.

they say it's a reset of your body, and it's a test of your patience and gratitude.

you know after you overcome the illness... you are actually grateful for having the ability to breathe through your nose again?

and here's the thing I like about being ill...

when I go back to normal... what identity do I fall back into?

do I go back into work... video games... a normal life which I dread?

or do I go back into money, fitness, and health?

I mean, it's kind of beautiful...

I remember in school I would love to feel ill.

I would love to feel ill because I knew it was a day off..

i'd pretend to be ill for days off...

having a blocked nose in the winter and playing fortnite season 2 while I knew that everyone was in school...

what a nostalgia inducing feeling.

but now...?

I'll do anything to not get ill.

I don't want the gym taken away from me...

I don't want to not be able to grind all day on my business... that's what fulfils me.

so what are my plans to recover?

honestly, here's what I'm doing.

it's 3:30 am rn, 14 minutes into writing this... I'm sipping my "potion" of a tea which I got from last years capital club.

so...

let me just diagnose myself with a treatment.

the gym opens at 6am, gonna do some hot and cold therapy...

will get in the sauna for 20 mins, then the cold shower for 3, repeat that 3 times.

that always feels like you've just factory resetted your body...

sweated out all the shitty toxins.

then what will I do once I get home from that?

I'll probably sleep, because I've been awake all night.

I would've done some work on my courses, but I've just been watching courses.

I mean I don't want to go on netflix put some shit on... it's just boring.

being sick is a perfect excuse to do that, but no, It's just not even "rest" to me.

so... we'll do that hot/cold therapy at 6am...

go sleep after that.. and I've got a cigar coming in tomorrow, which is very convenient.

the law of synchronicity is coming back into play.

little quorum churhill cigar... a long dark smoke.

pretty cheap... but it's never about the price.

handrolled by someone with intention, nice smoke, good length.

it's nice.

so... let me summarise my plan after the sauna.

wake up... drink some coffee with some fermented raw goats milk in it.. give my body some good bacteria to fight off this disease of a cold.

smoke my cigar alongside the coffee..

then yeah, should be feeling better by then.

sweated out some toxins...

gave my body some sleep to recover...

will give my body some good bacteria from the raw milk to fight this off...

a meeting with myself and my cigar to think about the future...

gratitude that I can actually do this because I don't work a normal job...

then we fuck up the day.

it all comes back to one thing really.

cigar, coffee, back in the game.

honestly, don't even know why I'm writing this...

i've gave myself a plan to return back to baseline.

and...

maybe this will help someone in the future.

so, not too bad is it?

i'll end it on this...

be grateful for what you've set your life up to be.

the automated systems... your physique in the mirror...

it's all from your past hard work.

have a break, have a cigar, just don't ever quit.

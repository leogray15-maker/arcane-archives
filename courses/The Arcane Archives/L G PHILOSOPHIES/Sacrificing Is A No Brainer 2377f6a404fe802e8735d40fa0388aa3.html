Sacrificing Is A No Brainer.

I don't know what to write about...

but I want to write.

why?

it's probably a mix between the love of writing and exploring my mind...

and the potential for one of my posts to blow up.

it's a mix of both.

which brings me to a point...

balance...

what is balance?

here's my philosophy on it.

there is always a ying to a yang.

of course, you need rest.

so why do I drink a black coffee when it's meant to be time for bed?

because I want to.

now, this isn't to sound like some guru entrepreneur...

but really, I want to.

there's unfinished work that I need to do.

there's projects that I need to complete.

I get up in the morning because of stress, that may be a part of the reason I don't want to sleep.

but is that really a bad thing?

why do I feel such a cortisol spike when I wake up?

it's because I know I need to work.

I know that I have hard work I need to face...

and it's not a matter of "will I do it?"

it's a matter of when.

that's why I'll wake up stressed.

but I love it...

if I'm sick, I have to work.

If I'm tired, I have to work.

If i'm depressed, I have to work.

that's why I  sleep late.

it's to wake up knowing that I've already done enough work for tomorrow.

i've completed tomorrow's work today...

but I'll still wake up with stress even after that...

there is never an end to business, there is always new things you can do.

in a day job you can have a 16 hour shift... then you can  have the next day off because you've done your 8 hours the day before.

in business you can do a 16 hour shift then you feel like you need to do another 16 hours tomorrow.

it's never ending.

you finish a project, but there's another project to finish after that.

honestly, I love it.

it's beautiful knowing that progress is simply limited to my personal capabilities.

I'm not limited anything else but the amount of work I put in.

if I do more... more money comes out.

but in a normal job...?

you can't do more, you're paid per hour.

I'm paid per decision.

all these gurus will tell you that "business is harder than a normal job"

but honestly have you seen how hard people actually work?

in mcdonalds you have to complete 50-100 orders per hour... in exchange for £12 per hour.

I don't understand it...

I'm sure you don't get paid in commission in every order you take...

so how can you see £500 being made in an hour inf ront of your eyes and be content with your little wage enough to buy one large meal?

it's pretty crazy to me... but back to the point.

you're paid per hour while I'm paid per decision.

you cannot ever become financially free until your time isn't directly linked with the amount of money you are making.

you have 24 hours in a day, if you worked all of them you will still make £240 after tax.

this "save until you're rich" mindset is absolutely horrible.

you don't save until you're rich.

you just increase the amount of money you make...

I mean, it's pretty simple.

the average man in the UK saves around £4k a year...

per year...

sure, you can get rich doing that...

you can drive around your luxury mercedes... at 65.

you won't attract women, they'll think you're a cute old man who finally got the car he dreamt of for the last 45 years.

what is the point?

dead at 18, buried at 70.

it's still a reality.

live your whole life for money...

to never even achieve it.

save every penny, no no don't tip the waitress.

cheap out on dates with your girl.

just for some guys who sacrifice the ages 18-25 and make your dream salary in a month...

and tick that box of freedom.

if you live like that guy who hopes to be rich by investing his savings...

you're living in the "slowlane"

slow and steady approach... no risk.

you can enjoy your dream car at 65 if you're not dead yet.

or you can live in the "fastlane"

all or nothing...

you either get rich young...

or you get rich young illegally...

there is no other option.

literally, I'm not doing this to sound like some motivational guru.

but you actually only get one chance, do you realise that?

let me throw a right hook at you.

you're maybe 18-25...

by the time you're 25 people are getting married and having kids.

if you're 18, you have around 2000 days left until you're 25.

you have 2000 days to secure everything you've ever wanted.

imagine that number going down to 1500...

then 1000...

then 500...

imagine seeing that you have 500 days left...

you thought so hard that you will be one of these young successful guys...

but instead you're a wantrepreneur, daydreamer.

you was scared to sacrifice these years for a better future, so you sacrificed the better future for these days.

you can live up your 20s or you can work them up...

but living up your 20s will not even feel like you've "lived them up"

because realistically, what is "living up" your 20s?

is it going out to places in your little city?

is it going on dating apps and grabbing whatever agrees to meet up?

is it going on 1-2 holidays a year?

is that what you want to be?

do you not see a better future for yourself?

do you not realise that you can live a life that you've always dreamed of?

listen to this...

if you visualise a life that you would dream of.

anything, no limits.

private jets, multiple houses in different countries.

you can achieve that.

but you have to pay the price.

the price is 7-10 years of hard work.

from 18-28 you just don't live, you just work.

honestly, it's so easy.

the first year... you start making 1k/month, now you don't need a job.

the next 9 years is scaling that up to whatever number you can reach, relentless grind.

that's all it takes...

money is your score in the video game of life.

you can say money doesn't matter, just have purpose.

money dictates everything, money is the essence of life now.

you can either play the game, or don't.

I mean, as a kid I always levelled up my character to level 100 in all skills before I'd go into multiplayer.

I just want to reach the highest echelon in every field in life.

honestly, I don't even know why I'm writing this.

I can't convince you to join this life... It's just such a no brainer for me.

you can have everything you want, and more.

if you don't find your fun in being the best at everything possible.

then I guess you're just not meant for it.

I guess I found what to write about.

that's what I'll leave you with.

take this as you will.

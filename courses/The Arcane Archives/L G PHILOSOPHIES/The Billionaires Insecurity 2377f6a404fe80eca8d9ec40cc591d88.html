The Billionaires Insecurity.

you’re scared you won’t make it.

but when you’ve made it, you’re scared you’ll lose it.

it never goes, trauma is deep instilled.

the billionaires insecurity.

you don’t want to let your family down, because they rely on you.

you’re the man of the house, even at such a young age.

you’re doing everything you can.

but it still doesn’t work.

but when you’ve finally made it.

you’re doing everything you can, but you’re still scared you’ll lose it all.

the journey will break you.

but it’s a journey for a reason.

success is full of ups and downs, which is cliche but the truth.

you will feel like you’re letting everyone down, when really everyone is proud of you.

even when you’re doing your best, you feel like you can do more.

when you work 12 hours a day, you feel like you’re neglecting your mothers pre sense, since she’s got limited time.

but when you spend time with her, you are neglecting her because she still has to work.

in any scenario, there will always be things that can be pointed as bad or good.

but sometimes, it’s hard to point out the good when you’re used to looking at things with the lens of scarcity and not abundance, it’s only natural when you’ve grown up in an environment where you didn’t have nothing.

the reason you’re trying to make it, is to get away from that past life.

but that past life will always be a part of you, you will never be rich, you will always be a broke person with money.

even with all of the visualisation, you can change certain things, but your childhood only happened once.

from ages 0-7 you are in theta state, programming mode.

whatever you saw in those ages programmed your mind.

you was born, and maybe you didn’t even have enough calories to eat in the house, thats why you grew up skinny and have a scarcity mindset.

to switch that mindset to abundance, you need to act abundant.

spend money like it’s nothing, even if it’s your last £5

but worrying about the future stops you from doing that.

but why? you wont consider that you made that purchase in 5 years.

it’s only £5, but you put value on it just because it’s scarce, it’s just perception.

this is why I’ll still be scared of losing it all, even when i have 100k in the bank.

it’s all perception.

some people are scared when they got a paycheck of £1200 instead of £1500.

but some people are also scared when they’ve only made £80K this month instead of £85K.

realistically, it’s all just numbers, but your brain divides them into sections depending on your “normal” state.

you will never outdo your standards, and that’s why you have to make success a standard.

but even when it is a standard, you’re always in caution of your life following below the standard, as if you can’t control it.

but why do you feel like you can’t control it? probably because when you do your best, you still don’t have enough.

the people around you still live scarce lives, nothing changes but a number in your digital banking.

you are shaped by your environment, but your environment cannot be comprised from a singular thing.

an environment is a lot of factors combined.

if you are living a life where the only thing successful about you is your digital banking app, you will always have a mindset of scarcity and feeling like you’re going to lose it.

but at the same time, you will always feel like you’re going to lose it, unless you’re in control, which is almost impossible when there are spaces in between your fingers which are designed to be slipped through.

but also, you can control every aspect of your life, you choose your fate, i don’t question that.

but sometimes it doesn’t feel like your fate can be chosen.

even if you know for a objective fact that it can be.

i think it’s all placebo.

The Beginner Tactic.

What we talk about here is to show you that this IS possible to sell a product as a beginner.

It's typical for business educators to tell people to start a service business first.

For those who don't know what that is, I'm talking about freelancing, agency work, coaching, or consulting. Really any type of client work.

A lot of people will tell you to do this... but with this author of this course(with more knowledge and experience to avoid a lot of pain) He would do it differently.

Creators are often dumbfounded when they find out that you can make around $3,000/month from 300-1000 followers.

It's unheard of for people to monetize out of the gate with a digital product.

Let's make sense of it real quick.

If I want to replace my salary, assuming it's the average US salary of $56,000 a year:

That means I have to make around $4,700 per month.

With a service, that means you need a few clients at $1,500 to $3,000 per month… sounds a lot easier, right?

I thought so, but cold emails and direct messages drain any passion you have for your business to land those clients. It takes time and failure.

Instead, let's say I sell a $150 digital product.

I have to sell around 31 of those products in a month to make $4,700.

So about 1 a day.

If I have a landing page that converts at 2.5% (which is more than possible with an audience and content as opposed to paid ads to people you don't know)…

That means I need 1240 people to view that landing page in order to make 31 sales at $150 to equal $4,700 in that month at a 2.5% conversion rate (how many people buy after visiting the landing page).

With me so far?

Even further, you need more people to view your actual content in order to visit your landing page.

but with only 300 followers… so do you get 1240 people to visiting your landing page over the span of a month?

Those people definitely weren't going to come from my audience, so they had to come from someone elses.

there's a few options here :

1) Hope That I Go Viral

On some platforms it's very possible to have a viral reel, video, thread, or carousel.

It's difficult to plan for this, so I wouldn't bank on it, but be sure than if and when you go viral, you have a product that you can promote to get people to the landing page.

The times you go viral are when you are going to have big spikes in revenue.

The worst thing you can do is go viral and not have anywhere to send that audience.

you can "burst the pipe" which is really the 80/20 and what I personally did, I think you should do it too... the highest leverage task is posting.

but here are other options :

2) Network With The Right People

People overcomplicate networking.

Networking as far as we are concerned is making friends.

Twitter is your platform is you want to go down the networking route.

Let's say you're selling a course on mindset...

Find a mindset page with around 15,000 followers. not "so big" that you would get ignored, but still a large audience with more than enough people to get 1240 of them to your landing page in a month.

By getting on their good side (by commenting on their posts to make myself known and DMing them with compliments on their content) they will be willing to help you.

they can become an affiliate for your product because they might not have a product, and an audience that could benefit and that was ALREADY interested in mindset.

others can share your content occasionally which get's you more impressions and views that could be funneled to my landing page when you promote.

This can result in the occasional large boost in traffic that you can send to my product.

If I write up a Twitter thread or Instagram carousel based on the topic of my product, and they go on to share it, and I promote my product, and the thread gets shared by more and more people, I don't see why you can't get hundreds of thousands of impressions a month without a large audience.

If your product and marketing are good, as we will learn in this course, those hundreds of thousands of impressions can turn into tens of thousands of dollars.

3) Pay For Shares

This one is rather "keep it quiet" but we will talk more about it later.

In short, if you have the mind that paying for shares is bad, frankly, you're a retard.

I've seen the crowd that virtue signals about not doing it, but they usually have no idea what they're talking about. It is a viable marketing strategy that massive brands and agencies use all the time. If you hire a social media marketing agency (that is actually worth their weight) they will be doing this.

Most people that talk bad about paid marketing from brands on social media (vs paying a platform an insane amount to run ads) are usually talking about the futility in paying theme pages for shares, and they are right about that, but they lack knowledge about the rest.

I'll leave it at that for now and you can sit with your thoughts. We will talk about it in "The Growth Strategy" module.

Summary) How The course author made $3000 A Month With 300 Followers

  • he wrote content, and posted until the "pipe bursted"
  • he had a good product
  • he leveraged other people's audiences for traffic by getting my content shared to their audience
  • he promoted my product on content relevant to the topic of the product
  • he stayed consistent, because traffic fluctuates every day.

it's an easy framework, no need to overcomplicate it.

hope you got the idea, let's move on.

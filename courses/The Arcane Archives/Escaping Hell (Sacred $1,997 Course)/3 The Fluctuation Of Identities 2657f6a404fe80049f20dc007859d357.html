You’re weird.

Winning the war against your old identity.

this is called a fluctuation.

So, let’s get into what is actually happening with your life.

I will be explaining the cycle of your life now.

You Build Yourself Up To A Peak.

You spend a couple weeks, or even a month, or even a couple days at the peak.

And up here you have loads of positive emotion.

You’re proud, you’re happy, you’re confident.

You feel like you’re ruling the world.

And listen to this…

Imagine you was that side of you for the rest of your life.

If we feel so good at the top, and we would achieve anything we wanted.

Why do we have this cliff that leads into depression and the lowest point of your life?

Sliding Off The Peak.

If we’re working hard, and we’re on the path to reach a meaningful goal.

Why do we fall off and have this trough?

You literally go from feeling at the top of the world, to feeling sorry for yourself and not even wanting to go out into the world.

You go from working relentlessly to destroying everything you’ve worked for and you just stop doing everything.

It’s weirdly almost like you’ve just created it to destroy it.

Which brings you to the next stage

Self Hatred, Anger, Shame, The Trough.

Here we have anger.

We have guilt.

We have shame.

We have self hatred.

We just have the worst emotions that a man could have.

And this transition could happen in a very short period of time.

You can be at the top of heaven, and in the depths of hell the next day.

I mean, it’s usually gradual.

You go and say to yourself “oh I’ve done a lot of work, I should probably just have a day off”

“I’m pretty tired today, I stayed up a little bit late, I’ll just wake up a bit later so my muscles can be repaired and my focus doesn’t suffer”

This is when your discipline starts to become shaky.

And before you know it… you’re at the depths of hell.

You do JUST enough to get by for the day so your mind doesn’t kill you.

Maybe you go gym, do literally 10 mins of work, and spend the rest of your day in hell in your mind.

Constant self talk, you have to sleep with YouTube on so your mind doesn’t attack you.

It’s just a stage of numbing, and doing EVERYTHING to get away from work.

Which is very weird… because a day before you was doing 8 hours of work.

Now you’re down to 10 minutes.

You are in the cycle of :

  • Your mind talking to itself of how shameful you are
  • Your identity then solidifies as a “shameful” and “guilty” man
  • You start to accept your new identity.
  • You stay in this rut.

But you always get out of this rut… which is a good thing.

But let’s get into why you get out of this rut, since you go from being very down bad to on top of heaven again.

The Recovery.

So, I hope you like my bent arrows.

Let’s dig into what happens in this stage.

So... you rise from the ashes.

Which brings me to a concept that we call an F-T-E

An F-T-E is short for “fuck this event”

It’s the point where your life gets so bad that you have no choice but to change.

Since you will only change if the pain of staying the same is greater than the pain of change.

This is what happens…

Your life is so bad that you actually just get fed up from the way you’re living.

And all these emotions come into one and build an F-T-E (the event where you say “fuck this”)

And at that point, all of the negative emotion builds momentum.

You have one good day, then the second day comes.

Then the first good week comes.

Now you’re back to baseline.

And this is where you tell yourself that you will never go back into a rut again, you feel like a new man.

The Repetition.

Now you’re back into the slippery slope of descendance into hell.

Just like an alcoholic who said “I’m never drinking again” after a hefty Saturday night, you’re back dropping down.

And the cycle, repeats, and repeats.

And at this point you’re living in hell all the time… because you realise the cycle will repeat and you can’t even do anything about it.

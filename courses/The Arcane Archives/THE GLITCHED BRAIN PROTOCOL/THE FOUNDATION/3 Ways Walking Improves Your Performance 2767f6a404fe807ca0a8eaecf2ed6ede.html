3 Ways Walking Improves Your Performance.

P.S if you want a guide on how to walk to make money… Read PRODUCTIVE ISOLATION

Walking is one of the best (and simplest) ways to quickly boost your performance. Here are 3 ways it does so:

1) Helps your brain solve problems

When you get stuck on a problem, going for a walk helps your brain find a solution. How? When you’re not really focused on anything, your brain uses a different network - one that’s better at creative thinking and problem solving. Ever notice how you’ll suddenly get ideas while showering, meditating, or trying to fall asleep? The same effect happens when you go on a walk.

You may be wondering “well then why don’t I just lie on the couch for a while so my brain can figure it out?” Walking increases blood flow to your brain - delivering more oxygen and nutrients to the supercomputer between your ears. The brain uses up around 20% of our body’s oxygen supply, so if it’s not getting enough, we start feeling mentally fatigued, foggy, and unfocused. So when you go for a walk, you’re not only giving it space to solve your problems, you’re also giving it more resources to operate better.

2) Stimulates Vitamin D production

When your skin is exposed to sunlight, it produces Vitamin D. A Vitamin D deficiency causes many issues, including:

Fatigue

Muscle cramps

Weakened bones

High blood pressure

Anxiety and/or depression

Weakened immune system

Many people spend almost their entire day indoors, which means they’re not getting enough sunlight and therefore not producing enough Vitamin D. While you can (and should) take a Vitamin D supplement, it’s always good to get at least some of these nutrients from a natural source. Taking a few short walks each day (yes, even if it’s cloudy) will help keep your Vitamin D levels (and your overall health) high.

3) Increases your happiness and decreases your stress

Walking triggers the release of endorphins, neurotransmitters that increase your happiness and decrease your stress. This is amplified even further if you can walk out in nature - studies have shown that viewing nature (trees, grass, sand, etc.) increases our happiness, focus, and productivity.

This ties back to the first point about problem solving. If you’ve been stressed about some work you’ve been putting off, a short walk will elevate your mood, decrease your stress, and give your brain space to develop ideas for getting that work done. Plus, it’s typically easier to do good work when you’re in a good mood!

Bonus round) Helps modulate your circadian rhythm

The biggest modulator of your circadian rhythm is light. Getting outside and getting direct sunlight for a few minutes in the morning will “anchor” your circadian rhythm to that time, making it easier for you to fall asleep around 16 hours later and for you to wake up around the same time the next morning.

Again, getting outside helps even if it’s a cloudy day. There’s far more light outside than there is inside - plus, viewing light through a window reduces the “quality” of light you’re getting. So go for a short walk every morning and enjoy the performance (and quality of life) boost you get from it!

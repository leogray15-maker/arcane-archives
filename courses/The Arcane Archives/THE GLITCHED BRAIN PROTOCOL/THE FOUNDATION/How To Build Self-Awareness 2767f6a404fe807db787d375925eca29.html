How To Build Self-Awareness.

Building Self-Awareness

Self-awareness is the first step to self-actualization. Why? Because you can't fix a problem you aren't aware of. It's kinda like trying to drive with your eyes closed - without an awareness of your surroundings, you're not going to make it very far.

Here are 3 ways you can start building your self-awareness:

1) Meditating

Your nervous system has a threat defense system called the "fight-flight-freeze" system. The initiator of this response system is the amygdala in your brain. When your amygdala detects a potential threat, it makes you attack (fight), run (flee), or get paralyzed (freeze).

This process typically happens too fast for us to even think about it. From an evolutionary perspective, this is a good thing.

When our cavemen ancestors were ambushed by a lion, they didn't have time to think about what to do next. So our threat detection system is not only a survival mechanism, it's a survival advantage. Or at least it was for our ancestors.

For us modern humans, acting without thinking is a disadvantage. In fact, doing so is the main cause of many of our problems. When we act without thinking, we tend to regret our actions later on. When we get cut off on the highway, we blow our horn. When a friend says something that hits us wrong, we snap at them. When we're faced with a difficult task, we procrastinate.

So how does meditation relate to this? Studies have found that after a few weeks of regular meditation, the amygdala shrinks, which in turn decreases the reactivity of your threat response system. This gives you what's often called "a little space" between your reaction and your response - and that little space is often enough for you to evaluate the situation and make a better decision.

2) Directed brain dumping

Another great way to start building your self-awareness is by writing about yourself in directed brain dumps.

By directed, I mean you will give yourself several questions to answer about yourself, your experiences, and your actions. What these specific questions are depends on what you're going through at the time. However, here are a few examples to help get you started:

  • What do I want out of life?
  • What have I been doing to achieve what I want?
  • What have I been doing that has been keeping me from achieving what I want?
  • What have I been experiencing with people lately? (this could be friends, family, coworkers, people in your community, and so on)
  • What actions have I taken that have caused those experiences to be worse than they could be?
  • What role(s) am I playing in the negative things happening in my life?
  • What actions can I take to improve the negative things happening in my life?

Have you noticed the trend in these questions? They get you thinking about what you've been doing that has either helped you or hurt you.

For example: imagine you've been fighting with one of your friends. By asking yourself questions like the ones above, you'll gain awareness of the actions you've taken that led you to where you are now. As your awareness increases, your ability to change increases as well.

Note: Do your best to be objective with your answers. Admitting your flaws is the only way you can begin fixing them.

3) Tracking over time

With meditating and directed brain dumping, self-awareness can be built quickly. However, changing yourself takes time. If you truly wish to achieve your goals and your best self, you must treat this process as a long-term commitment.

The best way to see if your efforts are paying off is by tracking your actions and your results over time. How do you do that? By going back over your recent brain dumps to see if you're making progress or not. If you can see a positive trend of progress being made, problems being solved, and goals being achieved, then you're on the right path. But if you're still struggling with the same personal (or interpersonal) issues you had a long time ago, then you have more work to do.

Keep asking yourself tough questions. Keep having difficult conversations with your notebook. Keep working to build self-awareness. The more honest you are with yourself, the more confident you are about your ability to change yourself, and the more responsibility you're willing to take, the sooner you'll start making progress toward your best self.

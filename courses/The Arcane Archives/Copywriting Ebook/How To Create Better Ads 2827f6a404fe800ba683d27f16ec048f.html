How To Create Better Ads

How To Create Better Ads Without Doing Any Extra “Writing”

I got this from the great Ken McCarthy - An internet marketing pioneer.

It's not sexy or out of this world but it works.

It's often the simple things that have the biggest impact.

Anyways, here's what Ken McCarthy was saying:

When writing your ads, pretend you're selling your product or service for at least $25,000.

This goes for anything you sell.

Whether it's a $5 eBook or a $5,000 seminar.

Now, why should you do this?

Because when you put yourself in this mindset, you will automatically start thinking of ways to add value to what you're selling.

I know it sounds simple.

But don't let the simplicity fool you.

Doing this will affect the detail you put into writing your ad, the energy you devote to getting inside the heads of the people on your list, and the time you commit to crafting an offer no reasonable person in your market can refuse.

Try it yourself and see.

Grab whatever ad you're working on now and ask yourself,

“What would I have to say and do to justify selling this product for $25,000?”

And then jot down the answers that come to you.

Do this and you'll be amazed at how much better your copy pulls.

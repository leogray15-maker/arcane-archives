How to create instant rapport (This is universal and best of all, no market research is needed).

Now that you've watched these two powerful videos, you'll have an understanding of what Barnum statements are...

Sentences that feel specific but are generalisable to everyone.

Barnum statements are mostly used by con artists, bullshit tarot card readers and people who 'cold read'.

It's all fake and people actually believe it which is the even crazier thing.

It works especially well in copy because people are in a high emotional state and when you're in a high emotional state, you're much more susceptible to believing things you want to believe.

This is powerful stuff.

Sprinkle some of these statements throughout your copy and you'll build rapport that much faster.

Now I'm going to give you some Barnum statements to use in your copy.

A word of warning, don't flood your copy with these statements because it will drastically reduce it's effectiveness.

This is where the 'less is more' principle will have much more impact.

Here's how they look like:

I see you are at the time of your life when certain new decisions are in order. You are at a kind of crossroads and need to make a choice, soon.

You are usually a person of strong character and determination, yet you are, at times so considerate of others' feelings that you do not assert yourself, even when you know are right. This also sometimes leads to undue delay or procrastination over a necessary action that is obvious to you.

If I'm right about you...

You have a need for people to like and admire you. It's important to you.

  • Who doesn't? It's hard-wired into us. This could lead into a pitch for..."How to win Friends And Influence People" right off the back of that statement.

You have a tendency to be critical of yourself.

  • Who doesn't? Most people are, at least in some ways, self-critical. There's something about you; you don't really like. You wish you had hair. You wish you were a few inches taller. You don't like the shape of your nose. We don't like the fact we don't know a foreign language. In fact, this course is an example of being self-critical. You bought this course because you wish to know more about copy. So there's always something.

You have a great deal of unused capability which you have not turned to your advantage.

  • You know those days where you go to bed, look at the ceiling knowing you could have worked harder? Exactly, it's universal. Same thing here.

Now we're going to talk about reverse Barnum's that take both sides of the coin. These get extended.

Now what's significant about almost all of them is that they are telling people something they like hearing about themselves then using that. So all of that can migrate right into copy.

Now here are some examples of Reverse Barnum's:

  • You have a need for people to like and admire you. It's important to you. Yet, you do not often let this stand in the way of asserting yourself. You are not easily intimidated.
  • You have a tendency to be critical of yourself, yet you can at times be bold and outspoken and have others turn to you for leadership.
  • You have a great deal of unused capability which you have not turned to your advantage, but you seem to be growing in self-understanding and are better prepared than ever for a great opportunity.

You see how it seems weirdly specific like it almost applies to just you?

Yeah exactly, but it's a generalisable statement that applies to everyone.

Task and Time Management: Bringing Order to Chaos

If you're like most entrepreneurs, your mind and your days are in constant chaos. You're barely staying afloat in a sea of to-dos. You don't have time to thrive because it's all you can do to survive. You've tried productivity systems and apps, but instead of saving time, they wasted time.

Here's a simple 3-step system you can use to bring order to the chaos in your mind and your days.

1) Do a brain dump

All you have to do is write down your to-dos, thoughts, and ideas.

You'll quickly fill a page (or more) with a chaotic mess. When you're done, you'll think two things:

"Whoa, that was all in my mind?"

"Whoa, my mind feels so much clearer!"

Once you have your messy page of thoughts, move on to step 2.

2) Organize and Prioritize

The time this step takes depends on how much you wrote in the previous step. But trust me - the time you spend on this step will save you literal hours in the future.

Take some time to organize the thoughts. You can either rewrite them on a fresh page, or type them into an app like Notion. Categorize them in a way that makes sense to you. For me, I organize things into business, personal, and other.

Next, start prioritizing the things in each category. You'll find some items are urgent while others aren't so urgent. Prioritize them how you see fit. Certain things will be obvious while other things aren't so clear.

If you get stuck here, just remember that getting something done is always better than getting nothing done. By that I mean it's better to prioritize something rather than nothing. If you can't decide on your own, flip a coin or roll a die.

At this point, you should have a (somewhat) orderly list of prioritized tasks. You can then move on to step 3...

3) Block Time on Your Calendar for Priorities.

This is where the magic happens.

Open your favorite calendar app (I use and endorse Fantastical for iOS/MacOS) and start creating time block "events" for each of your priorities.

If you've never done this before, start small. Time block 60 minutes of the next day for one of your priorities.

The key here is to plan a specific time to work on a specific task or category of tasks. The difference between planning to work on a specific thing or a category of things is up to you and how you work. Experiment and find out what works best for you. Either way, having a plan in advance greatly increases your chances of actually trying to do work and succeeding at doing the work.

From there, build it up like a muscle. Do 60 minutes of the next day for 7 straight days. The next week, increase it to planning 2 60-minute blocks of the next day. 3 blocks a day the week after that. Keep going at whatever pace feels slightly outside your comfort zone, yet doable.

Before you know it, you'll be a productivity machine.

This isn't a one-and-done system - it's a tool you can pull out of your toolbox any time you're feeling overwhelmed and/or underproductive.

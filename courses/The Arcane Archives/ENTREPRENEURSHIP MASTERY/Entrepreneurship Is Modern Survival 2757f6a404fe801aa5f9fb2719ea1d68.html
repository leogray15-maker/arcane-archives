Entrepreneurship Is Modern Survival.

I've never had a paycheck in my life.

and this is good, you know...

robert kiyosaki from rich dad poor dad says "never take a paycheck"

avoid it at all costs.

but you know why?

it's simply because you're training yourself to be a housecat.

you get fed at a specific time, and there's no doubts.

this is bad.

and that's because this is the complete opposite of what entrepreneurship is.

you see... entrepreneurship is the ONLY way to go, because it mimics our ancestors lives.

in business, you eat what you hunt.

you work for a sale, and you get that money.

you take a sales call, and you get that money.

but if you do nothing... you get nothing.

there is no "set" paycheck.

in business, you hunt.

by hunting, I mean getting sales.

getting sales consists of a couple factors...

  • getting potential customers
  • creating an irresistible offer.
  • driving those customers into that offer

and as a result of that, you get sales.

the more customers you get into that "offer", the more sales you get.

just how a lion hunts.

whatever he hunts, he eats.

the more you hunt, the more you eat.

the more customers you get... the better your offer is... the more you make.

that's really all it is.

there is no "cap" or "ceiling" on business money.

it's simply more customers = more money.

and you "hunt" for more customers by posting more content, more ads... etc.

and you make your "killing tool" better by enhancing your offer.

making it more persuading... more value.

learning sales and marketing is sharpening your "spear"

and with a sharper spear, you're more likely to kill.

that one "customer" is more likely to "buy in" when your offer is better, provides more "life changing" opportunity.

that prey is more likely to die with a sharper spear.

I think you get it...

when you don't hunt, you get nothing, it's common sense.

don't be a housecat that gets fed at the same time daily, be a tiger that hunts as much as it wants.

entrepreneurship is modern day survival.

if you don't go into business, prepare to remain a housecat.

and if you don't like the idea of "hunting" for your food, then remain a housecat.

it's only for people that are hungry.

so my message to you as an entrepreneur...

stop thinking of business as a "paycheck"

instead, start thinking of it as a hunting mechanism.

the more you hunt, the more you eat.

simple as that.

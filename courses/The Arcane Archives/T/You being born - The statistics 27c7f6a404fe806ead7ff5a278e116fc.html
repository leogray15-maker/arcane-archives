You being born - The statistics

First, the probability your Dad met your Mum.

He could have met nearly 200 million of the worlds women.

But let’s say over 25 years he met about 10,000.

The odds your Mum was in this group and met your Dad is:

1 in 20,000.

We know people don’t always stay together though…

What's the chance they stay together to have kids?

1 in 10 they talk to eachother.

1 in 10 they have a second date.

1 in 10 they keep dating.

A coin toss they stay together long enough for kids.

The odds them meeting resulting in kids is 1 in 2000.

Combined odds are now…

1 in 40,000,000.

About the size of California.

Now things get interesting…

Mum has ~100,000 eggs in her lifetime.

Dad makes ~4 trillion sperm in the years you could have been born.

What are the odds the exact sperm and egg met?

1 in 400 quadrillion.

Approximately the volume of the Atlantic Ocean in cubic metres.

But we’re just getting started…

All your ancestors had to live long enough to reproduce.

Not just back to the first Homo Sapiens, Homo erectus and Homo habilis.

But the first single celled organism.

‘You are representative of an unbroken lineage of life going back 4 billion years.’

What are the odds that...

your lineage remained unbroken for the history of existence?

1 in 2 odds that a child will be born, grow and reproduce per generation.

For 150,000 generations.

1 in 10^45000.

10 with 45,000 zeros.

That’s not just larger than the amount of particles in the universe.

It’s larger than all the particles in the universe…

if each particle were itself a universe.

But wait…

The right sperm had to meet the right egg for every single one of those ancestors.

That’s 1 quadrillion…

Multiplied by 1 quadrillion…

For each generation.

….150,000 times.

The odds for that are…

1 in 10^2,640,000

Let’s add it all up...

10^2,640,000 x 10^45,000 x 2000 x 20,000 ≈

The chance you were born is 1 in...

10^26,685,000.

By comparison…

The number of atoms in the body of an average male (80kg, 175lbs) is around 10^27.

The number of atoms making up Earth is around 10^50.

The number of atoms estimated in the known Universe is 10^80.

Here’s one last scenario to illustrate it…

It is the probability of 2 million people getting together, each with a trillion sided die.

They each roll the die and all get the same number on the first try.

For example, 657,274,589,495.

That is how the odds of you being here at all are near zero…

What will you do with this information?

Why do I care about this?

I use it to remind me how lucky I am and do all I can to live a life I don’t regret whilst helping others do the same.

Many don't get this chance.

We are here for a short time

Might as well make it a good short time. 🤝

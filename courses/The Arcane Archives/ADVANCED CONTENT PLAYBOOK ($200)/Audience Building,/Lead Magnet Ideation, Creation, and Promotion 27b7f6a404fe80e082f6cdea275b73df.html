Lead magnets are one of the best ways to grow your email list. You're essentially trading people a solution to a problem they have for their email address.

Here's a simple 3-step framework that'll take you from zero to lead magnet hero:

Step 1) Ideation

Here's the mistake 95% of people make when they first start planning a lead magnet:

They try to do too much.

For example, I've seen people write 30+ page PDFs for a free lead magnet. This has a few problems:

  • It takes too much time and effort to write something so long.
  • People won't have the motivation to go through that much information because they didn't pay anything for it. This relates to psychological investment - we're more motivated to make use of something when we paid for it. The higher the cost, the higher the motivation.
  • People won't get any kind of quick win from it.

A free lead magnet should quickly solve one problem your target audience has.

If you have absolutely no clue what you can write a lead magnet about, answer these questions:

  • Who is my target audience?
  • What problems are they struggling with that I can solve?
  • What's one problem I can quickly solve for them?
  • What are the steps they need to take to solve their problem?
  • How can I lay out those steps in an easy-to-follow sequence? Bonus points if you can lay it out as an X-step system, Y-tip framework, etc. People like numbers.

For example, say your audience is busy dads who want to lose fat. You could write a lead magnet showing them how to quickly set up a meal plan for themselves so they can eat healthier and lose weight.

I recently wrote a lead magnet giving busy entrepreneurs 5 tips for improving their focus.

Remember: Keep it simple. Solve one problem. Make it something you can write about in less than 10 pages.  Make it easy for your reader to consume, apply, and get a quick win from the information.

Step 2) Creation

The easiest way to start writing your lead magnet is in Google Docs. To make this even easier for you, here's a link to my Laser Focus lead magnet draft. You can copy the file to your own Google Drive, then replace the text and cover with your own.

The cover was created in Canva. When you create the cover image, make sure the size of the file is 8.5x11 inches. Don't worry too much about creating the perfect cover. Minimalism is usually the way to go. All you need for the cover is a dark background, light text, and a relevant graphic. Here's a link to mine on Canva.

If you want to get fancy with the cover or content, you can use Unsplash for free stock images. For example, if you're writing a lead magnet about meditation, you may want to insert a few images of people meditating. That's an easy way to spice up the PDF and make it more interesting.

Once you're satisfied with your draft, export it as a PDF file.

Here's your creation checklist:

1) Copy my template to your Google Drive

2) Replace the text content with yours

3) Find relevant images on Unsplash and add them to your draft

4) Create your cover on Canva and replace the existing cover in your draft

5) Export the draft by clicking File -> Download -> PDF Document (.pdf)

Step 3) Promotion

Before you can start promoting your lead magnet, you need to go into your email provider and:

  • Create a landing page
  • Add the PDF to the incentive email
  • (optional/advanced) Write a relevant welcome sequence

Once you have your landing page and email stuff set up, you're ready to promote!

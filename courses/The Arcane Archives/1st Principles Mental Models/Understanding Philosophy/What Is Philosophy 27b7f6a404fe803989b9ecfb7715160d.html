What Is Philosophy?

"Science is what you know. Philosophy is what you don’t know."

—Bertrand Russell

Philosophy is the study of deep, important questions about life, existence, truth, and how we should live.

The word philosophy comes from Ancient Greek, and it means "love of wisdom" (with "Philos" meaning love and "Sophia" meaning wisdom or knowledge).

Philosophy is the art of trying to figure out the fundamental truths about the world and ourselves.

In this sense, philosophy is a way of thinking carefully and logically about the things we experience in life, but also about what we can’t easily understand.

It deals with questions that science alone can't answer, like "Why do we exist?" or "What is the meaning of life?"

Philosophers are people who ask these big questions not because they want to achieve something practical, but because they are curious and want to understand reality at its deepest level.

The act of philosophizing—or thinking about these questions—is something that comes naturally to all humans. It's part of being human to wonder about our purpose and the nature of the universe.

So, philosophy is not just about learning facts; it’s about thinking deeply and methodically about the limits of what we know, what we don’t know, and what it means to exist.

Basically, philosophy is the art of pursuing knowledge, trying to make an understanding of life.

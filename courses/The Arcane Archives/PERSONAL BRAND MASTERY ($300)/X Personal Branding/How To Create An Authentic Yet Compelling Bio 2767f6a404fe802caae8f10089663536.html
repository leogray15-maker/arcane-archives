As some of you know, I'm notorious for changing my bio.

Well, I was... I haven't changed it in a while now that I've "figured it out."

But, I know people still struggle with crafting the perfect bio, and it makes sense as to why.

You feel like you have to:

  • Flex a crazy achievement to prove your authority
  • Be as clear as possible so people understand what you do
  • Fit every single thing imaginable into it (your topics, products, lead magnets, other links, and accomplishments)

With this post, I want to show you that all of this is as simple as being honest.

What Is Positioning?

Positioning is how you manipulate how your brand is perceived.

It is also how you manipulate your content, product, or copywriting of any sort – but we are here to talk about the top of funnel... how you position YOU and your brand.

It is the angle that people view you from.

It is the perspective that you plan to share with others, a perspective that they can follow along with.

So, let's just say that positioning is "perspective" in this case.

Now, what is a perspective composed of?

A goal that you are working towards – What broad goal are you trying to accomplish in your life? Financial freedom, mental clarity,

Where you are now and where you've been – What have you overcome in your life? How can you help others overcome those same things?

What you are doing to reach that goal – What are you learning? What are you building? How are those going to help others achieve that same goal? This is unique to you... others can have the same goal but a different way of getting there.

Take some time to answer all of those questions, as that will give you firepower to create a more authentic bio for yourself.

Let's run through an example:

My goal) Help others reach their potential.

I understand the market and know this sounds a bit cheesy, so I don't explicitly say this.

Where I am / Where I've been) Tried every creative or marketable skill, failed at niche business models, and now use all of those skills as a personal brand.

What I'm doing to reach my goal) Studying philosophy, spirituality, and mastery while building a portfolio of digital products.

To make this even more simple and avoid sounding cheesy, here's a great bio template:

"I write about X, Y, and Z. Building: X"

Why does this work? Because it implies the goal that you are trying to achieve. It shows that you are a leader.

It is unique to you and you can avoid cheesy buzzwords relating to the broad goal you are trying to achieve.

Just Be Honest

Too many people overcomplicate this, so I want to show 2 great examples from Sahil Bloom & Justin Welsh.

Sahil has 722 THOUSAND followers with this as his bio, "exploring my curiosity and sharing what I learn along the way."

Is Sahil worried about his niche? Or is he worried about distilling the information that interests him and sharing it in an interesting way so he can attract interested people?

"Building a portfolio of one-person businesses to $5M in revenue."

That is Justin's goal.

Notice how he doesn't say "I built a one-person business to $5M in revenue."

Instead, he repositions the goal in a way that makes someone want to follow along.

People don't care what you're going to do, they care about what you are doing and what they can learn from you.

The point being, if you are a true leader, and actively pursuing a better life for yourself, all you have to do is be honest in your bio.

What are you studying? What are you building? How does it help others?

Answer those 3 questions and you'll have a trustworthy bio that brings in more followers than the dude flexing $100 billion dollar companies.

How This Transfers Over To Content

This does not only apply to your bio.

It applies to the content you are posting as well.

People always think, "I can't write about that, I don't have enough experience."

The thing is, you don't have to give actionable advice based on what YOU have done.

Instead, you can reposition from creation to curation and talk about something rather than at someone.

Instead of the headline:

"I've made $1 million in 30 days. Here's how you can do the same."

You can write:

"Alex Hormozi made $1 million in 30 days.

I reverse-engineered exactly how he did it.

Here's how I will use these principles to make $1M in 365 days:"

Simple, believable, and relatable.

You see what we did there?

We are curating content and advice in alignment with the goal that your brand is leading people toward.

That's all content is.

You can read, repurpose, and copy ideas if you want to.

When you apply them to what you are building and the goal you are trying to achieve, they become unique to you.

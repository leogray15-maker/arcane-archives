Getting engagement or finding entertaining content on social media affects your brain’s reward system similarly to how drugs like cocaine do. Social media use creates a feedback loop in the brain.

When you’re in the “crave more rewards” stage of the loop, you get the urge to get back on social media. Your brain wants to get more of those rewards! Your brain is tricky - it may give you “good” reasons to get on social media, such as:

  • Taking a break from work
  • Emotional attachment to your account(s)
  • Keeping up with your friends and network

This is particularly dangerous for entrepreneurs like myself whose business is based on social media. Even if you get on to do something important, you get distracted and find yourself scrolling or engaging for a long time, which takes time away from your other work.

You can easily get trapped in a cycle. Using social media is easy, but since your days are getting wrecked because you use Twitter so much, doing actual work is hard.

Breaking out of this cycle by yourself is extremely difficult. But you’re not by yourself. You have everyone’s favorite brain enthusiast in your corner. So what do we do?

1) Dopamine Detox aka Dopamine Fast

Take a break from all social media for at least 24 hours, but preferably over 72 hours. The only exceptions are when you absolutely have to use them for business matters. Uninstall the apps. Use the Freedom browser extension to block the sites on your computer.

That way, when you get the urge to check X to see if I've posted any pictures of my cat useful content, you won’t be able to. It’s like a guardrail keeping you on the road to getting your work done.

It’s probably gonna suck at first. You’ll have a constant urge to check social media. You’ll feel like you might be missing out on something important. But that’s just your brain craving its dopamine hits like a Twitch streamer with 0 viewers craving validation.

What’s the point of this? Removing the constant dopamine allows your brain to reset. It’s similar to how fasting from food helps cleanse your body and reduce your sugar addiction.

2) Schedule time blocks which include work and rest

After your dopamine fast is over, you can start making a little time for social media. During these periods, you focus on social media - engaging, DMing, and scrolling for cat pics. But... when do you use social media?

I suggest creating time blocks dedicated to social media. I have what I call CSN blocks on my calendar. I have 4 30-minute blocks a day. CSN blocks are dedicated to checking on my Clients, Socials, and Network - in that prioritized order. I try not to check social media or emails outside of these blocks (or my end-of-day chill period) so I don’t get distracted from my focused work time.

3) Create a plan

Write down a plan for what you’re going to do when you feel the overwhelming urge to check social media outside of your breaks. This is a powerful concept called an implementation intention. That's just a fancy name for an “if-then” plan. If X happens, then you will do Y.

If you feel the urge to check social media, then you will (action that is not checking social media). I suggest getting up and going for a short walk, taking a shower, or reading. Implementation intentions greatly increase your chance of achieving your goal (staying productive) in the face of obstacles (social media addiction).

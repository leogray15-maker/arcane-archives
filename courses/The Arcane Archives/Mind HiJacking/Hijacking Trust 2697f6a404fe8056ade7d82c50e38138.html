Hijacking Trust

In the game, trust is everything.

Because, after all, if they don't trust you, they ain't buying.

Simple as that.

So, how do you get them to trust you?

Mostly by spending time with someone, getting to know them and learning what they're all about.

This makes us feel safe and secure with someone, hence trust

This is fine in real life, but in marketing, not so much as we want to get those sales NOW.

So how do we do that?

Well, you just have to know one thing...

...The future is based on imagination.

...The past is based on memories.

The thing is, imagination is future memories, and memories are past imaginations. They're one and the same, just pointing in different directions.

It's all in the mind.

And as it's all in the mind, there are two ways to get someone to trust you:

  1. Spend time with you and build up memories.

    or

  1. Feel like they've spent time with you by installing memories.

And that's why one of the fastest ways to build trust is to take them back in time and make it feel like they’ve known you for a long time; the way to do that is to explain and showcase your STORY and your HISTORY.

Once someone knows your history, then they will trust you because it feels as if they’ve known you for much, much longer.

And one more thing:

People don't lie because they want to lie; they lie to keep themselves safe. Make them feel safe, and they’ll always tell you the truth.

Because lying is a protective mechanism of the psyche - when it doesn't feel safe, it'll lie, and yet when it feels safe, it'll tell the truth.

This applies to personal, professional and to customer/client relationships, and can be used in personal relationships, marketing, sales, and everything else in between.

This is why building trust (making them feel safe) in modern marketing is one of the cornerstones of conversions.

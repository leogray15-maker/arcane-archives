Safety In Numbers

We're going to talk about proof by numbers.

There's this survey done by Skeptic Magazine of 1000 people who believe in UFOs and alien abductions.

There are people who make a living going around to events of believers in UFOs and alien abductions speaking about having been abducted.

They've written book and have DVDs. There're a million sub-cultures.

If you go to YouTube, you'll see this clear as day.

Of the 1,000 people who believed in both UFOs and alien abductions...

They were asked for the reasons why they believe such things.

816 of the 10000 stated as their number one reason:

"The sheer number of reports means there must be some truth to it."

So if enough people believe something, or are presented as believing something, there must be something to it.

It must be true.

Here's an Investor's Business Daily survey of 1000 active investors, so these are people who are actually in the market, 87% agreed with this statement:

"If stock prices are rising they are very likely to continue rising." 87% of active investors.

Pretty much people respond that way to all trends.

Whatever is happening, that you can demonstrate is happening, people believe it's going to continue to happen.

We all know that what goes up must inevitably come down, right?

But all investor balloons are caused this way.

They are caused again and again and again.

In other words, use big numbers whenever you can.

Empathetic Statements

Before we get into this, I want to ask you a question:

How many of you from somebody in your life periodically get papers you need to sign and you don't read the documents?

If I'm right about you, most of you sign papers without reading (or used to)

Why would you do such a thing?

Because we trust that person and of course, we don't want to read the documents, and we don't have time to read the documents.

In some cases, we might not understand what we read if we read them which is even a greater argument for not signing them without reading them, but nonetheless this is how we all behave with somebody.

One of the ways that it happens with most people is what this FBI agent calls a rapport bridge.

The Bernie Madoff case, one of the categories of people that took large sums of money from were rich Jewish people in New York because he's a rich Jewish person in New York and they all support the same charities, they're all at the same events.

Someone asked Joan Rivers if she lost any money to Bernie and she said, "No because I already took that class. I don't invest in anything I don't understand how the money's being made." She said, "But everybody I know lost money to Bernie."

These rapport bridges when we create them change the dynamic of how people respond to us.

This is an underlying thing if you make that happen with somebody, immediately their natural and their habitual and their logical defences start to go down.

one of the ways do it is through empathetic statements.

Get people to talk about their pain and make empathetic statements back to them about their pain. Why? Because it builds a rapport bridge.

This can also be used conversationally but we'll be using empathetic statements in our copy, we're introverts, we'd rather keep to ourself than talking to random people.

On that note, here are some empathetic statements:

"We know the struggles are tough for you right now"

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed..."

"We’re here to make things a little easier for you"

These are the most common appearing empathetic statements in the markets I've seen thus far.

These kind of statements work especially well in women dominated markets, not so much men

And the other scenario it works especially well if the guru is liked and/or it's a warm audience.

I know, I know, they don't seem out of this world, but this is how people, mostly women, communicate.

Yes this is how people communicate in other parts of the world - this is normal to them whereas for us, it's abnormal. Live with it.

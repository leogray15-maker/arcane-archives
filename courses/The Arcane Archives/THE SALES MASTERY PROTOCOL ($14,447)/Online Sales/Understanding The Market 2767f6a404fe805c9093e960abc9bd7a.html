Understanding The Market

You need to think about your marketing in the right context. You need to be able to pivot your messaging according to the market sophistication.

The 5 stages of market sophistication was coined by Eugene Schwartz, an elite marketer.

Stage 1: Brand new market - you don’t really need to worry about this one. This is when there is no competition, but this is rare nowadays.

Stage 2: There are some competitors in this market - but it is easy to stand out with your claims/promises (by being more specific and quantifiable than the rest with your promises/solutions). Chances are you don’t have to worry about this stage either.

Stage 3: More competition and better claims. This is where most markets live, and probably where yours does. Everyone is being bombarded with offers. You need to position yourself correctly (unique selling point and unique machine).

Stage 4: A ton of competition, having unique mechanisms, and large competitors emerge (Gurus, Apple, Tech Companies). You don’t need to worry about this stage too much. At this point you need to get creative with your marketing and show them how it works. You will learn to do this.

Stage 5: This is just a sea of competitors. The loudest and most valuable brands stand out.

It will ALWAYS be better to market to the stage your market is in or HIGHER.

You can determine your market’s level of sophistication by researching in the places we mentioned previously.

The more content/people/amount of offers there are, the more sophisticated your market. Are they excited or tired of the products being pushed to them?

If they’re tired, you will have to get more creative with your marketing (which is what we are here to do).

Your Target's Level of Awareness

There is no “right way” to post on X, create a blog post, write an email, or launch an ad each and every time.

Your goal is to speak to specific people within their given awareness level.

The best way to think about it is by looking at your brand through your target’s eyes.

The Buyer’s Journey - 5 Stages of Customer Awareness

Stage 1: Unaware. People won’t buy from you here. They don’t know who you are or what you sell. They don’t know they have a problem and that there is a solution for it.

Stage 2: Pain aware. They understand they have a problem but aren’t aware of a solution.

Stage 3: Solution aware. They know they have a problem and that there is a solution but they do not know about your solution.

Stage 4: Product aware. They know about your product but just haven’t bought it yet.

Stage 5: Most aware. People know you and your brand, they trust you. All it takes is a bit of scarcity to push them over the edge (discount or limited time offer).

We talked a bit about this in Solopreneur Foundations with your funnels/email sequences. Your "top-of-funnel" content (tweets, posts, videos) should focus on phases 1-3. Then you work them through your emails/backend funnel to introduce them to your product.

Now is your time to start researching current products/services in the marketplace. Take note of them - you can use them to create better offers and “steal” ideas from them.

If you currently have an offer, now is the time to think about how you should actually be promoting.

Are you going straight out of the gate asking people to buy in stage 1? Or are you capturing a lead and nurturing it into stage 5?

Research Your Ideal Customer

Based on your market’s level of sophistication and the majority of your targets’ awareness levels, answer the questions below.

These will be absolutely critical when creating your offer, writing copy, creating social posts, ANYTHING.

Most people shoot in the dark with this stuff, you want to be brutally aware of what your audience/potential customers are going through.

If you can, try to find these answers from REAL people on the internet. If you can’t, well... that's even more reason to start growing an audience or getting active in communities.

Important: Since you are probably looking for clients on social media - the BEST form of market research is to offer free help in the DMs and get them on a call OR pay attention to the engagement/response to your content.

On this call, you can ask the questions below or other questions you have about their life / business. From there, you can give advice and start to make connections to implement in your offer.

Questions to answer about your target:

  1. What is the typical story of this person that would buy what you plan on offering?
  1. Why do they buy it?
  1. What is their mindset towards their problem?
  1. What would make them buy your offer?
  1. What results do they hope they get?
  1. What have they done before to solve this problem?

Questions to answer about yourself:

Chances are you are selling something related to what YOU are good at. You may have purchased a similar/related product, or you have had these questions pop into your mind while learning.

The questions below are from Jose Rosado (a previous mentor of mine). Check him out on Twitter @joserosado.

  1. What was your mindset then?
  1. Why did you buy it?
  1. What results were you hoping to get?
  1. What problem of yours were you trying to solve?
  1. What did you struggle with?
  1. What were your biggest “aha” moments?
  1. What were your objections?
  1. What stopped you from buying?
  1. What do you wish you had done differently
  1. What do you hope to do differently?

Answer those questions, save this in your Google drive, and start thinking deeply about your marketing.

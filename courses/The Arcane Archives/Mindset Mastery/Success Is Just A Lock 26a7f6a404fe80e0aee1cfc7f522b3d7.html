Success Is Just A Lock

got this one from brian tracy...

if you can succeed at one business, you can succeed at them all.

why?

because it simply comes down to principles.

this is kind of expanding on my "1000 of it = success" module.

we know that 1000 days of work in any business will bring you success...

but you also need to see that the success will carry over.

let me give you an example so you can dilute it into your brain.

let's say you take a salesman.

this salesman went into the sales job with NO experience, nothing.

didn't know shit, just got a sales job.

he then worked his way up to rise to be the best salesman in the field.

now... what happened in the middle?

really? how did he go from not knowing shit to becoming the best in that field.

then answer is simple.

he learnt the ins and outs of sales.

he learnt the objections, and he handled them perfectly.

he learnt what body language was the most effective by just sheer testing.

he figured out the perfect way to greet a prospect.

he simply just built the character of a perfect salesman.

he acted like that "perfect salesman" for long enough...

and eventually, he became that "perfect salesman" as his natural character.

he simply adopted the traits of the successful, so he became it.

but alright.... how does this link back to success being a "lock" then?

imagine this for a second.

just visualise a lock with about 100 different numbers.

there's no way you can unlock that rn, never.

this is the lock of success.

to unlock this, you just have to get all the numbers right.

these "numbers" are simply pieces of information that the successful have.

and once all these numbers in this lock are correct, you have unlocked success.

and here's the key thing...

this lock does not care if you reach success, it's simply an object.

if you don't have the numbers, the lock doesn't open.

if you do have the numbers, the lock opens.

the lock doesn't care, it is just a lock.

but the thing that "cares"?

that's you

so to go in your favour... to unlock this "lock"

you simply need to learn the ins and outs of the things you're trying to achieve.

for example, the salesman.

he went into the sales job with NO experience, didn't know shit.

but then imagine he studied the best salesman in history.

imagine he obsessed over the video recordings of the best salesman in the world and just rewatched them 20x and took mental notes.

now he has a goal "identity" to achieve.

whenever he acts out of proportion with that goal "identity", he learns a lesson.

he catches himself, and he corrects it,.

now the next time he is about to act out of the accordance of that "identity" he catches himself and acts as the identity of the "successful" salesman

and after a while of this... he becomes the successful salesman character.

and with that character comes all the things that the successful salesman has.

in other words... the money.

so here's what i want to leave you with...

being rich just comes from having a rich "character"

a rich character is made up of characteristics.

characteristics are just the way that you do things.

you can learn characteristics, and you can mould them.

and you remember that "lock"?

that's just the lock of characteristics.

so here's the thing...

if you want to be a success, you simply develop the traits of success.

and how do you do that?

just have mentors.

have people you look up to, read their books daily, listen to them daily.

ALL billionaires read for atleast 2-3 hours a day, that's what I learnt from brian tracy.

but why do they do that?

because they're unlocking the "code" to trillionaire status.

so, unlock the lock.

start taking learning seriously, and don't stop.

as long as you learn, you earn.

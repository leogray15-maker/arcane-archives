12. Balance

Let’s take a deep dive into what "balance" means and how it can change everything for you. Imagine the world like a scale, always striving to stay even. Now, when you live in balance with everything around you, your life feels smooth, like a gentle ride down a calm river. You don’t need to fight against the current; things just flow. You’ll find that your goals materialize almost effortlessly, like magic, when you’re in harmony with the world.

But here’s the twist: when you make something too important—whether it’s a goal, a problem, or even your own self-worth—you throw that balance out of whack. It’s like you’re tipping the scale too far on one side. When you make something too important, you create what’s called excess potential. And that excess potential disrupts the balance. The universe, in a sense, sees that you’ve raised the stakes, and it creates resistance. It’s like you’re in a tug-of-war with life itself.

So how do you fix this? Lower the importance. Here’s where the secret lies. When you lower the importance of your goal, your problems, people, or even your fears, you stop fighting against the natural flow of things. You let go of that extra pressure, and just like that, obstacles fade away. The problem that seemed so huge? It starts to shrink.

For example, if you dislike yourself, you might work really hard to hide your flaws, trying to cover up everything you don’t like. But here’s the catch: the more effort you put into hiding your flaws, the more importance you’re giving them. And when you raise the importance of something—like those flaws—life will send you experiences that highlight them more. It’s like the universe is saying, “Oh, you think this is super important? Let’s make sure you get more of it!” But if you lower the importance of those flaws and just accept them, you stop attracting more of that unwanted energy.

you will go to a special event tomorrow and you worry about looking good, the next morning you wake up with an acne breakout.

The key is acceptance—not underestimating or ignoring what’s happening, but simply seeing the world as it is. Don’t see your goals or your challenges as these giant mountains you have to climb. See them as part of the natural flow of life, and focus on your intention to move forward, without overloading them with unnecessary importance.

When you lower the importance, you take the pressure off, and life gets easier. You stop fighting with the world, and instead, you glide along with it. You’ll realize that you don’t need to struggle to get what you want. The more you lower the importance, the more you create balance, and from that balance, everything you need will come to you with ease.

The world will always try to bring things into balance. When you lower the importance, you simply allow the universe to return to equilibrium, and suddenly, things start aligning perfectly.

you don't worry, you don't stress, you just flow.

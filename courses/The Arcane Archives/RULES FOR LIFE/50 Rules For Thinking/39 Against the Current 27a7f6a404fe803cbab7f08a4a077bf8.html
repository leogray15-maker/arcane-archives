39. Against the Current

The "Against the Current" concept emphasizes the importance of shifting from being an active participant, constantly trying to control and direct everything, to adopting the role of an observant. In this mindset, you stop resisting life’s flow and start observing rather than reacting.

observation>reaction

weirdly, this is a trait you see in men with higher testosterone.

more logic, less reactive.

Zeland's declaration suggests that many people try to control their circumstances or fight against the natural flow of life. This happens when we impose our own rigid expectations and plans on the world around us. When something doesn't go according to our mind’s plan, we can become upset, criticize, or push against it. This resistance is often the root of many unnecessary struggles.

ray dalio, billion dollar investor also touched on this.

"there are anxious times in every entrepreneurs career, when your expectations of what should be happening aren't aligned with what is happening, and you don't know if what you're looking at are opportunities or catastrophic mistakes" - ray dalio

Instead of reacting negatively when things don’t go according to our expectations, the practice is to step back and observe without judgment or emotional involvement. By doing so, we detach from the urge to control every situation and allow things to unfold naturally. The mind’s need for control only leads to frustration because life is constantly changing, and the mind can't predict or solve everything.

When you "go with the flow," instead of trying to force life in a particular direction, things tend to resolve themselves more harmoniously. Problems that initially seem insurmountable can often fix themselves or become less significant when you're not clinging to your mental script. The key here is to lower the importance of the situation and release resistance.

This approach allows you to move through life with less friction, experiencing less stress and more acceptance. The Universe is constantly in motion, and by surrendering control of the flow, you align yourself with a more effortless experience. It’s a shift from resisting the current to trusting that things will unfold as they should, even if they don’t look the way you expected them to.

things are going to go wrong in life, that's a fact.

so why react negatively to it? you're just sabotaging yourself.

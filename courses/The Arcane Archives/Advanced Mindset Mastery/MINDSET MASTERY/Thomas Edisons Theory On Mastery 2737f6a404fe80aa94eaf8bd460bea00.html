Thomas Edisons Theory On Mastery

you can become an expert at anything in 6 months.

you think you want to learn how to play the guitar?

6 months training, you're there.

you think you want to become good at copy?

6 months training you're there.

you see the point?

people think short term.

even though 6 months is short in the grand scheme of things, you can become an expert at anything in 6 months.

along with this comes the 20 hour rule.

if you want to become good at boxing, you book a 20 hour training program with a coach.

the first 20 hours have the HIGHEST ROI.

that's the 80/20.

after 20 hours of 1-1 boxing practice, you can beat 99% of people.

after 20 hours of analysing copy, you become miles better than most writers that don't analyse.

so, here's the main point.

if you want to become "good" at something, you set 20 hours.

if you want to become a mini expert at something, do it for 6 months.

6 months mastering a skill, then move onto the next one.

that's HOW it works, that's thomas edisons theory.

james dyson also had this..

he wasn't an engineer, he didn't have any qualifications, he had no clue.

but he had a vision of dyson, the company.

and with 6 months, he focused in on learning engineering, after that he knew exactly the in and outs of engineering.

uni courses are useless, they make a 6 month program into 3 years to milk your pockets dry.

with 6 months, you could have more knowledge on marketing than ANY uni student that studied for 3 years.

but here's the key :

obsession.

you obsess over something for 6 months, you learn it in and out every day.

you will become a master at it.

it's a good thought to have... knowing that you can "mold" your character to ANY SKILL you want.

all it takes is some dedication.

hopefully this unlocked something in your mind.

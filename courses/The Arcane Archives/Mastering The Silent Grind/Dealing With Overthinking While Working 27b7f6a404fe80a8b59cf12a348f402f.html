Dealing With Overthinking While Working.

this one was inspired by sladislov, appreciate the idea...

but here is how I would go about this.

What this is really is impostor syndrome, that’s what I thought first…

But in the middle of writing a module on impostor syndrome, I stopped because that’s not really the issue.

Here’s what it really is.

You’re just a perfectionist.

Now this isn’t inherently a bad trait, but it can play on your mental and play tricks on you.

And tell me if you’ve experienced this… this might be a separate problem.

You get into a flow state… write An amazing post, module etc…

Now you have thoughts of “I can NEVER replicate this quality again”

But then next day comes and you write a post even better than that, then you get the same thought.

Honestly, still happens to me…

But I’ve realised it.

And you know why this happens? Because you still have a little doubt in yourself.

When you write a post or module that is amazing… it just feels like it wrote itself.

Like you didn’t have control over it, it was just the flow state talking.

But here’s the thing, it will repeat again and again… and you WILL just keep on getting better and getting that same feeling.

You feel a little nervous when you write an amazing post… but that just means that you have to match that AND you don’t THINK you can… that’s why you’re feeling it.

And why don’t you THINK you can? Because it just felt like the flow state was talking.

But really, it’s you.

It’s your mind in a state where there are no distractions and it’s just you thinking.

So you can replicate that by going BACK into a flow state.

But here’s the thing...

You don’t think you can because the information comes from your subconscious mind.

But you will come back with a BETTER post the next time because your mind has learnt.

It’s a pretty interesting topic, but I just want to know that the doubt is pretty normal for a sane human being that doesn’t study the mind autistically.

But let’s move onto the problem that we was meant to talk about.

Having anxiety WHILE making a post.. email… module… whatever it is.

And you won’t expect this…

What If I told you that it was a personality trait of a winner?

You know what you’re doing? You’re just stressing that your writing might not be the BEST POSSIBLE.

And when you frame it that way… it does not seem like a bad thing at all.

So here’s the new mindset I want you to approach this with.

This way of thinking is good.

You may experience stress/anxiety WHILE writing it.

But who said stress was a bad thing?

let’s question reality for a second…

And let me tell you the parable of the Chinese farmer. (I mention a lot of parables, I just learn them because they’re interesting.)

Let’s get into it :

The Parable Of The Chinese Farmer.

Long ago there was a Chinese farmer…

And they only had ONE horse left.

This horse was used to travel through the hot weather… and without it, it would be hard to live.

But one day, the farmers son forgot to lock the Gate and the horse escaped…

The neighbours came and started telling the Chinese farmer about how much of a tragedy this is…

The farmer replied, “Maybe yes, maybe no.”

The next day… the horse came back.

But this time it had brand 6 other wild horses with it.

The neighbours came and started talking about how this is such a blessing…

The farmer replied, “Maybe yes, maybe no.”

The next day the farmers son tried to befriend one of the wild horses and ride it…

BUT then the horse didn’t comply, and the son fell onto some rocks and broke his leg.

The neighbours came and told them how this is such a tragedy and a curse…

The farmer replied, “Maybe yes, maybe no.”

The next day a general from the Chinese Army arrived to take in all the young men of the village into the army…

They was fighting against a terrifying strong force…

But the farmers son was not taken in because his leg was broken, his life was spared.

The neighbours learnt what happened and told the farmer of how much of a blessing this was…

And could you guess what the farmer replied with?

The farmer replied, “Maybe yes, maybe no.”

What a cold story, it’s stuck in my head forever.

But here’s the thing.

Why did I just talk about a Chinese farmer?

Here’s why :

The Lesson.

Nothing is good or bad, it just IS.

To put it in your writing context…

Stress isn’t bad when you’re creating a post, it just means you want to design it for perfection.

Look at it positively.

So what should you do next time you experience this?

You should stress and use that stress to make the best possible post.

When your brain can’t even think of anything else to add or get rid of…

You should go ahead and post it.

You’ll still have some stress when you’re posting it and reading it over.

But it will go…

Now the stress is gone, now what is left?

A piece of writing that was designed to your current minds perfection.

Your current mind has made this post the BEST possible post.

And as you learn more information, your mind will KEEP on lifting that “ceiling” of perfection.

You will repeat this and repeat.

Your writing will get better and better.

BUT remember this.

There will never be a “ceiling”, so you will always experience this "stress" when writing.

I mean… right now I’m editing sentences in this module and thinking “how can I make this more understandable?”

You just want to improve.

Don’t see it as a bad thing, rather a winners mentality.

Make it to perfection, and use stress to fuel that perfection.

But always understand that it will never be “perfect”, so you will always feel this emotion.

Just perfect it as much as you can.

Don't see the stress as a bad thing, because it is good in this context.

Bad stress will be something that will come from not doing the work...

That's where the "bad stress" comes in.

But even then... why are you stressing when you don't put the work in?

Because your mind is pushing you to DO the work.

So, it's not really bad.

Really, there's not a "good" or "bad" it just is.

To learn more about the fact that there is nothing "good" or "bad", you should read the thinking course.

The farmer replied, “Maybe yes, maybe no.”

Remember that.

P.S the only negative thing I see here is the thought of “has anyone else wrote about this?”

The answer is yes, there are no new ideas.

But only ones that you have been passed down.

Not everyone knows the things you know, that’s why you’re teaching it.

You’re not teaching the people that already know the info, you’re teaching the people who don’t.

The people who know this will scroll past and not bat an eye, but the person who doesn’t will stay and learn.

That’s who you’re aiming for anyway, so why are you concerned about the people who already know it?

Remember, 200x$50 is $10,000.

You’re not teaching the masses, you’re teaching people who don’t know.

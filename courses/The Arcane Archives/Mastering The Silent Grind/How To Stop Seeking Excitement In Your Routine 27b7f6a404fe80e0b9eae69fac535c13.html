How To Stop Seeking Excitement In Your Routine.

Submission from sam, active member

But this problem here… it’s a big one.

And I’ve came to a sustainable solution.

Really, I’ve preached this for a whole year now, and I’m going to give you my new and enhanced philosophy on it.

“Excitement” in your routines…

Firstly, what is excitement?

It’s probably quick dopamine.

You don’t get any quick dopamine from your work, the gym, etc…

So you want something in your day to get you away from that and finally receive a “hit” of quick instant dopamine.

Things like this could be scrolling on instagram reels, playing video games, smoking weed.

That’s instant dopamine, and we want to avoid that.

But really… ask yourself this.

Why do we want to avoid it?

It’s a simple answer.

It’s because we don’t want to condition our brain to look for “instant” dopamine, so we can get used to the “delayed” dopamine we get from work and progress.

When we have too much instant dopamine “habits” in our life, we’ll find it so much harder to do the “delayed” dopamine tasks like sitting down and working for 2 hours in a flow state.

Matter of fact, “instant” dopamine just makes it so much harder to get into a flow state.

And if I’m not wrong… sam you used to smoke weed often right?

Recently you got on the cigar wave which is cold, but here’s the thing…

It takes 21-31 days to really break that circuit in your mind of instant dopamine.

Just keep that in mind…

But yeah, let’s gets into the actual “actionable” advice for seeking excitement in your routine.

And there’s a couple ways to go about it, and a couple tactics.

And here’s how I would go about it.

This comes from a philosophy I got from sam ovens.

You really don’t need to cut out the “excitement” from your routine, but you could cut out the “instant” excitement

What sam ovens does is works all day and gets EVERYTHING done that he listed out the night before.

Then he would watch something that is entertaining, but not “instant”

AFTER everything is done, all the fires are put out, and all the things are going smoothly.

He would put on a documentary, a series, or do something of that nature.

And honestly? I do the same.

But HERE’S THE THING.

Sam would watch a tv show, series, movie…

But he would make sure that there are no ads.

Why? Because ads are designed to stimulate your brain.

Quick flashing colours, quick transitions.

He would watch something… but he would make sure there are no ads because that stimulates his brain.

So why is it acceptable for him to watch a movie?

Because it’s longform, it doesn’t fuck up his attention span.

Keep that in mind…

In the course productive isolation, I say this is how your day should be structured :

  • Eat the frog (hardest high leverage task first thing in the morning, this could be writing, recording, outreach, offer building)
  • Get everything else done in the day (gym, putting out daily fires in your life, learning, etc)
  • Feast and rest (just how a lion would… eat a huge meal, rest and digest)

I’ve split tested routines over the past 2 years.

I’ve even went so far to cut EVERYTHING out of my life for 2 months straight, even the small things like playing a game of chess.

And honestly, did it lead to ultimate productivity?

At the start, very much yes.

But maybe a couple weeks in?

It just started to decline…

Before every task, I’d just actually sit back in my chair and contemplate life.

And after 2 months of this, I burnt out, and I burnt out hard.

That’s because I had the mindset of “no breaks, discipline, grind”

“Sam ovens didn’t sleep for 5 days and just worked, you NEED to beat him”

Honestly, it was an iron mindset.

But after a while you realise that it just was not sustainable.

If I never went through that, I would still be telling you “just GRIND, and GRIND MORE!”

But really, that’s just what the gurus that have never lived it tell you.

The only gurus that will tell you to do that and they ACTUALLY live it, these are people like jocko willink, David goggins.

They’re very real, navy seal type of guys.

But here’s the thing…

Are we in the same field as them?

Not even close, unless you’re in some combat sports or military.

But no, most of us here create.

We’re creating trades, we’re doing the inbound method, we’re creating dropshipping stores, we’re creating a marketing offer.

And creators need to have space to think… after all that’s where ideas come from.

And creators BUILD on ideas, common sense, isn’t it?

Yin and yang.

Recovery and rest.

You need to have some time to rest, or else your brain will just feel like it wants to throw up.

BUT you should not do this through “instant” excitement.

Rather you should do this with rest at the end of the day.

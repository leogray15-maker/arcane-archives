A small-scale Marvel Universe or Star Wars.

Like putting the best parts of your mind in public for people to explore.

The Big Picture – 2 Levels To The Game

In short, here’s the entire process:

  • Find an idea with high potential
  • Write a newsletter based on that idea
  • Link your products (or whatever you are selling) in the newsletter
  • Create spin off short form posts from your newsletter
  • Plug your newsletter under your posts once a day
  • Take the best posts and use those starting back at number 1

Then, repeat for years.

But there’s nuance to this, and that’s only level 1.

You should start at level 1, by the way.

For level 1, you only need one place to write long form and one place to write short form.

In my opinion, the long form content should be a newsletter on something like Substack, Beehiiv, Kit, or any other email provider. It doesn't really matter which one. They all do the same thing… but I would personally recommend Substack because it is free and acts as your personal website. And… if you don't get around to building an actual product, you can simply start a paid tier of your Substack.

For short form, I recommend Substack, X, LinkedIn, or Threads.

Why? Because they’re writing based. You focus on the quality and engagement of your ideas before you start focusing on image designs, video editing, or speaking. When you can write an idea, then everything else grows in quality. If you start with something like video first, a lot of the focus is taken off the idea and placed on the editing or visual hooks. You can't make up for a bad idea with a good hook.

I’m late to the Substack game, and don’t want to recommend that as the “one way” too soon… but it does have both short form and long form in one place. So, that could be a solid option.

Caveat: Substack is still new and growing. Other platforms have more monthly active users, therefore they have more people who can discover you. Keep this in mind as we continue.

Caveat 2: If you know with absolute certainty that you want to go down the Reels, Shorts, or TikTok route, don't let me stop you, but know that you can write out those ideas first as short form posts on a writing platform that can be turned into a video script. Ultimately, this increases the quality of your videos.

If you have objections or questions about starting a newsletter over something like a YouTube channel or podcast… don't get too ahead of yourself just yet.

Once and only once you have level 1 down (don’t spread your focus thin too soon), you can move on to level 2:

  • Use your newsletter as a YouTube script
  • Post the YouTube video to all podcast platforms
  • Turn your best posts into images for IG, LinkedIn, or YT community posts
  • Turn some of your best posts into short video scripts for reels, TikToks, and YT shorts
  • Promote old newsletters once a day on all platforms

Now you have a circular content system that leads to more email subs, sales, and views on all platforms.

Please only start with one long form and one short form platform.

You need to get good at writing content first. And once you do, now you have a backlog of validated ideas that you can cross post to other platforms.

Your best newsletters can be used to kickstart YouTube on the right foot.

Your best posts can help you grow faster on a new platform.

That's the big picture.

Let's talk about the details.

The Newsletter = Lead Magnets & Stealth Sales Pages

A common question I get is, “Should I start a newsletter if I’m just starting out?”

And the answer from me is always yes. Not because you’ll get a ton of views or subscribers, but because:

  • Good ideas come from writing enough bad ideas
  • More newsletters = greater backlog of potential YouTube scripts and ideas you can repurpose
  • You need somewhere for new followers to go to get more value (social media favors shallowness and beginner level information… most "deep thinkers" should stop being clever on social media and shift their deep thoughts to a newsletter)
  • You need something to promote as you are first growing your audience

Also, stop thinking of your newsletter as just a newsletter.

If the newsletter is good, it also acts as a lead magnet or free download.

For those who don't understand the importance of those: in marketing, you offer a free download to collect email subscribers because it gives you direct access to your audience. In most cases, social media isn't where you make money.

Sure, you can go viral, promote your products, and make decent money, but after 5 years of doing this (and talking with hundreds of bigger creators), a 5-10,000 subscriber email list will almost always make you more than a 50,000 to 100,000 audience. That's for life changing money by the way. You can make a great income with much less.

Now, what most don't realize is that if your newsletter is good, it serves the same purpose as a free download. When you publish a newsletter, you can send out a post that looks like this to get people to read it (it won't get that much engagement though). Then, throughout the week, you can link the newsletter under a post for that day like this.

When people click and read, some of them will subscribe.

That’s a much better way to build your newsletter than linking to your sign up page and saying "sign up for my newsletter!" You need to entice people with the value inside the newsletter itself.

To make this clear:

  • You write a weekly newsletter
  • You promote the newsletter once a day on your main social platform
  • On the day you send the newsletter, post it as it's own post
  • On other days, link to a relevant newsletter you've written as a comment to your post
  • On platforms like Instagram, you can link to the newsletter in your story or use Stan's auto-dm feature to have people comment a specific word to DM them a link to the newsletter

That's it.

So far, you're writing content every day and will be writing a newsletter every week (as we will learn over the next few days).

So how do you make sales on the product you create?

Well, you launch the product on all social media and your newsletter. You have 3-4 weeks of extremely hard promotion. But you don't do that forever.

So how do you consistently make sales on the product you create, outside of the launch period?

You link to your product in your newsletter.

Why?

Because long form content builds trust. You will get many more customers if you promote in your newsletter vs on social media.

So, not only is your newsletter a lead magnet, it’s a stealth sales page.

Every day when you link your newsletter under one of your posts, some people will click through, some will subscribe to your newsletter, and some people will purchase your product or service.

Newsletter growth is a game of consistent promotion and quality content.

Remember from this section:

  • Link your product or service at least once in your newsletter. This can be as simple as “if you want [x, y, z benefits], check out [name of product]” in a relevant section of the newsletter and at the very end in a "P.S." section.
  • Link your newsletter under one social post a day to make sales and gain newsletter subscribers.

We're talking about the big picture here. The rest of the challenge will help you do all of this.

I don't want this to overwhelm you, so do your best to ignore this, but it's helpful to know where this ends up when you branch out of one platform and a newsletter.

For level 2, when you are cross posting everywhere:

  • Link your product or service in the YouTube and podcast descriptions
  • When you read the newsletter as a script for those, and have your product promotions in that script already, just read the line in your script followed with “the link is in the description for that.”
  • On instagram, the best way to link your newsletter for a post is either by sharing the post to your story along with the link, or writing a caption and using a DM automation with something like Stan (also hosts digital products)
  • On LinkedIn, you can either write a caption and end with a call to action with a link to your newsletter, or link it in the comments (spend some time on LinkedIn and you’ll see this everywhere, because it works)
  • For reels, TikToks, shorts, etc, you usually have to have the link in your bio and come up with a clever way to get people to look there. On Instagram, you can use the Stan DM automation to have them comment a word to receive it

How To Write Posts From The Newsletter

Writing a newsletter isn't only about writing a newsletter.

It's about developing depth behind your ideas.

When you only write short form content, you don't learn much, and you don't dive deep into understanding the idea.

One massive benefit of writing a newsletter is that you can use it as a source for content ideas.

I don't mean you should just copy paste sections of your writing onto social media.

That's what many people attempt to do when I teach this.

The thing is, copy-pasting text from your newsletter often doesn't do well because you still need a hook and standalone value. You need to be in control of those things. Most excerpts from a newsletter lack the context that make them work, so you need to take the ideas and make them punchy on their own.

So, yes, you will have to write the posts from scratch.

Or, use my Deep Posts Prompt. It doesn't write the posts for you, but it breaks down the newsletter you give it into the most potent ideas, problems, paradoxes, quotes, action steps, and key points. You can use any of those as a starting point for a great post.

Of course, keep in mind that the prompt is biased toward my writing style and process. You may have to tweak it a bit.

Another way I like to pull ideas from my newsletter is:

  • Start reading through the newsletter
  • Find an idea that is rather compelling (or think about the big ideas from the newsletter as a whole, don't confine yourself to just paragraphs and sentences)
  • Then start writing drafts of those ideas as if they were standalone ideas

Personally, if I write 1 newsletter a week, I aim to write 5-7 posts from it (one for each day of the week) so I can link my newsletter under that post. My other posts throughout the week are more around high performing topics that I've researched or ideas I get from reading books or articles.

Since you haven't written a newsletter yet, simply keep this in mind until you do.

The Simple Content Schedule

Now, how do we bake this all into a daily content schedule that you can follow?

This is how I batch my writing into one deep work block in the morning.

As you get better, this shouldn't take anything more than 2 hours a day.

Level 1 (one short-form platform and long-form newsletter):

  • Outline the newsletter on one day of the week and post on the other (2 days out of the week)
  • Write the newsletter for 30 minutes at a time for the other 5 days in the week (this is more than enough time) – spacing out your writing gives your mind time to think of ideas for the newsletter rather than trying to write it all at once
  • 30 minutes a day for writing posts (or less, if you turn ideas into posts once you have them throughout the day)
  • 30 minutes a day for being active on socials (link your newsletter on posts during this time)
  • After work blocks, generate ideas and take notes on books, podcasts, videos, content, etc

Level 2 (all platforms):

  • Same thing, but now that you’re better and faster at writing:
  • Exchange one newsletter writing day for filming the video based on the newsletter as the script
  • Exchange another newsletter day for editing, or hire an editor if you can. Your video edits will have to be pretty simple until you hire someone, unless you're okay with spending more time on this.
  • For your 30 minutes of being active on socials, bake in cross posting to all platforms and respond to a few comments
  • Yes, you can post the exact same thing to all platforms.

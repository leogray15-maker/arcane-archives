DAY 22 - MONETIZATION/YOUR FIRST PRODUCT

We are now moving onto monetization!

Exciting.

But this is going to be one of the most difficult sections.

Why?

Because I can't tell you what to sell.

Look around social media, Amazon, and anywhere else you can purchase a product.

You can sell literally anything and make money.

The problem is, you aren't selling anything. Therefore, it's impossible for you to start the process of trial and error. Therefore, without a product, it's impossible for you to improve the product to the point of being profitable.

The question isn't "What do I sell?"

The question is "How do I create an offer they can't refuse?"

Usually, that stems from taking an idea that has already been proven to work, and making ONE important part of it much better so you become the obvious choice.

That statement above is almost everything you need to know.

To answer that question even further, here's how you would create any kind of product or service:

  • See what's already selling (imitate, then innovate. Stick your hand where the money and attention already are and stop trying to attract people to a watering hole that doesn't have water yet).
  • Take that idea and sell it to someone else (who you can help the most - a different target audience - your audience).
  • Research and purchase other products on the market to understand how they created it, what's inside, and how they market it. Keep track of everything.
  • Create your own version with the best parts of the 3-5 other products you purchased.
  • Focus on making one important part of it (a part that's tied to getting results) better.
  • Test it on yourself first. Use it. Note places where it could be better.
  • Start getting customers. Work with them extra close. Give them an open channel for feedback so you can make improvements.
  • Make the product better over time, charge more, and leverage your customers' results.

That is how anyone creates an product or service.

Read through it again.

Do you need prior experience?

Do you need anything other than something to sell and a way to collect payment? (In other words, should you be worrying about a website, logo, and LLC right now?)

Do you need to be an expert to purchase productivity planners off of Amazon, create your own digital version that's slightly better, test it on your friends to get initial results, talk about productivity online, and promote your planner?

No. No. And no.

The only thing you should be focusing on for the first 1-2 hours of your day is:

1) Asking people to buy your product (one-on-one, in your newsletter, or in your content).

2) Creating content so you can attract more people to ask.

Anything else is a waste of time until you hit your first $5-$10K.

In fact, I would say anything else is a waste of time until you consistently pull in $50k/month, but that may be overwhelming, and is a bit high for the first version of our product or service.

With that out of the way, what are we focused on selling?

What is the product or service we're going to sell?

After selling every kind of digital product under the sun, I'm quite confident that most beginners should start off as simple as possible.

  • Create a simple digital product (a small course, ebook, or template) - something that can sell while you sleep - for $25 to $99. This is just a first iteration to start testing. It can turn into something larger like a full course, cohort, or community, but there's no reason to pour so much time into something you don't know will work.
  • Offer a pack of 4 one-on-one calls on the same topic for $1000. Think of it as guiding them through your product in a more personalized way.

When you're just starting out, you don't want to spend weeks upon weeks to start selling a product that may not even work.

You simply want to start selling something.

Something that would have benefited your past self, or something that you've purchased before with slight improvements.

When I first started out, I sold a "how to freelance with web design" product.

It was a 90 page ebook (with a large font size) that could have easily been half that size… and probably even less than that if I just used Stan to create a course style dashboard with 10 modules going over the process. You can think of those 10 modules as 10 newsletters worth of content. You write them in the same exact way.

Further, it could have been as simple as 1-3 ~hour-long videos with slides going over the same content.

All I did was break down exactly how I landed freelance web design clients.

I studied how other people structured their ebooks (ebooks were pretty popular at the time), and I was able to emulate their structure with their own ideas.

Notice how this is the same exact process we went through with writing content and newsletters.

If you wanted to, you could purchase someone else's product, paste each part of it into AI, and ask it to break down exactly how it's structured and give you a step-by-step plan to do the same with your idea. You have no excuse for learning how to structure a product when you have AI to help you and teach you.

That freelancing ebook I created could now be linked inside every newsletter I sent.

I could also launch it in public with the launch strategy we will talk about.

I could also promote it under my best content with the promotion technique we will talk about.

Beyond that, and to increase how much I could make, I could offer 4 one-on-one calls for $1000.

Fewer people would buy that outright, but all I would need is 4-5 people to buy it each month in order to make decent money that I could increase from there. (If you can't get 4-5 people out of the thousands of impressions you will be getting on socials, you're doing something wrong.)

In my newsletters, I could have a P.S. at the end or somewhere in the middle where I say:

"If you want to work with me one-on-one to [desired outcome], reply to this email and I'll respond with a few questions about you."

Or I could say the same thing as a reply to my social posts or in my Instagram story, asking them to DM me.

Then, I could ask about their goals, what they've already tried, what results they expect, a brief explanation about how I can help them, and then I can send a link to a product I have in Stan.

Once they pay, I would schedule a time for our first call.

As the cherry on top, I could also link my one-on-one payment link inside of the smaller product I'm selling for those who want to take it a step further.

And… since this is a numbers game, since I already know how to DM people to form connections – from yesterday's lesson – I probably have a few people who are willing to work with me.

Please don't overcomplicate this.

When you are posting content and writing newsletters, you do a lot of the selling beforehand because you build trust. You don't need to worry about optimization when you're a beginner, and you don't want to waste time optimizing something that isn't going to work in the first place.

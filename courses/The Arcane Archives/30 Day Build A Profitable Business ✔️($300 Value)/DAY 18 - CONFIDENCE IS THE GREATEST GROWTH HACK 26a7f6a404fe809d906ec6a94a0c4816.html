DAY 18 - CONFIDENCE IS THE GREATEST GROWTH HACK.

Many people will not like this.

I know that most of my readers are fans of depth and nuance.

But the part about growing on social media that nobody tells you about is intentionally leaving out nuance and setting up a tasteful debate in your comment section.

The more people agree or disagree with your post, the more likely they are to share, comment, like, and debate in your comments, boosting your posts an incredible amount.

This takes some practice and skill.

There are two extremes here:

  • People who value nuance will have a difficult time seeing that a lack of nuance can actually lead to more value.
  • People who only care about engagement are hyper-inflammatory and destroy their reputation without realizing it.

Ask yourself, what changes behavior?

Is it providing every option imaginable? Or having conviction in ONE opinion even if it's not the full picture? And is it ever possible to illustrate the full picture? When you are writing content, you have an image of who you are talking to in your head, and if you try to talk to everyone, you talk to no one.

Very few people are online to read a textbook of nuanced information. They want your raw and authentic opinions. They want a taste of the thoughts in your head. If they don't feel anything when reading your post, then any value you had to offer doesn't matter to them.

Of course, you don't want to be polarizing for the sake of being polarizing. You don't want to lie. You simply want to illustrate the importance of the point you are getting across in your content, and nuance doesn't cut it for getting one singular point across.

Now, you need to be careful here. If you are the type of person to lie and exaggerate the truth more than it needs to be, you will lose respect from people you respect. That's not good.

Instead, what you are trying to do is illustrate your opinion in such a way that people feel the need to heavily agree or disagree, or they add the nuance for you. You set the scene for a debate.

You need to be able to stomach negative comments, because there will be plenty if you do this right, but they will be outweighed by positive comments.

This is why having a newsletter is important.

That's where you give your full thoughts, nuance and all.

As your posts start to blow up, you can link a newsletter that has your complete thoughts on the subject.

To illustrate this further, one of my first viral posts on Instagram was a screenshot of this tweet I wrote previously:

"Be careful telling people about your goals. It releases dopamine similar to actually achieving them. Skip the instant gratification. Go quiet and build."

This got 443,000 likes.

That's insane.

Why?

It's not like that's an utterly false or terrible statement.

It did so well because people loved to debate in the comments or share it to their story with their commentary.

Most of them fell into the camp of "I tell people my goals and I've done just fine!"

And I get that. There are some people who can do that.

But I wasn't talking to them. I wasn't talking to the people who are highly self-directed.

I was, however, talking to the massive group of people who tell people what they are going to do just to get a dopamine hit and then never execute on that thing.

As you can tell, that tweet above isn't hyper-inflammatory. It's not going to make nuanced people not respect me because they understand that many people struggle with that problem.

That's the key here: place the burden of nuance on your readers. Trust that your readers aren't so stupid that you need to explain every corner of your argument.

If they can't see through the lines or think for themselves, then their only option is to boost the engagement on your post to reach the people you can actually help.

Confidence, clarity, and conviction are the greatest social media growth hacks.

But most people struggle with them because they're afraid to be seen as "mean," or they're worried they won't appear super smart like they see themselves.

Leave out the clever nuance and trying to flex how smart you are.

Voice an opinion that will help another person act with clarity.

Let those who don't understand fuel your growth.

Your action step for today is to write one post.

But before you post it, ask yourself two questions:

  • Can I remove part of this to make it sound more confident and clear?
  • Can I exaggerate a point for the sake of impact?

For exaggerating a point, think about it this way.

Which one will do better?

  • A potential way to get paid more, if you want to get paid more, is to learn copywriting.
  • The greatest skill to learn is copywriting. You will drastically increase how much you earn if you dedicate 30 minutes a day to studying it.

Both are true.

One sparks more action than the other.

The latter also pushes people's buttons if they think they have another skill that can do the same.

Try it out.

When you pair this with the next growth strategies we go over, you'll be surprised how quickly you may grow.

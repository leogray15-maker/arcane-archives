DAY 30 - HOW TO PROMOTE IN THE CONTENT ECOSYSTEM

On day 10 of this challenge, I gave you an overview of the content system that I recommend.

No long sales funnels or countdown timers (At least for now. When you practice enough to do those things with taste and authenticity, they can be very effective).

Just building an undeniable body of work, like a small scale Marvel cinematic universe with binge-worthy movies (newsletters), spin off shows (social posts) and action figures (products).

Please review Day 10 after reading this. I feel like more will "click" for you since we've covered a lot more ground.

The weekly process for this system is to:

  • Find an idea with high potential
  • Write a newsletter based on that idea
  • Link your products / services in the newsletter
  • Create social posts from topics in the newsletter and others you collect throughout the week (you can and should write posts from random ideas you gather throughout the week as well)
  • Plug last weeks newsletter (or any relevant newsletter) as a comment to your post once a day
  • Take the best posts and use those to write future newsletters

In short: Start with the newsletter → break that down into content → link your newsletter under the content once a day → since your products are linked in your newsletter, and newsletters build trust, you are likely to make sales and gain newsletter subscribers.

Of course, this increases in effectiveness over time. The more people who read your newsletter, the more authority you build, and the more people are likely to purchase what you sell.

Once you have that down and your socials are growing, you can consider turning your newsletters into YouTube scripts, cross posting your videos as a podcast, turning your best social posts into images or short video scripts to cover various platforms.

This system is the definition of consistency compounding over time.

Where do you promote in the newsletter?

The first question most will ask here is just this: Where do you link to your products or services in your newsletter?

There are two options here:

1) Pick a spot and write a short promotion.

Think about how newsletter sponsorships show up somewhere inside of a newsletter. Usually at the top, bottom, or somewhere in the middle.

They have a few sentences of persuasive copy followed by a call to action.

You are technically "sponsoring" your own newsletter with your own product.

When I go this route, I like to promote right after the introduction, before the first key point section.

2) Include points in your newsletter that align with your product.

If I sell a productivity product and write a productivity newsletter, this is pretty seamless.

Inside your newsletter, when you talk about something that your product helps with, link it there.

Say something like, "If you want a deep work tracker to form the habit faster, grab [your product name]." Change it to fit your product.

Now, if I sell a productivity product but write a business newsletter, then it's not obvious where to promote. You need to include a point in your newsletter that allows you to connect back to your product.

Most of the time, this happens when you're writing the outline. No matter what topic you choose to write about, you can always try to include one relevant point that connects to your product, then link your product in that section.

As an example, my newsletter could be about "how to start a business," but a key point could be to "get into the habit of deep work." That's not directly related in most cases, but it's useful and true, and allows me to link to my product.

How often do you promote in the newsletter?

Once or twice.

I usually try to include two links to my products or services and space them out.

The longer your newsletter, the more you can promote. I'd say you can promote once every 750-1000 words. Most people's newsletters will be around 1000 - 2000 words.

In that case, you can promote somewhere inside the newsletter, and in a "P.S." section at the end.

This isn't law. Play around with it a bit.

Now, one thing we didn't talk about too much in this challenge was creating a service: a pack of 1-4 calls that you charge $1000 or so for.

We didn't talk about it much because frankly, if you're good at what you do, it is pretty intuitive. And you can simply ask AI how to best structure a coaching call and have it interview you (and your customer avatar) to collect information about your expertise to help structure it.

However, to promote something like this, where you don't have a landing page, you would just ask people to reply to the email.

"If you want to work 1-1 with me on [what you help with], reply to this email with 'one on one' and I'll ask you a few questions to see if I can actually help."

Then, once you walk them through what the offer includes and they say yes, you send them a link to purchase, then schedule the calls with them.

You can promote this just like you would promote your product.

Can you promote your product or service on the timeline?

Yes, absolutely, feel free to experiment and not treat my systems as dogma.

The entire purpose of the content ecosystem above is to create a baseline level of work that you can return to every single day as a personal brand or content creator. Being consistent with the content ecosystem ARE the levers of your business. Isn't that freeing? To know exactly what you need to do every day without second guessing what will lead to progress?

If you aren't seeing results, it's not the system. It's how good your content is. Knowing that puts full control in your hands to get better. Of course, that takes a lot of practice.

In terms of promoting on the social media timeline, I wouldn't recommend doing it all the time, but it's worth testing it out once or twice a week to see what brings you more sales.

Remember – if you aren't making the sales you want to make, you need to test and experiment until you do.

You have two main levers: growth and promotions.

Creating content and promoting what you sell.

If either of those aren't going well, try new things. Look up new strategies. Ask AI to poke holes in everything you're doing. Ask AI why so few people clicked on a promotion and how to write it in a more persuasive way while taking your customer avatar into account.

A tripwire is a low-cost product that gauges seriousness and interest.

It is a piece of your product offerings that is low commitment and leads into your next product well.

Previously, my Websites That Work course was a $17 tripwire for my $97 Web Design Wealth (which became High Ticket Essentials).

I started out my journey with only WDW, and when I started piecing my email marketing together, I put Websites That Work in a sequence before it. Those that bought WTW were much more likely to buy WDW.

The Purpose Of A Tripwire

If you have a product or service AND are building an audience — you are guaranteed to have people in your audience that do not want to buy your offer.

So, what can you do? Teach them what is necessary to use your offer. You need to create customers in a sense.

A tripwire:

  • Is usually positioned to beginners. Your audience is 95% beginners.
  • It is low cost but not free — meaning you have higher quality emails and customers. Buyers are more likely to buy again.
  • Raises customer level of awareness surrounding a problem that your main product or service solves.
  • Builds trust in you and your future products (so make your tripwire high quality, overdeliver).

Where Does A Tripwire Go In Your Funnel?

You can sell it directly on the timeline of course, but in your email funnel it goes in between your welcome sequence and main sales sequence.

Ryan Deiss calls this Engagement, but he doesn't limit it to a tripwire. It can be a sequence dedicated to engaging your subscribers.

In this case it can be a free guide related to your product simply to educate them and see if they are interested in that topic (so you know if you should put them in your sales sequence).

I prefer adding a small price tag to guage their seriousness, so it is a tripwire.

I Recently Created A Tripwire — Here Are The Results

Back when I was working on my 2 Hour Writer course, I had an idea: A $7 product that would lead both into MMHQ (my previous community) and the new product.

I called it 7 Days To Genius Ideas and positioned it toward beginners.

BOTH those that are already creating content and those who just want to form a creative habit (so I can show them how simple it is to turn ideas into content).

Do you see how I am raising the awareness levels of my audience?

I am showing them that creating content, a product, or service is as simple as consistently executing on good ideas — so I help them have better ideas that separates them from everyone else.

I launched on a whim the other day — and the results were pretty surprising.

20% of the people that bought the tripwire purchased an MMHQ subscription .

And that was only after the first day. I structured it as a challenge. More may join towards the end of that challenge.

Why did they join MMHQ after purchasing the product?

  • I gave them a quick win by helping them have a "Genius Idea"
  • I told them what they can use that idea for (content, product, or service)
  • I positioned MMHQ as a way that helps with that

Should You Create A Tripwire?

The answer: probably.

  • Do you have a current product or service that you are selling?
  • Have you built out any email sequences yet?
  • Does your audience have people that aren't ready for your main product or service yet?
  • Can you target those people and educate them enough to be ready for your product or service? (that is, improve them to a point of having better problems that your main offers solve?)
  • Would a tripwire fit your offer stack / value ladder to perfection?

As a reminder, here is what a solid value ladder looks like:

  • Lead magnet that you promote on the timeline to get email subscribers
    • (Add the tripwire here if you'd like, it's optional)
  • Main product that is a "Do It Yourself" version of your high ticket offer
  • High ticket offer that is a Done With You consulting or Done For You freelance offer.
    • (You can eventually do super high ticket retreats or seminars if you'd like, again, optional)

Then, once you have all of those fleshed out via the modules on email marketing, all you have to do is focus on creating content, growing your social media, and plugging your lead magnet.

Then, the other products sell without people knowing it.

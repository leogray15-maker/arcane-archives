The Eternal Markets - How To Niche Down Smarter

The Eternal Markets - How To Niche Down Smarter

By now you have probably heard of 'niching down' and targeting a specific group of people. That is what we will discuss.

This is absolutely necessary for any online business (regardless of how much you niche down). You won't be able to target their pain points and present your offer as a solution any other way.

That is the point with this.

You are NOT niching down on a specific target customer.

You are defining a target customer so that you can niche down on a profitable problem and desirable goal.

This will help you frame your content and marketing 10x better than trying to write to a specific person.

Because when you market toward a specific problem or goal, you ARE marketing to a specific person.

In short, don’t focus on the person, focus on the problem or goal.

Of course, you want to keep in mind who your ideal customer actually is, because that will help you qualify and filter out who you don’t want to work with.

The only time you will have to be anal about this is when you are running ads or cold outreach.

If you are building an audience, niching down too far will only confuse you and cause trouble. Don’t get wrapped up in being hyper-specific with this. Your audience will tell you what they want eventually, until then, make your best guess, because that’s all marketing is.

Step 1: Choosing A Market

There are 3 main markets: health, wealth, and relationships.

These are where most profitable problems lie. Because everyone experiences them. It’s in our nature, biology, and psychology.

BUT, you can't just solve the "wealth" problem as a whole.

"I will help you acquire wealth" sounds nice, but we need to get more specific, especially since we are solopreneurs, not huge corps. Understand that we are talking about marketing a product or service here. It is often better to go broad with your profile and some content… then get more specific with your product or service.

Start with a market you have experience in or are interested in exploring.

We will choose health for this example.

Note: there are other markets outside of health, wealth, and relationships of course.

But, we are doing this to refine the positioning or angle of our marketing. We aren’t locking ourselves into these.

As an example, even a ‘self-improvement’ product can be positioned toward a problem in the ‘wealth’ market.

A self-improvement product can increase someone’s value, get them better opportunities, and lead to higher paying jobs. That is one example, but a self-improvement product can be positioned in infinite different ways.

Positioning your product or service toward a profitable problem in the health, wealth, or relationships markets is what we are trying to do here.

Step 2: Choosing A Submarket

For our health market, here are some example submarkets:

  • Resistance training
  • Nutrition
  • Yoga
  • Mobility
  • Medical fields

Again, choose one that you have experience in or are interested in.

Remember, we are narrowing down on a profitable problem. We are trying to create a compelling marketing strategy. Your product or service isn’t limited to anything you do here.

Marketing is perception.

Your offer can be marketed 20 different ways with the same material inside.

Step 3: Niche Market

Now, we narrow down further (this makes it easier to research, find specific problems, and create specific solutions)

Examples of niche health markets:

  • Bodybuilding supplements / sleep supplements / etc
  • Bodybuilding training regimens
  • Hot yoga
  • Crossfit

If you are building a personal brand, or theme account - you need to take audience first vs product first into account. Which we talked about a bit, and will talk about later. You MAY not want to niche down a crazy amount.

For freelancers and agencies that are targeting a specific customer avatar, the more specific the better.

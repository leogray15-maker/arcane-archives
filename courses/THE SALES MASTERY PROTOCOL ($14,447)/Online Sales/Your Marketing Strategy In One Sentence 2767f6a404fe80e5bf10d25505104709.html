Your Marketing Strategy In One Sentence.

To make things extremely simple, we are going to turn your entire marketing strategy into a one-sentence description.

This will help you regain clarity when you fall off track.

I learned about this sentence from Dan Henry, but before we dive into what it actually is, we need other parts of our marketing locked down.

Your Unique System

We haven't talked about this much yet - but your unique system is YOUR method / path / way for getting results.

Remember P90X? Their unique system was "muscle confusion," a completely BS term that spread across the world. (Do this ethically, don't just make shit up like them)

One unique system of mine for social media creators is the Social Matrix (content based around authority, growth, and authenticity).

Many people can show you how to build out a funnel - but very few people have a system specific to social media that brings an insane amount of traffic (without ad costs).

Another one is my Content Ecosystem inside 2 Hour Writer.

Similar, but illustrates how I write content from the top down. I write one newsletter a week and turn it into content for all platforms, in less than 2 hours a day. A unique system markets itself, and must be created with experience.

Whenever you see people promoting a specific method that gets results, that is their unique system.

Somebody that does Facebook Ads for local businesses can have the "local celebrity funnel" which is just a series of ads and landing pages that is spun in a unique way.

That's the thing about this - it may not necessarily be unique - but it isn't a generic solution to a burning problem. It isn't a commodity .

One last example for web designers, what is more enticing:

  1. A website that captures more leads
  1. The Appointment Accelerator (a landing page, lead magnet, and email followups to book appointments for service businesses)

Your Quantifiable End Result (OR Promise)

Think about the transformation that you offer your customers/clients. (That's what marketing / sales is, giving people a solution to a problem - causing some degree of transformation in their life).

Point A = where they are now

Point B = where your product / service can take them (where your unique system will take them.)

Point B is your promise. It is OK if you can't make it absolutely quantifiable.

We want to spice up point B a bit more by making it quantifiable.

When you are thinking about hiring a fitness coach, what is more enticing?

  1. Losing weight for the summer
  1. Losing 25 pounds in 3 months

Clearly number 2.

The potential client / customer can visualize number 2. It's tangible.

Number 1 means they can lose 5 pounds or 50 pounds, it's not specific. Specificity is always better in marketing.

When you're providing a marketing service for a company, what is more enticing?

  1. I will send you qualified leads each month
  1. I will send you 10 qualified leads per month or I will refund your investment and pay you $100 on top.

Number 2 wins yet again.

Be specific with the outcome that your offer provides.

(This becomes 10x more powerful when you can back it up with social proof and testimonials.)

Your Entire Marketing Strategy In One Sentence

From Dan Henry:

If I can make [ideal customer] believe that [unique system] is the only way to get [quantifiable end result] and the only way to do that is through [my offer], then all objections become irrelevant, and they must invest.

Let's plug in an example.

Ideal customer = creators (that are hovering around $2-$3k/month)

Unique mechanism = the social matrix and infinity funnel

Quantifiable end result = $1 per follower per month

Offer = The Brand Mastery Program

Here is my marketing strategy in one sentence:

If I can make creators believe that the social matrix and infinity funnel is the only way to make $1 per follower per month, and the only way to do that is through the Brand Mastery Program, then all objections become irrelevant, and they must invest.

Once you have this down, it really is all up to your offer.

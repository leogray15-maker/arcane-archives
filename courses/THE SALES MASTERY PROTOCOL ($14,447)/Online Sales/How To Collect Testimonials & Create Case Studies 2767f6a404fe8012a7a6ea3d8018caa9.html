How To Collect Testimonials & Create Case Studies.

Testimonials and case studies are absolute gold for your business.

Without them, making sales will be nearly impossible. With them, making sales will be significantly easier.

Why? Because they:

  • Show you can actually get the results you say you can
  • Let you borrow other people's social proof - if you've helped someone who's a big deal, that makes you a bigger deal
  • Make the path from A to B clearer in the prospect's mind and further convince them you're the one to help them make that transformation

A quality case study starts with a quality testimonial. A quality testimonial starts with...

Asking the Right Questions

You can't just ask a previous client/customer an open request like "hey, could you send me a video talking about your experience working with me?"

I mean, you can, but it's not optimal. The person needs direction in the form of specific questions . Otherwise, they'll just say a few sentences and leave out important details without knowing any better.

We want to get as much of the right information as we can. So we need to ask the right questions.

I've talked to several experienced people and have compiled this list of the best 5 questions to ask:

1) What were your biggest struggles before working with me?

2) What were your biggest objections/hesitations that could've stopped you from working with me?

3) What benefits did you get? Such as how improvements in your personal performance helped your business. Share revenue increases if you're comfortable, even as rough percentages.

Note: This is how I, as a performance coach, would ask this question. Customize this question to make it specific to the benefits of your own offer.

What's relevant? Revenue increases? Efficiency? Prospect calls? Energy? Body fat lost? Muscle gained? If your offer promises benefits X, Y, and Z, you want the client to say they got those benefits in their testimonial .

4) What was your favorite thing about working with me?

5) Would you recommend me? Why? What should others know about working with me?

Collecting Answers

You want to collect video testimonials instead of just text testimonials. Video testimonials are superior because seeing an actual person inspires more trust than just text on a page (even with a picture).

Video testimonials can also be turned into text testimonials and case studies (we'll come back to those).

To collect video testimonials, you have 3 options:

1) Send the questions and ask for a video

Pros:

  • Straightforward for you and the user since they just have to record a video of themselves answering your questions

Cons:

  • Can be harder for the person to complete since they have to remember your questions (or put the list somewhere where they can see it)

2) Use Videoask

Pros:

  • Puts the prompt for the current question on screen in big letters so the person can respond easier

Cons:

  • You get a separate video file for each question, so you have to manually stich them together, leaving your final video with awkward jump cuts
  • User experience for both you and the person answering the questions is rather poor

3) Use Testimonial.to

Pros:

  • Puts the prompt up on the screen so the person can easily see the questions (but there's a con to this)

Cons:

  • Significantly more expensive. The best tier is $150 a month
  • Since the prompts are all on the screen, the person may have to read them in an obvious manner, which can reduce perceived authenticity of their answers

I've used all three options, and in case you can't tell from my slightly biased pro/con breakdown, I believe the first option is the best . Just send them your questions and let them know if you'd like them to improve their response.

Whichever option you choose, ask the person to record the video in landscape format instead of portrait format. That way your videos will be consistent and look nicer on your landing page.

Note : What if the person is anonymous and/or isn't comfortable showing their face? Have them do a voice recording. You can then add their logo and create a video file from it.

In terms of value, in general, video > audio with logo > text.

Creating Case Studies

A case study is a story of how your offer helped the person transform from their starting point (point A) to their desired state (point B).

The general format describes how they:

  • Started from [point A]
  • Didn't like being at [point A] because [problems]
  • Wanted to get to [point B] because [benefits]
  • Couldn't get to [point B] on their own because [challenges]
  • Invested in your offer and you helped them get to [point B]

The absolute best resource I've found on this structure is Russell Brunson's book Expert Secrets . I highly recommend you get the book to learn his methodology, as he can explain it better than I can.

If you don't want to buy the book, watch this quick overview of his Epiphany Bridge method. That's basically what I outlined above.

If you asked the right questions and the person answered them adequately, you have all of the pieces you need to create a case study based on the above structure.

You can then write a case study in a newsletter, an email sequence, an X thread, an Instagram carousel, a LinkedIn post... the options are endless. You can and should use each of your case studies on pretty much all of your platforms . You can also reuse them - just because you've used a case study on a particular platform once doesn't mean you can't use it again after a while.

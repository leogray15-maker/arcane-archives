Sales Training 002

Sales Training 002 - How to Capture and Keep Attention Better Than 95% of Business Owners:

Here’s a secret:

If you can’t convey your ideas well to your target market, you’ll never hit your sales potential online.

And the solution isn’t what you think.

It’s learning how to clearly organise your thoughts and present the most relevant info to the prospect.

That’s what great selling is.

Get to the point and make them understand exactly how they benefit.

So here are the best sales principles that will help you clearly communicate to your audience and get more sales:

1. Be Clear, Not Clever:

Every time you are about to write something for your business, remember this:

A confused mind doesn’t buy.

And the more clever you try to be, the easier it is to confuse the client.

So if you were selling a course on scaling a business, here’s what a good example and bad example would look like:

Bad: “The Money Machine”

Good: “How to Scale Your Business Past £10k a Month Without Having to Spend 5-Figures on a Coach.”

See the difference?

When you’re able to communicate clearly to people what you do, it’s much easier for them to decide whether it’s for them or not.

When you try to be clever, you end up confusing the audience more than you need to.

And there has been no point where I was on the internet, possibly the largest creator of cheap dopamine EVER, and thought:

“Hey, this is super confusing, let me try to understand it for the next two hours instead of moving on to things that don’t make me think that I need to.”

2. If You Do Not Understand Your Audience’s Burning Issues, You Will Never Get Them to Buy.

Let’s say your friend is choking and needs CPR.

You call out for help and someone goes:

“I don’t know it, but I’ve seen the show Scrubs so I can probably make it work.”

You’d want to smack him, and you move on.

Another person goes:

“My friend knows it, but they’ll be here in 10 minutes.”

You’d keep looking around until someone goes:

“Yes, I know it, everyone else move out of the way.”

Most people scroll online with burning desires, and they don’t buy because lots of businesses don’t know how to find them out and appeal to them.

And that’s the key: people buy to solve a burning problem.

You buy food because you’re hungry and don’t want to die.

You buy new clothes because you feel bad about the clothes you currently have, or they don’t fit anymore.

So if you’re trying to sell online, you need to figure out what those burning problems are and speak to them directly.

Otherwise you’re not giving people enough of a reason to buy.

3. Sell the Benefits, Not the Features

Let’s say you’re selling a dating program for young men.

The first program says:

“I’m going to teach you how to approach women in public, practice affirmations, get a gym routine and find the best locations to talk to women.”

The other one says:

“I will make you the best she’s ever had.”

Which one pulls you in more?

Which one evokes a stronger emotion in you?

Most people make the mistake of selling features:

They tell you exactly what they’re going to get you to do and what their program entails.

You see this on amateur websites all the time:

“We provide SEO, web design and digital animation.”

What separates them from everyone else who sells those things?

This is why you need to sell benefits:

Benefits are of course the positive outcomes that the prospect desires.

Most people are lazy.

Sorry, but it’s true.

They don’t want to be told about all the things they have to do.

They just want to be told about the amazing outcome that they get if they sign up.

4. Take the Prospect Through a Transformation

Building off our last point, it isn’t enough to tell them the benefits.

You need to tell them that you can take them from where they currently are to where they ultimately want to be.

The transformation is what they’re really after.

Feeling understood and being shown a path to where they really want to be.

5. Don’t Forget to Twist the Knife

When you tell people about a problem they have, it’s important to not stop there.

You need to tell them how it hurts them.

That’s twisting the knife.

So with our dating program example from above, you don’t tell them that having bad social skills is bad.

You tell them that it’s ruining their confidence, hurting their ability to date women they find attractive, and will ruin their chances of ever starting a family or keeping a good job.

See how that hurts more?

Now they’re more likely to act because they know how badly not upgrading this area of their life is harming them.

Don’t just point out problems.

Twist the knife.

That’s it!

FORM

I got this from my mentor. FORM is acronym for Family, Occupation, Recreation and Money.

This is the order that most people talk in (not us).

I could end it here and just tell you to write a piece of copy that goes in this order but let's dive deeper into it.

If you join any multi level companies like Amway, Herbalife, Mary, this is the template you'll be given when talking to strangers.

Because think about it, if you go straight to talking about money to stranger, you'd find it uncomfortable

Especially when you're broke - it becomes a sensitive topic which is why FOR (family, occupation and recreation) are all used to build rapport to then eventually ask for their money.

Now if you're writing copy for yourself or for your clients, you'll come across this question:

"What's the point of talking about all this stuff?"

Well, to put it simply, to build rapport. This is the second most easiest way to build rapport, first being Barnum statements.

When you're saying your FORM, they're not buying your product/service, they're buying your story.

All of these are bridges designed to build rapport as fast as possible.

This is also the primary reason why there's some sort of life story in sales pages or emails.

One last thing:

This isn't the order in how we communicate, this is the order other people, average people, communicate.

Pair this with Barnum Statements and something amazing is going to happen.

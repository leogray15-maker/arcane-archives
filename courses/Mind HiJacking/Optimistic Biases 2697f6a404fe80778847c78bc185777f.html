Optimistic Biases

We've all had this experience. We get shit/mediocre results with something and this is, most of the time, the state of mind we'll be in:

"This time it's going to be different."

"Third times the charm"

It's extremely interesting.

Most of us have a relative that is a complete low life. Not doing anything with their life yet some of the time, us or others, we'll give them free advice into helping them but the result is the same every single time.

There's many factors because of this:

They could be a sense of obligation, blood, a number of things, but optimistic bias sets in. This time it's going to be different.

Another example is women:

My mentor, a long time ago, told me to watch this crime documentary when he bought the topic of optimistic bias upon me.

Women staying in abusive relationships for many, many years before they finally either die, "somehow" die or get out, because this is their line of thinking:

"He only hits me when he's drunk and loses his temper, and he says he'll never do it again"

You know what the statistical odds are of a second time after a first?

92%.

First time, pack your stuff, get out. But they don't.

So optimistic bias is there.

It's in everybody's personality.

It's in more than it is in some, certainly, but it is there in everybody.

Some people's own self-images make it more profound.

So the person who takes self-image pride in being an optimist, in being a positive thinker, they obligate themselves to high optimistic bias.

This is why the same fundamental thing can be sold to people over and over and over again.

Take diets. The odds are about the same, by the way.

Once somebody buys one diet, there's a 92% chance they're buying a second one, and there's a very high chance they're going to be addicts, so it moves into optimistic addiction.

They're going to buy the next diet, and if they're buying pills they're going to buy pills, and if they're buying shots, they're going to buy shots.

If they're buying plans like Weight Watchers they're going to buy another plan, they're going to buy another plan.

30% of weight watchers business is done with people who are on Weight Watchers for between the third and fifth time, so they go in a circle.

They do this one, they did this one, they did this one, they wind up back with the one they already failed on, and they buy it again anyway because they're locked into optimistic bias addiction.

It's funny.

I'll give you another example:

Santa Claus. Optimistic bias and lots of kids ask for stuff, and they don't get that stuff, particularly poor kids. They ask for stuff, and they get socks and underwear. They still make a list next year. It takes a long time before they stop making out the list.

This bias is there. People want to. They're hardwired. It's been embedded deep into us since we have been kids. It's pretty powerful when done right.

Now the way to implement it into your copy is simply by prodding this bias.

Here's what I mean:

When you're selling, especially on email, you could bring sentences of:

"This time is different, we'll know you succeed because you've learnt from your previous experiences"

"If you don't try another shot, you'll never genuinely know if it works for you"

Something along those lines.

It's powerful when you do it correctly and nearly every big market has this type of optimistic bias especially in the financial and health niches because there is so many ways to accomplish the same thing.

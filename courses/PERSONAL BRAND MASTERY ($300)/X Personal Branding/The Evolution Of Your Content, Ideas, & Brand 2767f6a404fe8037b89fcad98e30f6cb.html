I've seen way too many people getting discouraged that they aren't "on par" with bigger creators and brands. This is natural, it happened to me, so I want to break down my experience in the evolution of my brand — and the things you should focus on as you grow.

Your content, bio, message, and overall branding will be different at 100 followers than it will at 1,000 - 10,000 - and 100,000. You will refine your message, move away from "what works," and start creating ideas that can be tied to you.

This is the journey to exponential growth and authority.

(By no means is my journey perfect, but I will speak from my experience and what I have noticed working for others).

Under 10,000 Followers

This is arguably the most difficult part of the journey. You are testing what kind of content you like writing rather than what content works (because you don't have an audience for feedback).

The best thing you can do is to emulate bigger brands. See what is working for them but understand that they already have leverage. Naval can have nothing in his bio. If you did that you would just look like an anon account.

The secret to this is crafting your bio as if you were a 10,000-follower account. People should think, "Wow I've found a little-known gem!"

Your Profile

1) It needs to clearly state what you help people do.

You need to answer their question... "What's in it for me?"

You need to focus on the benefits of what you teach. If you talk about personal growth - what are the tangible benefits of that? (That they actually care about?)

A great way to get this across is with this template. You don't have to use it word for word, but it will help you structure what you are trying to say:

Learn to ABC so you can XYZ

Or

Lessons on ABC so you can XYZ - Tweets on ABC so you can XYZ

Example: Posts on personal growth and holistic health so you can live stress free.

Simple as that.

2) You should have a link to SOMETHING

People want to follow leaders. That's why they are called followers.

Leaders speak confidently and are open to feedback. (Confidence in your tweets is an engagement hack. People can sense it.)

Leaders provide value. They teach. They have some form of a resource for someone to download or buy. If you are afraid of selling, this is even more reason to do it. So you can get over that fear.

Start an email list (even if you aren't going to write emails yet.) Write a free guide. Create a product. Have a questionnaire to schedule a call with you. Anything. Show that you can give them something worth their time.

Consumers follow producers. So produce something deeper than surface level engageable content. Give them something MORE.

3) Your pinned post should show some form of authority, social proof, or authenticity... again, "What's in it for me?!?!"

\You should have either:

  • A tweet about your story / transformation
  • A tweet that has a lot of likes (social proof)
  • A tweet that gives away so much good information people can't ignore it

Your Content

“One must be cringe before they can become based."

For MOST people — not all — you will have to post some cheesy content at the start. I was recently listening to a podcast with Jen Sincero and this is something that holds a lot of people back.

"Oh, I don't want to post cliche and shallow content."

"List posts with tons of bullet points? That's lame, there are too many."

"All I want to do is joke around and meme..."

Sorry.. not gonna happen. You are the only one that is aware of them being "cheesy."

Don't play the losing fight against human nature.

Put in the necessary reps until you're at a point where you can show a bit more personality.

Why can big YouTubers post anything they want and still grow? Because people know them and they have social proof. They put in the initial reps.

They didn't start that way. Go look at anyone's account and you will see they all played nice with the algorithm. They got views, engagement, followers, and then slowly started to do whatever they want.

As Jack Butcher put it: "Build distribution, then build whatever you want."

If you want a cheat code to content you can create, check out my swipe file here and plug your ideas into them as templates.

Focus Areas

  • Writing 3 quality tweets per day (focused on growth)
    • Long-list tweets give people more reasons to follow you
    • Confident, pain or benefit-focused basic content - speak in absolutes
    • Highly actionable content. Step by step how to achieve a desired result, in the form of a list or thread.
  • Engaging with 10+ people per day
  • Forming mastermind groups and making lifelong friends
    • This is arguably the most important step
  • Getting your content retweeted by paying for promotions or networking with big people

Under 20,000 Followers

From 1,000 to 20,000 (ish), you will be introducing the authenticity side of your brand. Authority, authenticity, and growth all interconnect... and it’s not like you avoid any of them along the way — but now you are consciously putting effort into the more "human" side of your brand.

You are doubling down on what has worked for growth. Paying attention to what people respond to, and starting to form your message.

This is important. Stop stressing about your niche or content pillars. Talk about what you want in a way that will result in growth. You will naturally find your talking points. I cannot teach this. It must be done on your own.

Keep posting lists. Test new kinds of tweets. Incorporate your own flare or humor.

At this point, things are getting easier.

Focus Areas:

  • Consistent, high-quality content
  • Connecting with higher-level players since you have some social capital
  • Maintaining relationships with those you've connected with in mastermind groups (keeping friendships and helping each other grow)
  • Building out systems for your content creation
    • Turning tweets into email topics
    • Use TweetHunter to write week’s worth of posts so you can focus on building and selling offers
    • Spending time absorbing information from your "Tribe of Mentors" (big brands you aspire to be like)

Under 50,000 Followers

At this point, you have your main talking points. It's time to ideate these and make them yours.

Your job is to start coining names for your ideas or speaking on specific things.

Examples:

  • The One Person Business Model (freelancing, coaching, creating)
  • Lifestyle Design (productivity, flow)
  • A portfolio of small bets (Daniel Vassalo's way of saying "throw sh*t at a wall and see what sticks")
  • Build Once, Sell Twice (Jack Butcher's way of saying build digital products that you can sell infinitely)

Think of this as simplifying concept topics into a memorable "label"

This will only help you grow faster. People will mention you in posts when they talk about your concept.

Next step: start branching out to other platforms in a sustainable way.

You've solidified your message, offer, and what you want to get across.

Podcasts are great for your brand's authenticity pillar. It builds a deeper connection and allows you to be vulnerable.

Consistent newsletters allow you to show authority or entertain your audience.

YouTube is powerful if you want to speak and create captivating videos.

Instagram is just a no-brainer because everyone is on there.

You can use this template if you would like - it will help you break down a long form post (that you can use for blog, email, YouTube script, etc) into tweets, reels, Instagram posts and the rest.

Focus Areas:

  • X is on autopilot now
  • Start learning the other platforms you are trying to grow on
  • Create systems for content creation and make it easy on yourself
  • Have one project that you are building out at a time (offer, book, community)

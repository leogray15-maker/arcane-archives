The Viral Content Repurposing Machine.

I don't know why I hadn't made this connection before, so with this post I want to give you a simple, actionable, and highly effective strategy for social growth on all platforms.

We should all understand content repurposing at this point — but there is a foolproof way of going about it.

We will talk about

  • Generating ideas that have already done well and have viral potential
  • Structuring a list-style post that can be guaged for engagement
  • How to post that list on all other platforms
  • Turning the list into a viral thread
  • Using the thread as a carousel or YouTube video

In this case, X is our "virality tester" — you will understand soon.

And before we start — this can easily turn cheesy and hurt your account. Be mindful of the Social Matrix and mix it up between purely growth and authority posts.

Viral Idea Generation

We've talked about this before in the last module. Here's a brief overview:

The internet has already vetted ideas for us. We can have a rough estimate of what content will do well by what has already done well – or what is currently doing well. This means looking for

  • YouTube videos that have a lot of views (or are in your favorite creators videos sorted by popularity)
  • Medium articles that show up on your homepage based off of your interest
  • Top articles that show up when you search for your topic on Google (example: type "best habits" into Google)
  • Top posts from creators in your niche
    • You can type "from:thedankoe min_faves:500" in the X search bar to find posts of mine that have gotten over 500 likes. You can do this for any creator, just replace the handle after "from:"
    • I use TweetHunter both for ideas and to schedule my content with ease
  • Pinterest, Instagram, TikTok - I have not figured out how to find the best posts, but if they get high engagement you can take them

Now, we have an idea for a post — preferably one that aligns with your Content Pyramid and/or a section from your product/service if you plan to sell at the end.

Why a list-style post?

  • It gives people multiple options to "agree" with one of your points, making it more likely for them to engage
  • It acts as a pattern break on consumer's timelines because they follow their friends as well, who would never write a list post
  • Humans love lists. They are more likely to read bullet points over anything else.
  • They are the main style of content I wrote when I was a beginner and led to the most growth.
  • Since it is just a post, you can use it as a test to see how much engagement a thread (or other posts) would get

Turn The List Into A Viral Thread

I'm sure you've all seen what is doing well lately on X. Dakota as an example has been killing it with these threads (he has been somewhat of a trendsetter in this regard).

Others are copying him and following his lead quickly.

The easiest way to stand out and not be lumped into the bunch of copycats is to just change the hook or headline according to the thread writing material we will go over later.

I'm sure you can see how the list posts makes this process simple.

  • Post the list post
  • Use the points as subtopics in the thread
  • Expand on those points in 1-2 posts in the thread
  • Add a call to action to subscribe to your newsletter at the end

This is what all of the big accounts do. They don't sell on the timeline. They plug their newsletter and sell through there.

Repurpose The Viral Thread

Since these are so easy and streamlined to write (you already have the outline, it should take ~20 minutes to expand on each point into a thread), you can write 2-3 of these threads a week.

THEN, you can gauge engagement again, choose the one that did best, and post it as a carousel to Instagram or as a YouTube video.

Since these posts almost always do well on all platforms (because they are an evergreen content style), you will grow on YouTube. We will discuss why you aren't growing on other platforms in a bit.

The Daily Process

I would incorporate all of this into your content creation routine. It is simple and nearly automatic.

Remember, we are generating ideas from content that has already done well on other platforms. The idea is already validated and will do well IF you are getting eyes on it.

1) Post a list-style post once a day.

This will result in growth on its own, and has many other benefits for creativity and the mind.

Give it a day and gauge what its engagement is. I wouldn't pay too much attention to this if you have a smaller account because engagement is inconsistent.

You can either store these posts in a collection in TweetHunter or somewhere of your liking.

2) Post something to Instagram, TikTok, or LinkedIn

According to whatever post did the best, use that as inspiration for a post on another platform. These do not all have to be list posts.

3) Give it a day or two, then turn it into a thread

Again, simple. Craft a good hook, expand on each point of the list post, and add a CTA to your newsletter or whatever you sell.

4) Turn the thread into an Instagram carousel, LinkedIn article, Medium article, or YouTube script

Easy peasy and should take no longer than 3-4 hours a week for viral content that results in growth and sales.

Getting Eyes On Your Profile

If you aren't getting any traction on these posts:

  1. You are just starting out and don't have any followers
  1. You aren't playing the game. You don't have a network you can send these to boost exposure and leverage other people’s audiences (the only other way to grow aside from commenting on everyone's posts, but that is time-draining and demotivating. Just make friends and help each other grow)
  1. You are afraid to pay for reposts, story shares, or other types of paid growth that all of the big guys do. If I have an offer that sells, why would I not invest $500 to get my post exposed to 100,000 more people? The growth should pay for itself 5 times over.
  1. You aren't saying anything controversial, polarizing, or you aren't confident in the way you are writing it. Give people a reason to click on your profile.

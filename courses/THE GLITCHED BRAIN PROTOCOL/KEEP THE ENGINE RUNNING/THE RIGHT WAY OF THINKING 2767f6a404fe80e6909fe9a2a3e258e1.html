THE RIGHT WAY OF THINKING.

this is a very important section, pay attention.

I'm going to give you a habit to pick up.

and all it takes is 10 seconds every night.

what this will help with :

  • making decisions without emotion.
  • responding to situations logically.
  • a happier mood 24/7.
  • being grateful.
  • less likely to relapse on bad habits (I'll explain how)

and you probably already know what it is.

it's journaling for gratefulness.

you probably already do it, but I'm going to tell you in the way I do it.

  1. at night (preferably) get a notebook, do not do this on notion/screen.
  1. write down the SMALLEST things you're grateful for, that happened in the day.

why does this work?

think about it.

you're at night.

you may be having these thoughts of anger, frustration, anxiety.

which is natural for us man, on this hard path.

RELAPSING ON BAD HABITS.

you will see some truth in this.

whenever you relapse on porn for example.

before that point of opening up the porn browser.

what mood was you in?

you was probably alone, bored, it was late.

you was watching youtube, it got boring, so your mind just thought something.

"maybe we should look for a higher dopamine activity"

which is porn, and you caved in.

now, imagine you was in a happy/grateful mood?

in the marshmallow experiment I talked about in the DISCIPLINE/WILLPOWER COURSE.

if you haven't read that, I'll summarise it quickly.

a bunch of kids, had a marshmallow in front of them.

they was told if you wait 15 mins you'll get 2 marshmallows.

but if you eat it before those 15 minutes, you will get no second marshmallow.

so the smart option was to not eat the marshmallow right? more pleasure.

here's what was interesting about that.

THE EFFECT OF NEGATIVITY.

a group of kids was told to think of something that stresses them out.

probably asked them questions like "do you have homework to do tonight?"

which would stress a kid out.

they was WAY more likely to just eat that marshmallow.

why?

because it distracted them from their thoughts.

but here's the other side of it.

THE EFFECT OF POSITIVITY.

another group of kids was told/asked something positive.

"remember that one time your mummy pushed you really high on the swing?

they would smile.

and they would have a WAY less likelihood of eating that first marshmallow.

why?

because they didn't need to distract them from their thoughts.

SUMMARY FOR THE PRACTICE.

every night, coupled with your reassurance journaling.

just before you start that.

to warm up your journaling.

just start writing the really small things that just made your day 0.1% better.

"I'm grateful for that convo I had with the cashier at aldi"

"I'm grateful I talked about minecraft with my sister for 2 mins"

"I'm grateful my mum dropped me off to the gym"

these little things compound.

and here's the beautiful thing about this.

if you do this every night.

In the day, when those little things happen, you'll start to notice them.

"I'll write that in my gratitude journaling tonight."

which then leads you to a 1% more positive mood for the next couple minutes.

and you'll be more pleasant to be around.

SUMMARY.

  • EVERY NIGHT GET A JOURNAL
  • WRITE DOWN SMALL THINGS THAT HAPPENED THROUGH THE DAY YOU'RE GRATEFUL FOR
  • WRITE THIS DOWN IN YOUR DAILY HABIT TRACKER/ 10 GOALS A DAY TRACKER.

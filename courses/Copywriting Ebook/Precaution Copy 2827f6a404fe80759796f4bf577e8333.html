Precaution Copy

Why Using “Precaution Copy” Can Drive Your Sales Through The Roof

One way to illustrate the quality of your product or service is to talk about how it works so well... people may have to take some kind of precaution using it.

Let me give you a few real–life examples:

“...because it is possible to become dangerously thin by using Glucot–nin™ you should first consult with your physician to get advice on how fast it is safe for you to lose weight. Also, you and your physician... together... should determine your personal, perfect weight goal.”

See?

It's not a question of IF you will lose weight.

Instead, your concern should be losing too much weight too fast.

Changes the entire complexion of the pitch, doesn't it?

Here's another good one (from an old Gary Halbert ad)

“When you read Gary's report you truly are going to learn the real secrets of making a lot of money and making it fast! However, making a lot of money can be a 'mixed blessing' if you aren't careful. In fact, if you don't know what to watch out for, you could end up having trouble with the government, with lawyers, with jealous people who want to sue you and so on!”

Here's another:

“(Note: Because Anorex 2000 is such a powerful weight loss compound, doctors have advised the manufacturer to sell no more than one 60 day supply per customer.)”

And another:

“In fact, this is one of those things Art gets nervous telling people about. Not because it's crooked or illegal. But because it's a tactic for getting money that works so well – where the money comes in so fast and easy – that people who don't know how to handle a lot of money coming in rapidly, tend to get in trouble. Just like when someone who can't handle money wins the lottery – and ends up losing everything – because they can't deal with it.”

And last but not least, an example from D42:

Well… here’s the thing. I saw a gap in the market, and I’ve made it happen. The Superclean Pro. Here’s how it’ll change your house :

  • You’ll start hallucinating… “are my windows even there?”
  • Guests will awkwardly try to start a conversation about cleaning products to get your secrets.
  • Your carpet will look like it’s changed race.

The precautionary part is "Are my windows even there?"

Try it in one of your ads and see if the response doesn't improve.

You might just be pleasantly surprised.

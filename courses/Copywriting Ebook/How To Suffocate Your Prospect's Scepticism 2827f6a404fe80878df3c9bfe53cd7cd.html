How To Suffocate Your Prospect's Scepticism

One of the most effective mindset shifts you can equip yourself when writing your copy is with the assumption the person reading it will - right out of the gate - be shaking his head and saying to himself, "what a bunch of lies" to everything you say.

In other words, with every claim you make and every benefit you throw out there, just assume the person reading it doesn't believe a word you're saying.

He might want to believe you (if you've targeted your market right).

But chances are he doesn't.

And yet, there is a special way of selling to skeptics like this (besides just throwing in testimonials) that prevents them from ever thinking your claims are less than genuine.

Note: testimonials don't work well as they used to in this era of direct response simply because it can be manufactured too easily. It can be faked. The credibility of testimonials have been tarnished because of black hat copywriters/marketers. I'd argue because of this, having 0 testimonials is more effective than having hundreds of testimonials. Food for thought.

What is this special way?

Simply think of your copy as a sandwich.

Here's what I mean by that:

Your "meat" is the claims.

The two piece of "bread" are both some form of proof or credibility element (such as your background, lab tests, testimonials, endorsement from a respected person, etc).

And to make your copy "sceptic proof", every time you make a claim, simply put a credibility element - even if it's just a few words - that backs up that claim both before and after the claim.

That way it's kind of like a "double whammy" - where you won't give the reader any room to question or doubt you, no matter how fantastic the claim is.

Now obviously, this isn't always practical (or even possible).

But the more you can do this, the more believable and credible your ad will be.

Bottom line:

This simple little technique will make your ads a lot more likely to be taken seriously and acted on.

Especially if you sell to a sceptical market place that's hostile to the average sales letter's “hype” and language...

...And since most of you are in the self-development niche/biz-op/health...

Then it's crucial you put this into practise since there's a lot of scepticism in those markets and rightfully so.

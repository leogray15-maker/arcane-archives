Day 3 Excercies

  1. Analyse the swipes of the day:
    • What's the main idea behind this piece of copy?
    • Who is it directed to?
    • What emotion does it play on?
    • Why do you think it worked?
  1. Send your idea of the day on the skool.
  1. Write a personal letter to your grandmother or grandfather.
    • Prompt: recommend a new recipe
    • Hint (optional): pick an emotion from the downloadable 'emotion wheel' and do your best to trigger it.
  1. Reflect on one of your past purchase
    • Prompt:
      • Think of something you've purchased recently
      • Write down the practical reason you told yourself you needed it
      • Find the deeper emotional motivation behind your purchase. Dig DEEP and be honest
      • Describe how you felt after making the purchase

This exercise will help you develop empathy for your customers.

You will train yourself to see how emotions drive buying decisions, even when you think you're being rational.

See below for an example:

Purchase: high-end espresso maker

  • Practical reason: so I can make better coffee at home and save money on takeaway coffee.
  • Deeper emotional motivation:
    • I want a luxury experience in my morning routine
    • It makes me feel sophisticated and cultured
    • I want to impress my guests
  • How I felt after:
    • I'm excited to start my day with my "special" moment
    • I'm proud of buying high-end coffee beans
    • I'm confident in serving high-quality coffee to my guests
    • I feel slightly guilty about the indulgence, but I justify it as an "investment in self-care"

This coffee machine increases my status, confidence and sense of control.

It transforms ordinary moments into special experiences.

It improves my social interactions.

Day 3

Mastering Emotions

"People buy with emotions and justify it with logic."

You've probably heard this sentence before because it's true. The brain has two hemispheres: the left logical' brain. And the right 'emotional' brain.

The left hemisphere controls the right side of the body and the logical processes such as speech. When you talk, it's the left brain talking.

The right brain does not possess the ability to talk. It controls your creative thinking, your emotions, and your instincts.

Hence why it's called the 'reptilian' brain. It's the oldest part of our consciousness, and your unconscious' headquarters.

And if you want to become a master Copywriter, able to understand people at a deep level, the first step is to understand how this dichotomy works.

To do that I'm going to tell you a quick story.

In 1848 when a man named Phineas P. Gage had a life-changing accident.

This railroad foreman was packing explosive powder into a hole with an iron rod when the powder detonated.

The 3.5 feet (1.1m) iron rod passed right through his skull, destroying a chunk of his left frontal lobe in the process.

Gage survived this injury. But he did not fully recover. Because the left frontal lobe is linked with logic, speech, and reasoning... Gage lost the ability to talk and to think rationally.

After the incident Gage became emotional, rowdy, and impulsive. And guess what? He couldn't resist his buying impulses.

Everything he saw that tickled his fancy; he'd buy.

This led him to become an addict, to lose his money and job, and struggle for the rest of his life.

Gage's story baffled neurologists. They found out that buying is at its core an emotional response.

Meaning that even if you THINK you buy things for certain logical reasons... You don't.

It's the right (reptilian) hemisphere of your brain that dictates what you want and what you don't.

And it has even more power on you than you might expect.

There might be a second 'You' hiding inside your brain...

In fact, there might just be a 'second you' living inside of your own brain.

Let me explain.

In 1977, neuroscientists Michael Gazzaniga and Joseph LeDoux of New York University made a stunning discovery about our left and right brains.

They studied a patient named PS, who suffered an accident which left him 'split brained.'

This means that the physical link between the 2 hemispheres (the 'corpus callosum') of his brain had been severed.

This left both of his hemispheres separated, without a way to communicate between them...

...Now free of developing their own personal thought patterns and desires.

So of course, Gazzaniga and LeDoux tried to reach PS' reptilian brain to ask it questions.

At first, they couldn't. Because remember...? Only the left hemisphere controls speech.

For instance, when asked the question of what PS wanted to do after he graduated, he said- or rather his left brain said 'surveyor.'

Quite a boring job!

So LeDoux and Gazzaniga came up with a complex system to isolate the left brain, leaving only the right brain in command.

Since it was unable to verbally communicate, they gave it Scrabble letters it could assemble to create words.

They asked the same questions. And while the right brain It also had areas of complete shared some likes and dislikes... disagreement.

Now hear this: when LeDoux and Gazzaniga asked the right brain what it wanted to do after graduating, it spelt out the words: A-U-T-O-M-O-B-I-L-E R-A-C-E-R.

PS' true and hidden dream.

The thing he desired most, without even being conscious about it, cooped up in the right, emotional, brain.

The 2 neurologists concluded that:

"Each hemisphere in PS has a sense of self and each possesses its own system for subjectively evaluating current events, planning for further events, setting response priorities, and generating personal responses."

And that's the secret.

When you write Copy, you don't talk to someone.

You talk to their right brain. Quite literally because they're a person of their own.

Meaning that you mustn't care about making things too logical as long as they're emotionally compelling.

Your prospect has dreams, desires and aspirations buried deep in their subconscious, most of them unachieved.

How to infuse emotions in your writing

Once you know that... How do you talk to people's right brain?

Well this is the whole reason why Copywriting is so hard to master. Because it forces you to stop THINKING and start FEELING.

In our daily lives feeling isn't well seen. We tell our children not to cry. We hide our tears. We don't want to admit when we're unwell.

We build layers of protection around our unconscious mind to protect us from shame, self-pity, desperation, and even existential dread.

Copywriting is about peeling these layers. Because our emotional brain is the most vulnerable, and valuable, part of ourselves.

It is our true identity. And that's the secret.

Identity stems from the Latin idem, meaning 'same.'

Meaning that we can only define ourselves in comparison with others.

To know yourself, you must know who you're not. This is why babies always put things in their mouth: to understand what they're not.

We define ourselves in relation to others.

To talk to the right brain, you must talk about relationships.

Relationship = how they compare to others.

  • How they're going to be seen.
  • What status they will get.
  • How others will consider them.
  • Etc.

In every good story, it's never about what happens... But about how the characters react.

Take any good story: it will revolve around a character and their relationship with the world and others.

Your product is the catalyst triggering a change of identity in your reader.

When you know that, it's never about the money, the calls booked, the clients signed, the relieved pain, etc. It's about how they see themselves. And how other people see them.

Hence why John Caples' "They Laughed When I Sat Down At the Piano... But When I Started to Play!" is one of the best headlines ever written.

Forget about the obvious benefits of a product. And start looking at what moves people without them even knowing.

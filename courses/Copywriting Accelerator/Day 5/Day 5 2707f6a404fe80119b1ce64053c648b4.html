Day 5

The Cascading Effect

Now that you know about the 2 most important principles of copy:

  1. Writing clearly...
  1. Talking to the "unconscious," emotional brain...

It's time to zoom out and talk about the process of turning pretty much anything into cold hard cash.

Ready? Let's go.

First, look at this Rubik's Cube:

This is Reality (the whole of what exists.)

And it's a solid MESS of information with no identifiable pattern.

Everything seems random.

And this leads to unhappiness and lost dreams:

  • Most people live unfulfilling lives.
  • They never amount to much.
  • They'll never be remembered.

That's where you intervene.

Your role is to solve this Rubik's cube FOR your prospect. And they pay you to do it.

To do that you can't create new colours, or new sides. You can't just hit the Rubik's cube until it breaks either.

You've got to play the game and turn each side until you harmonize the colours.

Each 'turn' of the hand being a step towards order.

That's copy.

And we can in fact break down the process into 5 important 'turns':

  1. You start with a rough idea worth sharing.
  1. To refine this idea, you find its Unique Selling Proposition.
  1. Once you've got it, you want to find its Mechanism.
  1. You then find the best way to show it: a Big Idea.
  1. Finally, you make it readily accessible through an Offer.

Each 'turn' refines the idea.

Refining means: "Making finer," i.e. separating the wheat from the chaff.

You will hear me compare Copywriting with sculpting and mining a lot, because they follow the same path from CHAOS to ORDER.

From the ore to the jewel.

And if you go back to the 5 turns I've described, you'll note the language I'm using.

I'm not saying that you should CREATE, or even GENERATE each element.

I'm saying 'find' them. Meaning that they already exist somewhere. And don't worry, in the coming days we will talk about each one.

The lesson today is about the Cascading Effect.

Or how to go from this...

...To this...

This is the basis of Copywriting. And it works by putting your thinking in motion.

That's the 'cascade. It falls. It flows.

It requires looking at things not as a set reality, but as a process.

This is your WHY as a Copywriter.

Your true purpose, whether you know it or not: You're entangling reality for the rest of us, and showing us the way.

Which is why you carry a responsibility....

Because there are two forms of Copywriting: Whitehat and Blackhat.

Whitehat improves the world and the lives of your readers. It makes them like you, and come back for more.

Blackhat manipulates, deceives, and betrays for your own gain.

You will be tempted to go Blackhat. You will be tempted to choose the left-hand path of personal accomplishment. Maybe you even have to go this way to realise it's not the right way.

Remember that you are the sum of your actions.

Persuasion and Copywriting are not neutral tools. Every sales letter you put out will have real life consequences on real people.

Choose your path carefully.

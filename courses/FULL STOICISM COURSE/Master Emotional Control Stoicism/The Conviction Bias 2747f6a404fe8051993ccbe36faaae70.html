The Conviction Bias.

The Conviction Bias Is Simple.

IT’S ALL ABOUT TONE.

imagine there are 2 salesmen.

One Who Is Extremely Extroverted.

One Who Is Extremely Introverted.

and they’re both selling you a vacuum cleaner.

but with their personalities.

Extroverted salesmen.

he’s selling a shitty cheap vacuum cleaner.

but this salesmen STRONGLY believes that this vacuum cleaner is amazing. because of his confirmation bias.

he ONLY looked at the 5 star reviews.

When he speaks to a customer, his conviction is evident in his tone.

body language, choice of words.

He speaks about the features with confidence.

its powerful suction, advanced filtration system, long battery life and shit.

he shares his personal stories with it, how he uses it himself and loves it.

His enthusiasm is contagious.

which makes the customer feel more assured about the product’s quality and effectiveness.

this conviction makes the customer buy.

with NO doubts.

it could be the shittest vacuum cleaner ever, but the customer won’t know.

he gets the sale, he's convinced them.

Introverted salesman.

he’s selling one of the BEST vacuum cleaners in the world

but one thing has changed.

he’s less convinced about the vacuum cleaner’s quality, because of his confirmation bias.

he skipped to the 1 star reviews and ONLY looked at them.

approaches the same customer.

His lack of conviction is apparent in his hesitant speech, lack of eye contact.

and less animated body language.

he goes through the product's features in a monotone voice

and without any personal stories or enthusiasm.

the customer asks him about any flaws.

he remembers one of the 1 star reviews.

“Some customers have mentioned that the battery life isn’t as long as expected.”

His lack of confidence makes the customer feel shitty about the product.

even though it’s the best one in the world.

THE MORAL OF THE CONVICTION BIAS.

if someone is enthusiastic about an idea.

you are WAY more likely to believe them.

and believe that their decision is right.

we’re tribal creatures, we’ll follow along.

THE MESSAGE.

don’t trust anything, especially not leaders.

if they word something in a certain way.

in a nice enthusiastic tone.

Tate did this AMAZINGLY.

and blew up everywhere.

but look beyond that, and look into the details.

USE THIS FOR YOURSELF.

NEVER be shy about your product, or anything.

or if you go up to a girl, don’t be shy.

it’s way more likely to convert the sale.

as seen in the example.

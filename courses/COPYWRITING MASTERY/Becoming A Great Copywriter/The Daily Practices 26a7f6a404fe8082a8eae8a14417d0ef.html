The Daily Practices.

To become a great copywriter, and to immerse yourself in the language of copywriting, there are three things you must do.

The Daily Practice:

  1. Read a piece of copy a day
  1. Write a page of copy a day
  1. Come up with an idea a day

WHILE READING THIS COURSE.

understand that you don’t need to take action on this yet.

I’m just getting you to see the bigger picture.

understand that, and understand what these practices are for.

we will go over the actionable steps later in this course.

Read a Piece of Copy a Day

When you read a piece of copy, you should practice“active reading.”

Passive reading is what the majority of people do when they read.

The passive reader just absorbs the material and doesn’t question the SUBTEXT behind the words

what emotions they’re trying to make you feel, etc.

As An Active Reader…

Your task is to tease out subtext and try to infer the writer’s purpose behind what was written.

This entire course is dedicated to teaching you professional level active reading skills…

becoming fully fluent in the language of sales, copywriting.

Write a Page of Copy A Day

Writing a page of copy a day isn’t as daunting as it sounds.

Simply write a page of copy.

It doesn’t have to be for a big project, but it can be.

It could be a series of five short emails that you sum up into one page.

And you don’t have to use this page of copy you wrote.

If you want, you can throw your page away after you write it.

You could also just hand-copy a page of copy from a successful copywriter.

Sometimes I do that, then active read and mark up what I wrote.

Don’t get too hung up on this stuff.

The benefit in this, is simply engagement with the language of copywriting, keeping you refreshed.

and not getting rusty with the language, forgetting words.

Come Up With An Idea A Day

When coming up with an idea a day, you should not be hunting for a “big idea.”

For instance, your one idea could be a simple way to improve a sales process…

how to get that 1% extra in sales.

A cool subject line for an email…

It could be anything, really.

I know you’ll easily check this one off every day… it takes 5 minutes.

Everything in this course will open your eyes and mind to a brand-new way of perceiving everything you read…

Particularly text in that tries to sell you something, or tries to give you some emotion…

it could even be a message from a girl, trying to get you angry.

you’ll see THROUGH the message, and see her real intentions, or anyone's intentions with words.

So you basically become a superhuman.

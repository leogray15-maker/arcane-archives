How To Capture Attention.

Section 1: The Headline.

A headline is simply the first part that the reader sees.

don't see the word "headline" as just one line.

see it as the top part of your piece of copy.

whether it be :

  • the top of an email
  • the top of a tweet

A crazy amount of the information must be shown very early in the piece of copy.

Unfortunately, most readers won’t read much further than the headline…

So you’ve got to cram a lot of stuff into your copy right away.

You’ve probably heard this before, but if you haven’t:

“The purpose of a headline is to grab attention.”

That’s a VERY simplistic way to put it and there is A LOT to unpack from that line.

In fact, that’s the “plain English” way to put it, and this section will uncover the underlying assumptions that often go undiscussed.

First, we should discuss the structure of a headline…

The Structure of the Headline.

The 4 U’s

The quickest method for translating a headline is to identify four elements labelled as “The Four U’s.” (lots of 4s in this course lol)

A google search said that the 4 U’s were originally developed by legendary entrepreneur and copywriter, Mark Ford.

So in this section, I’m going to be explaining what the four U’s mean.

The four U’s are:

 Urgent

 Unique

 Useful

 Ultra-Specific

All four do not have to be present for a headline to work.

But if 2 or more are missing, the headline will probably not hold attention.

Urgent

P.S honestly, if you're writing content for a personal brand, this is not "needed"

but it is always nice to add some urgency into the mix so the reader will ACTUALLY take action and read.

so...

Urgency is easy.

Just convey that there is a limited time to read this piece of copy and get in on this opportunity.

When you see a date in the headline, its sole purpose is to convey urgency.

“Take action by December 21”

“Sale ends this Friday”

Usually, when one conveys the Big emotion in the headline, it also creates urgency through FOMO.

FOMO is like a cousin of urgency.

Ultra-Specific

The next U is Ultra-specific.

Find some way to make the opportunity “weirdly” specific.

Don’t tell someone they can make $10,000.

Tell them they can make $10,251 (odd numbers supposedly work best).

Don’t tell them they’ll look 10 years younger…

Tell them they’ll look 12 and a half years younger.

Starting to get it?

Don’t tell them their strength will improve 100%..

Tell them it will improve 251%.

this is known as a “pattern interrupt”

people will see proper specific numbers and think wtf is this? i should look.

Alright. So that’s one way.

Another way to plug in some ultra-specificity is with speed.

Selling a karate course?

“A 3-second karate move that can take out someone 134 pounds heavier than you!”

The specific numbers imply that the headline’s claim has been tested and proven before.

The line: “A 3-second move that can take out someone 134 pounds heavier than you!” implies that this move has in fact worked in 3 seconds, which is specific and ALSO quick.

AND the move worked for someone who weighed 134 pounds less than their opponent, which is very specific.

The reason ultra-specificity is so important is it shows depth into the claim, and makes the reader look.

because most copywriters won’t use this.

In short, it makes the headline seem REAL and STAND OUT.

It increases believability.

At least enough that the reader will investigate further.

In The example we showed before the promise was “can turn the average retirement into $588,000 or more… BEFORE this company goes public!”

the number is specific.

In the 70-Cent Crypto example above: “turn a small sum into $2.7 million.”

Go as specific as legally possible, and don’t lie.

then expand on it like it makes sense.

Unique

Unique is non-negotiable.

Because unique is a synonym for New and novel.

We know from The Big 4 Emotions that New is the foundation of strong copy.

If your headline offers up something the reader has seen before, they will not waste their precious time reading it.

Think about the health niche, where diets come and go.

it always has to be the “magic solution”

New diets will continue to emerge and dominate simply because the market demands it.

People who struggle to consistently diet and exercise will always be looking for the easier solution.

That’s one of the things that makes “unique” so captivating.

remember, the person who will be interested in your headline will have been having that problem for a LONG time.

so you need to get a new solution for them.

Take a look at this Headline

he copywriter infers “this is different” by starting with “99.99% of American Investors Trade During the Day…”

and that just makes the reader think "wtf" and have to carry on reading, because of their lizard brain.

And then moving into…

“While other people toss and turn…”

This conveys the subtext that he is doing something New…

He’s the Only person doing it (or at least in the 00.01%)…

And that makes it Unique…

If the reader has been struggling for success in the stock market, this headline offers up some secret knowledge about a New, unique method that they’ve never heard of…

This headline offers hope because it is unique.

the “new unique method” Is where the power of the headline came from, and keeps people reading.

When you plant something unique in your headline, you are conveying the New emotion in the subtext.

To convey something unique, just pull one item from your product or offer, and rebrand it.

for example.

the “productive isolation protocol” i have is basically monk mode, modified.

showing “a new protocol”

The point is… finding something unique in your offer or product just requires a little critical thinking.

Just Highlight Something

Don’t spin your wheels trying to make your idea totally “revolutionary.”

Finding something unique isn’t that hard.

In fact, that is probably the hardest part…

There is no “right answer” for you.

It probably won’t “jump out at you.”

Because your unique element could be any insignificant thing.

There just needs to be a unique element in your headline that the reader has never, ever seen before.

An easy “hack” to think about this: “The first X to ever…”

“The first skin cream to ever use volcanic ash.”

“the first ever business model you will actually enjoy”

“the first ever step by step protocols that will ACTUALLY change your life”

the word “first” conveys New, and New conveys unique.

BUT NEVER USE TEMPLATES.

don’t just use this line, and use it in every thing.

it’s a language, remember?

In fact, I almost deleted this “hack” example from this course…

For fear you would simply use it as a bland template.

This “hack” is supposed to get your mind thinking about the subtext you need to convey: NEW.

Once you can speak the copywriting language, your headline will use words that are natural to YOU.

never follow a template, be authentic to YOUR own words, remember, you are a brand.

Think about this…

If you wanted to convince me to go to your favourite restaurant, you probably wouldn’t say:

“How do I get d42 interested in this restaurant?

”the first restaurant ever to give me two loaves of bread before dinner?”

If you try to fit your writing into a tight template, it will fuck your abilities...

It will kill your ability to create something New...

And that will kill your success.

We are not putting together a piece of furniture when we build a headline…

We are speaking a language of subtext and emotion...

that is also authentic to our brand.

Always, always, always, start your process IN THE SUBTEXT.

Think to yourself “I know the subtext I must communicate, what words might do that best?”

And make sure your headline has some unique element or idea in it.

Useful

The reader will not care to read your copy if they do not feel they will draw something useful from it.

This means you will already need to know what your audience finds “useful...”

This might require some research, but you probably already know what your audience wants.

money, girls etc.

but if you don’t your customer and you’re trying to write something useful…

you’ll want to know:

 Who your ideal customer is, what do they want, what pain do they want to avoid.

 What topics they are curious about today

 What promises or benefits would compel them to continue reading on

One approach is to directly offer a benefit:

“Make money”

“Look younger”

Etc…

Or you can offer information on a specific topic that is top of mind, that they are interested in…

When you know what is top of mind for your reader, you can write something that strikes a specific chord of intrigue.

and it is best if you USED to be the dream customer.

you know what your younger self wanted.

and you could simply write words to him.

what would appeal to him?

Intrigue Alone is NOT Useful – What’s In It For Me? (WIIFM)

experienced copywriters make this mistake even...

They’ll make a headline and say, “What do you think?”

you’ll search and search but then I’m forced to ask, “What’s in it for me?”

And then they’ll say something like, “Well, this is a topic they should really be interested in reading more about...”

Then they frown and realize that intrigue alone is not going to cut it.

But great copywriters also know that intrigue alone is not enough to pull a reader into your piece of writing.

Once this error is pointed out to them, experienced copywriters immediately go back to the drawing board without argument.

Remember, everyone wants to know “What’s In It For Me?”

Your headline must be useful to the reader.

going to a headline and it says : “We Snuck Through!”

That is a cool, intriguing headline… Snuck through where???

they might see it as VERY intriguing.

but they won’t click on it, because what’s the point?

it doesn’t have a benefit.

it could have a normal story of you sneaking through somewhere.

or it could be about sneaking into finding some secret info you want to share.

but if the reader doesn’t know that from the headline.

they won’t click.

intrigue alone isn’t enough.

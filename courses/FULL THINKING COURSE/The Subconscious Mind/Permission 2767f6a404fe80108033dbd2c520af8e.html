Permission

The mind is a concept making machine first and foremost.

Then secondly, it's a meaning-making machine.

We create concepts such as Love, Hate, Morality, Identity, Good, Bad, Cause, Effect, Numbers, Country Etc...

The ability to create concepts in itself is a concept in itself.

Out of these concepts, you create "your reality" All self help and personal development thoughts, concepts, ideas, formulas, methods, secrets, products, programs, events, and workshops came down to this one thing.

...Having self-permission to believe what you want to really believe and do what you want to do.

Once you start looking at self help as "self-permission," you can help yourself without having to go through the maze of "secrets" to get to the good stuff.

"Self Help" = "Self Permission," easy as that.

So, whose permission do you need to believe what you want to believe and do what you want to do?

Answer that, and you're good to go.

How do you detect permissions seeking?

Here are some questions of permission seeking you've asked yourself or to others:

  • Is it possible…
  • Should I…
  • What do you think…
  • Can I…
  • What if...

What you're really seeking is someone's permission and on a deeper level, someone's acceptance of you if you did that.

But because that person has to exist in your own mind as a CONCEPT, that means that you are unable to give yourself permission.

Whenever you ask someone "What do you think about _____", what you're really getting is "What's really important to you about ____".

What people think about you is what's important to them about you.

Then you realize that… why are their needs more important to YOU, than YOUR own needs to you?

The key to success is to make more decisions so you can move forward.

But you can't move forward if you need someone's permission.

The permission you are seeking is to FEEL GOOD.

So if you are in control of feeling GOOD, then you can move forward.

Instead of outwardly directing the questions, you direct them inwardly.

Questions of permission change:

Is it possible --> It's possible because I ______

Should I --> I should because _____

What do you think --> I think because ____

Can I --> I can because ____

What if --> "I know that if ____

The more of these that you ask, the less personal agency you have.

A Note From My Unconscious.

had a talk with my parallel self

had a dream last night, and I was in crippling debt…

been reading a lot of carl jung recently. he’s a big believer in…

dreams.

in his philosophy :

Jung believed that dreams are a direct window into the unconscious.

that means that dreams are the only way you can really “talk” to your unconscious mind.

by “talk”, i mean that it gives signals in the form of dreams.

your dreams are not coincidental, none of them.

they all come from somewhere. and that “somewhere” is your unconscious mind.

even the randomest dreams…

A purple elephant might seem random, but perhaps purple is your mother's favorite color and elephants remind you of a childhood toy.

Emotional tone: The feelings in the dream often matter more than the specific events.

the emotion in your dream may signify how your brain is ACTUALLY feeling.

if you felt trapped somewhere, you’re probably stressed.

in my example… I was in crippling debt and I couldn’t get out.

on a deeper level. that’s being “trapped”

andrew tate, controversial, not getting into it, but he talks about this…

you may like him or not, it doesn’t matter.

money talks, and you listen.

here’s the thing that he talked about…

lucid dreaming, that’s when you can actually “control” your dreams, and you can let it go in your favour.

when you have a lucid dream, you can do whatever you want, even past physics.

but here’s the thing that matters…

you live 2 lives.

one when you’re alone with nobody watching. one with a social “mask”

when you’re alone, you let your subconscious go on autopilot, because you don’t have to consciously make the right decisions that fits your “social mask”

to simply put it… you’re a different person when you are alone, and when you’re in a social setting.

let’s get deep…

why are you different when you are alone VS when you are surrounded by people?

because when you’re alone, your subconscious mind plays AND TAKES OVER your conscious mind.

your subconscious mind has all the memories stored from your past…

your traumas, your fears, your past experiences.

when you’re living on “autopilot” without thinking about what you’re doing… that’s your subconscious mind “playing”

and here’s where it gets interesting…

what if you could just program your subconscious to live the way you NEED to live.

what if you didn’t have to consciously have to adjust your actions to fit it to the character you want to be?

here’s the problem with you. you’re very aware.

it’s not a problem, but it is.

when you’re in public, you can’t help but think “what are these people thinking?”

you oddly psychoanalyze people.

even when you’re just chilling with friends or whatever…

each thought and thing they say, you can dissect where that thought that they spoke CAME FROM.

why did they choose to say that out loud? is it for a desire to be noticed? is it for a sense of wanting a particular reaction from someone else?

some people weirdly can just read others, most of the time these people are quiet.

David Oglivy, The father of advertising, said this about the best copywriters in the world :

“The best copywriters are a strange breed. They are self-absorbed, uncommunicative, and often depressed. They are not the easiest people to live with, but their talent more than compensates for their shortcomings.”

and weirdly, the quote sticks in my head.

you see… copywriting is all about knowing human emotion in and out.

what words make people feel a type of way, what are the “levers” that you can switch to make someone feel some type of way?

if you’ve mastered that, you’ve quite literally mastered copywriting.

that’s why they say the best salesmen are often playboys, they know what emotions to strike with words.

but knowledge is a double edged sword…

with mastery in human emotion and actions, you also tend to analyse yourself.

“they are often self absorbed, uncommunicative.”

that’s because they spend time alone, learning about why humans do what they do.

once you know, you can’t really think about anything else.

and you can’t help but to analyse yourself.

so in that way… life kind of loses the spark, you don’t really get “joyful” happiness after you know that “joy” is just an emotion on the colourful wheel.

not saying this to try sound edgy or cool, but it’s just the truth.

you can be in awe of something, like nature. or the way your cigar burns and lights.

but you can’t have the happiness where you jump up and down with joy.

but honestly. is it a bad thing?

the point is, it doesn’t matter.

what matters is that I’m trying to tell you that you may be in this category of people if you read my emails.

but back to the fact that you oddly psychoanalyze people, and that you’re a bit weird.

the fact that you’re in this “group” means you probably autistically analyse yourself.

and that’s ONLY bad thing about this trait.

knowing how people work and how to play 4d chess is good, sure. you probably want power.

but when it turns to low self esteem because you see that you can’t reach “perfection”, it’s not good.

so let’s get into how to reverse this, and it’s simple.

you need to carve your subconscious into the mind that you WANT it to be.

listen up…

you need your “autopilot” mode to be the person you say you are to the public.

you need to have discipline on autopilot… to the point where you don’t procrastinate, you just get it done.

you can fake your “successful personality” to social media or real people, but you will break eventually and it will seep through.

until you make your “social mask” personality into your real one, now you have nothing to hide.

so… what do I want you to do?

you need to get to the point where you have nothing “bad” to hide.

to the point where you are the person you say you are, and you don’t have to put an “act” up in front of people.

that’s where the real “separator” is.

you need to turn your "social mask” personality, into your REAL one.

instead of having 2 split personalities, have one REAL one.

by that i mean don’t break promises to yourself, don’t let yourself down.

and you won’t have to live in this depressing life where you’re going from low to high.

and here’s a weird viewpoint from me…

I barely talk to people, really.

unless im out with a girl in an outside cafe smoking a cigar, or i’m in the shisha lounge with my boys, or downstairs with my family, I won’t talk.

will be called a loner, but I’m a writer… it goes hand in hand

but what’s weird?

I don’t feel weird.

I am who I say I am online, I don’t have a “split” personality.

there’s these internet guys who will tell you their fairytale of the life their living, I don’t.

I don’t do that because living in 2 seperate worlds will affect everything.

when you aren’t who you say you are, your real self will be transparent. you'll see the cracks. so will others.

If I’m being a loner and I’m living a life of greed and sloth…

I’d probably kill myself, because that’d be THAT depressing.

that’s the cold dark truth, that’s why so many guys are killing themselves.

no purpose, lonely, living a life of greed and sloth.

if you have a goal, you’re actively working on your purpose… that’s happiness.

but not “joy”, just fulfillment.

that’s what you need.

be who you say you are.

Night Game Flow (February 12, 2014)

a proper example of how a night out should look like

Starting Out Parked.

Hit the switch for the tunes to get into the mood.

Slip on the outfit you wouldn’t have fun without.

Start the Engine

The engine is humming its time to get running.

Approaching the club no need for lines they are a waste of your time.

Glancing at the bouncer the music is getting louder.

Both of you open up the gait.

Flag down the bartender the drinks are on the house but you throw down some cake - it’s not worth a costly social mistake.

You’re sippin, slippin into a trance as people begin to dance.

Ten minutes to eleven you spot a seven.

Up Shift

Someone gets there first but fortunately all he does is mumble. Welcome to the jungle he’s about to take a tumble. Pick up this young man’s fumble.

You can’t overcome the temptation and ask her about his skills in the conversation.

A laugh and a smile, you’re turning up the dial.

She throws a curveball by asking something logical and you respond with something comical.

Before she can think you have two orders in a blink and are having a drink.

The dance floor is just as crowded as the door.

Up Shift

You’ve been here before . She’s about to talk… but someone is coming over with a confident walk.

Handshake. All smiles. A quick exchange and your group of three spawns to seven as you’re always situated in the traffic zone.

After holding conversation you realize the younger crowd is starting to get loud… It’s time to…

Up Shift. Up Shift

At a regular pace you swiftly move to the pulse of the party and accidentally bump into a group of five.

The younger guy is on drink nine but that’s fine you see girl number two has no interest in you. (win-win in your head)

Dancing and entertaining four, girls number two is forced closer to man on drink nine. Naturally a sloppy makeout begins.

In typical fashion… A chain reaction. Several pairs have already split and the club is at its max patrons for the night.

People are spilling, it’s time to make a killing.

Up Shift

You pull one out of the group and dance on one as we all know you’re nowhere near done.

You exchange a quick glance with the girl from before, she’s in a conversation but her body is positioned towards the dance floor…

Before the song ends you drop the group and stop to recoup:

“OMG thank you for saving me I was so bored!”

Your only response is a devlish smile. You take her left hand and walk towards the bar.

No line for you. No rookie mistakes. A simple motion for two and you’re both on drink number three.

As you’re taking your first sip, you slip a tip over the ledge… just out of her line of sight

Down Shift

“How come everyone knows you?” she asks…

As mentioned already when asked something logical and you respond with something comical.

You decide to dance for twenty and realize its time to leave, while others are slurring all engines are purring.

“Let’s go”

She nods and agrees. (your right hand is already setting up a cab)

Grab the purse and coat and as you exit the car is right out in front.

“How did you know I wanted to leave?” she asks.

The flirting is over, no need to remain in entertainment mode.

You acknowledge her feelings - “I thought you were feeling tired from standing in those heels all night.”

“Ya I was!” she says and responds with a smile.

Shut Off the Engine.

Posts like this are for people who have a decent amount of experience.

Basically, the entire goal is to control the environment so you’re bringing the party and controlling the “flow” of the club/bar.

Conversations get easy and the trick is to get a feel for where the groups in the room are going to move.

As an example, a conversation is of no value if you know the group is not ready to be approached.

This is similar to approaching someone as soon as they walk in the door, it is awkward and uncomfortable if they are not situated.

Once your conversation skills are on point, the rest of the game is simply knowing where to move and why, don’t stand in X spot simply because.

There has to be a method to the madness. Like a “bank robbery” you’re casing the joint and should know where the traffic will move.

DAY 24 - CREATING A CUSTOMER AVATAR WITH AI

Now that we have a product idea (or at least a starting point, just pick something and it'll refine itself as you go), this is where AI really starts to come into play.

When most people think about AI, they either think of it as a new type of search engine or as a way to strip all of the soul and creativity from their work. They view it as all or nothing. You either use AI to automate everything and outsource your thinking, or you don't use it at all.

This is the wrong way to go about it.

In business, there will never be a time where you can just tell a bunch of robots to go and make you money so you can sit around with the poor habits you don’t want to change and do nothing.

Not how it works.

If everyone could just do that, nobody would get results, and the market would evolve beyond it.

The reality is that AI hasn’t changed a thing.

  • You still need traffic
  • You still need content
  • You still need promotions
  • You still need a valuable offer
  • You still need a customer avatar
  • You still need a landing page
  • You still need copywriting

And of course, you still need to understand what you’re doing to orchestrate all of those things well.

So no. AI won't do this for you out of the box.

It's not difficult to test this. Go and try it out. See how far you get.

The only thing that AI has changed is the speed and quality at which you can do the things above.

But here’s the cool part:

I enjoy writing content and books. That’s what I want to spend my time doing. That’s my differentiator. My ideas and novel perspectives.

I do not, however, enjoy marketing. I’m good at it if I take the required amount of time to prime creativity, but it’s a drag to get myself to actually care about optimizing headlines and leads and offer positioning and all of that stuff. I’d rather spend my finite creative firepower on the writing I enjoy doing.

That's why this can be so powerful.

Most starving artists focus only on what they love. And by doing so, they avoid the things, like marketing, that actually make them an income from it.

In business, when you don’t enjoy doing something, it’s wise to outsource it to someone – or now, something – that can do it better.

The thing is, AI is not a good marketer (or writer) out of the box. AI has a swath of general information that needs to be made specific. You make it specific by writing specific instructions and context to have it write exactly what you would have wanted if you wrote it yourself.

So, if you want to outsource your marketing, you have 2 options:

  • Become very good at marketing and create your own AI system.
  • Find someone who is good at marketing, understand the process, and use their system so you can focus on what you enjoy doing.

With that I say you’re in luck!

Today, we're going to create a customer avatar.

That's the starting point for all marketing.

Once we have a detailed customer avatar, we can use that to turn our product idea into an irresistible offer. Then, we can use that to have AI guide us through creating our first product with our own ideas. Last, we can take all of that and turn it into a landing page or promotions that speak to our target audience much better than we could have on our own.

What's fascinating about AI is that you can "train" it on the knowledge of legendary marketers. You can create an "employee" (the AI prompt) that writes at the level of Alex Hormozi or David Ogilvy.

It may not be 100% the same as what they would do, but it is a thousand times better than anything a beginner could create.

Creating A Customer Avatar

Market research is annoying.

I’ve always hated doing it, and because of that, my customer avatars were always half-assed.

I never felt like I dug deep into who they were, and the rest of my marketing suffered because of that.

With your strategy from above, we need to dig deeper into your target audience.

We need to understand their demographics, psychographics, buying behavior, where they hang out online, and more.

People get stuck on this part way too much.

Let’s make it simple.

You need a target persona – who you are positioning the product toward – so that your marketing is specific and tangible. That’s it.

The thing is, there is a much larger market of people who will also resonate with your marketing. Especially if you are marketing with social media. You don’t want to be too specific or else your content won’t get spread to more people.

Personally, I think you should choose from two options:

  • Target yourself or your past self.
  • Target people who have a lot of money (founders, executives, those with high paying jobs).

The problem with targeting people who have a lot of money is that you often don’t understand them. You can’t relate with them. That will seep into your marketing. However, it’s a great option for those who have either worked with these people before or are following a lot of them on social media.

That’s all you need for now.

A vague idea of who you want to target.

That will come in handy when we start this process with AI.

How To Create The Customer Avatar Prompt

In order to create a great AI prompt, you need a few things:

  • A list of expert level instructions to accomplish the task you are trying to do. In this case, that would be a detailed customer avatar.
  • Exactly what the AI needs to know from you in order to complete that task without making too many guesses. In this case, that would be interview questions to guide you through creating the customer avatar.
  • A way to turn all of those into a detailed prompt. If you're too general with AI, the results aren't going to be very good.

So, to do this well, you can start with this prompt to get the instructions you need:

You are an expert marketer. Write a comprehensive guide on how to create an extremely detailed customer avatar for my business. Include all of the necessary components of a customer avatar and how to fill out each part in the most effective way.

Once you send that to AI, either Claude or ChatGPT, you should have a list of instructions for how to create a customer avatar. Save that output somewhere safe so you can copy and paste it into the next step.

Note : this isn't the only way to get "instructions" to replicate. You can ask AI to break down a type of marketing or content you like into it's structure with step-by-step advice to replicate it, or use the transcript from a YouTube video that teaches you how to do something.

You just need some way to get detailed, expert level instructions that you can feed to AI to turn it into a prompt.

Now, we need to turn these instructions into the prompt.

First, and as we've done before, start a new chat and paste this prompt into it.

That prompt helps you create detailed prompts much better than you could write on your own.

Once you send that prompt in a new chat, respond with this:

I want to create a prompt that interviews me to create a detailed customer avatar that I can use for my marketing. You will act as an expert marketer that uses the information I provide to research my target audience to extract relevant pain points, demographics, psychographics, etc. Structure this prompt in 2 phases:

Phase 1) Context Gathering

Use the customer avatar instructions below to create interview questions. Your job is to get all of the necessary information needed for a complete customer avatar. Ask me one question at a time.

Phase 2) Customer Avatar Creation

Once you have all of the information you need, generate a full customer avatar profile that I can use for my marketing.

Customer avatar instructions:

[paste the customer avatar instructions from the last step here]

Before you hit send, make sure to paste the instructions from the last step into the brackets at the end. That's the most important part.

Once you send that, it should spit out a prompt similar to the one I provided above.

You can use this process to create prompts around almost any part of your workflow, writing, or marketing.

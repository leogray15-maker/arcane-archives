DAY 9 - A SHORT GUIDE TO PROFILE OPTIMIZATION

Welcome to day 9.

I am not going to spend a lot of time on profile optimization for the following reasons:

  • As discussed, how pretty your profile is leads to 5% of the results. Your content over time leads to 95% of the results.
  • Too many people get way too wrapped up in creating the best profile when it barely matters.
  • Most of your ideas for improving your brand messaging will come from writing content, because writing about ideas leads to more ideas.

Now, your profile is important in many cases… and you kind of have to have one in order to start posting.

It's the first touch point for any person who's about to follow you, but they get to your profile from seeing your content.

Here is everything you need to know to make your profile good enough to follow.

1) A banner that matches your vibe and shows what you do

Depending on the platform you are using, you may need a profile banner (YouTube, X, LinkedIn).

You have a few options here:

  • Have an image that is symbolic of the lifestyle you want to live. I've seen people have pretty sunsets, beaches, or cities. I had skyscrapers as my banner for the longest time. You can find these on a site like Unsplash or Pexels .
  • Create a design that showcases your product, service, or brand message. When I launched my book, I put that in my bio with the tagline. Some people have a quote on top of an image that is symbolic of their brand.

We'll go over a few examples below.

2) A profile picture that isn't terrible quality and matches your level of professionalism

If you follow enough creators, you'll notice that some have anonymous pictures, others have a singular color, others have professional headshots (that you can now generate with AI), and others have a picture that others have taken of them.

In my time as a creator, I've seen all forms of accounts grow. Their profile pictures have ranged from the most professional headshot to a barely visible image of them at night sitting on a lawn chair.

It really doesn't matter.

I mean, hell, Devon Eriksen built his entire brand with an AI-generated cat as his profile picture because he likes cats.

If you don't know which to choose, and don't want to spend time learning how to shoot or edit a headshot, you can take a picture of yourself and purchase a software like HeadshotPro to generate images for you.

Or, you can search YouTube for a tutorial on how to create AI headshots with something like ChatGPT or Midjourney.

3) A bio that includes top-of-mind topics and a desirable outcome

Remember that most people visiting your profile aren't looking to be sold on anything, nor are they looking for anything in particular.

You can go the route of being edgy with no bio or being salesy with a direct call to action for your product or service, but I like to do it this way:

  • List out your 2-3 main topics – Just say "I talk about x, y, and z."
  • Tell people what you help them achieve – Something like "Build a physique that people respect"

The reason I like to just list the main topics is that people are trained to follow topics they want to read about. When you go to a blog site like Medium, the first thing it asks for is what topics you want to see.

In my opinion, having something as simple as "I write about productivity, creative work, and fitness" is a lot more follow-worthy than any clever saying you spent 10 days stressing over.

The desired outcome (what you help people achieve) gives people another chance to see your value.

4) Links and projects that show you are creating value off platform

This can be a product, free guide, email list, or personal website.

Just show people that you have somewhere that they can learn more about what you do and receive deeper information than what you post on the timeline.

We'll talk about creating a newsletter and product in future lessons.

I'm going to leave it at that for today.

As mentioned, and as you can see, your profile can be a work of art with a lot of intention, or it can be close to nothing. The choice is yours and you will refine it with time.

Most people get stuck on creating their profile for months (I know this from many past students).

With that, this will be the last time we discuss this in the challenge. Stop worrying about it and focus on your content.

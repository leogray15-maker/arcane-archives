The Lead Magnet.

A “lead magnet” is a free product that builds trust and authority with those who download it.

You allow people to download it in exchange for their email, now you have them in your email list.

you can do this, or you can simply tell them "join for more ____ like this" if you're using the inbound method...

Don’t overcomplicate that part. If you are offering an ebook, then upload it to Google Drive and paste a share link inside your welcome email.

If you want to build a more complex lead magnet, you may have to use a software like Gumroad and use Zapier to transfer the emails over to your email list.

There are a few other purposes of a lead magnet:

  1. Customer creation – your job is to educate your audience to the point of becoming customers for your other products.
  1. Writing what you want – when you have a lead magnet, all of your authoritative information on one subject is in one place. You don’t have to worry about writing so much about it on the timeline. You must plug the lead magnet often.
  1. Selling your product – your lead magnet should give people enough information to trust in buying your product. You should plug it toward the end of your lead magnet to make sales when the “leads” are hot.

When you create multiple free downloads / lead magnets, you substantially increase your authority.

You teach people on a deeper level.

They like you more. They engage with you more. They start conversations in your DMs. They feel like you are contributing more value than others.

Don’t take this lightly. Free downloads help you indirectly grow on social media faster because you are creating more true fans.

A lead magnet only needs to be built once. Then you can resume focus on newsletters from the subscribers you get from the lead magnet.

Step 1) Choose A Lead Magnet Topic

How do you choose a topic for your lead magnet?

You should create it based on the main skill or interest you want to monetize, or a valuable topic from the product you are building.

If I'm building my 2 Hour Writer course which goes over posts, threads, newsletters, and social growth – then I could create a "Digital Writer 101" lead magnet or a swipe file of my best content with some writing tips.

A lead magnet isn’t only about making more sales, but establishing authority in one domain.

That way, you are able to write about more complex topics on the timeline and in your newsletter.

Think about it…

We’ve been writing beginner-level content until now so we can grow and spread to new audiences.

An organic social media audience will almost never understand high-level topics. If they do, you should still act like they don’t.

So… we create a lead magnet to teach people the basics.

As more of your audience downloads it, you open up more room to dive into advanced topics. This builds your authority even more.

If I create a lead magnet on writing, I don't have to focus on creating content around writing that much. I just have to worry about getting people to download the lead magnet because that has all of my best content on writing.

Creating free downloads / lead magnets are how you talk about whatever you want on social media while retaining authority.

Simple Examples

One easy way to come up with an idea for your lead magnet is to think about one big problem you can solve for your audience.

For example, if you’re a fitness coach, you could create a lead magnet helping your audience:

  • Understand basic nutrition (calories, macronutrients, etc.)
  • Lose 10lbs in 4 weeks
  • Eat right on the go (including how to stay in shape while eating out on business trips)

Or, if you’re a productivity coach, your lead magnet could be about:

  • X ways to improve focus to get more done
  • The top habits of successful business owners
  • A productive morning playbook (a basic morning routine for productivity)

Your lead magnet would be doing one of those things - not all of them. You can - and should - create new lead magnets in the future to do the other things you can think of.

Emulate What Works

Another great way to come up with ideas for lead magnets is to study what’s already working. Chances are, your favorite creators in your space have lead magnets of their own. Download them, figure out what works, and create something similar with your own words, style, and packaging.

(Don’t ever copy someone else’s work. We are emulating. Not stealing.)

You can also look around for high performing YouTube videos in your niche. Chances are, a video is solving a problem (or creating a benefit) for an audience, and therefore you can create a lead magnet doing something similar.

A Reminder

Make it basic, beginner level, and action-oriented.

You can get deeper into your topic(s) in your course, coaching, or other paid offers. For your lead magnets, make them easy to consume and simple to implement.

Try writing down a few lead magnet ideas right now. If you need inspiration, try checking out YouTube, scroll X to see threads, or scope out your favorite creators.

Step 2) Choose The Type Of Lead Magnet

For your first lead magnet, I would recommend:

  • A Notion template – like a habit tracker, writing template, productivity system, or similar.
  • A swipe file – based on your topic, you can create a swipe file of your favorite posts, quotes, or strategies that you’ve gotten from other people.
  • A checklist – this is fairly simple. Give people a checklist for whatever it is you teach about. For me, that would be a writing checklist. But I’ve done an SEO and web design checklist in the past.
  • An eBook on the basics of your topic – give people everything they need to know as an ebook download. Use Google Docs to write and format it, download it as a PDF, and give people that download.
  • How to get a desired result – write an eBook on how to get a highly desired result in that topic.

THE COPY ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

most people read copy and think "that's good." winners dissect it like a surgeon.

here's the exact method:

STEP 1: FIND THE BIG IDEA

not the promise. the idea beneath the promise.

example: promise: "make $10k/month"

big idea: "the economy is shifting and early movers will get rich"

the big idea is WHY the promise is possible. write it at the top of your doc. everything else flows from this.

STEP 2: IDENTIFY THE LEAD TYPE

is it:

  • problem/solution
  • secret/revelation
  • story/narrative
  • shocking truth

why does this lead work for this market? what emotional state does it create?

STEP 3: MAP THE PROOF CHAIN

every claim needs proof. track how they prove each claim:

  • social proof (testimonials)
  • evidence/data proof
  • demonstration proof
  • celebrity/authority proof
  • historical proof
  • logical proof (if/then)

no proof = no belief = no sale

STEP 4: NOTE ALL DIMENSIONALIZATIONS

this is where copy gets powerful.

basic: "$1000 profit"

dimensionalized: "that's your rent covered"

further: "never stress about bills again"

further: "your kids see you relaxed for once"

each dimension adds emotional weight.

STEP 5: STRUCTURE BREAKDOWN

mark every section:

  • headline
  • lead
  • credibility
  • problem agitation
  • solution reveal
  • mechanism
  • proof stacking
  • offer
  • close

what's the PURPOSE of each section? how does it move toward the sale?

STEP 6: EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS

what emotions are they targeting?

  • fear of missing out
  • fear of being left behind
  • desire for status
  • need for security
  • want for freedom

track the emotional journey.

REAL EXAMPLE BREAKDOWN

analyzed a financial newsletter promo last week:

lead type: secret revelation

proof chain:

  • historical charts (visual proof)
  • expert credentials (authority)
  • testimonials (social proof)
  • live demonstration (evidence)

dimensionalization: "$10k gain"

becomes... "quit your job"

becomes... "never miss your kid's game"

emotional journey: frustration → curiosity → hope → excitement → urgency

result: 4.2% conversion rate

THE CONTEXT LAYER

always note:

  • where was this running? (email, FB, YouTube)
  • who's the audience?
  • what's the price point?
  • what worked before?

context changes everything. same copy, different audience = different results.

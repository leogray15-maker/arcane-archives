36. Quit the Battle with the World

if you believe the world is against you, it will be.

if you believe the world is working in your favour, it will be so.

"Quit the Battle with the World" is a call to stop the struggle, both internally and externally, with the world around you. It’s about realizing that life is not about fighting for what you want or against what you don't, but rather understanding that the world mirrors your attitude. When you fight it, resist it, or hate it, the world pushes back with the same energy you’re putting out. This becomes a cycle of resistance where you're stuck fighting reflections instead of changing the source of the problem—your own mindset.

Zeland emphasizes that this is all about shifting your attitude toward the world. If you approach life with a combative mindset, everything will feel like a battle, and your results will match that struggle. But if you stop fighting, stop resisting, and instead take the first step toward the world you desire, things start to shift.

people struggle getting into the gym because it's seen as a struggle and a battle...

2 years in, they go to there as their source of daily habit, and it's "unwinding"

The world will start to walk toward you, not because it’s changing but because your perspective on it is. It’s a matter of allowing yourself to have what you want, believing that what you desire is already yours, in the metaphysical sense, and simply waiting for it to manifest in physical reality. When you stop doubting and accept that your desires are already done , the universe can work with you to bring it to fruition.

This ties into the idea of desperation . When you're desperate to achieve something, it indicates that you don't believe it's already yours. That desperation comes from a place of lack and doubt. When you're constantly grasping, reaching, or trying to force your desires, you're telling the Universe that you don't trust its ability to deliver in the perfect timing. Desperation is a form of internal resistance and prevents the external intention from doing its work.

Pendulums, as Zeland describes, thrive on this internal battle and the energy you feed into it. Society, and the people around you, often encourage this struggle by promoting the idea that you must fight for everything, change yourself to be "better," or prove your worth. But this, Zeland suggests, is an illusion designed to drain your energy. Every time you react negatively to the world or let these pendulums pull you into their dramas, you're giving away your energy, lowering your vibrations, and moving yourself to a less favorable life line. You can’t afford to do this—because as your vibration lowers, you move further away from the life you want to create.

The key to achieving your goals, Zeland argues, is to allow yourself to have them right now. If you can’t take what you want, if you feel like it’s not possible in this moment, postpone it only if it’s in alignment with not postponing your happiness. If you constantly live in anticipation of a better future, you’ll find that future never arrives. You’ll spend your whole life waiting for things to improve, without ever truly experiencing the joy and abundance of the present moment. The point is to stop viewing life as something that needs to be improved later and start living as if it is already the way you want it now.

So, when you stop the internal battle, stop fighting against the reflection in the mirror, and instead adjust your attitude—things begin to flow. The world starts to respond to you differently, not because you changed it, but because you allowed the right changes within yourself to unfold naturally. It's not about battling the world into submission; it’s about coexisting with it and making sure your internal world reflects the life you want to see.

don't be desperate, just go and take it.

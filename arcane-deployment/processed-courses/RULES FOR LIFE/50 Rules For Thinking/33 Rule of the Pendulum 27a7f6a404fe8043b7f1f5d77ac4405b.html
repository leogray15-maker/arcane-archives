33. Rule of the Pendulum

break free from the idea that success and happiness come from imitating others or following established societal norms. The Pendulum here is a metaphor for the social forces and expectations that drive people to conform, to try to be like others, and to fit into the mold of what is considered "successful" or "right" by the world around them.

The "Do as I do" mantra of the Pendulum suggests that society, through its norms and standards, demands you to copy or mimic what others do in order to gain acceptance or success. This external validation becomes a form of control, pulling you in a direction where you constantly compare yourself to others, never truly feeling like you measure up. You may try to emulate their success, thinking it will bring you the same outcomes—but Zeland warns that this path will only lead to misery because it disconnects you from your authentic self.

True success, according to Zeland, comes when you break free from this cycle. You have to establish your own standards and follow them, unapologetically. This doesn’t mean rejecting society entirely, but rather choosing your own path within it . When you give yourself permission to set your own norms and confidently follow them, you become an individual who doesn’t need to compare themselves to others, and that confidence and uniqueness can make you stand out in the world.

Zeland doesn’t expect you to fight every pendulum or rebel against all societal norms. That’s not realistic. Instead, it’s about acting consciously . Recognize when you’re being influenced by these external forces and use your intention to stay true to your path, without releasing negative energy back into the world. When you act with intention, you’re no longer a passive player in the game of life—you’re the one in control, setting your course, and avoiding being swept away by the pendulum’s back-and-forth motion.

Ultimately, the rule of the Pendulum is about owning your own story. Stop trying to copy others. Your soul has its own unique blueprint, and success isn’t about achieving someone else’s vision of success; it’s about creating your own, based on what feels true to you. And when you do that, you create a magnetic energy that the world can’t help but notice. You can still be part of society, but you don’t have to be defined by it. You set your own standards and follow them . That's the real path to fulfillment.

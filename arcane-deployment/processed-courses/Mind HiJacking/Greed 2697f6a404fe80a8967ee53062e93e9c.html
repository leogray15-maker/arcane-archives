Greed

A lot of people think you can't motivate rich people with greed because they're already rich. Wall Street crime would tell us differently.

I always say if you put 10 billionaires in a room, you ask for a show of hands on how many of them need more money and you use the word need, at least nine out of 10 hands are going up.

Something like the Martha Stewart case is profound evidence.

Here's someone, with millions of dollars committing the act of illegal insider trading to prevent a loss on a stock that is equivalent to pocket change in her world.

Of course, ultimately, there was no underlying crime.

First of all, the Martha Stewart case is classic and, as an aside, what I learned is this 5% of the population is hardwired so that they cannot lie, cheat, or steal.

They are the Jim Carrey character in the movie that just could not do anything but tell the truth.

There's 5% of the population that is this way. Most of you will find that hard to believe because I doubt any of you are in that 5%.

Then there's 5% of the population that is hardwired exactly the opposite way.

They must lie, cheat, steal all the time even when it does not serve their purposes whatsoever.

So they could be a self-made billionaire entrepreneur and they'll lie about their income, that sort of thing.

They just cannot help themselves.

That takes care of 10% of the population.

The rest of it is all situational, which is where we are.

We require three basic things in order to be greedy, in order to want something for nothing, in order to steal.

We need three things.

We need a perceived need.

We need the ability to rationalise the behaviour.

So again, employee theft this is, "I'm doing all the work, you're getting all the money. You just got a new car. You're taking a big vacation. I can't take a vacation. Boom. I can take a little. I deserve it."

The third thing is the belief you can go undetected. If those three things are present, hence Martha, then greed in general, and in specific, theft, will occur with 90% of the population.

Greed is visceral.

It's not really consciously managed.

It's like a full dog will still eat a hunk of steak on the floor.

That's what dogs do.

It's visceral.

Therefore, it's stimulable in just about anybody.

So in short, there are three things needed:

  1. A perceived need.
  1. The ability to rationalise the behaviour.
  1. The belief you can get away with it.

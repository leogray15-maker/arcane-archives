How To Overcome Business Stress Once And For All.

Listen, it’s easy for your brain to shut down because of the amount of shit you have to do.

For this example, I’m talking about specifically in business.

You’re stuck at a bottleneck, and you just can’t seem to get out.

So I just want to tell you one thing.

One. Step. At. A. Time.

It’s common advice, but hear me out.

Humans work in systems.

Video games are so easy to get hooked on because they have a “progression ladder”

In a story game they’ll give you small easy wins to keep the momentum flowing.

You first kill the shitty enemies that won’t be able to kill you, then you move to your first little boss.

That boss was pretty hard, but you took him down.

Then you move onto the other little enemies, but now they’re a bit stronger.

You get stronger gear, and you get the big achievement that says “levelled up!”

Now you can kill the enemies that are “harder” with a little more effort, but it’s fun, because it’s not “too hard”

Then you move onto the next boss, which is also a little harder than the first one, but it’s still manageable.

And it keeps going, until you hit the final boss, and you defeat him.

This is the progression cycle In games.

Now imagine this…

Imagine they just threw you in a pit of the hardest enemies possible at the start.

Without even teaching you the controls, without telling you how to upgrade weapons and armour.

You’d die, and you’ll quit the game.

That’s exactly what your brain does when there’s too much shit going on.

When you’re at a “plateu” in business, this is exactly what it feels like.

You’re stuck at a certain income point and you just CANT get up.

There’s so much shit to do to improve this.

Improving your sales page, improving your emails, improving your free content, improving your value in your product, thinking of bonuses, thinking of new growth strategies, thinking of new ways to go viral, thinking of new ways to provide value, thinking of new books to read, thinking of new courses to add, thinking of new videos to put out, thinking of EVERYTHING.

It’s so easy to be overwhelmed.

If you can get overwhelmed in a video game, imagine how this feels to your brain…

Imagine how this REAL LIFE struggle feels.

Your brain ends up shutting off, overwhelmed… and start to move away from work.

That’s when it starts to feel painful to work.

I know because I’ve experienced this, critical burnout.

Critical burnout came from me waking up at 3:30am every morning none stop and working until 6pm.

All while making MINIMAL progress.

It was not fun, my mind was plagued with thoughts like “why am I not growing?”

That made my brain shut off, and I did the bare minimum for 2 weeks after that.

Until I started to think I need to hop back on it.

So you know what I did?

I started one step at a time.

I know everyone tells you do one step at a time, I’ll tell you how to do one step at a time.

First, start by outlining your issue.

What is your MAIN problem.

First, we need to identify this.

And the GOAL that we all need is money. Right?

So we see what brings us money.

It’s going to be our product.

Okay, sure.

What is the “bottleneck” of your sales?

Are you not getting enough views?

Are you not getting enough conversions on your sales page?

Are too many people refunding and leaving your product?

Fuck, maybe it’s all at once. That’s real hell. And you’re due to see it, so buckle up.

But really, let’s start from scratch.

The main 3 problems are these, and we’ll go in order.

1. Not enough eyes/views

A lot of people will be stuck here.

And let me tell you one thing.

If you’re stuck here, don’t concern yourself with sales pages or conversions.

Block them out.

Focus on this first.

If you’re not getting much views, then it means that you can’t even get people to your landing page, so that’s a problem you need to fix.

And if this is your current problem, simply focus on that.

Simply study the algorithm, study the ways to capture attention, study the way to write, study the way to speak, study the best writers in the game, study the people that have the results that YOU WANT.

Study how to get views, and focus on that.

Identify why you’re not getting views.

There is no luck in this game, every piece of content or an ad blows up for a REASON.

Remember that.

When you’re not getting enough views, simply study WHY you’re not getting enough views.

Write to yourself, think of all the problems with your content.

And keep posting, realise that this is a game of consistency.

I could start a new tiktok/twitter tommorow and I won’t blow up the next day.

I know how to write, I know how to capture attention, I know how to make people act.

But it still will be consistency, just remember that with views.

But listen to this..

Don’t turn a blind eye to your content.

You should study winners actively, you should study the people doing well in your field, what are THEY doing that YOU’RE not? That’s the difference between you and them.

Once you see what’s wrong and you fix it, you will inevitably get more eyes on your works.

2. Conversions from sales page > sale

On any landing page software, you will be able to see how much people are going to your page, and how much are converting.

If that number is low, below 1%

Then it’s going to be a marketing issue on your end.

You see how this goes step by step?

Your views are sorted now, but the views on your sales page aren’t converting.

They aren’t converting because of your marketing.

It’s as simple as that.

If that’s not happening, you simply need to make a better sales page.

But of course, it’s easier said than done.

You can’t just “make a better sales page” when you don’t know how a “good sales page” looks like.

This is why you need to study winning pieces of copy.

We talk about this in the copy course, you study one piece of copy a day, and “active read” it.

When you “active read”, you start to see patterns.

And once you do this for a long amount of time, you start to develop your third eye.

Your “reading eye”

You realise what sells, and what doesn’t.

Someone can send you a sales page and you can tell them “this will sell” or “it won’t”

The simple answer to a low conversion rate is literally just this :

You don’t show enough perceived value.

The value of your product is lower than the price that you set out for them to pay.

And doing this, you don’t lower the price, you simply put the value up.

I used to sell 5 courses from the silent network for a pricepoint of £197 (£236 for them after tax)

And it still sold, even when I had under 1k followers.

Crazy to believe, isn’t it?

The courses were much smaller, I have a lot more knowledge now.

But I would still get good testimonials.

You know why?

The perceived value was HIGH.

The sales page did the WORK.

If people buy off a bad sales page and for a low price, they would be unlikely to take action.

If they saw a good sales page, a sales page that shows HIGH VALUE, A LOT OF INFO TO BE UNPACKED.

Then they will buy, and when they buy, they will take action on it.

The reason you’re not getting conversions is not a “glitch”, it’s just because your marketing probably isn’t very good.

The fix for this?

Study marketing, study selling, study the best in your game, study the winning pieces of copy, study, study, study.

After enough studying, you will be able to craft a sales page that is a WINNER.

After studying enough winners, you make your own through the LENS of the winner.

Reread the copy course, find some top people in the game, and study their works.

There’s no shortcut, you just have to study.

3. People refund/leave your product.

This one is the highest tier of problem.

Business really comes down to these 3.

  • Eyes on your content
  • Conversions
  • Churn rate (leaving/refunding)

That’s the 3 pillars of business.

You find out how to get eyes, you find out how to sell, you find out how to provide value.

Once you realise this, it will be much harder to become “overwhelmed”

If you’ve overcame getting eyes on your content, and your sales page is now converting, but your audience is still leaving.

It’s simple.

Provide more value.

Give them “onboarding”, give them more clarity.

The reason people will leave your product is simply because of 2 reasons.

They’re lazy or they don’t have clarity.

The steps are too hard, or they just don’t even know how to start properly.

You need to give them “small wins”

Remember how I told you in the video game example?

You give them small enemies, a level one axe, they can kill them.

Then you level them up step by step.

Hence why I have a mindset course, and an advanced mindset course.

You level them up slowly.

And on TOP of that, you give them more clarity.

But here, let me tell you one thing.

If you don’t want to listen to me, you can simply give them more value.

If they leave your product after you give them clarity, small steps and you keep on adding new value daily.

If you do everything in your power to get them NOT TO leave.

Then honestly, let them leave.

If people don’t act on your product after you’ve done EVERYTHING in your possible power to make them stay and SEE The value, they just wont do it.

You will inevitably get some refunds, you will inevitably get some churn.

If you look online and see the average refund, churn rate, it will NEVER be 0%

Even the best companies in the world, they will always get shitty customers.

But you know what the key is?

Control what you can, FORCE it down their throat, FORCE THEM to act.

And if they still don’t act, they can leave.

The goal is to reduce it, not get rid of it.

Because getting rid of it is perfection.

And we all know perfect doesn’t exist.

If it did, life would be boring.

I hope this gave you some clarity.

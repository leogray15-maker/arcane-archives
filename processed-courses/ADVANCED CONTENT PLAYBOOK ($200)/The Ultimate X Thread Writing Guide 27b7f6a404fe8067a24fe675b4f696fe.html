The Ultimate X Thread Writing Guide.

Why should you care about threads?

  • They bring in a massive amount of followers. The longer and more in depth they are, the more followers they bring.
  • They are great for sales. Just plug your product / lead magnet at the end.
  • You can repurpose them into posts, emails, blog posts, YouTube scripts, and any other content.
  • They build authority, trust, and a deeper connection with your audience.
  • If you can get people to repost them, you can grow from 0-1K followers faster than ever.

Threads are just expanded posts. Keep this in mind when you are deciding the intention of your thread.

It’s important you carefully read the previous lesson so you understand the growth, authenticity, and authority pillars.

Is it for growth?

  • Post your story or a part of your story
  • Most threads (no matter growth, authenticity, or authority) will result in growth.

Is it for authenticity?

  • Post your personal experience or struggles you've overcome
  • Document what you are going to do while you are "building in public"

Is it for authority?

  • Drop massive value relating to what you teach.
  • Give beginners step by step advice.
  • Call out your audience and tell them what they are doing wrong.
  • IF IT IS FOR AUTHORITY - Call out your audience in the eyebrow.

Threads, when done right, are a mixture of all 3. The primary intention of the thread should be focused on growth and authority - sprinkle in authenticity by writing in your own voice, like you are talking to a friend.

How To Pick A Thread Topic

There are a few types of threads that do very well:

  • A numbered list (like a blog) with short or long insights about each point.
  • A story of yourself or a client that accomplished something your audience wants to accomplish. (Think transformations)
  • Search for multiple variations of an idea you have on Google and make your own spinoff of a blog or YouTube video headline.
  • Calling out a problem your target audience and teaching them how to solve it.

This part should be easy. Type parts of your Content Pyramid into Google, YouTube, or just go off the top of your head.

(Your content pyramid is a breakdown of your main topics into sub-topics into content ideas).

Do not neglect the power of this method. It has brought previous people in the program hundreds of followers a day. The Google, Medium, or YouTube content is already proven to do well. Take the idea, watch the video, and write about the topic in your own words. "Steal like an artist."

Principles of Thread Writing

Don’t be afraid of writing in-depth content, but make sure that your first impression will leave the reader hungry for more.

Threads are about two things:

  1. Giving your unique perspective on a particular topic
  1. Building your expertise and authority on the X space

Threads are like writing a sales letter.

The components are the same:

  • Hook - attention-grabbing first line (optional)
    • If this is for authority - call out your audience here.
    • "If you are X, Y, Z..."
    • Describe the big problem about the topic you are going to discuss. How does it affect your audience? BE SPECIFIC.
  • Headline - mention the big problem you plan to solve and why it needs to be solved.
    • Read this blog article on how to write killer blog headlines (these work well for threads)
  • Deck - Introduces the story with curiosity (optional)
  • Lead - AFTER the first post in the thread. The lead paints a picture of the problem people are having and hints at a solution.
  • Social Proof - Tell people why they should listen to what you have to say on that topic. (optional)

All are important, but the first post is the MOST important post of the thread. The first post contains the eyebrow, headline, and deck - but sometimes it just works well with the headline.

If your first post sucks, your thread won't perform, so a lot of emphasis on writing your first post well (Which should be your eyebrow-headline-deck).

The Rest Of Your Thread

The way you want to structure a thread (or any type of content really) is by having a beginning/middle/end structure to it.

  • Beginning = hook + problem
    • The hook includes the eyebrow, headline, and deck
    • The problem is the lead (and usually takes up 1-2 posts in length)
  • Middle = key talking points
    • Think about how a blog article is structured with subcategories
  • End = closure/lesson learned and call to action
    • Wrap up the thread with the lesson you learned or a recap on what the reader learned
    • You have their attention - so ask them to do something - like buy a product, DM you for your services, download a lead magnet, and ALWAYS ask them to repost the first post, it helps.
    • If you have a product, you can offer to give it away free to one reposter.

🔑 Remember, it's not about content length. It's not about the number of posts. It's about providing valuable information and making your reader feel something.

Getting Maximum Exposure

We all know networking is good - but being able to send a thread to your network to repost is an INSANE growth hack.

If you haven't already, start making friends in the DMs. DO NOT ask for a repost if you have never DMd the person before. Make friends first, even if it's a very short conversation.

Once you have a network - send them the post and be authentic:

  • "Hey man, just wrote up this thread, would truly appreciate if you gave it an RT. If not, no worries, and if there’s anything I can do for you let me know!"
  • If you have a decent following, you can offer to trade reposts (an example of exponential growth and social capital).

Adding Examples of People That Align With Key Points

If you want to guarantee that some posts get good exposure, tag people who understand whatever topic you are talking about well.

If I write a thread on my fitness transformation and going on walks is a big part of that - I could tag someone who also is a proponent of going on walks when I mention walks.

If I write a thread on building a X account and mention learning how to write well, I can tag a big writing account and give them some praise.

If I write a thread on X and want to give the readers Y resources, I can tag someone who provides Y resources.

A lot of the time the accounts you tag will repost the thread as a whole.

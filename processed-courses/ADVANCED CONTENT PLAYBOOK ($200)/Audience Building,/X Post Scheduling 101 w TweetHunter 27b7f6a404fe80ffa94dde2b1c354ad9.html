The best tool you can invest in at any stage of X is a post scheduler.

The best post scheduler you can use is TweetHunter . It has the best features, the best user interface, and the best support. It's an investment, but it's 100% worth it, especially if you're new to X.

Why? Because it's a pain in the butt to post content manually. Having to go post on X manually multiple times every single day is one of the main reasons why people get burnt out, give up, and quit.

With TweetHunter, you can schedule content in advance, so you can sleep, do your other work, and go on vacation without having to worry about posts being posted.

Setting up your schedule

The best time to post posts, if you're targeting a Western audience, is 7am to 12pm Eastern Time.

You can set up your post schedule by going to My Queue -> Edit Queue (top right) and deleting/adding time slots from there.

The "Make my schedule more natural" button randomly adds or subtracts 1-5 minutes from your time slots to make them look more natural and less automated.

So why did I pick those times? I based them off the times Dan Koe and Dakota Robertson post most days. You can pick the times that feel right to you.

But there's one important factor to consider when you're creating your schedule:

Auto-reposting

Another one of the benefits of TweetHunter is the ability to automatically repost your posts X times every Y hours. I asked Dan right before writing this article and he told me reposting 2 times every 6 hours is optimal.

So, if you post 3 times a day, you should separate your posts enough so you have something going out or being reposted every 2 hours or so.

For example:

Post 1 posted at 8am

Post 2 posted at 10am

Post 3 posted at 12pm

Post 1 reposted at 2pm

Post 2 reposted at 4pm

Post 3 reposted at 6pm

and so on...

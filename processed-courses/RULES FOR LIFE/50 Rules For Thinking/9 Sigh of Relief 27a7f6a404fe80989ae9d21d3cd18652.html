9. Sigh of Relief

Imagine you have a big backpack filled with things you say you’re going to do but never actually do—things like "I’ll start working out tomorrow," "I’ll stop smoking next week," or "I’ll finally get to that project someday." Each time you think about these things, it’s like adding another heavy rock to your backpack. And these things, even if you’re not actively doing them, are still using up your energy. You might not realize it, but your mind is constantly holding onto these unaccomplished goals, and in doing so, it drains your energy—energy that you could be using to actually make progress on something that matters now.

This constant thinking about things you never finish doesn’t just waste time—it wastes your free energy too. You could be using that energy to move forward in life and bring your dreams to life, but instead, it gets stuck in all these unfulfilled potential goals that you may never act on. Think about how much energy it takes to keep telling yourself you're going to do something but never doing it. It’s like carrying around a heavy weight that you don’t need.

Now, the solution is simple: You have two choices. Either finally act on those potential goals and make them real—or let them go . By letting go, you stop wasting energy on them. Imagine how light your mind and body would feel if you could drop all that unnecessary mental clutter and use your energy for what really matters to you. All those "one-day" promises are only holding you back.

when you tell yourself "someday", you put that in the "someday isle"

someday isle is the place that should be empty, "someday" is never good.

Every time you delay or procrastinate on something, it’s like putting more energy into that delayed goal. And that means you won’t have enough energy left for what you actually want to achieve. You’re trapped in an endless loop of postponing your desires.

So, here’s the trick: Let go of those endless promises to yourself or turn them into reality. By doing this, you’ll instantly feel lighter, more focused, and have all the energy you need to move forward. When you do this, it’s like taking a big sigh of relief —you free up your energy and start using it for things that truly matter, pushing you forward into the life you want to create.

remember, let go.

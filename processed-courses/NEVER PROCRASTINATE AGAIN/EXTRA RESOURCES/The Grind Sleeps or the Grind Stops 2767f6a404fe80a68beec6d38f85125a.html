The Grind Sleeps or the Grind Stops.

Lamborghinis. Men in suits with Rolexes. Lions with crowns Photoshopped on their heads.

These images are always overlaid with text such as:

"I'd rather hustle 24/7 than slave 9 to 5."

"Sleep deprivation is my drug of choice."

"While you're sleeping, someone else is outworking you."

Hustle culture defined

These are slogans of hustle culture - the glorification of putting your work over your health.

This attitude is quite common with "entrepreneurs'' and "influencers" on Twitter and Instagram. These people (claim to) work almost nonstop - staying up late and getting up early. They ridicule rest and glorify grinding.

They care more about how many hours they work than how much they get done and how they feel. But here's the cold hard reality: You have to rest to be your best.

Physical performance requires rest

Want to perform well on your priorities? Then you have to make your mental and physical health your top priorities.

There are parallels to mental and physical performance. Imagine your favorite athlete. Mine is Roger Federer, the GOAT of tennis (who sadly just had another major surgery).

To perform at the highest level, an athlete must do two things:

1) Train their body every day

2) Rest their body every day

Rest is a must. Elite athletes recognize that their rest is arguably even more important than their workouts.

Athletes don't bust out a hundred squats all at once. They do squats in sets. Then they rest and recharge between each set. They know they have to balance work and rest to achieve peak performance .

If they go to the gym and try to do too much, they injure themselves. They have to take time off from the gym and they're sidelined from play. They lose experience, money, and maybe even groupies.

This works for mental performance too

This is true for both physical performance and mental performance. If you try to do too much at one time, you won't be operating at peak mental performance. You'll get burnt out. You'll have to take a break, or you'll be in a constant cycle of half-assing everything you do.

I've been there. I've been the guy trying to do too much. I half-assed everything and burnt myself out, which led to many other problems.

So, what's the moral of the story? Hustle culture is a lie. You need to be taking breaks. You have to rest to be your best.

You don't have to hustle 24/7. You don't have to cut Netflix, sports, and video games out of your life. What you do have to do is balance work and rest.

The optimal balance and rest can be found out for you...

and one way to rest is have a reward system.

Remember: the grind sleeps or the grind stops.

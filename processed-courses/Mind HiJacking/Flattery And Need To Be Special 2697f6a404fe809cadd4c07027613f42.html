Flattery And Need To Be Special

People want to be special.

Here's the formula for getting cooperation from people by making them feel special.

It's mostly used in fund-raising.

First you explain a situation for which something is at stake.

Secondly, you create a role, if you have to, so that the person and that person ONLY can play in that situation,

Third, you emphasise the importance of the role by saying they're one of the only ones that can do it.

People want to do things that are important.

They also always need explanation.

Then you personalise it. Flattery will get you lots of places.

You openly acknowledge that your request asks for sacrifice of some kind, and then you put them on the spot, and you ask if you can count on them.

Need To Be Special

THE FORMULA

  1. Explain the situation as a whole - what's at stake?
  1. Explain the role the person can play in the situation (Nothing is more interesting to the person than himself)
  1. Emphasise the importance of the role (Nobody wants to volunteer for something mundane or insignificant)
  1. Explain why (you know/believe) the person is exceptionally well qualified, perfectly fit, uniquely able (Flattery!)
  1. (Openly acknowledge that your request asks for a sacrifice on their part)
  1. Ask if you can "count on" them

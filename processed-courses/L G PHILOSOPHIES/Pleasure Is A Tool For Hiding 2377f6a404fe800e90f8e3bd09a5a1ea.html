Pleasure Is A Tool For Hiding.

when there is nothing to distract you.

your thoughts attack you.

this is a symptom of breaking promises to yourself.

the times where you don't want even want to be seen in the sunlight.

the times where you don't even want to go talk to your family, but you feel bad when you don't talk to them.

the times where life is just a contradiction.

it feels like you're two faced.

there is one side of you that has monk like discipline, but it felt like he had just slipped away.

and there is one side of you that has addict like instincts.

you can't sit in a room by yourself for 5 minutes with no distraction, because you might lose your mind.

but you aren't an addict, you're a person who is lost.

a person who simply is only doing these things because you're scared of what will happen if you didn't do these things.

but on the other side, you have a different side of you which is smiling to himself 24/7.

the side of you which actually can go to sleep without watching youtube on your phone.

you can't face your thoughts.

and this has been happening from your childhood.

there are even whole companies that profit off it.

you go to a cinema, and there are people with no thoughts behind their eyes.

watching the movie while stuffing their mouth with whatever man made chemicals that was engineered to occupy your taste buds.

not to mention, the taste is a very strong sense.

when you are eating something, your sole focus will be on the food.

and this is heavily capitalised on.

companies pay millions of dollars to have the best food scientists to split test which ingredient is most captivating.

this works because people live for captivation and distraction.

people get addicted to these foods, these drugs, these habits,  but not for the reason you may think.

"the dopamine reward system" is a common answer.

but i don't think that's the real reason, or atleast it is not my philosophy.

it is simply so capitvating because it is so good at making people forget their reality.

as i mentioned before, taste is one of the most captivating senses for your human attention.

i don't think companies actually care about the taste, they make it so sweet, so salty, so spicy to maximise the amount that the user can be distracted from their reality.

when you are captivated in the heaven of sweetness, you do not think about the reality of things.

but when the taste on your tongue has disappeared, it comes back.

reality comes back, and harder, this is because of the dopamine to crash ratio.

i'm not religious as of now, but god knew to make this bad.

why do we get fat and die if we eat sugar and processed foods?

it is often overlooked, and just seen as the truth, but I like to ask why.

why do we get a sugar crash after eating sugar?

sugar is the sweetest thing to eat, but it gives us the worst health symptoms.

but I'd like to ask why.

why does the thing that gives us the most pleasure, or distraction from reality always have a downfall?

why is there a crash to the sugar?

why is there a hangover after being drunk?

why does ecstasy lose it's magic?

why does weed build up a tolerance?

why does nicotine withdrawal give you a headache?

why does everything that distracts us have a bitter aftertaste after the initial sweetness?

i think it's for a reason.

we wasn't created to live like this.

we was created to create, that line makes sense.

the leader will become a mentor sooner or later.

everybody around us lives for distraction, isn't that a reality?

it's ironic, because it is a reality of people trying to distract themselves from reality.

you procrastinate work by watching youtube instead.

because you know that if you was on your own without any outside stimulation, your brain will keep on repeating to get that work done.

but you don't want to face it.

but why? because the aftertaste is eternal and is sweet, but the initial taste is bitter.

it should make sense to do it, doesn't it?

it'd be easy if life wasn't so easy.

I think we're living in a reality that isn't a reality.

but only not a reality by perception.

No Top Entrepreneur Is Sober.

no top level entrepreneur is sober.

they might microdose psilocybin mushrooms before a deep work session.

they might take adderall before a deep work session.

they might chew nicorette gum while working.

they might smoke some top shelf cali before a writing session.

they actually might drink 10 coffees a day.

it's actually does seem like a joke, but it's not.

it's actually so common once you do some research.

the top 1% of entrepreneurs are not sober.

but the question is, why do they actually do this?

the secret lays in something we like to call a flow state, this is the state athletes call being in the zone.

personally, i remember flow states from gaming back in the day, funny how I'm using it in business now.

but why is a flow state so magical?

it's because when you are in one, it feels like you have no thoughts in your brain.

it's just you, and a mission.

but this always brings me back to this point.

isn't a flow state just a state where you can't hear the voices in your head? is that why it is so sought after?

the common advice telling you to find your purpose is just to find out what task brings you into the flow state the most.

and the best entrepreneurs even combine that with things like drugs to enhance the experience.

but are they taking them to enhance their business progress, or get away from their thoughts? or both?

some things are hidden in plain sight under the cloak of productivity.

such as the gym.

on one side it seems productive, on the other side it could be the only place where you are in a flow state and you have no negative thoughts.

combine that with other drugs like steroids, that just shuts up your brain more, as in you get less negative thoughts when you look at yourself in the gym mirror.

isn't it just a way of getting away from reality masked by the cloak of productivty?

this is a odd predicament, since I feel like i've just entered a flow state while writing this, there are no thoughts in my brain.

and it's oddly peaceful, I have my ambient playlist on, and I'm just speaking my thoughts.

I think i have found a purpose, but isn't a purpose just something that you can find a lot of flow states in?

to contradict that, you can get into a flow state with any task with the underlying requirement that you are good at it.

to enter a flow state is simply to have a task that is not too easy or too hard.

but it interests me to why the peak human experience is simply being present, since presence is just forgetting about all thoughts and being focused on external factors.

it's an odd way to live, and this way makes me view everything as just a temporary escape just masked by the cloak of productivity.

or as I would call it, lost in the resonant frequency of a pendulum.

after you start to understand how the mind works a little bit, you start to question everything.

however, I have not even scratched the surface.

this builds me excitement and makes me think my purpose is to study our thoughts and the brain.

but isn't purpose just something that puts you in flow?

I think life is just the pursuit of unanswerable questions.

maybe we're chasing a carrot on a stick.

but it makes sense to be in the present moment and enjoy it, right?

because you don't want your thoughts to take over.

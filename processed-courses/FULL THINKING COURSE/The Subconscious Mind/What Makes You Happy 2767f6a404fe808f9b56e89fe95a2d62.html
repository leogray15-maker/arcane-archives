What Makes You Happy?

I want to ask you something.

What makes you happy?

List down all the things that would make you happy.

Here’s mine:

  • Freedom
  • Options
  • Laughter
  • Knowledge

Just let it all out, no one else is going to see this apart from you so there's no need to be ashamed.

Now another question: What would make you happy?

Do the same as the above — you might think it's the same question as above but it's not.

Now that you've done this, do you notice anything about the language?

The language or the words specifically are: "Makes me" If something makes you, it's bigger than you.

If something can make you, then that something could also unmake you.

You become subservient to it

Let's say one of the items on your list that would make you happy is a house.

Now answer this: House makes me happy because it represents _______ (Replace house with whatever item that resonates with you)

When you've answered that, that is what you are lacking — what you don't have.

When I focus on how or will this make me happy, I am saying I have no choice.

When I go, how am I going to enjoy it, use it, whatever…

This puts me in a position of choice.

Because HOW and WHEN or WHERE is a choice direction.

When you say this makes me, this owns you.

Makes me = desire.

When you enjoy it, you own everything. I enjoy it = will power (choice) This makes me = you're in a position of nothing.

I'm less and less if I have this.

Do you see how this works?

So when you're about to ask yourself, "this or ______ will make me happy"

Say this: "I'd enjoy this" See the difference?

Understanding Your Ideal Customer

This is where our research begins and how we find pain points/problems that we can use as firepower for creating a solution and attracting the right people.

Where to research:

1) Your Audience : this is the best option. By building an audience, you have direct access to people that will validate your ideas.

  • Tweet engagement about a specific problem is data to leverage that in your marketing.
  • Social media posts with a lot of replies, engagement, or shares is another sign you should use that somewhere in your marketing.
  • You can run polls, interact with people in the replies, or DM people directly to talk about what they want.

Market research is a LONG and ONGOING process that is not going to be finished, even after creating your first landing page or offer. You should make it a habit to log on social media as a researcher , not a consumer.

2) 1 on 1 Calls : If you can get on a call with people related to the problem or audience you are targeting, even better. Offer free consultations, dig into their problems, and see if your solutions can help.

3) Facebook groups : you can join groups in ANY niche. Bodybuilding (health), picking up chicks on Tinder (relationships), marketing for personal brands (wealth)

Start requesting to join some in your niche and pay attention to people that are asking questions (people that need help with specific problems)

Keep a running market research Google doc for all of this. You WILL NOT remember it in your head. Copy and paste everything.

4) Amazon Reviews : Specifically the books section. There are books on literally EVERY topic in almost every niche.

Find a popular book in your niche and read the 2-4 star reviews. These will tell you what was MISSING from the actual product - that is how you can make yours better.

Again, write all of this down and start connecting the dots.

1 and 5 star reviews are mostly worthless as they don't give feedback, they are either angry or happy with the product.

6) Answer The Public : I do not have the most experience in this as I rely on the other methods + my audience on social, but it can be very powerful. I would recommend playing around on the website and possibly watching videos on how others use it.

7) Social Media In General : Same with facebook groups. Look for questions that people are asking or areas where people are struggling. Facebook groups gives you access to an audience - this is another reason why building an audience is powerful.

Painting Your Ideal Customer

Some may know this as a customer avatar - it is very common in the marketing space because it is necessary.

These are the answers you are trying to find when you are going through the FB groups, amazon reviews, and other market research methods.

Copy/paste this into your Google doc and fill out the answers.

Btw, you are not limited to ONE customer avatar. You will be refining, iterating, and creating new ones for different products/services you create.

Last, if you are targeting yourself (you are the niche), then you will fill this out with YOUR information and refine it as you find better ways of articulating it through market research.

Specify Your Target

Write out a summary of your target.

Example: John is a 26-year-old college grad that became a financial advisor but he has a very hard time finding/attracting women into his life. He is worried that he is getting too old for the dating market.

This is who you are marketing to. Do not get too caught up on the actual ideal customer - pay close attention to their deep, biological, and burning problems.

Where will you use this information?

  • On your sales pages
  • In your social media promotions
  • In your content when you are leading into a promotion, launch, or just to build authority around your product or service
  • When you are DMing prospects or potential customers

Day 5 Exercises

  1. Analyse the swipes of the day:
    • What's the main idea behind this piece of copy?
    • Who is it directed to?
    • What emotion does it play on?
    • Why do you think it worked?
  1. Send your idea of the day on the skool or discord
  1. Write a personal letter to your aunt or uncle
    • Prompt: encourage them to start walking 10,000 steps every day
    • Hint (optional): use vivid imagery to highlight the benefits (weight loss, stress reduction, clear thinking...)
  1. Idea refinement chain
    • Prompt:
      • Start with a broad product idea
      • Refine the idea through each of the 5 "turns"
        • Rough idea
        • USP
        • Unique mechanism
        • Big Idea
        • Offer
      • Write a brief (2-3 sentences) description for each step
      • Hints:
        • Your rough idea is a starting point, a general concept without specifics. It's broad enough to encompass many possibilities but has no distinguishing features.
        • The USP narrows the focus. It highlights key differentiating features and benefits. It moves from generic to specific language.
        • The unique mechanism explains the inner workings of your product, making it more tangible. Showcase the technology and method behind your promise.
        • The Big Idea reframes your main benefit into a universal opportunity for personal growth.
        • The offer makes the product real and appealing. Think of clear pricing options.

This exercise will help the 'flow' of your thinking and will train you to see how you can find the value in a rough idea.

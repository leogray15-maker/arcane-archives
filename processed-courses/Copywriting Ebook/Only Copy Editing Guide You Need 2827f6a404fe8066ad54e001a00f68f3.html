Only Copy Editing Guide You Need

The ONLY copy editing guide you will ever need that will put you miles ahead of the competition. (I stole this from the bestselling author, Stephen Kings).

If you'd like to make all of your ads, sales letters and marketing pieces a LOT more likely to be read and responded to, then here's a little technique you should know about.

Listen to this:

In his book, On Writing, Stephen King, the world–famous horror novelist, said the biggest “breakthrough” he had when getting his writings published was a note an editor sent back to him that said:

“Second Draft = First Draft – 10%”

In other words, he needed to trim at least 10% of the “fat” in his stories, and make them razor sharp.

And you know what?

This advice is even more relevant to copywriting—where there's no room for any “slop” or extra words that slow down the sale.

And there's a simple trick you can use that'll get your copy down to the absolute core information, with all lingering fat eliminated.

Here's what you do:

  1. Get your copy down to what you think is the final draft.
  1. Do a word count on the copy.
  1. Subtract that number of words by 10%.
  1. Get your copy down to whatever that number is.

For example:

If your ad is 5,000 words, you subtract 5,000 by 10% and get 4,500. Then you'd just go through your copy again and again—until you get it down to 4,500 words.

This is a great way to make your ads razor sharp.

Why?

Because it forces you to use shorter words and say things in less space.

In fact, when you do this, you'll see entire paragraphs can be re–worded and re–phrased in half the space.

All of which can increase your readership... which can increase your response... which can then increase the amount of money you put in your pocket.

For more information on this tactic—as well as some of Stephen King's other excellent writing tips (many of which can be directly applied to copywriting)—get his book On Writing.

I try to read it at least once per year and get something new out of it every time.

I bet you will too.

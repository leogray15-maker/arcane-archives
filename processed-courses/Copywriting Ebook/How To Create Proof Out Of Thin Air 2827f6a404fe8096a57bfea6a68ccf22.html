How To Create Proof Out Of Thin Air

In today's day and age, where scepticism is at an all time high, proof isn't just important -- It's essential.

Without believability, there's no sale and as AI continues to generate more mediocre copy, proof becomes your best tool to differentiate your product and writing.

There are three ways to creating proof and one of them being is:

Implied proof.

Proof doesn't have to be stated directly, it can be implied.

For example:

  • "If lines are busy, please call back" - A subtle implication of high demand.
  • "Be careful not to overdo it" warnings - This is an aspect of precaution copy (this will have it's own module.) In essence, it's saying the product is so good that it can be dangerous.

The second way is Eating Your Own Cooking. Got this from Kyle Milligan.

  • Describing how you yourself using your own product or service powerfully validates it's effectiveness, safety and value. This is one of the reasons why there is always some sort of story in winning copy.

Now, lastly, the third way of creating proof is through admitting your negatives. When you're upfront about your failures, it lends credibility to your positive claims.

Transparency is disarming. One of the reasons Trump got so many votes is because he was transparent about some of the things in his life and when election time came, his positive claims 'popped' more.

Use this for good not evil.

So... as you can see in my reply, this is literally how habits are made.

For example you brush your teeth then you go and use mouthwash, that becomes a habit.

The point it, it links the 2 together.

So if you’re linking productive habits with bad habits, it's not good.

However, there is a way to reverse it.

First, let’s go into why this actually happens first…

You see… your brain is linked to look for 2 things.

Comfort and avoidance of pain.

I got that from the book of human nature by Robert Greene.

When you’re doing these things that aren’t “comfortable”, your brain will tell you to go back to the “comfort” to avoid the pain.

And honestly, it’s common sense for your brains circuit.

But it’s not common sense if you actually want to succeed.

Here’s what you need to do…

You need to start recognising these thoughts as “not you”, but simply your brain trying to go back to comfort mode.

It’s like a car being scared to enter sports mode and just stays in comfort, that’s you rn…

So what would your prescription be?

Whenever you work and you hear the thoughts of “I’ll go do x then I’ll do my work”

Realise that it’s not you.

Realise it’s the “devil” in yourself trying to fight the angel.

As we know in the alchemy of self, you stack rocks.

The more you do the “devil” activities, the more rocks you stack in that side of the scale.

And with enough rocks stacked there, that will become your new “norm”

So how do you break this new “norm”? Should be common sense.

You need to start stacking rocks in the correct direction.

THE FRAMEWORK NO ONE SEES

military doctrine identifies 3 ways to defeat an enemy:

  1. pre-emption - act before they're ready
  1. dislocation - make their strength useless
  1. disruption - attack what holds them together

this isn't just war theory. it's how all influence works.

PRE-EMPTION IN PRACTICE

Traditional war: Pearl Harbor, blitzkrieg, first strike

Modern application:

the job interview hack: instead of answering "tell me about yourself"

pre-empt: "let me share why this role matters to me"

you just controlled the narrative.

the sales pre-empt: "you're probably thinking this is too expensive" said before they say it. defuses their main objection.

the relationship pre-empt: "i know you think i'm just another guy who..." acknowledge their fear first. removes its power.

pre-emption isn't speed. it's controlling the timeline of decisions.

“i know you probably think (insert objection)”, and defuse.

DISLOCATION IN PRACTICE

Traditional war: germany had the maginot line defense. nazis went around it through belgium. made france's strength worthless.

Modern application:

the competitive dislocation: your competitor has better features.

make it about community, not features.

makes their advantage = meaningless.

the argument dislocation: they want to debate facts.

make it about feelings.

makes their preparation = worthless.

the market dislocation: taxis had citywide monopolies.

uber made jurisdiction irrelevant.

makes decades of regulation = meaningless.

don't compete where they're strong. move the competition elsewhere.

you can’t win in some areas, but you can shift angles.

after all, life is all perspective, especially marketing.

DISRUPTION IN PRACTICE

Traditional war: ignore the army, destroy supply lines.

ignore the fortress, poison the water.

ignore the front, attack morale.

Modern application:

the trust disruption: competitor has better product?

attack their founder's credibility. product quality becomes irrelevant.

the confidence disruption: can't beat their logic?

make them doubt themselves.

"are you sure about that?" their certainty crumbles.

disruption targets the invisible bonds. break those, everything falls.

THE INCEPTION PRINCIPLE

the highest level isn't using these tactics. it's making targets think it was their idea.

military calls it "reflexive control": feed them information that makes them "voluntarily" choose what you wanted.

you allow the sale, you never push for it.

you allow them to make the sale because it’s their idea.

the best sales pages don’t sell, they make it stupid not to buy.

key in making it stupid not to buy, because the reader will know that, and they will make a decision which is confirmed in their mind that they got a good deal.

convey that message in your marketing, and you’re winning, there’s thousands of ways to do it, you just gotta be creative and align to your market.

plant the seed. let them grow it. claim it as theirs.

THE COMBINATION FORMULA

real power comes from combining all three:

Example: Amazon's retail domination

  • pre-emption: built infrastructure before profits
  • dislocation: made it about convenience, not price
  • disruption: destroyed local retail ecosystems

Example: TikTok's platform takeover

  • pre-emption: targeted gen z before others noticed
  • dislocation: made it about algorithms, not followers
  • disruption: fractured instagram/youtube dominance

Example: Dating app success

  • pre-emption: message immediately after match
  • dislocation: move from app to phone quickly
  • disruption: break their normal dating pattern

THE COGNITIVE BATTLEFIELD

here's what the military knows: every decision has three phases:

  1. observe (input)
  1. understand (process)
  1. decide (output)

pre-emption attacks phase 1 (control what they see)

dislocation attacks phase 2 (control how they process)

disruption attacks phase 3 (control their options)

control all three = control the outcome.

WHY THIS ALWAYS WORKS

humans think they make rational decisions. they don't.

the brain uses shortcuts:

  • pattern recognition
  • emotional responses
  • social proof
  • cognitive biases

these tactics exploit every shortcut.

pre-emption exploits: availability bias (first information weights heaviest)

dislocation exploits: framing effect (context determines meaning)

disruption exploits: loss aversion (fear of losing > desire to gain)

DEFENSE IS EXHAUSTING

to defend against pre-emption: you'd need to see everything coming

to defend against dislocation: you'd need strength in every domain

to defend against disruption: you'd need unbreakable internal cohesion

no one has all three. that's why these tactics are undefeated.

PRACTICAL IMPLEMENTATION

In business:

  1. pre-empt customer objections in marketing
  1. dislocate from competitors' strengths
  1. disrupt their customer relationships
  1. make them think switching was their idea

example : “i know you’re probably thinking that this is just a rip off of (insert brand), but what if i told you that (destroy competitors credibility), and that’s why their customers are starting to do xyz instead”

In negotiation:

  1. pre-empt with aggressive anchor
  1. dislocate from their preferred terms
  1. disrupt their coalition/support
  1. let them "win" what you wanted to give

In any conflict:

  1. act before they expect (pre-empt)
  1. change the game (dislocate)
  1. break their unity (disrupt)
  1. make them own the outcome (inception)

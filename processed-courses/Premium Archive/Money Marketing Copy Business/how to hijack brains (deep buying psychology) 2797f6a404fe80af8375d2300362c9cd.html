2) Hijacking The Brain And Memory By “Peak/Ending” Manipulation.

remember the last time you was on a rollercoaster?

you don't remember the slow climb. the boring middle section. the safety instructions.

you remember two things: that holy-shit-i'm-gonna-die drop and walking off with shaky legs and a massive grin.

your brain just deleted 90% of the experience. kept 10%.

how they discovered this:

daniel kahneman (nobel prize winner) ran the most uncomfortable study ever. colonoscopies.

group a: 8 minutes of pain. standard procedure. get in, get out.

group b: same 8 minutes of pain PLUS 2 extra minutes of mild discomfort. literally made it longer and more uncomfortable.

here's where it gets fucked up.

group b rated it as LESS painful overall.

read that again.

longer procedure. more discomfort. but they remembered it as better.

why? the ending was slightly less horrible. that's it. the last 30 seconds overwrote 10 minutes of memory.

kahneman tested this everywhere. vacations. movies. meals. relationships. same pattern.

your brain doesn't keep a recording. it takes two snapshots:

  • the most intense moment (peak)
  • the last moment (end)

then convinces you that's the whole experience.

what this means for you:

that 47-point customer journey map? trash it.

obsessing over every email? pointless.

trying to make every touchpoint perfect? you're fighting biology.

your customer will remember exactly two things:

  1. their "holy shit" moment with your product (i.e the reveal after the problem)
  1. how they felt at the very end

examples that print money :

saas: killer feature discovery (peak). offboarding that makes them feel smart for leaving (end).

coaching: breakthrough session (peak). graduation ceremony (end).

disney knows this. why you think they put gift shop at exit? last memory = buying happiness.

restaurants know this. why free mint with check? last taste = sweet.

your customer's entire memory of you comes down to two moments.

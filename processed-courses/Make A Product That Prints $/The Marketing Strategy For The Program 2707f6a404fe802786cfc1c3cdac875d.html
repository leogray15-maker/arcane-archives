The Marketing Strategy For "The Program"

once again, book of secrets people, you can skip this. read it if you want, don't get confused.

So far, we've come up with a product idea and understand who we are selling the product to.

Now we need to understand the "pieces" of a compelling marketing strategy.

These will all be used in your content, sales pages, and anywhere else you mention your product.

For the time being, we will be "ideating" a lot.

When we get to future sections you will start to see how all of these piece together.

1) High-Performing Angle

There are a few base human desires that are wired into our psychology.

All of your marketing should be based around one of these, or a few.

  • Career advancement
  • Skill acquisition
  • Social acceptance
  • Sexual companionship
  • Life enjoyment
  • Status and success
  • Freedom from fear

Whenever you write content, sales pages, or promotions for your product write it from the lens of one of these desires.

2) Clear & Believable Goal

There is a concept called "market sophistication" in marketing.

In brief, it means that the more developed the market, the more strategic you have to be.

The market is tired of false promises, countdown timers, marketing gimmicks, and bold claims.

I've experienced this for myself, but most people just want to know exactly what they're getting and why it impacts their life.

This is good news for us because it makes our job a lot easier. All we have to do is be honest and authentic.

The forefront of our product and marketing is a clear & believable goal.

What makes a clear and believable goal?

  • A desired outcome that is 1-2 steps ahead of where they are, not 10-20 steps.
  • A timeframe for when they can achieve that goal.
  • A quantifiable metric.

As an example, the goal of one is 7 Days To Genius Ideas product is "10x Your Creative Output In 7 Days."

For this course, it's "Build A Product That Prints $" – in the sub-headline of the landing page I could include "the roadmap from $0 to $100K to $1M as one-person."

So, what is the clear goal you are helping people achieve?

Think about your high-performing angle and the idea for your product.

You can make a creative angle for ANY topic, no matter how niche it is.

"Become in full control of your dreams in under 30 days and master your conscious actions to achieve unbreakable confidence through hacking the subconscious"

Get creative.

3) Desirable Transformation

Marketing is about transformations.

In essence, you are helping people go from where they are now to where they want to be.

You are helping people overcome the qualifying pain points and reach the clear & believable goal with The Mechanism you create in the next module.

Why am I telling you this?

Because again, marketing is about transformations.

Nail that into your head.

People don't care about the features of your product, they care about how it impacts their life.

If they're selling a productivity product, they'll say something along the lines of "this product has a habit tracker" rather than "eliminate anxiety and overwhelm with our habit replacement system, 4 hours of deep focus becomes seamless."

Nobody cares about the habit tracker.

They care about the problems it solves and the goals it helps them achieve.

Now, I am telling you this to always think in terms of transformations when creating anything relevant to your product: the material, the marketing, the content.

The transformation is how you show people proof that they can use the product to impact their life.

It's how you ground the claims you are making with tangible data.

You've will have already written out your personal transformation in the last section. That is a useful starting point for your marketing.

In the future, you'll be able to use customer success stories in your marketing. I'll show you how to collect these testimonials later.

4) Bonuses

Bonuses are a powerful way to add perceived value to your product.

Some people will buy just for the bonuses.

Some corporate examples:

  • An SEO guide so people can get more organic traffic to their site
  • A free 30 minute coaching session to help them make use of it
  • A Slack or Discord community where buyers can interact
  • A website checklist to make it easy for them to create websites
  • A complimentary training video or screen recording going over a bonus strategy
  • Templates or frameworks to help them with guided education and work

or in my course, you get loads of courses as a bonus, it's simple as that.

I tell you how to make money online with writing, and I have all of my other courses as a bonus.

for my OG silent wealth mastery when I made my first money, it was a mindset course.

but with bonuses I added my gym workout, how to install endless discipline, how to maximise your testosterone.

In short, make a list of bonuses that you can include with your product to increase its perceived value.

I did this by listing what i could add THEN I wrote a course about it.

Pricing

Pricing is difficult.

There's no one-size-fits-all answer.

  • The same product with added bonuses and templates can cost even more.

Aside from that, I like to price most of my products at an "impulse buy" price.

I learned this from Justin Welsh.

The logic is that the price shouldn't be so high that your marketing has to compensate for it.

In other words, if you price your product at an "impulse buy," you don't need to have 100% optimized marketing.

Paired with a personal brand and nurturing your audience with content and emails, your marketing becomes less of a priority and you can sell authentically.

An impulse buy price, in my eyes, is under $225.

Often closer to $150 or $100.

I have mine at $97 a month because it doesn't go into 3 figures, but It's enough to keep the retards out.

But, as a beginner, I highly recommend that you start even lower.

You can always increase the price of your product, but you can rarely decrease.

If you start too high and aren't making as many sales as you want to, it's going to be hard to justify to your current customers why you lowered the price without having to refund them.

Start low and give yourself room to increase the price a few times.

Price increases are a "scarcity event."

They are as powerful as the launch itself.

Meaning, if done right with the launch strategy in this course, you will make the most money during your product launch and price increases while sustaining a baseline number of sales in between.

6) Risk Reversal

What will make the reader understand that there is very low risk from buying from you?

This comes down to a few things.

  • Payment options (split payments, financing, etc)
  • Money back guarantee (a bit generic)
  • Social proof throughout the page
  • Other proof that shows your offer WORKS, tell them the "unique mechanism", the step by step guide and HOW IT WILL WORK to make the buyer think "so that's how it works"
  • Your authority on social media and in the general eye (your content and value you provide outside of the product)
  • Guaranteeing the clear promise or else you receive X

You are attempting to reduce the perceived risk of investing in your product.

This goes beyond the traditional money-back guarantee, but does not eliminate the need for some form of a guarantee.

For products, you can get creative or just offer a money-back guarantee.

I personally don't offer refunds on my products, but I wouldn't recommend doing that as a beginner.

Just say "due to the nature of digital products, we don't offer refunds"

they'll understand it, I was taught that by a $70K/Month copywriter.

What's Copywriting?

It doesn't matter whether you're a complete beginner or an intermediate Copywriter; you need to keep in mind what Copywriting is at its very core:

Let's start with what it is not:

  • Copywriting is NOT content writing.

We're not interested in writing articles, blogs, or general content. We're not here to inform or to teach.

  • Copywriting is NOT Ghostwriting.

We're not here to boost our 'vanity metrics' such as likes, reposts, or follower count. We're not here to write books on someone else's behalf.

  • Copywriting is NOT search engine optimization (SEO.)

We're not interested in keywords or how well our page ranks on Google. Usually our landing pages aren't meant to be public.

  • Copywriting is NOT publicity.

We couldn't care less about slogans or design.

  • Copywriting is NOT Corporate wishy-washy.

We do not want to sound like a company. Or worse, an HR department...

  • Copywriting is NOT about writing product pages.

Yeah, turns out writing product descriptions doesn't make you a Copywriter.

...So what is Copywriting then?

Copywriting is:

  • The tool...
  • The language...
  • The attitude...
  • The purpose...
  • The belief system...
  • The transfer of certainty...

...Of human beings who love to help other human beings and be paid to do so.

Copywriting is at its core a personal relationship between an author and a reader. This is why we call sales letters... letters.

A letter is a piece of paper you write on and send to someone else. It's a communication between 1 author and 1 reader.

What I'm going to say right now might sound obvious or logical, but it is something Copywriters miss: You are not writing to yourself, other Copywriters, or your Copy Chief.

The truth about quality Copy is that it talks to its true reader. Meaning that it doesn't care about RULES, STRUCTURES, BIG IDEAS, THE 4 US, NESB, AIDA. Or any other element of Copy.

Quality Copy is whatever makes the true reader care and act.

Now don't get me wrong, you NEED to know these rules by heart. And apply them. Because 99% of the time they're correct. You cannot break the rules before knowing them.

BUT... applying the rules to please your fellow Copywriter is a stupid idea. And when you have less than 3-4 years of experience writing Copy daily... That's what you do 99% of the time!

Copy is a tool. It is a means to an end: making someone act. It serves a distinct purpose.

But it's not just a tool.

Copy is a language. If you don't learn it, you can't speak it. It follows its own codes and rules.

But it's not just a language.

Copy is an attitude. It reflects your love of knowledge and people. It's for weird, unique, 'out-of-the-box' thinkers who love to study human beings. It's designed for both manipulators and empaths.

But it's not just an attitude.

It's a purpose. Oh, the joys of writing a 'winner!' Knowing that hundreds, if not thousands of people got their credit cards out just because of a few words you wrote. Copywriting is about finding the truth.

But it's not just a purpose.

It's a belief system. It has its own definition of right and wrong, correct and false. You must first learn what's right and what's wrong according to this system before you can write effective Copy.

It is not just a belief system...

It's a transfer of certainty. You must make them absolutely certain of the quality of your product, and its ability to solve their problem and help them achieve their dream.

It's not just a transfer of certainty...

...It's the alliance of everything all at once.

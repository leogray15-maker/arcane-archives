A personal brand is not a "business..." it is a lead generation and leverage system.

The nature of social media relies on individual experiences to grow and gain authority. Remember the Social Matrix?

In short, personal branding is a long-term game. Your personal brand is a lead generation mechanism like no other. You are nurturing potential customers long-term (potentially for the rest of your life if you enjoy the work that you don't want to outsource.)

Direct response marketing is a short-term immediate selling game (that gets your loyal fans to make a purchase).

This is very difficult to replicate with cold emails, cold DMs, etc etc. NOBODY CAN REPLICATE YOU.

People do not choose to follow your cold emails or DMs.

Building a personal brand will have people wanting to work with you whether you are good at selling or not. Now imagine when you ARE good at selling. Double whammy.

Bigger Is Better For Brands

There is one common theme for personal brands that nobody seems to take into account.

Everyone is building a BRAND rather than an agency, coaching, or freelancing business.

You don't see any agency accounts on social media trying to land clients. There are some, but who wants to follow a business account that just tries to sell you the whole time?

You see individuals on social media that use their experience to land clients for their agency (or coaching, freelancing, ecom store, or product sales.

Why is bigger better? MORE LEVERAGE & NOT PUTTING YOURSELF IN A BOX.

As Naval said:

"Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication (code and media)."

A personal brand can cover many of those bases. Primarily, "people."

That is, building an audience by producing content.

Then, creating digital products or SaaS based around your interests, things you talk about, and problems you solve. (code and media)

As Jack Butcher said:

"Build distribution, then build whatever you want."

The cool thing about this is that your personal brand can pivot and sell anything in the Big 3:

  1. Health
  1. Wealth
  1. Relationships

If you end up hating fitness, sweet - you can slightly rebrand to something that interests you without destroying your entire business.

If you end up transitioning out of freelancing or client work (like I did) - you can slightly rebrand as well.

Same goes for everything.

We are building a personal brand for cash flow and fulfilling work, not to sell it like a business. This should give you the opportunity to pursue your interests at any time. Then, if you want to fuel a 'sellable' business down the road with your personal brand's cash flow, easy peasy.

People care about the person behind the ideas, not the ideas.

Your job is to:

  • Grow via posting about your experiences in an engageable way
    • See: Social Matrix, Master Swipe File, and most of the content in the Personal Branding, Content & Writing, and Audience Building sections
  • Get in the habit of bettering yourself and consuming GOOD content
    • Set aside time for idea generation (listening to podcasts, reading books, getting content from OUTSIDE of social media so you aren't posting the same things as others)
  • Give yourself time.
    • Money loves speed, but socials love experience.
    • The information, money, trial & error, and life experiences in general compound over time into an incredible personal brand. This may sound silly, but you will only understand this through experience.

Example: I wrote an ebook called The Path To Power at the start of my X journey. My later brand Modern Mastery was THE EXACT SAME PHILOSOPHY. I'm just much better at getting my message across now.

"Play iterable games." as Naval said.

Hit publish on the things in life / business that will make you look back 1 year from now and cringe at your work.

You Must Be "Interesting"

Interesting does not mean traveling the world, being surrounded by women, and living in mansions. Sure, this is interesting and does well, but is unrealistic to most.

If you have the time, I would highly recommend studying mimetics - why humans desire specific things.

In short - people hop on the bandwagon. If you are enjoying something and sound like you have experience with it, they will want to enjoy it too.

This is why it is important to be confident in what you post + have strong convictions. Everyone is second-guessing themselves every second of the day. They will follow your steps if you are confident in your life choices.

(And this will bring them the most results, even if they fail. At alteast they failed fast in one direction).

The thing with conviction - what you believe now isn't what you will believe down the road. This is fine. Your content, audience, and self will evolve the longer you are in the game. Personal development = business development.

How to be "interesting:"

  • Do things that the masses don't do, this can be as simple as:
    • Going to the gym
    • Skipping breakfast (lol)
    • Drinking a specific kind of water (like San Pellegrino, a meme on Twitter)
    • Building a business
    • Literally anything that isn't mediocre.

Take note of ALL of the life choices you make and which ones are different than what others do.

I'm serious. What are the weird things that you do?

Do you eat Brazil nuts like Dr. Lagakos? Do you do your own kind of meditation (can you give that kind of meditation a name)? Do you make a smoothie everyday (like my enlightenment elixir)?

Take some time to think about this. This concept started to register with me by studying Matt Ogus' old videos. He used to have his Ogus Cake (an oatmeal cake) and Ogus Salad (a Chipotle bowl thrown on top of a bag of spinach).

The longer you are in the game, the better. You will start making more mental connections. Your quality of life will increase. Your skillset will improve. Online business = personal development in the creator economy.

Putting Off The Right Energy

This is a weird phenomenon, but people can sense your energy in posts and are attracted to it.

This does not mean you have to be extremely energetic and hyped up all the time - you simply have to be consistent with how you speak.

For myself, I aim for calm and insightful - kind of like a philosopher - because one of my goals in life is to be able to articulate my points like a philosopher. I want to achieve depth and impact.

This is incredibly difficult, if not impossible, to teach.

Study how accounts speak on all social platforms. The accounts that YOU vibe with the most. These will not be the same accounts other's like.

Doing this will turn you into an amalgamation of the top players in their space.

Creating a mood board for your brand has proven helpful in doing this well:

  • Find 1-2 scenic images on Google, Unsplash, or Pexels that represent your "vibe." (Think of your end goal - what environment do you want to live in? What environment does your audience want to live in when imagining their future?)
    • Examples:
    • A cabin in a forest with dark tones (lumberjack vibe)
    • A beach with palm trees (adventurous and serene vibes)
    • Skyscrapers and cities (I use this - because it represents structure and "big city living")
  • Find 1-2 images of a human that shows your "vibe" - like a customer avatar for yourself. If you want to choose an image from the accounts you want to emulate, that makes it easier. Be sure it matches the vibe you put off in real life.
    • Examples:
    • A stoic philosopher for deep thoughts and insights
    • A bronze age bodybuilder for "the good life"
    • A businessman in a suit near a rooftop pool

Start thinking about what imagery attracts YOUR attention.

  • Who do you follow on social media?
  • What kind of lifestyle do they promote and what does it look like?

In essence, you are painting a picture of the lifestyle that YOU and your audience want to achieve. YOUR VISION.

Broad Catch, Specific Sale

With a personal brand, you are defining the vision, "why," and mission of the company.

In this case, YOU are the company. YOU are the niche.

You need to understand yourself to be able to effectively communicate your vision for others.

Your brand is what pulls in loyal followers that join in and spread your message.

Direct response marketing is targeting one specific persona with one specific offer that solves one of their burning problems via emotional leverage.

Direct response is best used:

  • In paid advertising
  • In promotional posts/content
  • On your landing page
  • In your sales emails
  • Offer creation
  • Cold outreach

Direct response = sales. You are trying to get an immediate action/conversion out of someone.

General marketing / Branding is what a wide range of people are attracted to. This is best used to gain loyal and fanatic followers (refer back to the Social Matrix to understand this deeper.

Think of it like this - your brand is for showing them the lifestyle you are helping them work towards - your sales are for giving them the tools to do that.

This is also an introduction to marketing angles and positioning.

I.E. If your brand promotes financial freedom - you can sell a product/service on web design, philosophy, marketing, writing, or anything else that can be tied to financial freedom.

What you help them accomplish + what you've done and can teach that will help them get there (even if it's only a small step towards their desired end goal).

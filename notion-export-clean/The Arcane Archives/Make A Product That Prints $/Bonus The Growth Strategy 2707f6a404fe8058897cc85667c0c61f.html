Traffic Mechanisms – How To Get Your Writing Shared

When you understand how these allow you to grow, you can get creative with how you get your content shared.

These are in no specific order.

Test them all and see what works best.

You are hopefully in this for at least a few years (because that’s how much time anything new takes to see some traction and success (this doesn’t mean you won’t see success along the way)).

These are THE ONLY WAY TO GROW AT ANY TIME ON ANY PLATFORM WITHOUT RELYING ON THE ALGORITHM.

If these aren’t a normal part of your day, along with writing content, you need to change your daily routine.

1) Replies

This is the most obvious one that everyone does.

If you reply to somebody else’s posts, and readers go into the comments, they have a chance of seeing your reply.

If your reply is good, they may click on your profile, read your content, and follow you.

There are a few things here that people miss:

  • Boring content doesn’t make people read the replies. I see people reply to boring posts all day and get a few impressions on their replies. Be conscious of what content you are replying to. If the creator’s post doesn’t spark discussion, few people will actually go into the replies of that post, and your reply won’t get seen.
  • Boring replies don’t make people click on your profile. Stop repeating what was said in the first post or saying things like “Completely agree” or some boring compliment that doesn’t add a shred of value. Talk about yourself here. Tell people what you’ve experienced in your life relating to whatever the creator said in the content you are replying to. Use aspects of your life’s story to add value to the post. Talking about yourself leads to the most profile clicks.
  • Small accounts get fewer impressions overall. Reply to small accounts, medium accounts, and large accounts. Small accounts are for building your tribe, not gaining new followers. Large accounts are for making your name known in the space and gaining followers.

It's not just spamming, "great post!" or "I completely agree."

I talk about my experience with the topic and give a bit of practical advice that people can implement. Then, I mentioned that it happens "maybe 1 out of 500 emails."

People will see that and wonder, "Who is this guy who's getting 500 cold emails?"

When you talk about yourself, people are curious to click on your profile.

  • People can relate
  • People want to know more about what you do
  • People appreciate a comment section that isn't filled with bots

You provide value by talking about what you've experienced on the topic you are commenting about.

2) Quote Posts

Quote posts are when someone reposts your content with their own commentary. This can be on X or if someone shares your post to their Instagram story with their own commentary.

On X, this can work very well if you know how to write persuasive content.

If you quote post someone with valuable advice or something interesting, the original creator may repost you to their audience. This means you get more eyes on your content, which increases the potential for people to click on your profile and follow you.

Be wary here; don’t aim too high with this one like you would with replies.

Bigger creators have so many notifications that they will probably miss your post.

I don't prioritize quote posts too much, but if you have something to say and want it to be in front of your audience rather than in the replies, go for it.

I would personally focus on replies because it still gets shown to your audience and the creator can still repost them or share them to their story.

He quote posted a viral post with a long, in-depth analysis on a very important topic.

If you are going to quote post, I'd recommend taking a lot of time to think out your argument or post around a topic that you know will do well.

3) Reposts

Your content has to be good enough and relatable enough for people to want to share it on their feeds.

Always think this through before hitting post.

Will people share it? Why should they share it? Do they identify with anything I am saying? Is it polarizing enough to spark discussion and get comments?

4) Manual DMs

This one is more difficult and slow, but it works for networking and getting big accounts to share your posts (if you’re a normal person who isn’t a LinkedIn corporate robot in their messages).

Nobody wants to receive a message like, “Hey! Great to connect! I love your stuff! We should have a virtual coffee and talk about the complexities of zapier automations to enhance the overall experience of your loyal email subscribers so that you can build an incredible digital presence!!!!”

You can make friends online to the point where they are happy just to share whatever you send them.

Sending 1 DM may get 1 impression on your posts, but the potential for them to share those posts is higher if you have a genuine conversation.

If the account has a lot of followers, and they share… boom, now you have a large audience seeing your posts.

How To Control Your Growth On Any Platform

If you are smart, you can use any of the traffic mechanisms we discussed in creative ways to control your growth.

Those are the only tools you can use to grow if you don’t want to be a slave to the algorithm.

1) Short Form To Long Form

Short-form platforms like X, Instagram, and TikTok are inherently more easy to grow on than something like YouTube or a podcast.

You can write more content that gets shared with the traffic mechanisms we discussed.

I couldn’t grow on YouTube for the life of me, but I knew how to control my growth on X.

So, I posted to both and funneled my audience from X to YouTube with time.

You don’t have to do anything that I’m saying here, but this route makes sense, considering everyone wants to be a YouTuber nowadays but YouTube is difficult to grow on from scratch.

You’re probably going to have to take a smarter path, and this is one of them.

In short: control your growth on a platform like X, then send people to an algorithm-based platform like YouTube.

This is why the 3 Point Content Ecosystem from earlier is important.

2) Curation, Quotes, & Remixing

DJs and producers grow by remixing popular songs so they can “steal” the traffic from that popular song.

Sometimes, they post reels on Instagram with their remix, and the original artist (usually with a lot of followers and clout) shares them on their page or story.

This was a similar case to when I was doing digital art.

I could remix someone else’s art, tag them, and possibly get shared.

The same is true for writers.

You can:

  • Quote them in a thread or newsletter. Use their ideas in your longer posts to illustrate a point. Then DM them the post saying that you mentioned them. They may repost it without you asking.
  • Commentate on their content. Similar to how YouTubers make reaction videos to leverage the traffic that the larger creator is getting. Make a post with their ideas, give your commentary, and tag them.
  • Get people on a podcast. People love getting on podcasts. This is how you can get exposed to a larger creator’s audience. They will share the podcast to have their audience watch or listen.
  • Talk about their ideas and credit them. A simple post where you say “The best idea I’ve seen this week:” then go on to explain how it impacted you and credit the original author.

Why is this important?

Because bigger accounts have audiences.

AKA, that’s the game you are playing.

You are trying to get your content shared by people with larger audiences by getting your writing in front of them in the replies, in your content by tagging them, and in the DMs by befriending them.

Another way of doing this is "leveraging authority."

This is when you write a post about the ideas of someone famous or worth talking about. Someone that a lot of people know.

>> Here's the link to this post if you want to read it.

>> Here's the link to the post if you want to read it.

They probably won't repost the thread, but if you can get it shared by a few people by whatever means you can, it will do fairly well.

Remember, this works for any social media platform. You just have to format the content to fit that platform.

As an example, people do this on YouTube all the time. They create videos about other big YouTubers so that their audience watches it and you show up in the algorithm.

If you write a Twitter/X thread, you can turn all of those posts into a carousel on Instagram or LinkedIn.

You can also do this for people who are popular on social media. When you tag them, they are more likely to engage because it adds to their social proof.

Taylin took an idea from Justin Welsh (who is fairly popular on X and LinkedIn with a large audience) and wrote a thread/carousel about the idea.

He tagged Justin Welsh so he would see it.

Justin is inclined to engage with this because it adds authority and social proof to what he teaches. If he shares it, his followers will respect him more.

On top of that, Taylin will also gain followers from Justin's audience.

Win win.

4) Masterminds

Harsh truth:

Every creator you know is in a group chat with other creators.

They talk strategy and have fun building businesses together.

They also share each other’s posts and help them grow.

They use the traffic mechanisms to create a strategy they can bake into their routine.

Some groups comment on each other’s posts each morning.

Others repost the posts that they share in the group.

Most of all, they form a tribe and start to identify as a part of a group.

You can notice that there are tribes of 5-10 creators that all talk to each other online. This is why.

  • They comment on each other's posts everyday
  • They share each other's posts occasionally to help with growth
  • They make it known that they are an online friend group building together

If you are a beginner with 500 followers and create a tribe of people 5-10 people with 500-1000 followers, then you don't only have 500 followers, you have 2,500 to 10,000 followers in your tribe.

Yes, you will have to DM people, make friends, and let these relationships form almost naturally.

  • Create a Twitter list with those people (go to lists on the left sidebar in Twitter)
  • Engage with them every day to start building the online friendship
  • Add each other to some kind of group chat on Telegram, Whatsapp, or wherever you please

(No, we will not create a separate channel for this in Circle, this is on you. We want you to be independent of us and do the things that lead to growth rather than us giving it to you. Do the work.)

Even then, you can still use other strategies to monetize as you grow.

You don't have to be in a group for this to work. You can just have individual friends that you talk to often to see how they are doing and talk about your interests. Then, when you have a post that you think will do well, you can send it to them and ask them to send you one in return to share.

Paid Growth For Those Who Want The Info

This one gets touchy.

People who are “super authentic” and moral and noble (who also have zero experience with social media) love to say how authentic and moral they are for not using paid growth strategies.

I’ve observed these people over the years, and 4 years down the road they’ve either not grown at all or have lost followers.

People don’t like bitter complainers who stop focusing on providing value and pushing a more holistic worldview.

All you do by complaining is attract an audience of trolls. You feel like you’re making progress, but when you sell something, they will turn on you. You don’t want that audience.

Now, I’m not talking about running ads to gain followers. That’s the worst thing you could do. Do not do it. I repeat, do not do it.

I’m also not talking about paying for Instagram shoutouts, where you pay a large theme account to post your content and shout at people to follow you.

Those are the accounts that have 500K followers with zero engagement.

I’m talking about a truly authentic post and using a little money to boost your results.

Good content is a prerequisite.

The paid growth I’m talking about is paying larger accounts that you resonate with to spend their time on one of the traffic mechanisms from above.

Large accounts are busy and probably won’t do this unless you pay them to pay attention to you. This isn’t unfair. It’s just reality. You don’t understand what they are building that occupies their attention.

You can ask them to repost you.

On X, this is as simple as having them share your post.

On Instagram, this is having them share your post to their story.

Do not have them say anything or tag you.

Let the readers see your content and follow you if they want to.

You can ask them to reply to you.

This works well on something like X when the platform starts to favor comments.

This is often easier on the creator you are paying because they aren’t sharing something to their feed.

You can really ask them to do anything.

DM them, ask if they offer help to grow, take the conversation elsewhere, and negotiate a deal.

In my eyes, this is a far better approach than paid ads or anything else to monetize your products or services.

Build your audience with authentic shares, let people follow you because they like your content, and then promote your offer to your audience as it grows.

If you think it’s fine for advertisers to promote a product randomly in your face, then I don’t see why you shouldn’t think it’s fine for creators to accept money to share content (that you’ll probably like) without asking you to buy anything.

So, let’s start with some initial beliefs:

1) Paying For Shares Is Authentic

Objection 1: people should just find you online and follow you.

How do they find you?

2 ways, people sharing your posts and the algorithm.

They find you online because a POST was put in front of your face.

Lesson: big accounts are busy. They probably aren’t reposting people like a consumer would. You have to pay for their time and attention. It’s a reality you have to face.

If I pay someone who would have shared my content already but didn’t have the time, is it really inauthentic? Or am I just playing the smart game?

Objection 2: I feel weird that posts are paid to be retweeted and show up on my feed.

So you’re okay with paid ads that give zero value and sell you immediately, but you’re not okay with a piece of life-changing content that you hadn’t been exposed to before?

Do you just want to stay in the bubble of people you choose to follow and never be exposed to new ideas?

Why are you on social media then?

Lesson: paying for shares is a way to get your content in front of a larger audience without relying on the algorithm that keeps most beginners from ever advancing.

2) If Your Content Sucks This Won’t Work

Paying for shares is not a quick fix to your skill set, it is an amplifier.

Objection 1: paid reposts don’t work!

No, my friend, your content just isn’t good.

Objection 2: paid content attracts dead followers who just unfollow you!

Again, your content isn’t confident, polarizing, or valuable.

If you can’t keep an audience that follows you by the only way they would follow you anyway (a post showing up on their feed) then something is wrong. It’s not them, it’s you.

Lesson: it helps to get more eyes on your content so you know how to improve.

If it doesn’t work, review writing advice and practice more.

3) Return On Investment

Most people who don’t like the thought of paid growth don’t understand ROI.

People are okay with wasting money on ads because they are selling a product that can earn more than they spend on ads.

Paying for shares on social media is arguably more powerful than paying for ads because:

  • You KEEP the audience that follows you. They probably won’t buy from you immediately, but may 3-6 months down the road from your content, lead magnet, or newsletter.
  • They follow you because they like you and your content (you aren’t having people retweet your promotions, you’re having them retweet content.

Lesson: you should either have a product to sell so you can make more than you spend. And if you don’t make more than you spend, your product/service isn’t broad or good enough.

If you don’t have a product, you must be okay with investing money without an immediate return, but it will pay off long term.

How To Invest In Growth

How do you choose an account to reach out to?

First, there’s no magic marketplace where people are just selling reposts. If there are, avoid it.

The only place you can get true recommendations is from ghostwriters or social media managers who have a list of creators they pay. You can ask if they know someone selling reposts or shares. Yes, this is how social media managers grow accounts for their clients in a way that actually works. Shoutouts and paid follower ads don't work 99% of the time.

You will have to reach out to people you find on social media and send them a message like this:

Hey, do you help with growth at all? I’ve been writing content for a bit and want to invest in some help. If so, what do you offer? I’m happy to talk about this somewhere else if you want.

I wouldn't directly mention paid reposts or comments at the start.

Start the conversation and then make your offer.

My guidelines:

  • Reach out to personal brands with 20K-80K followers (don’t go too big).
  • Be willing to pay more for quality ($250 to $2000 for 30 reposts).
  • Make sure the account has decent engagement on their posts.
  • Make sure you want the audience that they have.
  • You can reach out to large anonymous accounts, just be sure that they have some personality and aren’t posting shallow basic content that gets LOADS of engagement.

For each platform, here’s what you’re asking for:

X / Twitter

Ask for packs of 10-30 comments or reposts. Preferably reposts.

LinkedIn

Ask for packs of 10-30 comments, not reposts. Comments are best.

YouTube

I haven’t done this, but I’ve heard you can pay people to add your video as the “watch next” to someone’s high-performing video.

You will have to reach out to them on a different platform since YT doesn’t have DMs.

Newsletter

Like newsletter sponsorships, ask people to plug your newsletter in their own.

Instagram

Ask for packs of 10-30 story shares, not shoutouts.

We want organic followers.

Meaning, we want them to see our post and follow us because of our post. Not someone shouting you out and telling them to “follow this guy.”

So, when you share your post with someone on Instagram, tell them to share it to their story. Don’t tag you. Don’t shout you out. Say nothing unless it’s commenting on the post.

Making The Most Of Paid Growth

You will gain followers if your content is good and you get your content shared.

But, with that influx of followers, you want to send them somewhere while they are “hot” and newly introduced to you.

At a minimum, you should be:

  • Writing 1-2 threads, long-form posts, or carousels a week (prioritize these for paid reposts for the most followers)
  • Plug a lead magnet or product under those threads to get an ROI on your investment
  • Plug your lead magnet or product at least once a day under your posts (in the comments) or in your stories on IG

Aside from that, the posts you have shared by the people you pay should be your best ideas.

Rewrite and send posts that have continuously done well for you in the past to amplify how many followers you get from them.

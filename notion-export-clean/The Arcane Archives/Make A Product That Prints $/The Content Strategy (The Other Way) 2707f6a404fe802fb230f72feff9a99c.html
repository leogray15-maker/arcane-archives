The Content Strategy (The Other Way)

there's 2 different ways of growing.

one way is to do the "learn and teach" method, it's a good system.

the other way is just to do it like a professional.

honestly, I prefer learn and teach because it's just simple, works, and benefits me and the reader.

but here... if you want a different approach, I got this from the $225 course.

If you've studied content at all, you understand how complex it can get.

For the sake of building authority in one topic and getting customers for your product, things get quite simple.

In short, your job is to write content (and get eyes on it with what we talk about in the next section) that:

  • Handles the objections of potential customers.
  • Makes them aware of pain points that you product can solve (without pitching your product, just make them aware).
  • Gives them actionable insights and advice – they probably won't implement them because they aren't putting money on the line, but they will see you as an authority and look for a product to buy from you.
  • Introduce them to the importance of what you have to offer and how it can change their life.
  • Introduce them to the opportunities your product topic can introduce them to – like how web design can help them freelance, build a portfolio site, get a better job, or just add to their skill stack.

REMEMBER, your product is the single tool to "funnel" them into your product.

your content has 2 purposes :

  • Build authority by giving them education and steps they didn't know before (become a leader in their eyes)
  • Give them the "opportunity" to buy your product and GO into that transformation that it gives.

remember, selling is not a bad thing... even though a lot of people think it is.

THE SINGLE most important thing in sales is not tactics, it's belief in your product.

if you ACTUALLY BELIEVE that it changes lives, you will feel like it's A DUTY to sell it.

but anyway, back to the content...

You're allowed to talk about other interests of yours.

That's actually recommended so you don't look like a glorified search engine.

You need to be a leader that actually looks like a human, not a chatgpt account.

A human who has interests AND is smart and a leader figure.

Just how I talk about cigars, nature, and women sometimes...

I'm a pretty simple man, but I'm actually seen as a "person" and a "leader figure" not chatgpt giving advice.

Your personality is your USP when building an audience.

So, here's what you can start writing about:

  • Your extreme beliefs (like "nobody should drink alcohol"). You should provide a good argument as to why to change people's minds
  • “Damn I wish I wrote that” ideas from people you love reading and following. Turn them into your own.
  • Problems people face relating to your topics or interests.
  • Arguments to content you read. After you read a post you disagree with, write your own post arguing the other side.
  • Personal opinions on popular topics.
  • Interesting facts about the topic (Google them).
  • Your take on top performing content.
  • Lessons you are learning on your journey.
  • Advice to your past, present, or future self.
  • Harsh truths about achieving a goal.
  • Aspirational advice (telling people what to do and why they should do it).
  • Anything you want (as long as you hit on “What’s in it for me?" for the reader).

You can use those for any topic or interest, preferably angled in a way that helps people achieve the goal your product helps them achieve.

Now, when you get specific to content about your product, which you should incorporate at least 30% of the time you are writing content, here's what to do.

remember, it's a DUTY to sell if you believe your product will actually change lives.

that's why it's important to test the system and see if it actually works, just for belief.

1) Turn Your Marketing Into Content Ideas & Create Your Own Customers

Pretty simple, whatever your product topic/idea is, break that down into content ideas.

This will be easier once you have an outline for what will be included in the product.

With social content, you aren't trying to do all of this in one piece of content. You are trying to attract and nurture an audience over years and years to the point of becoming customers.

Nurturing them is like feeding your cat a slice of meat, and your course transformation is like giving them the full steak.

With an audience, we aren't trying to craft a hyper-specific piece of content that doesn't get reposted to the point of you not getting in front of an audience to grow.

Your job is to write content on:

  • The pain points your audience faces and how to solve them.
  • The importance of the skills, interests, or lifestyle you live.
  • The potential opportunities people can have from listening to you.

You may not resonate with them the first time around, and that's okay. The more consistent you are, the more people you attract, and the more people trust you enough to invest in your product.

Just give them value, act like a human, relate, and JUST GIVE ADVICE WHILE SEEMING LIKE A HUMAN LEADER.

2) Create A Swipe File Of High-Performing Posts & Accounts

For most people, learning how to emulate content is enough.

You really don't need any type of course to help you do anything.

They help, of course, but anyone can get any result they want in life with enough trial and error. Courses just help people skip a few of those trials and errors.

You see high-performing posts all the time.

You can probably write with the same structure that makes their content persuasive.

But, you view those posts as a consumer, not a creator.

You soak in the information in the post rather than studying why that post did well.

  • Anytime you see a post that is doing well, try to rewrite it in your own words with your own topics or ideas.
  • Save those posts to a notes document, folder on your computer, in a Notion page.
  • Break down each line of the post and question: "What made this do well? What made this catch attention? Why do people see this as valuable? What can I learn and implement from this post?

As time goes on, you will find accounts that consistently put out great ideas.

They have the writing style and perspective you want to adopt.

Bookmark their accounts and study their content on a weekly basis, minimum.

I guarantee you won't only find content ideas and how to write them, but potential ideas for your marketing, product, and sales page.

When you find these ideas, save them.

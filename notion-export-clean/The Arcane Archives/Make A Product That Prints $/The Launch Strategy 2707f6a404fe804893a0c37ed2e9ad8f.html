Nuances & Tactics To Pay Attention To

You do not want to waste a product launch.

This is when you are going to make the bulk of your money (until you increase the price or run a special with the same strategy).

You want to exhaust any and all firepower you have:

1) Reposts, shares, and comments from your network.

You want as many eyes as possible during this period.

2) Make sure you have scarcity baked into the promotion.

What is going to happen on the launch date?

Does enrollment close?

Does the price increase (so buyers now get early bird pricing)?

Do not just launch a product without having some form of benefit to those buying when it does launch.

You need scarcity to promote leading up to the launch over 3 weeks that pushes people over the edge to buy.

3) This becomes more powerful as your audience grows on all platforms.

It’s good to focus on one platform, but once you have your content flow down, your promotions transfer seamlessly.

promote where you can.

4) Trust the process.

Sales will be very slow in the first week.

They will pick up in the second.

They will go bonkers in the third.

You will question your sanity by how many sales you make in the last 2 days leading up to launch.

Stick this entire process out even if the first few promotions don’t bring much results.

All of this, of course, is also dependent on your audience size and how much traffic you can generate through people sharing your posts (through networking or paid means).

With 1000 followers, I could outearn someone with 50,000 if I paid for help from a larger account and understood how to build a network that shares my content.

Plan 3 Weeks Ahead (For Awareness & Authority Building)

Set a launch date at least 3 weeks ahead of when you will start promoting.

If you are writing about topics unrelated to your product to build your audience, that’s fine and encouraged, but we want to narrow our focus to the topic that your product is about for this 3-week period.

This will prime your audience's minds to be ready for the product when it drops.

This is where your brand builds the most authority.

Here’s a soft breakdown of the promotion strategy:

Week 1 - Soft Promotion In Your Long-Form Content & Introducing More Specific Short-Form Posts

During week 1, you want to mention that you are launching a product in your newsletter or other long-form.

You do this at the top and bottom of your newsletter.

I tend to promote after my introduction, and in the “P.S.” at the end of my newsletter.

You can pull straight from your product if you want to.

Now, under your short-form posts, you are going to plug the link to your newsletter that contains the promotion. Do this once a day under your top-performing post.

This will lead to more email subscribers (so you can reach them with harder promotions), or they will see your product and buy it there.

Week 2 - Hard Promotion In Your Long-Form Content & Plugging The Product Under Your Short-Form Posts

You are now in your second week of writing a newsletter and short content about your product.

Do the same thing as last week, but promote a bit harder in your newsletter.

Give a list of the problems your product solves and position your product as the solution.

This will lead to some sales depending on the size of your email list.

In your short-form content, instead of plugging your newsletter, you are going to plug the product and flex your scarcity mechanism. Do this once a day under your top-performing posts again.

Week 3 - Hard Promotion & Direct Promotion In your Newsletters & In Your Short-Form Content

Week 3 is when everything starts to pick up.

You are going to do another hard promotion in your newsletter.

Be sure to remind them of either a price increase, enrollment closing in X days, or whatever else you use as scarcity.

You are still going to promote the product under one of your top-performing posts each day.

For the final 3 days:

You will make a direct post to the timeline on social media, not under your content.

Like this promotion.

Do this every day for those 3 days.

On the last day:

You are going to send out a promotion email using a solid copywriting framework like PASTOR. The headline will be something like “today is the final day.”

Then, you are going to send out a short email reminding people that the price increases or enrollment closes 4-6 hours before it does.

So, 2 emails on the last day.

On social media, you are going to make a post where you count down the time left to enroll.

This is a normal promotion post, but then you add a reply every 1-2 hours that bumps it back up in the feed so more people see it.

In these replies you say something like “2 hours left.” You can leave it at that or add pictures of what’s inside the product or pictures of testimonials to help.

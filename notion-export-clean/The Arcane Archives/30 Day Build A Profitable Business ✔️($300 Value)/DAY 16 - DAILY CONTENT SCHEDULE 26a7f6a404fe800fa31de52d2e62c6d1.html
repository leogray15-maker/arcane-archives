DAY 16 - DAILY CONTENT SCHEDULE

Writing your first newsletter is a small feat.

So this is more of a "filler" day in the challenge, but I believe it will still be useful.

And if it's your first time writing something that long, it will be difficult.

Your brain hasn't formed the pathways (skills are neural pathways) that lead to the right ideas surfacing in your brain at the right time.

When I wrote my first newsletter, it felt horrible. I felt like I was just making up sentences to fill up a certain length. It would take me 5 minutes to even jot down the first word.

This is how it's supposed to be when you're learning something new.

Think of it as nature's filter for those who are serious about hitting their goals.

If you can get through beginner hell, you can do anything.

After your fifth or sixth newsletter, you will notice that it is much easier to write.

With that said, I want to go over my general posting schedule so you can see how all of my content fits into a few time blocks throughout my week.

Sunday

My week starts on Sunday (at least when it comes to content).

I don't like taking days off completely, but Sundays are a very chill workday. Even on vacation, my days are better when I wake up at the same time and do a bit of work I enjoy rather than going full degenerate.

I use 30-60 minutes in the morning to start outlining my newsletter for the week. That way, I have a place to put ideas when they come to mind throughout the week.

While outlining, I'll take a few ideas from the outline and start turning them into social posts. Usually 2 or 3 of them.

Writing social posts during this time is extremely helpful because you create compelling and fleshed-out ideas that you can use inside the newsletter.

Or, at least you have a few ways to articulate the ideas inside the newsletter.

After that, I post my YouTube video for that week. This takes about 30 minutes.

So, Sundays are for outlines, a few social posts, and posting on YouTube.

My workday is considered "complete" after that, but I usually do administrative and management stuff throughout the day. When I have larger projects I'm working on, I'll work longer. It all depends, but my baseline is a newsletter outline and posting the video.

Monday Through Friday

Throughout the weekdays:

  • I write my newsletter – this usually takes 30-60 minutes. I aim to complete one section at a time and sometimes finish before the week is over. Finishing one 500-word section in 30 minutes shouldn't be that difficult of a task if you already have an outline.
  • I film my YouTube video – One day out of the week, usually Tuesday, I exchange writing the newsletter for recording a video from last week's newsletter. I pull up my newsletter, put it on my laptop or phone with the camera in front of me. Read a section, attempt to recite it to the camera, mess up a lot, and read the best parts while looking down at my phone. My editor cuts out the bad parts and adds text screens or b-roll over the parts where I'm looking down at my phone to read.
  • I write social posts – after I stop my newsletter, I start searching for ideas that I've saved in my notes or from my idea museum.
  • I post to all platforms – I spend about 15 minutes copy/pasting the posts to various platforms.
  • I read and consume information – I go on about 2 walks a day and listen to YouTube videos, audiobooks, etc. I'll save ideas as they come to mind while on social media too.
  • I plug my newsletter on various platforms – I'll comment a link to a past newsletter under one of my posts for the day.

If you'd like, you can use another 30-60 minutes to engage with people on social media and reply to comments. We'll learn more about growth strategies soon.

In terms of personal branding and content creation, this is really my entire day. I have no idea why people say that content creation is long and grueling. If you are editing your own videos and reels and stuff like that, then yeah, I get it, but that's not really necessary if you are focused on writing.

If you are trying to have incredible editing and cinematics as a beginner, you're going to be working 16 hour days. Probably not worth it. If you want to start on YouTube, just start with the most simple video you can. Barely any editing aside from normal cuts.

If your videos don't do well, it's not the editing, it's the ideas and how they are articulated.

Saturday

Saturdays are also pretty easy for me.

I publish my newsletter.

I write posts.

I may start the next newsletter outline or other things of that nature.

Simple.

Caveats

This is my base routine.

It's the bread and butter of my entire business, both for my personal brand and my software company.

If I don't post content consistently, my distribution and traffic go down.

However, this isn't all you do as a personal brand. Content creation is just the base.

When you are building a digital product, that takes another few hours out of your day, but you only have to build it once, then you can go back to your base routine.

If you have a more intensive business like software, eCommerce, or a client-based business, then you will probably have to spend more time depending on your role in those.

This is why we focused on content at the start. Because it's the traffic source that fuels whatever business you pair it with. We needed to nail that down before we started talking about monetization.

Next in this challenge, we're going to talk about ~5 growth strategies you can use, because writing content may not lead to any kind of growth. I don't like trusting the algorithm.

After that, you will be able to generate views and traffic. That way, when we create our first product, you have a "faucet" that you can turn on or off when you want to make sales.

Keep writing your newsletter for this week.

Pick a day to post it on, doesn't really matter when.

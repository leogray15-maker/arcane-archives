DAY 20 - LEVERAGING OTHERS AUDIENCE/AUTHORITY.

I love to think of creators as DJs, but with ideas.

It helps a lot with overcoming the creative blocks that come with branding and content.

A great newsletter or YouTube video doesn't have to be anything new to be impactful.

Instead, you can mix multiple "songs" (or other content/ideas) into a long enough set that you play for a wedding. In other words, you take a bunch of ideas, string them together into a new narrative that will resonate with a new group of people, and many will love your work for that.

For individual "songs" (or social posts), you can remix other people's ideas with a different "sound" or "vibe." Like taking an old philosophical idea and talking about it from a new angle.

But since we're on the topic of social media growth, we can pull insights for that as well.

DJs and producers grow by remixing popular songs so they can “steal” the traffic from that popular song.

Sometimes, they post reels on Instagram with their remix, and the original artist (usually with a lot of followers and clout) shares them on their page or story.

This was a similar case to when I was doing digital art.

I could remix someone else’s art, tag them, and possibly get shared to their large audience.

The same is true for writers and creators of all types.

I mean just look at YouTube… how many people go viral just by talking about a popular book or creator?

"I tried Steve Jobs daily routine for 30 days, here's what happened."

"Atomic Habits Will Change Your Life"

"Ali Abdaal's YouTube Strategy Is Pure Genius"

Now, for social media platforms in specific, you can:

  • Quote them in a long post or newsletter. Use their ideas in your longer posts to illustrate a point. Then DM them the post saying that you mentioned them. They may repost it without you asking.
  • Commentate on their content. Similar to how YouTubers make reaction videos to leverage the traffic that the larger creator is getting. Make a post with their ideas, give your commentary, and tag them.
  • Get people on a podcast. People love getting on podcasts. This is how you can get exposed to a larger creator’s audience. They will share the podcast to have their audience watch or listen. (Pro tip: Many beginners should start a podcast not to get famous from it, but to network.)
  • Talk about their ideas and credit them. A simple post where you say “The best idea I’ve seen this week:” then go on to explain how it impacted you and credit the original author.

Why is this important?

Because bigger accounts have audiences.

That’s the game you are playing.

You are trying to get your content shared by people with larger audiences by getting your writing in front of them in the replies, in your content by tagging them, and in the DMs by befriending them.

Another way of doing this is "leveraging authority."

This is when you write a post about the ideas of someone famous or worth talking about. Someone that a lot of people know.

>> Here's the link to this post if you want to read it.

>> Here's the link to the post if you want to read it.

Both of the examples above are "threads," which we didn't discuss in this challenge because they are almost identical to long posts.

Since X and other platforms allow you to continue typing as a long post now, the need for threads (tweets strung together as a thread of replies) aren't that necessary.

If that doesn't make sense, think of them like Instagram carousels, but instead of being a carousel of images, it's a list of written tweets (you can look at the example links to see what I mean).

If you want to learn how to write them, I would recommend exactly what we did for both posts and newsletters. Use a prompt like this, then attempt to replicate it:

Do a comprehensive analysis on this X thread. The overall idea, how the sentences are structured, how each tweet within the thread is structured, and choice of words. Analyze why people engage with it, why it works so well, what psychological tactics are being used, and how I can replicate this style of social post step-by-step with my own ideas.

[paste the each part of the thread]

Now, back on topic, people of extremely high caliber probably won't repost the thread or even see it, but if you can get it shared by a few people by whatever means you can (like by the people you've built connections with by commenting, it will do fairly well.

Remember, this works for any social media platform. You just have to format the content to fit that platform.

As an example, people do this on YouTube all the time. They create videos about other big YouTubers so that their audience watches it, and you show up in the algorithm.

If you write a Twitter/X thread or long post, you can turn all of those ideas into a carousel on Instagram or LinkedIn.

You can also do this for people who are popular on social media. When you tag them, they are more likely to engage because it adds to their social proof.

Taylin took an idea from Justin Welsh (who is fairly popular on X and LinkedIn with a large audience) and wrote a thread/carousel about the idea.

He tagged Justin Welsh so he would see it.

Justin is inclined to engage with this because it adds authority and social proof to what he teaches. If he shares it, his followers will respect him more.

When you speak positively and accurately about another creator, it benefits them to share it, because they receive followers and status from it as well.

On top of that, Taylin will also gain followers from Justin's audience.

Win win.

A Very Effective Tactic For Threads Or Carousels

I’m assuming you follow 5-10 accounts that talk about similar things as you.

This is how you can get on their radar and have them share you.

Let’s say I want to make a post about writing.

I will search for those 5-10 accounts best tweets on writing.

Then, I will create a thread out of those tweets.

Here’s the hook I would write:

Most people suck at writing.

Their content flops. Their emails flop. Their landing pages flop. Their entire business flops because of this skill.

Here are 7 top tweets to become a better writer in 5 minutes:

Then, each tweet in the thread will look something like this.

The greatest skill is writing because:

1) It forces you to articulate your value

2) It is the foundation of all media

3) It can be repurposed into any other medium

4) Any other skill you acquire enhances it

5) It brings immense mental clarity

Learn to write.

From @thedankoe

Notice the "from @thedankoe" at the end… that is me showing how you add their posts to a thread and tag them.

Now, the easier way to do this might be to link the tweet and write your own summary in it as your own post. Screenshots would work too.

I found that tweet by going to my profile, clicking the search icon at the top, and searching for the term "writing."

Why is this important?

Well, you are (almost) guaranteed to get a lot of engagement because the tweets in the thread already did well, that’s why you are using them in the thread.

Second, the accounts you tag will see the notification and potentially share your post. You can DM the thread to them saying you mentioned them.

Bigger accounts know how much followers these types of threads bring in. They know if they engage with it that it will benefit them too, so they are more likely to.

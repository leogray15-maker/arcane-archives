I've gotten a lot of questions about The Content Pyramid lately, and when I think back to the first X course I bought — mapping out my content pillars and ideas was the first thing I did.

If you are struggling with idea generation for content, this article is for you.

Let's start with these questions:

What are you good at?

What can you talk to your friends for hours about?

What do you want to learn more about that will make you (and those around you) a better person?

This is what you will be creating content about, and preferably helping others accomplish in exchange for money.

Following The Content Pyramid will give you unlimited ideas for posts (and all other social posts and videos).

Pillars

Most personal brands, creators, coaches, or freelancers on social media have 2-5 content pillars. These are usually sub-markets.

For clarification, the main markets are:

  • Health
  • Wealth
  • Relationships

Sub-markets are branches off of those:

  • Health
    • Exercise
    • Nutrition
    • Mental health
  • Wealth
    • Business
    • Investing
    • Money management
  • Relationships
    • Dating
    • Marriage
    • Friendship

What are the sub-markets that you have the most experience in and want to pursue?

Don't overthink this too much - it doesn't really matter if it's classified as a sub-market or not. What matters is that you have a broad market to work down from for relevant content ideas.

Topics

Think of topics as something you discuss with your friends for a few minutes - or what you see in a YouTube video.

Topics are things that you can write a blog post on, create a video on, or just discuss (works for threads too).

On X - you don't really post about topics as there is too much to fit into the 280-character count.

We need to write down topics for the sake of creating content.

Examples for bodybuilding:

  • Weight training
  • Gaining muscle
  • Cutting

Examples for business:

  • Audience building
  • Freelancing
  • Marketing

You get the point - and you can probably see the connections with "niching down" with everything related to marketing that we've talked about here.

Content

You can see where we are going with this... now we break them further into content ideas.

These can be used in many ways:

  • Blog posts
  • YouTube videos
  • Posts/threads
  • Instagram posts/carousels

Pretty much the same thing as Topics, but a tad more refined.

The Secret Sauce

Now we need to turn these content ideas into high-engaging posts.

I've been using Tweet Hunter as the start of all of my content creation. You can plug any of your content pillars, topics, or favorite accounts and study all of their best posts. A post that does well is then turned into more content across all social media channels.

The BEST posts are relatable.

How do we make these pillars/topics/content relatable in a short and punchy way?

By understanding the benefits that they provide and the problems they solve.

Humans are emotional and selfish creatures... if something can help them, they will listen. If you pinpoint the problem they are having, they will listen.

  1. What was your former mindset on the problem?
  1. Why did you implement the advice?
  1. What results were you hoping to get?
  1. What problem of yours were you trying to solve?
  1. What did you struggle with?
  1. What were your biggest “aha” moments?
  1. What were your objections?
  1. What did you struggle with the most?
  1. What do you wish you had done differently?
  1. What are you going to do to prevent that problem from happening again?

Ideas For Unlimited Content Creation

Per The Content Pyramid - all of your posts, in essence, are just words that dance around your reader's emotions. They should always have a heavy focus on:

Transformations

  • Where they are now - how it feels to be there, common experiences
  • Where they want to be - how it feels to be there, why its better than where they are now

Exaggeration

  • Make the deep pain points sound painful
  • Use absolutes
    • Never, always, best, worst - no middle ground
    • Stand firm in your beliefs (but be open enough to change them if you are proven wrong)

Think about how the problem affects them from a health, wealth, or relationship standpoint.

  • Building a business allows people to work from anywhere, from their laptop, and do what they want with their time (benefits). It also allows them to avoid wage slavery, fire their shitty boss, and escape the life-sucking cubicle (exaggerated pain points).
  • Exaggerated pain points and benefits are key for one-liner posts or at the end of a longer post to tie everything together.

Start looking for this in good posts you see. Start forming mental connections about what makes a post do well.

Good Headlines Make For Good Posts

If a blog post is doing very well - that usually means it will do very well on X (this works for YouTube as well, not just blogs).

One-off headlines are great for short posts.

  • Example: If you want your content to stand out, learn how to break the rules.
  • I just found that on Medium. I could post it out right now and probably get 300+ likes. (Remember to not just copy things word for word - FOCUS ON THE IDEA YOU ARE TRYING TO CONVEY BEHIND THE POST. Be clear about it.)

Headlines make for great post topics.

  • Example: 9 signs a person is really happy with their life.
  • That is the first line of your post, then list out the 9 things.
  • These 9 points can also be turned into individual posts.

Once you get the hang of high-performing post structures, these will start to write themselves naturally.

Find a huge community blog like Medium, Lifehacker, or one in your niche. Bloggers will usually post their own articles to these platforms - pay attention to the ones that do well and use the headlines/content tactically.

Idea Generation Tactics

One of my favorite times of the day is my "Idea Generation Session." In other words, an intentional learning period where I:

  • Read articles on Medium or elsewhere related to my target audience and content pillars.
  • Watch YouTube videos of the same.
  • Read books or listen to audiobooks pertaining to my own interests or my audiences.

Keep a notepad, journal, or note-taking app next to you to jot down any idea that comes to your mind.

In short - set aside time for consuming content that already does well.

Another great way to do this - that we already touched on - is creating a 'list' on X of the accounts you aspire to be like, write like, and impact like. Spend some time reading over their posts to keep their writing style fresh in your mind.

Again, I'd like to give a special mention to Tweet Hunter for doing this all for you. (this is the first step in my creative process).

This is a great way to "force" yourself to have relaxation time. It calms your mind and starts to make you think for yourself again. X is notorious for polarizing views. Most other content is not.

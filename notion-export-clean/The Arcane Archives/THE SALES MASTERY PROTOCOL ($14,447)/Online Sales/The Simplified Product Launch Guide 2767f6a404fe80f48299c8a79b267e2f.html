The Simplified Product Launch Guide

Product launches should not be taken lightly. They have the potential to make you a lot of money. There are 2 ways to go about this:

  1. Simply not care and launch (has its benefits)
  1. Care a lot, prepare, and make a ton of money

The first is fine if you are a new creator with a few followers. It is a good thing to just say "fuck it" and launch your minimum viable product.

  • You can always turn the product into something else
  • You can always use that product as promotional firepower elsewhere
  • You can iterate on the product and launch a better version when you have more followers.

The second is clearly the best option - but I recommend doing it with a minimum of 5,000 followers.

  • You will have connections that can affiliate and blow up your launch posts
  • You can leverage their audiences and your own since they will be over 5,000
  • You will ACTUALLY have an audience that you can nurture and season with content before you launch.

Let's dive into the actual phases of launch.

Pre-Requisites

The product does not have to be complete - but you should have a general outline of the problem you are solving and how exactly you are solving that problem.

Seasoning & Pre-Hype

The great thing about a personal brand is that you can talk about whatever you want, but as we learned earlier - when it comes to sales - we need to get specific and authoritative.

1-2 weeks leading up to your launch you will be posting to start moving readers up the levels of customer awareness.

From unaware of their problem to aware of it and wanting to fix it. When you put your product in front of their phase everything will make sense, and they will buy if done right.

What Do You Post?

Answer the questions from your market research in the form of posts and threads. Threads are the ultimate authority builder.

  • If you can write 2-3 threads on the subject before you launch, that's amazing (these threads can be turned into emails, videos, articles, and multiple posts.)
  • You can turn sections from your product into threads fairly easily. Don't be afraid to give away too much value. Free value is forgotten and isn't acted on.

Show VS Tell

  • Show people what to do instead of telling them.
  • Tell example: Do market research, create a product, and sell it on X
  • Show example: Actually show them your market research process, how you create a product, and how you promote it on X
  • Have at least one post a day related to your product.
  • Have an occasional post (2-3 times a week) letting them know that you will be launching a product that solves X problems for X audience.

Preparation For Exposure

By now there should be at least 5-10 people that you can reach out to and let them know that you are preparing to launch a product. Offer to pay them for exposure if you want, but most of them will help out for free if they believe in you and your product.

If you do not have 5-10 people who can help bring you more exposure (and possibly affiliate for your launch) then you need to start now.

Assets You Should Prepare

  • Multiple launch emails hitting different levels of customer awareness
  • Multiple affiliate launch emails to leverage their email lists
  • Multiple promotional posts (test them all and double down on the copy / angles that bring the most sales)
  • Multiple affiliate launch posts
  • 1-3 launch threads (take information from the product and turn it into a thread, remember: tell don't show)
  • General communication with affiliates and others that are just helping support the launch. Consider putting them into a TG group together.

Take the time to plan this out to the best of your ability. Launches are where the most money is made and can build a LOT of authority for yourself when done organically.

This will be a point in your entrepreneurial career where you will gain many followers (because they are interested in what you are creating), and make a ton of cash.

Presale

Presale is optional.

It is also a great way to make sure your product gets done.

This is an example of the term I like to throw around, "tactical stress."

If you are procrastinating in creating your product and deciding on a launch date - start a presale.

This will force you to set a launch date and stone and get your minimum viable product out before then.

This will also let you know what areas need improvement based on the sales you get.

(Presales won't get a ton of sales, but they can get quite a few.)

Crucial Presale Points

  • Start your price low
    • Depending on your confidence in the launch, start at $17 - $47
    • This is heavily dependent on the actual offer and can go up to $497
  • As you promote, continuously mention the launch date and that the price will increase (scarcity).
  • Shamelessly promote more and more - up to 2 times a day - as you near launch date.

Launch Week

Launch week is simple.

PROMOTE. PROMOTE. PROMOTE.

Attack from all angles you can. Write different posts handling different objections and use as much social proof as humanly possible.

Every time you put out a post, ask your network to engage with it. They don't have to RT, just ask them to like and reply to it for social proof reasons.

Promote 1-2 times a day for the entire week. Make sure everyone in your audience knows that you launched a product.

It is also crucial to remind affiliates to promote. Either have them in a Telegram group or DM them individually and ask them if they need anything for their promotions.

Leverage their audience as much as you can.

Post Launch

If you plan to have this as a core offer in your value ladder - it doesn't stop there.

If this is a micro product that you don't really care about - you can skip to the second part of this section where we talk about repurposing the information.

Extending The Lifetime of Your Product

After launch, your job is to get creative with promotions to keep sales coming in steadily.

  • Having guest appearances to teach a subject (to leverage their audience and broaden who your product targets)
  • Adding on a bonus of your own or another product
  • Offering to pay for products you affiliate for (this works well for memberships because you get MRR)
  • Building out a follow-up product to sell to buyers.
  • Slowly increase the price by $10-$20 at a time when it feels right. This is usually every month or so but allows you to keep scarcity high.

Repurposing Dead Products

Eventually, your product will lose the hype, and that's okay, here's what you can do:

  • Launch another product, wait until it "dies" and then bundle them together for a cheaper price.
  • Use the dead product as a bonus for a new one.
  • Using them as incentives for video testimonials.
  • Using them as a very cheap tripwire so you can get more "buyers" and put them into buyers email sequences (segmentation.

This lesson is crucial - you should plan this launch out carefully and strategize with your network. If you have any questions about this, be sure to ask.

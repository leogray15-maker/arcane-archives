Step 1) Creating The Digital Product

My goal was to teach developers how to freelance with their web development knowledge.

I had bought many freelancing and web design courses in the past, so I knew it would sell. The idea was validated.

I could have been an affiliate for any one of them, but I figured I might as well make my own (since I had results and a different perspective) and take 100% of the profits.

Here's what I did to create the product in under a month:

  • Created a rough outline of the chapters I wanted in there
  • Went back through the courses I previously took to fill in the gaps
  • (Keep in mind this was for inspiration only, to make sure I was covering all bases in terms of topics I taught)
  • Wrote the ebook during my focused work blocks (it ended up being ~100 pages double spaced)
  • Brainstormed a title, subtitle, and cover image
  • Went through the course landing pages that I had taken before, wrote down the benefits and pain points I could use for my own landing page
  • Uploaded everything to Gumroad (no prelaunch or anything)
  • Started promoting (more on this later)
  • Built out my welcome and sales sequence for this product so I could sell on autopilot (with the Email Marketing Course)

If I had taken my time to market it correctly (via the Advanced Marketing course), I probably would have made much more, but I just went with it.

Step 2) Getting Traffic To The Offer

This is where many people go wrong... they want to make money without spending money. I wanted to grow a digital asset and sell my product, so I cut back my spending and started investing $500/month into my growth.

YOU NEED SEMI INTERESTED TRAFFIC (AND A GOOD OFFER) TO SELL YOUR PRODUCTS.

You cannot get these results at 300 followers if you are not willing to invest in paid traffic. (In this case, paid traffic = reposts).

I paid a big account that was already selling a web design product for 30 retweets. I figured if they were teaching people web design, I could teach them to freelance with that skill (eventually I went on to create my own web design product because it paired well with the freelance product).

I would send the account my posts daily, they would repost, I would gain followers and profile visits, and I would promote the product accordingly.

(I also had the link and a call to action in my bio - because I knew that many would look at my profile without following me... this got more sales than you would think)

It was that simple.

  • Create decent content
  • Give actionable advice according to the product you are selling
  • Promote the product
  • Get eyeballs on your profile (free = slow, paid = fast, nothing wrong with either)

You are selling and growing at the same time.

Step 3) Networking & Getting Affiliates

This happened by luck, but down the road (when I was closer to 1000 followers) I was trying to network with developer accounts.

I tried offering to pay one of the accounts, he was at 16K followers at the time and declined because he didn't think his reposts were worth the money.

Time went on and he realized that I was selling a freelancing product. He said, "oh man people ask me all the time how to freelance" so I offered to make him an affiliate.

He started promoting my product, bringing in an extra $1500 rev or so a month.

After my product had decent reviews and traction, I started looking for other affiliates.

Step 4) Creating Complimentary Products and Iterating

After some time, I realized I didn't want to be the web design guy, so I started to pivot in how I was selling my products.

First, I created a web design product (Websites That Work) and launched that. I sold that for a bit as a standalone product.

Eventually, I combined the 2 into the "Modern Money Bundle" and started to target those that wanted to learn a skill and learn to sell it.

This extended the "shelf life" of these products. I was able to continue pulling in consistent revenue because I tested different angles and created new offers out of things that were already made.

Once I hit 10K followers, I created my X course. Sold that on its own for a bit, then added it to the Modern Money Bundle.

I hope you are all starting to see how this is a long-term game of persistence and iteration.

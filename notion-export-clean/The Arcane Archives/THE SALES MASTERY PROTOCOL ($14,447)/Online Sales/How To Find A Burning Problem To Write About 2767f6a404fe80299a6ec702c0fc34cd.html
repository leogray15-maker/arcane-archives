How To Find A Burning Problem To Write About

We all know that selling is an emotional game. You have to toy with your potential customer's heartstrings (and for good reason, your product will help them avoid that feeling again).

Negative emotions sell more than positive. Both are useful, but we are providing solutions to a problem that creates those negative emotions.

The more specific you get, the better.

Like telling a story, you want to paint a picture of where their life is now (negative impact of the problem) and where their life will be (solution).

A great way to do this is by breaking it down and understanding the biological root of the problem.

Quite frankly, the potential customer thinks they have a certain problem - when in reality it is something else.

Here's what you should do.

Step 1: List Out Your Target’s Main Problems

Refer back to what you wrote down for your market research.

Take note of what they are struggling with the most and normal pain points within that niche.

Order them from most pressing to least pressing. Which causes them the most emotional pain?

All of these pain points will play a role in our marketing, but the main problem is the most important, and helps you understand your customer more.

Step 2: Discover The Biological Root

Emotions > Logic

Especially in marketing. People buy results. Results stem from greed and necessity.

Now, we are going to break down the #1 problem from above and break it down even further (people who are familiar with sales understand the power of this.)

Let’s pretend we are on a sales call here - I am pitching a Twitter follower on high-performance coaching (self-improvement/productivity stuff).

I get on the call, let him know how it is going to go, ask some questions, and get to this part of the call:

Tell me man, what are you struggling the most with right now? Whats preventing you from reaching X result?"

“Well, it seems like I’ve lost focus during my work and I have a LOT to get done. It is stressing me out.”

In your daily life, what is this costing you? Why does it matter that this work isn’t getting done?

“I’m not able to give the time and energy to my wife and kids at night. I don’t see an end to the work I’m doing.”

(We are getting somewhere here).

What do you think is causing this to happen right now?

“Answers the question”

Is this affecting other people in your life, how?

“Yes, my wife is getting tired of me, my kids are upset, and I haven’t seen my friends in months.”

(Ending up alone is a biological problem/fear.)

What will happen if you don’t solve this problem in 90 ish days?

“I may never talk to my friends again… and who knows, my wife could consider leaving me”

See what we did here? We broke down a surface-level problem of a lack of focus and turned it into something much much deeper, and much more pressing.

What you need to do, is take your #1 ranked problem and ask questions about it until you get to the root.

Your job is to make them realize what the biological root of the problem is. 99% percent of the time you don't explicitly say it.

Then, you can transition into pitching your solution.

This skill will come in handy when writing copy for your landing pages (or even your content to make them more potent).

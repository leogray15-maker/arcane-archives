Charlie Morgan On NESB Headlines

if you've watched charlie morgan recently, you'll see that he loves this idea of

(never heard of idea) (fastest path to end goal) in 2025 (now)

a very simple use of NESB in headlines, but highly effective.

it uses all of them :

  • new
  • easy
  • safe
  • big

NEW

dopamine transfer (never heard of before, new concept)

this is simply dopamine detoxing in a way with a little twist.

this comes down to the marketing lesson that there are NO "new ideas"

the one and only lesson i learnt from high school business is simply that "there are no new ideas, just repackaged ones"

I turnt "monk mode" into productive isolation. making it seem new.

if you've read the copy course you'd understand the idea of the lizard brain and how it cannot move on when it sees a "new" idea, remember... categorization is DEATH.

you can use this in headlines or ideas by simply "repackaging" something that you're teaching.

if you're talking about "monk mode", give it a spin like i did with productive isolation.

EASY

this ones simple.

you'll see how he blatantly says x is the easiest way to get rich in 2025.

get creative with this one.

something like "a 12 year old can become a millionaire using dopamine loading"

that's "easy"

once again, reference to the copy course, people are lazy and have most have an inferiority complex

that's why in my sales page i use :

"I know. I know, it seems like everyone can do it but you can't get a hold of it"

this sooths them and gives them a green light to click, purchase, whatever.

make them feel like a special snowflake, "you can do it!" is a shitty example, but you get it.

SAFE

"safe" here is conversed in the subtext, reference to the copy course again, it's simply the unspoken message.

easy and safe go hand in hand, figure this one out.

reference to the copy course again, take a look at the study in the safe section, it'll shift your perspective.

BIG

big/fast

it's big or fast, or better yet, BOTH.

charlie morgan blatantly uses "fastest" in this headline, easy to digest.

humans like quick results, that isn't nothing new.

a more advanced example that i've used before on an email subheading

"floodgates have opened"

this tells them that something QUICK is happening, and quick results are there.

get creative.

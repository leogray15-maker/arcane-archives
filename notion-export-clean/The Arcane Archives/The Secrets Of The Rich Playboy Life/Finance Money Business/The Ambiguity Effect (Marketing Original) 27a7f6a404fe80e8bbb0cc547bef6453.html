The Ambiguity Effect (Marketing Original)

boost engagement by 300% and sales by 38% with this simple note

The Effect

Humans hate things they don’t understand. That means they avoid options that aren’t clear.

Think of the last time you had a very ambiguous experience in life - e.g. you applied to 10 jobs and didn’t know if (any) will take you. Was it enjoyable?

The same cognitive bias exists in every human - we avoid options when they lack clarity (or are missing information).

  • According to research, Facebook engagement is 3X higher if a regular URL is used instead of a shortened one.
  • That is, you will get 300% less clicks if you use bit.ly/1234 versus yourwebsite.com.

The Snickers Example

Spoiler: Being Specific Sells.

A study A/B tested two displays in a grocery store:

  1. “Snickers Bars, Buy Them For Your Freezer”
  1. “Snickers Bars, Buy 18 For Your Freezer”
…Which one do you think did better?

The 2nd one increased sales by 38%.

2 Reasons Why It Worked

  1. Rule of Thumb - humans rely on mental shortcuts to make quick decisions.
    1. The word "them" leaves everything to their imagination - and humans hate that burden.
    1. The number "18" (completely arbitrary), gives them a frame of reference to start with.
  1. Anchoring and Adjustment - another cognitive bias where your initial idea is heavily influenced by the first piece of information you encounter.
    1. "18" places a high anchor to come down from.
    1. This is the same reason you buy more when it's labeled 10 for $10 instead of $1 each.

Actionable Advice

1. Information is Power

In pages comparing your product to competitors - provide all the information you can. Even if insignificant.

The extra info makes your offer seem safer and kills ambiguity.

2. Be More Specific

Show everything.

  • Specify the total cost before checkout
    • Amazon & Booking do this
    • Some may not do it to make it “look cheaper” - but it loses customers

(source)

  • Specify the delivery/waiting time.
    • One of Uber’s marketing breakthroughs was showing the time it takes for the taxi to arrive. The thing that bothered people was the uncertainty in not knowing when the taxi will come.

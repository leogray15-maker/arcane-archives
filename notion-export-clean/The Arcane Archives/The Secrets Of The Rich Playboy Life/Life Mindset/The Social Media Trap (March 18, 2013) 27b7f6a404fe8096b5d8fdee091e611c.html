The Social Media Trap (March 18, 2013)

WSP called it all the way back in 2013

Reading Time: 5 minutes.

WSPArchive: We are surprised by how well this post has aged. Social media has only skyrocketed in both its usage and detriment since the 10 years that this was originally posted.

Today, everybody spends their whole existence on social media. We don’t have to prove anything - simply look at everybody outside and notice their face buried down their phone.

Also - feel free to just ask acquaintances for their time spent on Instagram or any other social app. The numbers are absurd.

This is good for you - it means two things:

  • much weaker competition - how can someone spending 6hr distracted on social beat you?
  • High-distribution platforms (channels) to advertise your products on.

Now onto the 2013 archive:

Is everyone else doing it? It’s probably a bad idea.

A few emails have come in asking about Facebook, Twitter and increasing your net social capital on the Internet.

The solution? Delete.

If it’s not helping you get laid or not getting you paid. Stop.

There are very few people who receive clout from connecting on the internet via Facebook, let’s take a deeper look at the issues with having a profile that seem to come up continuously.

What If She Asks for My Facebook?

Get stronger game.

When a girl asks you for your Facebook she is either:

  1. looking at you as a long-term partner which means you’re not all that attractive to her.
  1. she wants to vet you out.

Trapped.

The only people who should be asking you for a Facebook account are friends of friends purely as a courtesy.

To which you should respond “Don’t have one”.

How Do I Keep In Contact With My Friends?

Yeah, okay bro. You got 200+ “friends” on the internet who really care about your recent trip to the burger joint and the upload of your green garden burger with “extra guac”.

Get a grip.

At the end of the day, the people near and dear to you are a single phone call away, a single email, or a single text message.

The rest? They do not matter.

If it’s not helping you get laid or not getting you paid. Stop.

How Do I Stay Up to Date on Events?

Get a feed if you must but at the end of the day you should be too busy to keep up with the Kardashians.

Events on Facebook are certainly not going to be filled to the brim with knockout dime pieces either, so best to hit the ground stop making excuses and find the right spots yourself.

WSPArchive: One of the best bang-for-buck social things you can do is curate and find events that have more high quality people.

The most dead-simple basic thing to do is to NOT go out at the start of the week - what kind of people would go out on a Monday?

But the real return comes from more complex setups - paid/exclusive/VIP events, private friends of friends invites, etc…

How Do I Stalk/Keep Up With the Cute Girls I Know?

This is another easy one:

Unless you’re having sex with her why do you need to keep up?

Go outside to the mall and you’ll find some cute girls immediately.

You don’t need to force social status to get girls, roll solo dolo and you’ll be fine.

How Do I Find Better Jobs?

Linked-in.

That’s the only legitimate network you should have an account in. Plus. The best jobs come from real-life connections, not online job boards.

In fact this is yet another reason to delete your Facebook account, the worst thing that could happen is one bad photo gets loaded up on the web. You get tagged. Game over to your career.

How Do I Prevent Guys and Girls From Wrecking My Social Value?

Again, another reason to hit delete.

If you hold with extreme confidence your lack of desire for Facebook and any sort of “look how cool my life is” interactions that is another level of confidence.

You should be autonomous.

Unwavering a group of “friends” should have zero impact on your net social worth.

People should be clamoring for your attention, you should not be clamoring for their approval.

The above should answer the majority of questions on why you should not be dealing with a webpage that spills out your life story.

An interesting man does not show all of his cards up front.

He does not need to inform the world on his life activities 24/7.

The roles should be reversed.

Both men and women, should want to dig into your life because they know you’re holding out on them.

WSPArchive: In that viewpoint, it makes sense to have a little social presence on e.g Instagram. The reasoning is to have a simple “landing page” of your life to a new “lead”. If you curate it well, it should spark more curiosity in the lead and get them to wonder even more what you’re holding out on them.

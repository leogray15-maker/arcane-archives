7 cheat codes to learn anything 230% faster:

Learning is the foundation of society.

Ironically, school never taught us how to learn.

Here are 7 ways you can start being a better learner today:

1. Saying it out loud

Studies show saying something out loud improves your retention of it.

As you’re taking notes on paper, say what you’re writing out loud.

As you’re studying a book, say what you’re reading out loud.

This will make the things you’re reading stick with you.

2. Drinking more water

Minor dehydration destroys your cognitive function.

This includes:

  • Memory
  • Learning
  • Critical thinking
  • Decision making

Aim to drink 2-4 liters a day.

Keep a water bottle near you so you don’t forget to drink enough.

3. Creating mnemonics

Using a sequence of words helps you remember things.

For example, to remember the EADGBE tuning of a guitar, try:

E lephants

A nd

D onkeys

G row

B ig

E ars

Create your own mnemonics when trying to remember information.

4. Writing instead of typing

We live in the age of technology.

But that doesn’t mean digital tools are always best.

Studies show writing is better than typing for:

  • Recall
  • Critical thinking
  • Information processing

Try taking notes using pen and paper.

5. Changing your approach

86 volunteers were asked to learn a computer-based skill in 2 separate sessions.

Group A learned the task exactly the same way in both sessions.

Group B learned the task slightly differently in the second session.

The result?

Group B was twice as fast and accurate as Group A.

Remember the 4 learning styles:

  • Visual
  • Auditory
  • Kinesthetic (touch)
  • Reading and writing

Changing your approach to a different style will help you learn faster.

6. Rewarding yourself for learning

Rewards release dopamine in your brain.

A reward after learning teaches your brain to crave learning.

Some potential rewards:

  • Netflix
  • Video games
  • A healthy snack

Making these a reward for work will make learning more enjoyable.

7. Using the “study, sleep, study” system

Follow this science-backed routine:

  • Study a subject for a few hours in the afternoon/evening
  • Sleep for 8 hours
  • Resume studying for a few hours in the morning

One experiment showed this system increased retention by 33%.

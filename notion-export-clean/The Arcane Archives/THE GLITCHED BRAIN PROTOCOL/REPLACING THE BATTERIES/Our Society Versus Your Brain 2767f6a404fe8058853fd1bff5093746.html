Modern society is turning your brain to mush. I mean, since it's mainly made of fat, it's already kinda mushy... but I'm talking mushy in a bad way.

Your brain wasn't made for this world. This is true regardless of whether you believe in evolution or creation. As Dr. Andrew Huberman says, "I wasn't consulted during the design phase."

The human brain evolved to cope with the world where we lived out in the open and had to struggle just to survive. Not for the world where we can sit at a desk all day long and stuff our faces with food.

The brain doesn't know how to effectively cope with what the modern world has to offer us. Smart people are taking advantage of this, and so, there are many things in modern society which are destroying our brains.

Here are 3 ways our society is mushifying your brain. I want to make you aware of these, because knowing is half the battle...

1) News

Ever heard the phrase "if it bleeds, it leads"? Ever notice how there's never any POSITIVE viral news?

It's all doom and gloom. We're all gonna die. Everything everywhere sucks 24/7/365.

Why is the news like this? Because positivity doesn't sell. Humans have something called a negativity bias, where we care more about negative events than positive events.

This means we care a lot more about bad news than good news. And so, as a result of this, all the news is bad. The news caters to - and takes advantage of - our negativity bias.

Reading negative news for a few minutes can cause negative moods for a few hours.

So if you have a habit of reading the news on your phone before you get out of bed? That's screwing up your day. It's a big reason why you feel so stressed before your day even begins.

You probably keep up with the news just to stay informed. But all you're doing is hurting yourself.

You don't need to check the news. If it's important, you'll hear about it. I promise. I've muted hundreds of people on Twitter and yet I still see people talking about the same thing every day.

2) Food

A lot of the food available to us today is terrible for us. Our diet is packed with sugar, simple carbohydrates, and nasty seed oils.

Even worse, these foods are intentionally engineered to be hyperpalatable. That means they're designed to be super tasty and super addictive. Want proof? Look up what's actually in Nutella.

Frequently eating over-processed junk food is normalized in our modern society. Next time Karen from Accounting brings a box of donuts to the office, ask yourself:

Why is it normal to eat a bunch of sugar early in the day? Also, why is it normal to have an energy crash around the middle of the day?

Turns out, those two things are related. I'm not saying breakfast itself is a bad thing... I'm just saying that eating a bunch of carbohydrates isn't a good way to start the day.

That's why we're having those midday energy crashes. Our blood sugar crashes... and so do we. To reduce this effect, have less carbs early in the day.

3) Social Media

This is pretty ironic coming from someone who built a business on social media... but as a brain enthusiast, I have to say that social media is one of the most destructive things humanity has brought onto itself.

It started with a great idea - to help us stay connected with each other. That's not the problem.

When we post about ourselves on social media, it feels good. When we get engagement on those posts, it feels even better. In fact, it lights up our brain's reward network to a similar level of getting drugs, sex, or money. This makes us addicted to posting only the best things going on in our lives... and keeping the other things quiet.

Finding interesting and/or entertaining content on social media has a similar effect on our brain's reward center. It makes us want to scroll for hours so we can keep finding those cute cat videos. This is why whenever you get bored, go to the bathroom, or feel like procrastinating from work... you grab your phone.

Take a break from social media from time to time. You'll feel happier, more motivated, and less stressed. The cats will be there when you open up the app again.

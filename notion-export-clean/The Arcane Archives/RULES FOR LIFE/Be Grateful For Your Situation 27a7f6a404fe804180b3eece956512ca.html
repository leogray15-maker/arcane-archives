Be Grateful For Your Situation.

I thought fuck it, I'll start writing a book on my life.

for some reason, my intuition told me so.

so, I started.

and here's a key lesson from it :

I moved to this school in year 2, I was maybe 9, I don't remember.

I came up around 11-12 o clock, they didn't introduce me, they just said go make friends with the kids in the playground.

I go into the playground. My first greeting was "are you asian"?

I didn't know what that meant really.

I didn’t know what "asian" was.

But my dad was brown. That's why he asked me that.

I'm not dark in skin tone. I'm not light.

I'm in the middle. People now say I look spanish/egyptian/arabic, my goatee probably speaks a lot.

i answered "yes" to his question.

I don't remember exactly what he said.

Maybe it was something like "I hate people like you"

I was confused, what is asian? I must've said something wrong, it was probably something funny.

Anyway. I brushed it off.

Next thing I remember is walking around the playground. I didn't know how to socialise, so I'd just stay there. Away from people.

The playtime ends.

The teachers come, they introduce me to my "year"

They ask "who do you want to be your buddy?"

The guy at the front.

He looked like me with a nicer haircut.

He looked friendly. Like me. And also high status. He was in front of the line.

He was a great guy. I liked him.

I made a nice friend group.

They were nice. I don't think they'd want to hurt me.

One kid, we'll call him S.

S was cool. He was asian ans muslim, but he also looked chinese asian. not too much, but a little bit.

He must've been part of that afghani tribe where they look like asians.

He was fastest in the class, along with K.

K was cool too, he was polish.

He was surprised when I told him I was too. He kept it friendly. Didn't want to hurt me.

K was also quick. Very quick. Almost at the same level as S.

The guy at the front of the line?

His name was also an S.

But you know what? I don't remember much of him. So we won't even have to mention him much.

Now I'm 18 writing this, I go to the shisha lounge pretty often.

His dad works there.

A great guy, but I call him "brother" now, as I do with everyone.

I noticed him when I was in the shisha lounge with my friends, I was pretty high, and he put me on facetime with his son.

I've seen his son in the gym, calm guy.

I was high tho, didn't know much to say.

But you know... let me skip a bit.

S, the quick guy that looks asian.

He was the "group leader"

Knew what to do. He was cool with everyone.  He'd stand up for everyone. He'd make the girls laugh, he was top dog.

i soon became best friends with him.

He would come to my house in the morning before school, we'd play the wii.

At the time, everyone had ps3s, xbox 360s. I had a wii, only thing my mum could afford.

He would come to my house in the morning and we'd play a football game on the wii, it was inazuma 11. What a cool game.

It was I think one of the only things my dad bought me.

It was £40.

That was fucking expensive.

I kept begging for it to my mum, I never asked my dad for anything, I barely saw him.

Working night shifts, sleeping all day.

One day. He buys the game for me.

I just simply think "what the fuck"

How have we got the money?

interesting story with my dad, we'll go talk about him in a different chapter.

It came the next day.

It came specifically before school, I lied it down on the desk so I'd look at it right before I go school.

The whole school day, I'd imagine playing it.

The day finishes, I run home.

I'm playing it.

I love it.

And that fast kid?

S?

He comes over before school, and he's amazed.

"my house has none of this"

I relate, I didn't have anything before either.

But still, he has none of this?

I thought all the kids have xboxs and playstations.

This kid... has nothing?

How is he the highest status in the class. How is he so confident. How is he such a leader?

Anyway. I didn't question it much.

We was best friends.

Each day after school we'd take my bmx and we'd both use the same bike to get home.

i'd stand on the "pegs" of the back wheel, he'd drive. he was athletic.

Our house was 2 opposite ways.

School bag, school clothes, we'd somehow get home.

We would fall a lot, get a lot of bruises and cuts. That was fun.

But every time we'd get near his house...

I'd think "is this it?"

He lived in a kind of "immigrant" estate in the uk.

You know what I mean if you're here.

A place where all the immigrants live.

His house wasn't a house.

It was a door.

A door.

I'd see him go in and open it.

There was a staircase.

That's it.

I always wondered "what actually is this?"

I'd come to his place later on. We was best friends.

His mum was a nice lady. She'd teach me the quran when I came.

I'd never seen his dad. probably working.

He had a very small room.

No consoles in the house.

No tvs.

No phones.

Just a bouncy ball he got from school, and a slingshot.

His mum didn't even have a phone, I don't think.

i had a samsung galaxy young 2.

It was a small phone. It was my first "real" phone, my other phone was a nokia that my mum used to have.

But he was fascinated by my phone.

Not fascinated, but it was cool to him.

He had a bouncy ball, and some slingshot.

That was his entertainment.

We would go outside, and we'd play with his deflated football on the road.

My mum would come pick me up after I messaged her later.

But that's all I remember from S.

My next memory was rather disturbing.

I had a couple friends over. This is a couple years later.

I had an xbox 360 I'm pretty sure.

We had fun. Watching scary movies like chucky.

it was dark.

We heard a knock on the door.

"my mum will open it"

She did open it.

"d, it's your friend, he's worried"

My friend?

Who?

S.

He remembered where I lived.

I lived 20 mins away from him.

He ran away from home.

He had a big scar on his back. Red and bloody.

His dad beat him with a stick.

He was a very good kid.

Stood up for everyone.

Fastest in the school.

Funny.

He'd ran away from home.

But you know what?

He wasn't crying.

He was still cheerful.

He didn't want anyone to be worried, he just joined in with us.

We was teaching him how to play black ops 2, and showing him movies.

The setting is chill now. Normal.

But i'm in my mind thinking "It's crazy, how did S remember my house?"

He doesn't have a phone.

But now I realise. He may of remembered it because of a dark reason.

Incase he had to run away.

He stayed here for hours.

we'd have fun. Watch movies. Play bo2.

But then. We heard a knock on the door.

S said "it might be my parents, please don't open it."

we shut all the lights, I told my mum.

They knocked, to no answer.

They went.

The night ended off with my mum calling social security,  he was later on picked up by them. And I think he went into a foster home.

I forgot about him shortly after.

Maybe a year later. My mum calls me downstairs.

"its your friend"

My friend? Must be (insert name)

No. It was S.

What?

Where did he come from?

he was wearing normal clothes.

He never had any "normal" clothes when we was younger.

He would show up to school "non uniform days" with school clothes.

you know how kids would pick their outfit their coolest outfit the day before?

he didn't he couldn't.

but he'd still remain the same person, still confident, still smile on his face, normal.

while other kids would silently compete with eachothers outfits, he wouldn't even bat an eye.

And when he's not in school, he'd wear a thobe, the one he went to mosque with.

But he's not at my door, he's on the other side of the fence.

"how you doing bro?"

I'm in shock

"good, how you doing?"

"I'm good. Thank you"

"I gotta go now"

"alright, see you later"

He went.

I've never seen him since.

He didn't message me on facebook, he didn't text me.

He came to my house, from I don't know where.

I hope he's doing well.

I know he is.

He's 18-20 now. Doing something I don't know. But I know he's good.

That was the story of S.

he didn't have shit, he didn't have a tv, a phone, normal clothes.

but he was still confident, made the girls laugh in school, the "group" leader, quickest in the class.

even though his dad beat him daily, no normal clothes, no technology, a house with one small floor.

he was cheerful, happy.

9-10 years old at the time, you would've thought he was living in a mansion.

but no, quite the opposite.

I didn't have much either, all the kids had an xbox 360, ps3, iphones.

I had a wii, and a tiny phone that couldn't install most apps.

I wanted more. I wasn't happy.

S, with nothing, he was happier than me, more confident than anyone in the class, even the guys who had ps3s.

he didn't have anything.

he learnt not to compare with others at a young age, rather compete.

he'd compete in races with the other kids, he'd compete in skills.

he wouldn't let his way of living change him one bit.

so, here's the lesson.

don't compare yourself with others, rather compete.

accept your situation, everyone runs their own race.

be grateful, there's people who would love to be in your position in life.

be like S. don't be envious of others. rather be curious in life.

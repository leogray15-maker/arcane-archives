50. Failing Pendulums

Failing Pendulums emphasizes how to handle negative thoughts and situations without allowing them to control you or drain your energy. It is about shifting your mindset and energy to avoid falling into the traps that pendulums set.

Declaration:

When you actively focus on what you do not want in your life, you inadvertently invite those very things into your experience. This is because thoughts are not inherently yours; they come from external sources and affect your vibration. When you experience a negative thought, the first step is to accept it without judgment. By accepting that the unpleasant situation or thought exists in your life, you can then release its power over you.

Instead of denying the thought or suppressing it, admit its truth for the moment. For example, if you are thinking, "I am so broke," acknowledge it, but do not linger there. Once you accept it, shift your thoughts toward the direction you actually want. You can say something like, "Yes, I am broke, but every day I am improving. Life is getting better, and money is coming to me. all in due time."

This process of acceptance and reaffirmation raises your vibration, which in turn disconnects you from the pendulum. By doing this, you avoid feeding the pendulum with negative energy, which would otherwise latch onto you.

Interpretation:

Pendulums are like external forces that are constantly trying to provoke a reaction from you, drawing you into negative emotional states. When you react emotionally, particularly with anger, frustration, or other negative feelings, you fall into their trap. The pendulum feeds off your energy, and as a result, you feel exhausted, frustrated, or defeated.

The key is to recognize that pendulums only gain power when you react to them. When you give in to their provocations, it's as if you're falling asleep while still awake—making decisions or behaving in ways you later regret, because you're acting under their influence.

Instead of focusing on the obstacles (which are not created by you but are part of your life path), focus on your attitude toward those obstacles. Your attitude determines the quality of your life. The goal is not to block or suppress your emotions, but rather to shift your perspective on the situation.

  • Accept the situation as it is (without resistance, stoicism is about acknowledgement)
  • Change your attitude toward it. See the positive in the situation and recognize that it will pass.
  • Do not try to fight pendulums; they exist, whether you like it or not. You cannot defeat them, but you can ignore them. By doing so, you stop feeding them with your energy, and they lose their grip on you.

Practical Steps to Failing Pendulums:

  1. Accept Negative Thoughts: The first step when you have a negative thought is to accept it. Don’t deny that it exists or try to push it away. Acknowledge that it’s true for the moment and validate your feelings.

    Example: If you’re thinking, "I’m so broke," just admit, "Yes, I’m broke right now." Accept the situation without guilt or shame, when you fight it, you're just falsifying reality.

  1. Shift Your Focus: After acknowledging the negative thought, immediately shift your focus to a more positive direction. Change the thought by affirming something better. For example, instead of staying in the belief of being broke, shift to, "Every day I’m getting closer to financial freedom." This positive affirmation helps to raise your vibration and creates a new energy in your life.
  1. See the Positive in Negative Situations: Rather than blocking or suppressing negative emotions, find the lesson or the opportunity in the difficult situation. This helps to raise your frequency and frees you from the hold of the pendulum.
  1. Do Not React Emotionally: When faced with provocations, try to avoid reacting emotionally, as this gives pendulums the energy they need to grow stronger. Instead, respond with neutrality or positivity. This detachment weakens the pendulum’s hold.
  1. Ignore the Pendulums: Once you recognize a pendulum, simply ignore it. Don’t feed it with your emotions, thoughts, or energy. The more you resist or engage with the pendulum, the more power you give it. By choosing not to play in the pendulum’s game, you maintain your energy and prevent it from affecting you.
  1. Reaffirm the Positive Outcome: When negative thoughts or situations arise, affirm the positive future you are moving toward. Even in difficult times, keep your focus on what you want to create rather than getting bogged down in what you don’t want.
  1. Shift from Tragedy to Comedy: If you face a challenging or negative situation, try to change your perspective and find the humor or lightness in the situation. This shift in attitude transforms the energy of the event and helps you avoid falling into the pendulum’s trap.

Key Takeaways:

  • Pendulums feed off negative energy. By focusing on what you don’t want, you inadvertently attract more of it into your life.
  • The key to dealing with negative thoughts is to accept them, then shift your focus toward what you do want.
  • Your attitude toward situations determines your experience—change your attitude to stay positive and avoid feeding pendulums.
  • Do not suppress emotions but shift your perspective on them, seeing the positive side of even negative situations.
  • Ignore pendulums rather than trying to fight them, and in doing so, you take away their power to affect you.

Success in Transurfing comes from the ability to change your attitude and perspective in the face of life's challenges. By accepting the negative, then transforming it into something positive, you are able to stay aligned with your true path and keep your energy free from the influence of pendulums.

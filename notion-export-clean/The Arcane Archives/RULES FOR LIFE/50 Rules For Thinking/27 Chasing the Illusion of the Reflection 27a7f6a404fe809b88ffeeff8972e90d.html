27. Chasing the Illusion of the Reflection:

Imagine standing in front of a giant mirror, staring at the reflection of your life. You look at it, and it doesn’t match what you want. You get frustrated, you try to change it, you try to manipulate it. But here's the truth: you’re focusing on the wrong thing. You're trying to change the reflection in the mirror, but you should be changing the image behind the mirror—the image that comes from your own thoughts, energy, and perspective. The reflection is just a result of your inner world, so trying to change it directly will only get you stuck in a loop.

This is what happens when people react to their reality in frustration—when things aren’t going the way they hoped. They look at their current situation, and their mind tries to control or manipulate the outcome, as though you can somehow influence the external world by sheer willpower. But the real key isn’t in pushing or forcing the world around you to bend to your will. The key is in changing what’s behind the reflection: your mindset, your thoughts, and your internal energy.

What you need to do is stop focusing on the external world for a moment. The universe, in this case, is like a giant mirror, reflecting back to you exactly what’s happening inside your mind. You’re not meant to change the reflection; you’re meant to change the image you are projecting onto it. This is why Zeland talks about shifting your focus. You must begin forming new, positive thoughts that align with your desires and intentions. It’s not about trying to fix the mirror—it’s about changing your thoughts to match the world you want to create.

It’s easy to get frustrated when things don’t seem to be changing, when it feels like your efforts are futile. But the key is consistency. If, despite what’s happening in your life right now, you can still hold on to those positive thoughts and keep focusing on the life you want, the reflection will change. It might not happen immediately, but over time, as you maintain this new mindset, your reality will shift. The universe will reflect those changes back to you.

However, here’s where it gets tricky: this process isn’t instant. You may find that for a time, nothing seems to happen. It’s like you’ve planted seeds, but there’s no visible growth yet. This can be discouraging, but the key to remember is this: it’s not that the universe is delaying your desires. What’s happening is that the changes you’re making in your mind, in your metaphysical world, take time to be reflected in the physical world. The energy you release, your thoughts, your focus—they all take time to manifest.

Now, in this waiting period, when nothing seems to be happening, life will try to throw you off course. Life will tempt you to look back, to get distracted, to fall into negativity. These distractions are what Zeland calls “pendulums.” These pendulums are like forces that swing back and forth, trying to pull you into negative patterns—whether that’s doubt, frustration, anger, or fear. The universe may try to provoke you to react to these pendulums, but here's the thing: if you react, if you give in to that negativity, you’re only slowing your progress. You’re giving away your energy, which ultimately delays the reflection of your desires.

Think of it like this: the physical world is like a mirror. It can’t change instantly—it takes time to reflect what’s happening inside. And while you’re in that waiting phase, when you feel like nothing is shifting, remember: this is the moment of testing. The moment you decide not to react to the negative, to stay focused on your positive thoughts, is when the universe starts to shift things for you. It’s like walking through a storm while keeping your focus on the destination. The storm may rage, but your focus remains unwavering.

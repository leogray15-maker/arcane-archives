11. Confidence

Let’s break this down in a way that makes it super simple.

You know how sometimes we feel unsure of ourselves? Like we don't have enough confidence to do something? We might think we need to "gain" confidence or try really hard to feel sure of ourselves, but here's the surprising truth: you don't need to chase confidence at all. In fact, the secret to having confidence is to stop chasing it.

Here’s the trick: Confidence comes when you let go of the need for it. It’s like this: imagine you’re holding onto something really tightly—like a rope, or a dream, or a goal. You’re so focused on it, you’re afraid it’ll slip away. This fear makes you hold on even tighter. But, when you let go and stop putting all that importance on the rope or goal, you feel free, relaxed, and naturally confident. You stop worrying if it’s going to work out because it’s no longer about “winning” or “losing.” You just trust that everything is unfolding as it should.

Here’s how this works:

  • Lack of confidence happens when we make things too important. We fear we’re not good enough, we worry about others’ opinions, or we stress about not achieving enough. And the more we focus on these things, the more they grow. It becomes a cycle: the more you care about being perfect, the more you feel uncertain. uncertainty is the exact opposite of confidence. uncertainty is thinking about the way you walk. when you do... people see it subconsciously, and others see you as "trying too hard"
  • To break this cycle, you have to stop making things so important. You don’t need to fight for your self-worth or constantly prove yourself to others. When you stop raising the importance of anything—whether it’s a task, an opinion, or a goal—you stop creating pressure, and the fear and worry go away. LIVE IN YOUR OWN BUBBLE. act as if no one is even there. it's just you and your goal.
  • The moment you stop needing to prove something to others is when you naturally become more confident. You stop feeling the need to justify your actions, and suddenly, you feel more at peace and in control of your life. You start living authentically, and that brings you confidence.

"One Particular Myth : That One Of The Hero"

I just talk about quotes that stick out to me.

And this one weirdly did.

I did some digging into it, and I found out what he meant by “one particular myth was given a central role : that of the hero”

It goes into say that for Jung it was this :

“For Jung, this represented the life of the individual, attempting to become independent and to free himself from the mother”

I don’t know why it says incest after that, let’s just ignore it and take it as old language.

But anyway…

Let’s actually dig into what he meant by this.

First... I want to give you the big picture of what he meant by “one particular myth was given a central role : that of the hero”

This breaks down what it MEANS to be a hero.

This is what reverse engineering a hero in the modern day looks like :

We start dependent on others (like living at home)

We face challenges that force us to grow (like the hero's battles)

We emerge as independent individuals (the hero's victory)

I asked Claude what Jung meant, and he meant exactly this :

  • The hero starts by being dependant on others
  • The hero then faces challenges on the JOURNEY to become independent
  • Then the hero emerges as an independent individual.

In stories and fiction, the hero has a journey.

Let’s take batman for example.

He has a mission to get rid of crime in Gotham, it’s something like defeating the joker

And then after that’s done, he has the “hero’s victory”

For us and carl Jung, it’s something more on the minor scale.

He talks about the heros “myth” is the journey on becoming an independent person.

And this now is so true…

Everyone in life experiences the same thing

  • They live at home with their family
  • They find their path to become “independent” which is make money and move out… etc.
  • Once they move out and are “free” and independent, they then find their true selves and that’s the rest of your life.

When you look at the big picture that’s really what it is.

You cannot experience life until you’ve actually lived alone and became independent.

So let’s take this into our perspective….

The “heros” battle is actually just moving out.

And moving out takes one thing…

Money.

We have a mission to slay the dragon as the hero, and this dragon is literally making money.

After you become financially free, you actually “unlock” the whole world.

Right now you’re pinned to a certain location on the map, because you don’t have financial freedom and location freedom.

BUT once you “slay the dragon” you have unlocked the whole map.

Crazy that even carl Jung says it… and it applies to us VERY directly.

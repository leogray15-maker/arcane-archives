The Secret Behind Bullets That Sell

The secret behind bullets that sell (and how it works) - so you can install it into your copy - making it the single biggest hidden, untapped asset in your copy PLUS the exact AI prompts to repeatedly create selling bullets with ease.

Note: Use Claude AI

ChatGPT is inferior to Claude.

Bullets are one of those things is if you're good at them, you utilise it. If you're not good at writing bullets, then don't ever include it in your copy.

Shitty bullets cheapen your copy. So it's either add some good bullets or don't add them at all.

It's that simple.

First let's cover the basics. Single stack bullets look something like this:

"The secrets behind ___"

"How to use ___"

"The single biggest ___"

These bullets are ok. They don't have enough pulling power to get you to think.

Here's what the difference is: GOOD copy gets you thinking/feeling about it while they're reading the page, GREAT copy gets you thinking/feeling/obsessing about it when they're off the page.

So let's write some great copy, and here's how you do that using triple stack bullets.

A bullet summarises the outcome, the product, the mechanism, removes objections and describes the product that, makes it way more appealing than it would be — in short, they add dimension and tell you things that you never knew.

Here's the structure of triple stack bullets.

Statement/benefit/mechanism/feature format / etc… + (“by the way”) + and ________.

The addition of “by the way” + and ______ is what allows you to pack more punch, so here’s what that looks like by taking the single stacks above and triple stacking it.

Notice the three thoughts in there that support each other and make each other stronger, making them complete.

Here are some examples:

  • The secrets behind fame (and how it works) – so you can engineer it in your business – making it the single biggest hidden, untapped asset in your business…
  • How to use the fame framework to increase the perceived value of your products and services – allowing things that cost next to nothing to be sold up to 1,000,000% mark up (and it works because of this single human psychological trick that’s coded in our DNA) –hint: humans are incapable of putting value on things in isolation…
  • The single biggest mistake businesses make (and why it’s a mistake to focus on marketing) – and what to focus on instead – hint: it’s problem-solving, but it’s not what you think (because we use the solution/problem model instead)…

You will notice how under each bullet, I’m adding where they can find the information. This connects it to the product and adds a payoff so they anchor it mentally as to where to find it.

And the other idea is that each should be able to carry the sale by themselves.

And one more thing…you’ll notice I trail off each bullet with …, the reason is it seeps into the next bullet, whenever you add the … to sentences, that causes the mind to seep down into the next bullet creating connectivity.

Now you know my stance on templates but this is the exception simply because bullets are easy to comprehend and understand.

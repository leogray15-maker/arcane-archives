I WILL EXPLAIN THE TACTICS NOW.

BLACKOUT CURTAINS/SLEEP MASK.

self explanatory you don't want ANY light in your room while sleeping to max out your sleep quality, and they are like £20, cheap.

you can also accompany this with a sleep mask, which has helped me massively and I can never go back to sleeping without it.

honestly, if you have the sleeping mask you don't need the blackout curtains.

but the blackout curtains help me focus much more in work too.

COVER EVERY LIGHT IN YOUR ROOM.

I am just pretty extreme with this, I cover every single light in my room with like a piece of clothing, even if it's placebo, in my mind it helps me.

or alternatively... you can just get a sleep mask.

NIGHT SHIFT AND NIGHT LIGHT MODE ON YOUR PHONE AND PC.

just gets rid of blue light, you should be consuming as much blue light as possible in the morning, preferably from the sun and shit, but at night, avoid, there's a reason the sun goes down.

turn on the "night shift" on your phone and pc a couple hours before bed, you'll see the effects.

and you can accompany that with...

RED LIGHT BEFORE BED.

there was a study or something that red light actually produces melatonin in you.

so... you probably have leds or something.

you should switch them to red maybe like an hour before bed, also bring a girl over, it also stimulates something in them.

EIGHT SLEEP MATTRESS

an eight sleep mattress is a company that makes this mattress cover that regulates the temperature to your body temperature every night.

so you will never have to worry about waking up sweating, etc, I hate that shit.

however... the only downside is that this costs £2,000.

I mean, have it in your wishlist, the vision will be actualized anyway.

LIGHT ALARM

I don't have this, but I was looking into it the other day, it's basically this thing where you don't have an alarm on your phone, but you have a huge light next to your bed that turns on at a specific time, and mimics sunlight.

which is probably way healthier to wake up to than a shitty iPhone alarm.

SLEEP CYCLE ALARM

I use this one, its an app that has an alarm feature where there's like a 30 minute time period where the alarm can go off.

and the app listens to any movement that you make in those 30 minutes, so you might shuffle around or some shit, which means you are in a light sleep mode at that point, then the alarm goes off, and you won't wake up in the middle of REM sleep so you will not feel groggy and tired.

this will kickstart your day in a positive mood, might be placebo, might be not...

either way it's good.

I KNOW PEOPLE LOVE TO USE AN AURA RING.

but I personally don't see the point, my body will know when it is rested.

and I don't want to base my day off the online stats that tell my body how it feels, trust yourself.

COLD ROOM TO SLEEP IN

self explanatory, sleeping in the cold is amazing for me, I go into a warm shower at night before bed, and keep the window open during it so my room gets cold.

just my preference.

EMPTY YOUR MIND/ JOURNAL/ PLAN FOR NEXT DAY

this one is very important to me, trying to sleep when you have thoughts in your mind is the hardest thing.

because you are about to fall asleep then you wake up thinking shit... I should've done this instead of this, etc

this is why after my shower, in the cold room, red light on, ambient music, I journal about any thoughts that i'm having, in my little death note journal.

when I can't dive in to my thoughts because there is nothing there, I just journal about my day structure tomorrow, just so I have a clear direction.

now I've planned my day out, I don't have to worry at night about what i'm gonna do tomorrow, I can just sleep knowing there's a new day tomorrow, and any things I had to do, will move on to tomorrow.

I can sleep at rest.

EXHAUST YOURSELF DURING THE DAY

you have probably had your mum say after a long walk or a long day.

"you'll sleep well tonight"

its true.

if all you did was play video games for a whole day, you are probably gonna stay up the whole night, because humans are meant to use their brain and move around, not sit on fortnite 1v1 pandvil boxfights all night listening to unreleased juice wrld

HOW DO I EXHAUST MYSELF?

I exhaust my mind in the morning, with deep work.

exhaust my body straight after, in the gym.

exhaust my body even more in the evening, with jump rope and or boxing bag.

sleep is for recovering so you can put your body to good use, if you haven't done anything, what does it need to recover from?

I will not eat until the hunt is finished.

NO HYPE MUSIC, NO DOPAMINE ACTIVITIES BEFORE BED, NO VIDEO GAMES.

listen, your brain will fire off those dopamine signals which were meant to be used for in the day.

like in the caveman days, dopamine was fired off when they saw an animal they could kill for food.

not when they were scrolling on an app which mimicked a casino slot machine animation.

and no hype music, because dopamine.

listen to lo-fi or some shit, that's what I do for fun anyway.

you can find my personal no lyric playlist here.

NO EATING 2 HOURS BEFORE BED, NO DRINKING 1 HOUR BEFORE BED.

no eating because we want our body to be focused on recovery not digestion.

and honestly, you can eat something before bed, but I would stick to only fat/protein, since carbs are harder to digest.

no drinking anything because we don't want to wake up in the middle of a night for a piss, interrupting our sleep.

CLEAN ROOM BEFORE BED

for peace of mind, you don't want to go sleep knowing you have a dirty room to work in tomorrow that you'll have to clean.

also it will give the effect of, the day is done, now its a clean room, new day.

you will wake up with a positive vibration.

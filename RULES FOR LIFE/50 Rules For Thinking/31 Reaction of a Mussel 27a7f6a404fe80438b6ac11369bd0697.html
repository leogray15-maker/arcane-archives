31. Reaction of a Mussel

When you encounter a negative situation in your life, what's your immediate response? For many of us, the instinct is to react strongly—maybe with frustration, anger, or fear. You feel the tension rise, your body responds, and you’re caught in the emotional undertow. But here’s the interesting part: When something positive happens, people often have a much more muted reaction. It’s almost like it doesn’t even register. It’s like we’re conditioned to react to the negative with intensity and the positive with indifference.

Zeland calls this the “mussel” reaction. Picture a mussel: it’s a creature that closes up tight and doesn’t respond to much. It’s almost as if it’s hiding or protecting itself. A lot of people act like this, closing themselves off to positive events and only reacting to the negative ones. This is a subconscious habit, a way of protecting ourselves from disappointment, but it’s also a habit that reinforces negativity in our lives.

Now, what if we could step out of that mussel-like state and react with intention? What if, instead of allowing circumstances to control our emotional state, we actively chose how to respond to both positive and negative situations? That’s where Zeland is guiding us: the power is in conscious choice. You are not a slave to your automatic reactions. You can decide how you want to engage with the world, no matter what is happening around you.

When something negative happens—let’s say someone cuts you off in traffic or you receive bad news—it’s easy to spiral into anger or frustration. But if you can lift yourself above that instinct, you can choose a response that aligns with your desired outcome. Instead of getting angry, you can let it pass without attaching any emotion to it, or you can decide to be peaceful, knowing that getting upset will only add more negativity to your life.

This is all about controlling your thoughts, because your thoughts directly influence your reality. If your dominant thoughts are negative—if you let anger, frustration, or fear control your mind—the world reflects that. The Universe will mirror your emotional state, and you’ll find that life responds with more of the same negativity. On the flip side, when you consciously choose positive thoughts, when you aim for peace and calm no matter what’s happening around you, your life will start to reflect those things.

This doesn’t mean suppressing emotions. That would only backfire and lead to internal conflict. It means adjusting your attitude toward life. You don’t need to be reactive. You don’t need to let the negative things that happen dictate your mood or energy. You can be like the sun on a cloudy day—shining through regardless of the clouds that try to cover it up.

You control the narrative instead of being controlled by it. And once you start making this shift, you’ll notice that the world starts to change around you, too. The negative events will lessen, and you’ll experience more of the positive things you’ve been focusing on.
